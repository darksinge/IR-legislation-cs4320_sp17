UTAH CHILD CARE LICENSING ACT                         .  Short title.              This chapter is known as the  Utah Child Care Licensing Act.               Section  .  Section            is enacted to read                         .  Definitions.              As used in this chapter                     Child care  means continuous care and supervision of four or more children under             years of age, in lieu of care ordinarily provided by parents in their own home, for at least four but          less than    hours a day, for compensation.                    Child care program  means a child care facility or program operated by a person          pursuant to a license issued in accordance with this chapter.              Section  .  Section            is enacted to read                         .  Child Care Licensing Advisory Committee.                   There is established the Child Care Licensing Advisory Committee to advise the          department on rules promulgated by the department pursuant to this chapter.  It shall be composed          of nine members who shall be appointed by the executive director.  Two members shall represent          consumers of child care programs licensed pursuant to this chapter, four members shall be          providers licensed pursuant to this chapter at least two of whom shall be facility owner operators,          two members shall be health care providers, and one member shall be an individual with expertise          in early childhood development.                   Of the initial members of the Child Care Licensing Advisory Committee, the executive          director shall appoint three to four year terms, two to three year terms, and two to two year terms.           Thereafter, appointments shall be for four year terms, except for those members who have been                 appointed to complete an unexpired term.  The committee shall annually elect a chairman from its         membership.                  The advisory committee shall meet at least quarterly, or more frequently as determined         by the executive director, the chairman, or three or more members of the committee.  Five         members constitute a quorum and a vote of the majority of the members present constitutes an         action of the committee.                  Advisory committee members shall be reimbursed for their actual and necessary         expenses incurred in the performance of their duties as established by the director of the Division         of Finance, pursuant to Section   A      .  Advisory committee members may decline          reimbursement.              Section  .  Section            is enacted to read                         .  Duties of the department. Day treatment is provided in lieu of, or in coordination with, a more restrictive         residential or inpatient environment or service.                         Department  means the Department of Human Services.                         Director  means the director of the Office of Licensing. Executive director  means the executive director of the Department of Human          Services. Inpatient treatment  means a    hour hospital based, live in treatment service          for persons with emotional, psychological, developmental, or behavioral dysfunctions,          impairments, or chemical dependencies, whose current functioning is such that they cannot live          independently or in a less restrictive environment.  Inpatient treatment services may include, but          are not limited to, assessment, examinations, diagnosis, individual, group, and family counseling,          skills training, medication, or detoxification. Residential support  means arranging for or providing the necessities of life                  as a protective service to individuals or families who are disabled or who are experiencing a         dislocation or emergency which prevents them from providing these services for themselves or         their families. In residential         treatment, individuals are assisted in acquiring the social and behavioral skills necessary for living         independently in the community.                            Secure treatment  means    hour specialized residential treatment or care for          persons whose current functioning is such that they cannot live independently or in a less          restrictive environment.  Secure treatment differs from residential treatment to the extent that it          requires intensive supervision, locked doors, and other security measures which are imposed on          residents with neither their consent nor control.

 Committee Note:

 General Description:
            
12

    
    This bill modifies the Uniform Driver License Act by amending provisions relating to
            
13

    
the motorcycle rider education program.
            
14

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
15

    
    This bill:
            
16

    
    .    provides that a person who is at least 15 years 6 months of age and has been issued
            
17

    
a learner permit may enroll in and complete a motorcycle rider training course if the
            
18

    
course is conducted on a closed course that:
            
19

    
        .    is not conducted on a public highway;
            
20

    
        .    is approved by the Driver License Division; and        
            
21

    
        .    meets or exceeds established national standards for motorcycle rider training
            
22

    
courses prescribed by the Motorcycle Safety Foundation; and        
            
23

    
    .    makes technical changes.
            
24

    
 Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
            
25

    
    None

 Other Special Clauses:

    None

     53-3-903.  Motorcycle Rider Education Program.
            
35

    
    (1) (a)  The division shall develop standards for and administer the Motorcycle Rider
            
36

    
Education Program.
            
37

    
    (b)  The division shall make rules in accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah
            
38

    
Administrative Rulemaking Act, to implement this chapter.
            
39

    
    (2)  The program shall include:
            
40

    
    (a)  a novice rider training course;
            
41

    
    (b)  a rider training course for experienced riders; and
            
42

    
    (c)  an instructor training course.
            
43

    
    (3)  The division may expand the program to include:
            
44

    
    (a)  enhancing public awareness of motorcycle riders;
            
45

    
    (b)  increasing the awareness of motorcycle riders of the effects of alcohol and drugs;
            
46

    
    (c)  motorcycle rider skills improvement;
            
47

    
    (d)  program and other motorcycle safety promotion; and
            
48

    
    (e)  improvement of motorcycle licensing efforts.
            
49

    
    (4) (a)  [Rider] Subject to the restriction in Subsection (4)(b), rider training courses
            
50

    
shall be open to all residents of the state who:
            
51

    
    (i)  are at least 15 years 6 months of age; and
            
52

    
    (ii)  either hold a valid [driver's] learner permit or driver license for any classification or
            
53

    
are eligible for a [temporary] motorcycle [learner's] learner permit.
            
54

    
    (b)  A person who has been issued a learner permit may enroll in and complete a rider
            
55

    
training course if the course is conducted on a closed course that:
            
56

    
    (i)  is not conducted on a public highway;
            
57

    
    (ii)  is approved by the division; and
            
58

    
    (iii)  meets or exceeds established national standards for motorcycle rider training

            
59


    
courses prescribed by the Motorcycle Safety Foundation.
            
60

    
    [(b)] (c)  An adequate number of novice rider training courses shall be provided to meet
            
61

    
the reasonably anticipated needs of all persons in the state who are eligible and who desire to
            
62

    
participate in the program.
            
63

    
    [(c)] (d)  Program delivery may be phased in over a reasonable period of time.
            
64

    
    (5) (a)  The division may enter into contracts with either public or private institutions to
            
65

    
provide a rider training course approved by the division.
            
66

    
    (b)  The institution shall issue certificates of completion in the manner and form
            
67

    
prescribed by the director to persons who satisfactorily complete the requirements of the
            
68

    
course.
            
69

    
    (c)  An institution conducting a rider training course may charge a reasonable tuition fee
            
70

    
to cover the cost of offering the course.
            
71

    
    (d) (i)  The division may use program funds to defray its own expenses in administering
            
72

    
the program.
            
73

    
    (ii)  The division may reimburse entities that offer approved courses for actual expenses
            
74

    
incurred in offering the courses, up to a limit established by the division based upon available
            
75

    
program funds.

    (iii)  Any reimbursement paid to an entity must be entirely reflected by the entity in
            
77

    
reduced course enrollment fees for students.

    (6) (a)  Standards for the motorcycle rider training courses, including standards for

80

    
approval of instructors shall meet or exceed established national standards for motorcycle rider
            
81

    
training courses prescribed by the Motorcycle Safety Foundation.

    (b)  Motorcycle rider training courses shall be taught only by instructors approved under

Section 

53-3-904

.

Section 52-3-1 is amended to read:24          52-3-1. Employment of relatives prohibited -- Exceptions.25          (1)  For purposes of this section:26          (a)  "Appointee" means an employee whose salary, wages, pay, or compensation is paid27     from public funds.28          (b)  "Chief administrative officer" means the person who has ultimate responsibility for29     the operation of the department or agency of the state or a political subdivision.30          (c)  "Public officer" means a person who holds a position that is compensated by public31     funds.32          (d)  "Relative" means a father, mother, husband, wife, son, daughter, sister, brother,33     grandfather, grandmother, uncle, aunt, nephew, niece, grandson, granddaughter, first cousin,34     mother-in-law, father-in-law, brother-in-law, sister-in-law, son-in-law, [or] daughter-in-law,35     stepdaughter, or stepson.36          (2) (a)  No public officer may employ, appoint, or vote for or recommend the37     appointment of a relative in or to any position or employment, when the salary, wages, pay, or38     compensation of the appointee will be paid from public funds and the appointee will be directly39     supervised by a relative, except as follows:40          (i)  the appointee is eligible or qualified to be employed by a department or agency of41     the state or a political subdivision of the state as a result of his compliance with civil service42     laws or regulations, or merit system laws or regulations;43          (ii)  the appointee will be compensated from funds designated for vocational training;44          (iii)  the appointee will be employed for a period of 12 weeks or less;45          (iv)  the appointee is a volunteer as defined by the employing entity;46          (v)  the appointee is the only person available, qualified, or eligible for the position;

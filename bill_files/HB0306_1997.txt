CHOICE OF PROVIDERS IN HUMAN SERVICES                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  J.  Brent Haymond          AN ACT RELATING TO HUMAN SERVICES  REQUIRING SERVICES AND SUPPORTS         TO THE AGED AND PERSONS WITH A DISABILITY THROUGH THE USE OF         VOUCHERS  AND REQUIRING THE DIVISION OF HEALTH CARE FINANCING TO         SUBMIT A DEMONSTRATION PROJECT.         This act enacts uncodified material.          Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .   Use of vouchers for services and supports.                   As used in this section                a    Approved provider  means a person, partnership, or corporation that has been          approved by a service providing agency to provide services to eligible persons.               b    Directors  means the directors of service providing agencies.               c    Eligible person  means a person who is eligible for services and supports                i   under Tile   A, Chapter  , Services to People with Disabilities                ii   for intermediate care facilities for the mentally retarded financed by the Division of          Health Care Financing under the Medicaid program  and               iii   for home and community based services to aged persons under Title   A, Chapter  ,          Aging and Adult Services                d    Service providing agency  or  service providing agencies  means an agency that          provides services and supports to an eligible person, including                i   the Division of Health Care Financing within the Department of Health                ii   the Division of Services to People with Disabilities within the Department of Human          Services  and               iii   the Division of Aging and Adult Services within the Department of Human Services           and              e    Voucher  means a document that               i   is issued by a service providing agency to an eligible person, his parent, or his guardian               ii   describes the services and supports that may be received in exchange for the voucher               iii   lists approved providers with whom the voucher may be redeemed in exchange for         services               iv   may be used by an eligible person, his parent, or his guardian to purchase services and         supports from an approved provider               v   includes a maximum dollar value                vi   states the period of time within which the voucher shall be used by the eligible person,          his parent, or his guardian to purchase services and supports from an approved provider  and               vii   is redeemable by an approved provider for cash payment from a service providing          agency up to the dollar value of the voucher.                   a   To promote individual choice, the allocation of resources through market forces,          and the diversification of service and support options, beginning on July  ,     , an eligible          person shall receive services and supports through the use of vouchers issued by a service          providing agency, except as provided in Subsection     b .               b   The requirement of Subsection     a  does not apply to                i   services funded directly by the federal Older Americans Act and provided by local area          agencies on aging                ii   protective services provided by the Division of Aging and Adult Services within the          Department of Human Services under Title   A, Chapter  , Part  , Abuse, Neglect, or Exploitation          of Disabled Adult                iii   the determination of whether a person is eligible to receive services and supports from          a service providing agency                iv   the determination of the scope, amount, and duration of services and supports to be          provided by a service providing agency through vouchers  and               v   service coordination or case management services provided to an eligible person by          a service providing agency, unless the eligible person chooses not to receive such services.                   Before September  ,     , the Director of the Division of Health Care Financing shall          submit to the Secretary of the United States Department of Health and Human Services a proposal                 under Section      of the Social Security Act to demonstrate the use of Medicaid funds consistent         with this section and shall include a statement that the state desires to begin operating this         demonstration project on July  ,     .                  Service providing agencies shall periodically report on the implementation of this         section to the appropriate committees of the Legislature, including recommendations for         legislation to be submitted to the      General Session of the Legislature.

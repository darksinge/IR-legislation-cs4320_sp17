 REGISTRATION PENALTIES

2009 GENERAL SESSION

            
4

    
STATE OF UTAH

 Chief Sponsor:  Paul Ray

Senate Sponsor: 






  Daniel R. Liljenquist

 LONG TITLE
            
9

    
 General Description:
            
10

    
    This bill modifies the Motor Vehicle Act by amending provisions relating to the penalty
            
11

    
for false evidences of motor vehicle title and registration information.
            
12

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
13

    
    This bill:
            
14

    
    .    provides that it is a third degree felony, rather than a second degree felony, for
            
15

    
providing false evidences for certain motor vehicle title or registration information.
            
16

    
 Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
            
17

    
    None
            
18

    
 Other Special Clauses:
            
19

    
    None
            
20

    
 Utah Code Sections Affected:
            
21

    
AMENDS:
            
22

    
     41-1a-1315, as renumbered and amended by Laws of Utah 1992, Chapter 1
            
23

    

            
24

    
Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
            
25

    
    Section 1.  
Section  
41-1a-1315

 is amended to read:
            
26

    
     41-1a-1315.  Third degree felony -- False evidences of title and registration.
            
27

    
    It is a [second] third degree felony for a person with respect to a motor vehicle, trailer,

            
28


    
or semitrailer to:
            
29

    
    (1)  fraudulently use a false or fictitious name in an application for registration, a
            
30

    
certificate of title, or for a duplicate certificate of title;
            
31

    
    (2)  knowingly make a false statement or knowingly conceal a material fact in an
            
32

    
application under this chapter;
            
33

    
    (3)  otherwise commit a fraud in an application under this chapter;
            
34

    
    (4)  alter with fraudulent intent a certificate of title, registration card, license plate, or
            
35

    
permit issued by the division;
            
36

    
    (5)  forge or counterfeit a document or license plate purporting to have been issued by
            
37

    
the division;
            
38

    
    (6)  alter, falsify, or forge an assignment upon a certificate of title;
            
39

    
    (7)  hold or use a document or license plate under this chapter knowing it has been
            
40

    
altered, forged, or falsified; [and] or
            
41

    
    (8)  file an application for a certificate of title providing false lien information, when the
            
42

    
person named on the application as lienholder does not hold a valid security interest.

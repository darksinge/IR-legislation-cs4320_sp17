Section          is enacted to read             CHAPTER   .  OUTFITTERS AND GUIDES ACT                      .  Title.             This chapter shall be known as the  Outfitters and Guides Act.              Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                      .  Definitions.             As used in this chapter                    Consideration  means something of value given or done in exchange for something         of value given or done by another.                  a    Guide  means an individual who, for consideration, accompanies a client of an          outfitter on an expedition for the purpose of taking or pursuing protected wildlife.               b    Guide  includes an individual who is an outfitter who personally accompanies a client          on an expedition for the purpose of taking protected wildlife.                    Outfitter  means any person who                a   for consideration, provides any of the following to enable a person to take or pursue          protected wildlife                i   a saddle or pack animal                ii   lodging or other accommodations                iii   camping equipment                iv   a vehicle, watercraft, or other conveyance  or               v   a personal service  and               b   accompanies the person, either part or all of the way, on an expedition for the purpose          of taking or pursuing protected wildlife or supervises a guide who accompanies the person. Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  Written information for clients    Remedy.                   Before providing any services to a client, an outfitter must provide the following          information, in writing, to the client                a   a description of the type of services to be provided                b   dates of service                c   transportation arrangements                d   fees for services and payment schedule  and               e   provisions, including related costs, for the recision of service arrangements.                   Upon the division s request, an outfitter must make available to the division for its          inspection the information required in Subsection    , except information relating to fees for                 services.                  An agreement or contract for the services of an outfitter shall be void and         unenforceable by the outfitter if the outfitter has not obtained a certificate of registration from the         division.             Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                      .  Records    Annual report.                  Each outfitter shall maintain the following records until the report required by         Subsection     is filed with the division               a   the number and species of protected wildlife other than aquatic wildlife taken by each          client                b   each client s license, permit, and tag number  and               c   the name, address, and certificate of registration number of each guide employed or          under contract by the outfitter.                   The records must be kept current and available for inspection by the division during          reasonable business hours after the division has provided    hours notice.                   By January   , each outfitter shall submit a report for the previous calendar year          containing the information specified in Subsection    .                   If an outfitter is required to report the information provided in Subsection     to a          federal agency, a copy of that report may be submitted to the division to satisfy the requirements          of Subsection    .              Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  General duties.              Before accompanying a client on an expedition for the purpose of taking or pursuing          protected wildlife, an outfitter or guide must                    be sure that the appropriate licenses, permits, and tags required under this title and the          rules and proclamations of the Wildlife Board are in the client s possession                    procure the required permits if pursuing protected wildlife on land owned or leased by          the state or federal government  and                   obtain written permission from the landowner as provided in Section         , if          pursuing protected wildlife on private property.              Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                              .  Revocation.                  A hearing officer appointed by the division director shall revoke the certificate of         registration of an outfitter or guide for the reasons provided in Section        .                  A hearing officer appointed by the division director may revoke the certificate of         registration of an outfitter or guide for a violation of Title   , Chapter   , Utah Consumer Sales         Practices Act, or rules adopted under the act.                  a   A person whose certificate of registration has been revoked may not continue to act         as an outfitter or guide.              b   A violation of Subsection  a  is a class A misdemeanor.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Rulemaking authority.              The Wildlife Board may make and enforce rules consistent with this chapter specifying                    application procedures and the form of applications for outfitters  and guides           certificates of registration  and                   recordkeeping and reporting requirements.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  List of registered outfitters and professional guides.              The division shall make a list available to the public of all registered outfitters and guides          without any preference indicated.              Section   .   Effective date.

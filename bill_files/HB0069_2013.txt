 EXPUNGEMENT OF RECORDS

 Chief Sponsor:  Richard A. Greenwood

 LONG TITLE
            
8

    
 General Description:
            
9

    
    This bill makes clarifying technical corrections regarding the number of convictions
            
10

    
necessary to be eligible for expungement.
            
11

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
12

    
    This bill:
            
13

    
    .    clarifies how the number of class A and B misdemeanors necessary to make a
            
14

    
person ineligible for expungement is taken into consideration.
            
15

    
 Money Appropriated in this Bill:
            
16

    
    None
            
17

    
 Other Special Clauses:
            
18

    
    None
            
19

    
 Utah Code Sections Affected:
            
20

    
AMENDS:
            
21

    
     77-40-105, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2012, Chapters 136 and 145
            
22

    
     77-40-108, as renumbered and amended by Laws of Utah 2010, Chapter 283
            
23

    

            
24

    
Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
            
25

    
    Section 1.  
Section  
77-40-105

 is amended to read:
            
26

    
     77-40-105.  Eligibility for expungement of conviction -- Requirements.
            
27

    
    (1)  A person convicted of an offense may apply to the bureau for a certificate of

            
28


    
eligibility to expunge the record of conviction as provided in this section.
            
29

    
    (2)  A petitioner is not eligible to receive a certificate of eligibility from the bureau if:
            
30

    
    (a)  the conviction for which expungement is sought is:
            
31

    
    (i)  a capital felony;
            
32

    
    (ii)  a first degree felony;
            
33

    
    (iii)  a violent felony as defined in Subsection 

76-3-203.5

(1)(c)(i);
            
34

    
    (iv)  automobile homicide;
            
35

    
    (v)  a felony violation of Subsection 

41-6a-501

(2); or
            
36

    
    (vi)  a registerable sex offense as defined in Subsection 

77-41-102

(16);
            
37

    
    (b)  a criminal proceeding is pending against the petitioner; or
            
38

    
    (c)  the petitioner intentionally or knowingly provides false or misleading information
            
39

    
on the application for a certificate of eligibility.
            
40

    
    (3)  A petitioner seeking to obtain expungement for a record of conviction is not
            
41

    
eligible to receive a certificate of eligibility from the bureau until all of the following have
            
42

    
occurred:
            
43

    
    (a)  all fines and interest ordered by the court have been paid in full;
            
44

    
    (b)  all restitution ordered by the court pursuant to Section 

77-38a-302

, or by the Board
            
45

    
of Pardons and Parole pursuant to Section 

77-27-6

, has been paid in full; and
            
46

    
    (c)  the following time periods have elapsed from the date the petitioner was convicted
            
47

    
or released from incarceration, parole, or probation, whichever occurred last, for each
            
48

    
conviction the petitioner seeks to expunge:
            
49

    
    (i)  10 years in the case of a misdemeanor conviction of Subsection 

41-6a-501

(2) or a
            
50

    
felony conviction of Subsection 

58-37-8

(2)(g);
            
51

    
    (ii)  seven years in the case of a felony;
            
52

    
    (iii)  five years in the case of a class A misdemeanor;
            
53

    
    (iv)  four years in the case of a class B misdemeanor; or
            
54

    
    (v)  three years in the case of any other misdemeanor or infraction.
            
55

    
    (4)  The bureau may not issue a certificate of eligibility if, at the time the petitioner
            
56

    
seeks a certificate of eligibility, the bureau determines that the petitioner's criminal history,
            
57

    
including previously expunged convictions, contains any of the following:
            
58

    
    (a)  two or more felony convictions, each of which is contained in a separate criminal

            
59


    
episode;
            
60

    
    (b)  any combination of three or more convictions that include two class A
            
61

    
misdemeanor convictions or greater, each of which is contained in a separate criminal episode;
            
62

    
    (c)  any combination of four or more convictions that include three class B
            
63

    
misdemeanor convictions or greater, each of which is contained in a separate criminal episode;
            
64

    
or
            
65

    
    (d)  five or more convictions of any degree whether misdemeanor or felony, excluding
            
66

    
infractions and any traffic offenses, each of which is contained in a separate criminal episode.
            
67

    
    (5)  If the petitioner has received a pardon from the Utah Board of Pardons and Parole,
            
68

    
the petitioner is entitled to a certificate of eligibility for all pardoned crimes.
            
69

    
    Section 2.  
Section  
77-40-108

 is amended to read:
            
70

    
     77-40-108.  Distribution of order -- Redaction -- Receipt of order --
            
71

    
 Administrative proceedings -- Bureau requirements.
            
72

    
    (1)  The petitioner shall be responsible for delivering a copy of the order of
            
73

    
expungement to all affected agencies and officials including the court, arresting agency,
            
74

    
booking agency, Department of Corrections, and the bureau.
            
75

    
    (2)  Unless otherwise provided by law or ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction to
            
76

    
respond differently, a person who has received an expungement of an arrest or conviction
            
77

    
under this chapter may respond to any inquiry as though the arrest or conviction did not occur.
            
78

    
    (3)  The bureau shall forward a copy of the expungement order to the Federal Bureau of
            
79

    
Investigation.
            
80

    
    (4)  An agency receiving an expungement order shall expunge the petitioner's
            
81

    
identifying information contained in records in its possession relating to the incident for which
            
82

    
expungement is ordered.
            
83

    
    (5)  Unless ordered by a court to do so, or in accordance with Subsection 

77-40-109

(2),
            
84

    
a government agency or official may not divulge [identifying] information or records which
            
85

    
have been expunged regarding the petitioner contained in a record of arrest, investigation,
            
86

    
detention, or conviction after receiving an expungement order.
            
87

    
    (6) (a)  An order of expungement may not restrict an agency's use or dissemination of
            
88

    
records in its ordinary course of business until the agency has received a copy of the order.
            
89

    
    (b)  Any action taken by an agency after issuance of the order but prior to the agency's

            
90


    
receipt of a copy of the order may not be invalidated by the order.
            
91

    
    (7)  An order of expungement may not:
            
92

    
    (a)  terminate or invalidate any pending administrative proceedings or actions of which
            
93

    
the petitioner had notice according to the records of the administrative body prior to issuance of
            
94

    
the expungement order;
            
95

    
    (b)  affect the enforcement of any order or findings issued by an administrative body
            
96

    
pursuant to its lawful authority prior to issuance of the expungement order; or
            
97

    
    (c)  remove any evidence relating to the petitioner including records of arrest, which the
            
98

    
administrative body has used or may use in these proceedings.
            
99

    
    (8)  The bureau shall provide clear written directions to the petitioner along with a list
            
100

    
of agencies known to be affected by the order of expungement.

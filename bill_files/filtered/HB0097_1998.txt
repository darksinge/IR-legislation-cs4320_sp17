GOVERNMENT IMMUNITY AMENDMENTS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Greg J. Curtis          AN ACT RELATING TO STATE AFFAIRS IN GENERAL  AMENDING THE         GOVERNMENTAL IMMUNITY ACT TO PROVIDE SANCTIONS FOR EMPLOYEES         WHO COMMIT PERJURY.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          ENACTS                       . , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section         .  is enacted to read                       . .  Waiver of immunity for perjury or false statements by a government           employee.                   Immunity from suit for all governmental entities is waived as to any action brought          seeking damages or any other lawful relief arising out of, in connection with, or resulting from the          perjury, the intentional making of a false statement, or the making of a false statement with          reckless disregard as to its truthfulness, by a government employee in any court or administrative          hearing.                   If a plaintiff prevails through declaratory or injunctive relief for damages against a          government employee or governmental entity for the making of a perjured or false statement,          attorney fees and costs, if sought, shall be awarded to the plaintiff against the agency or          governmental entity employing the person or official making the perjured or false statement and          the individual involved.                   If a plaintiff prevails against a government employee or governmental entity because          of a perjured or false statement, the order of the court shall require the governmental entity to take          disciplinary action against the employee.  The governmental entity shall be required to report to          the court within    days of the judgment the action taken against the employee or be liable for civil         contempt.      Legislative Review Note      as of                 AM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

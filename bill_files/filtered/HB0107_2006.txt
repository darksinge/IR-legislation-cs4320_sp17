FULL DAY KINDERGARTEN                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Kory M. Holdaway                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill establishes a voluntary full day kindergarten program, with certain funding                           priorities.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    establishes a program, administered by the State Board of Education, through which                           school districts can provide voluntary full day kindergarten                                .    amends the weighted pupil unit for a kindergarten student participating in the                           program                                .    establishes program funding priorities                                .    requires districts to report certain information on the program s outcomes to the                           State Board of Education  and                               .    requires the Superintendent of Public Instruction to report to the Education Interim                           Committee on the program.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                This bill appropriates as an ongoing appropriation subject to future budget constraints,                             ,   ,    from the Uniform School Fund for fiscal year         to the State Board of                           Education.                           Other Special Clauses                                This bill takes effect on July  ,     . Section      A  a         is enacted to read                            Part  .  Voluntary Full day Kindergarten Program                                  A  a    .   Voluntary full day kindergarten program.                                    As used in this section                                 a    full day kindergarten  means a kindergarten program that provides at least the                          same number of instructional hours per school year as a nonkindergarten grade, as defined                          under State Board of Education rule  and                                b    school  means a public school, including a charter school.                                    a   If funds are appropriated for this purpose, the State Board of Education shall                          allocate available funds to schools that apply to offer full day kindergarten.                                b   In allocating funds, the State Board of Education shall                                 i   for each school that requests funding, determine the percentage of students who                          receive free or reduced price school lunch                                 ii   beginning with the school with the highest percentage of free or reduced price                          lunch recipients, allocate funds to schools in descending order of percentage of free or reduced                          price lunch recipients, until available funds are allocated  and                                iii   allocate to each recipient school an amount calculated by                                 A   subtracting the minimum school program amount corresponding to a kindergarten                          pupil under Subsection       A   a            from the minimum school program amount                          corresponding to a full day kindergarten pupil under Subsection       A   a             and                                B   multiplying the result by the school s number of full day kindergarten participants                          under this section, as measured by average daily membership.                                c   i   A school district shall coordinate program application, funding, administration,                          and reporting for its schools that participate in the program.                                    ii   The State Board of Education shall allocate funds to a school that is not a charter                          school through the school district.                                    A school or school district may not require a student to participate in full day                          kindergarten.                                    Each participating school shall annually report to the State Board of Education                          regarding                                 a   the number of students served                                 b   the specific results achieved by the program, including any standardized testing or                          district directed assessment  and                                c   challenges encountered in administering the program and suggestions for                          improvement.                                    Until     , the Superintendent of Public Instruction shall annually report to the                          Education Interim Committee on the program, including a summary of information reported                          under Subsection    .                                    In accordance with Title   , Chapter   a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act,                          and consistent with this part, the State Board of Education shall make rules establishing                          application and reporting procedures necessary to administer this part.

FIREFIGHTERS  DEFERRED RETIREMENT                           OPTION PLAN                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Mark W. Walker                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill modifies the Utah State Retirement and Insurance Benefit Act Code by                           authorizing a deferred retirement option plan for firefighters.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    provides definitions                                .    authorizes a deferred retirement option plan  DROP  for firefighters  and                               .    provides and regulates DROP eligibility, application procedures, plan accounts, and                           plan limitations and conditions.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            ENACTS                                         , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                                , Utah Code Annotated                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                            Part  .  Deferred Retirement Option Plan                                         .  Definitions.                               As used in this part                                      Account  means a member s DROP account created in accordance with Section                                          .                                     Date of the DROP retirement  means the date selected by the member on which                          the member s participation in DROP shall become effective, which date may only be the  st or                            th of a month.                                     DROP  means a deferred retirement option plan.                                     DROP period  means the period between the date of DROP retirement and actual                          retirement.                                     Participating member  means a member of this system who is eligible for, and has                          applied to participate in, the DROP.                               Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Eligibility.                                    Beginning on July  ,     , a member of this system is eligible to participate in the                          DROP if the member has at least    years of service credit in this system.                                    A member may purchase retirement service credit, as allowed under this title, prior                          to participation in the DROP.                                    Participation in the DROP is voluntary and at the discretion of the member.                                    a   A member with                                 i      or more years of service credit may participate in the DROP for a period of                          between    and    months  or                                ii   less than    years of service credit may participate in the DROP for a period of                          between    and    months.                                b   A member may not elect to participate for partial months.                                    a   The member shall continue active firefighter employment after the date of the                             DROP retirement until the member terminates active firefighter employment.                                b   The member s election to participate in the DROP is irrevocable after the date of                          the DROP retirement.                                    At the end of the DROP period, the member shall terminate active employment and                          retire from the system.                               Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Application.                                    A member shall apply for participation in the DROP on a form provided by the                          office.                                    a   Upon application for participation in the DROP, a member shall also make                          application for a future retirement, including all necessary retirement elections.                                b   Changes may not be made to the elections made in accordance with Subsection                              a  except as provided under Section                  .                                    The date of the DROP retirement may not be more than    days after the date the                          application is received by the office.                               Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Plan accounts.                                    Upon receipt of a member s application to participate in the DROP, the office shall                          create a DROP account for the participating member.                                    An administrative fee equal to the first month s DROP benefit shall be charged at                          the time the first month s benefit is to be paid to the account.                                    Each month the office shall credit the participating member s account with                                 a   the participating member s allowance calculated as if the member had retired on the                          date of the DROP retirement  and                                b   the member s system cost of living allowance adjustments as provided under this                          title.                                    Interest may not be credited to the member s account.                                    Within    days of the end of the DROP period, the participating member shall elect                          to take the balance of the account as                                 a   a lump sum payout                                 b   a direct rollover, as allowed by the Internal Revenue Code  or                                   c   a combination of a lump sum payout and direct rollover.                                    At the end of the DROP period and upon the actual retirement of the member, the                          allowance shall be paid directly to the member.                               Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Plan limitations and conditions.                                    If a participating member dies after the date of the DROP retirement, but prior to                          the retirement date selected in the DROP application, the death benefit is                                 a   the participating member s account balance paid to the designated beneficiary  and                                b   the system death benefit for retirees.                                    If a participating member becomes unable to perform firefighter services during the                           DROP period due to a physical or mental condition, as determined in accordance with Section                                           , the account shall be paid in accordance with Subsection                      and the                           allowance shall then be paid to the member.                                     If a participating member leaves active employment during the DROP period other                           than on the date specified on the DROP application for any reason other than death or                           disability, an additional      administrative fee shall be charged to the member s DROP                           account.                                     If a participating member does not leave employment by the end of the member s                           maximum DROP period                                  a   the participating member s account shall be forfeited to this system                                  b   the participating member s benefit shall be calculated as though there were no                           election to participate in the DROP  and                                 c   the participating member may not elect to participate in the DROP again.                                     Nothing in this part shall allow a member to receive benefits in excess of those                           allowed by the Internal Revenue Code for qualified pension plans.                                     At the end of the DROP period, the participating member                                  a   is subject to the employment restrictions in Section                                                    b   may not accrue additional retirement service credit under this title  and                                 c   may not participate in the DROP again.                                     A participating member s account is subject to division with respect to domestic                           relations matters in accordance with Section                  .                                        a   During the DROP period, the participating member and the participating                           employer shall continue to pay required contributions to the office.                                 b   The contributions shall remain within the fund created under Section                                            and may not be part of the member s account or retirement allowance.

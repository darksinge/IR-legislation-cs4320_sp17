TEXTBOOKS IN PUBLIC SCHOOLS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                           Sponsor  Lloyd W. Frandsen                           AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC EDUCATION  PROVIDING THAT TEXTBOOKS SHALL                          BE PROVIDED FREE OF CHARGE IN THE PUBLIC SCHOOLS  PROVIDING THAT A                          REFUNDABLE DEPOSIT MAY BE REQUIRED FOR DAMAGE TO OR LOSS OF                          TEXTBOOKS  PROVIDING SCHOOL DISTRICTS WITH     FOR EACH STUDENT IN                          GRADES   THROUGH    TO ELIMINATE TEXTBOOK FEES  MAKING CERTAIN                           TECHNICAL CHANGES  PROVIDING A REPEALER  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE                           DATE.                           This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                            AMENDS                                  A       , as enacted by Chapter  , Laws of Utah                                      A       , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                                REPEALS                                  A       , as enacted by Chapter  , Laws of Utah                                      A       , as enacted by Chapter  , Laws of Utah                                This act enacts uncodified material.                          Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A            is amended to read                                   A       .  State policy on providing textbooks.                                      It is the public policy of this state that public education shall be free.                                     A student may not be denied an education because of economic inability to purchase                          textbooks necessary for advancement in or graduation from the public school system.                                      A       Beginning July  ,     , a school board may not  sell textbooks or otherwise                            charge textbook fees or deposits except as provided in  Title   A  Section   A       .                                  Section  .    Section      A            is amended to read                                   A       .  Purchase of textbooks by local school board    State board approval                              Free textbooks    Textbooks provided to teachers.                                    A local school board, under rules adopted by the State Board of Education, may                           purchase textbooks approved by the state board for use in the public schools directly from the                           publisher at prices and terms approved by the state board  and may sell those books to pupils in                          grades nine through    at a cost not to exceed the actual cost of the book plus costs of                          transportation and handling .                                    a   Each school board , however,  shall provide, free of charge, textbooks and                           workbooks required for courses of instruction for each  child  student attending public schools                            whose parent or guardian is financially unable to purchase them , except that a textbook fee may                          be assessed for a textbook required in a concurrent enrollment class under rules adopted by the                          State Board of Education.                                b   i   The school board may require a refundable textbook deposit in grades   through                             to offset costs associated with lost or damaged textbooks.                                ii   Each school board that requires a textbook deposit under Subsection     b  i  shall                          provide for a refund of the deposit at the conclusion of the school year under rules adopted by the                          State Board of Education.                                     Children who are receiving cash assistance under Title   A, Chapter  , Part  , Family                          Employment Program, supplemental security income, or who are in the custody of the Division                          of Child and Family Services within the Department of Human Services are eligible for free                          textbooks and workbooks under this section.                                           The school board shall also purchase all books necessary for teachers to conduct                           their classes.                                     The cost of furnishing textbooks and workbooks may be paid from school operating                          funds, the textbook fund, or from other available funds.                                           Books provided to teachers and  pupils without charge or at less than full cost are                          paid for out of funds of the district and  students remain the property of the district.                                     In school districts that require pupils to rent books instead of purchasing them or                          providing them free of charge, the school board shall waive rental fees for a child whose parent                          or guardian is financially unable to pay the rental fee.  The children considered eligible under                             Subsection     are also eligible for the purposes of this subsection.                                Section  .   Elimination of textbook fees in secondary schools    Reduction or                           elimination of other fees.                                    a   For the school year beginning July  ,     , each school district shall receive                              for each student in grades   through    to eliminate textbook fees in secondary schools.                                b   Each district s allocation shall be based on the previous year s October   student count                          in grades     , with adjustments for anticipated growth under rules adopted by the State Board of                          Education.                                    If a school district s anticipated textbook fees for the      school year would have been                          less than     per secondary school student, then the district shall use its allocation under                          Subsection     to eliminate its textbook fees and apply the balance to reduce or eliminate other                          anticipated course fees and activity fees that would be charged to secondary students but for this                          allocation.                                    The State Board of Education, in consultation with the state s    school districts, shall                          make a recommendation prior to December   ,     , for consideration by the Legislature that                          would incorporate the funding provision in Subsection     into Title   A, Chapter   a, Minimum                          School Program Act, as a line item, with weighted pupil units that take into account growth and                          inflation factors.                               Section  .  Repealer.                               This act repeals                                Section    A       ,  Free textbook system.                               Section    A       ,  Repurchase and resale of textbooks.                               Section  .   Effective date.                               This act takes effect on July  ,     .

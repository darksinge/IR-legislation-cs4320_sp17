PUBLIC SAFETY COMMUNICATIONS                           NETWORK                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor   David  Ure                             This act modifies the Public Safety Code to repeal the Utah Communications Agency                          Network and move its functions into a division of the Department of Public Safety.  This act                          creates the Public Safety Communications Network Division to be the lead agency to develop,                          implement, administer, and maintain the Utah Public Safety Communications Network to                           coordinate communications between public safety agencies.  This act creates the  Utah Public                           Safety Communications Network Committee to advise the division.  This act requires cost                           sharing between agencies involved in the network.  This act takes effect on July  ,     . Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Establishment of the Public Safety Communications Division    Duties.                                    There is created within the department the  Public Safety Communications Division,                           which was formerly the Utah Communications Agency Network.                                    The division shall                                 a   be the lead agency to develop, implement, administer, and maintain the network to                          provide public safety communications services and facilities on a regional or statewide basis for                          the benefit and use of municipal, county, state, and federal government agencies                                 b   assume the operations, contracts, and obligations of the network on July  ,                                      c   coordinate with public agencies to link public safety and emergency services agencies                          together through a single communications system network to be known as the Utah Public Safety                          Communications Network                                 d   in accordance with Title   , Chapter   , Interlocal Cooperation Act, enter into                          agreements or contracts with government agencies to                                 i   implement this chapter  and                                ii   share the costs of implementing and administering the network as provided in Section                                            and                                e   in accordance with Title   , Chapter   a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, make                          rules, with committee approval, as necessary to implement this chapter.                               Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                            Part  .  Committee                                         .  Utah Public Safety Communications Network Committee    Appointment                             Duties    Per diem    Technical committees.                                    There is created within the Department of Public Safety the Utah Public Safety                          Communications Network Committee comprising up to    members knowledgeable about public                          safety communications and the networking of communications systems as follows                                 a   three members designated by the commissioner of public safety                                 b   two members designated by the Utah Sheriff s Association                                 c   one member designated by the Department of Administrative Services                                    d   two members designated by the Utah Police Chief s Association                                 e   up to three additional members designated by the committee for one year terms  and                                f   a designating entity under Subsections     a  through     d  may designate an                          alternative member to serve in the absence of its designated member.                                    The committee shall                                 a   advise the division on matters related to the implementation and administration of this                          chapter                                 b   make recommendations to public safety agencies related to coordinating                          communications                                 c   make recommendations to the department, counties, and municipalities on increasing                           the efficiency and effectiveness of the network, including development, implementation,                           administration, maintenance, and coordination                                  d   evaluate the cost effectiveness of implementing network improvements                                  e   develop broad policies for the long term development, construction, and operation of                           the network                                  f   review and approve contracts and other instruments prior to the division executing them                           on behalf of the network  and                                 g   review and approve rules proposed by the division.                                     The committee shall annually elect a chair and a vice chair from its members.                                     Each member of the committee shall serve until the appointing authority removes the                           member, which may be for any reason.  When a vacancy occurs in the membership appointed                           under Subsections     a  through  d  for any reason, a replacement shall be appointed.                                     The committee shall meet as it determines necessary to accomplish its duties.                                     Reasonable notice shall be given to each member of the committee prior to any                           meeting.                                     A majority of the committee constitutes a quorum for the transaction of business.                                     a   i   Members who are not government employees may not receive compensation or                           benefits for their services, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the performance of                           the member s official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections                                A           and       A          .                                 ii   Members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their service.                                    b   i   State government officer and employee members who do not receive salary, per                           diem, or expenses from their agency for their service may receive per diem and expenses incurred                           in the performance of their official duties from the committee at the rates established by the                           Division of Finance under Sections       A           and       A          .                                 ii   State government officer and employee members may decline to receive per diem and                           expenses for their service.                                 c   i   Local government members who do not receive salary, per diem, or expenses from                           the entity that they represent for their service may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the                           performance of their official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance under                           Sections       A           and       A          .                                 ii   Local government members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their                           service.                                     The committee may establish technical advisory committees as needed to assist in                           accomplishing its duties under this section.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                             Part  .  Cost Sharing                                          .  Cost sharing    Additional agencies.                                     a   The cost of implementing and administering the network shall be shared pro rata                           by the division and the counties and municipalities using it.                                 b   The division shall enter into an agreement or contract under Title   , Chapter   ,                           Interlocal Cooperation Act, with a county or municipality to share costs incurred under this section.                                     Additional county and municipal public safety agencies may be added to the network                           upon application of a county or municipality after                                  a   a recommendation of the committee                                  b   approval by the division                                  c   determination of the appropriate cost share of the addition under Subsection     a   and                                 d   an agreement under Subsection     b .

TECHNICAL REVISIONS TO PAWNSHOP STATUTE                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Angela Romero                                Senate Sponsor             Karen Mayne                                                         LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill modifies the Pawnshop and Secondhand Merchandise Transaction Information                           Act regarding disposition of property.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    corrects a cross reference regarding disposition of property after it has been                           removed from a law enforcement hold and is not needed as evidence.                           Money Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                This bill provides an immediate effective date.                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            AMENDS                                       a      , as enacted by Laws of Utah     , Chapter                                                        Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section         a         is amended to read                                      a    .  Investigation phase    Victim s responsibilities    Property                           disposition.                                    If the property pawned or sold to a pawn or secondhand business is the subject of a                              criminal investigation and a hold has been placed on the property under Section         a         ,                           the original victim shall do the following to establish a claim                                 a   positively identify to law enforcement the item stolen or lost                                 b   if a police report has not already been filed for the original theft or loss of property,                           file a police report, and provide for the law enforcement agency information surrounding the                           original theft or loss of property  and                                c   give a sworn statement under penalty of law that                                 i   claims ownership of the property                                 ii   references the original theft or loss  and                                iii   identifies the perpetrator if known.                                    The pawn or secondhand business shall retain possession of any property subject to                           a hold until a criminal prosecution is commenced relating to the property for which the hold                           was placed unless                                 a   during the course of a criminal investigation the actual physical possession by law                           enforcement of an article purchased or pawned is essential for the purpose of fingerprinting the                           property, chemical testing of the property, or if the property contains unique or sensitive                           personal identifying information  or                                b   an agreement between the original victim and the pawn or secondhand business to                           return the property is reached.                                    a   Upon the commencement of a criminal prosecution, any article subject to a hold                           for investigation under this chapter may be seized by the law enforcement agency which                           requested the hold.                                b   Subsequent disposition of the property shall be consistent with Section                                                         regarding property not needed as evidence and this chapter.                                c   If a conflict exists between the provisions of Section                           regarding                           property not needed as evidence and this chapter, this chapter takes precedence regarding                           property held by pawn or secondhand businesses.                                    At all times during the course of a criminal investigation and subsequent                           prosecution, the article subject to a law enforcement hold shall be kept secure by the pawn or                           secondhand business subject to the hold unless a pawned or sold article has been seized by the                           law enforcement agency pursuant to Section         a    .      .                                  Section  .   Effective date.                               If approved by two thirds of all the members elected to each house, this bill takes effect                          upon approval by the governor, or the day following the constitutional time limit of Utah                          Constitution, Article VII, Section  , without the governor s signature, or in the case of a veto,                          the date of veto override.

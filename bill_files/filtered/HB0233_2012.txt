POLITICAL PARTY REGISTRATION AMENDMENTS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Jim Nielson                                Senate Sponsor             Jerry W. Stevenson                                                         LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill amends provisions in Title   A, Chapter  , Political Party Formation and                           Procedures related to a political party.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    increases the number of regular general elections in which party participation is                           calculated in order to remain a registered political party  and                                 .    makes technical changes.                           Money Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            AMENDS                                  A      , as last amended by Laws of Utah     , Chapter                                    A      , as last amended by Laws of Utah     , Chapter                                                        Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A           is amended to read                                   A      .  Definitions.                                  As used in this chapter                                      Continuing political party  means an organization of voters that                                 a   participated in the last regular general election  and                                b   in at least one of the last two regular general elections, polled a total vote for any of                           its candidates for any office equal to    or more of the total votes cast for all candidates for the                           United States House of Representatives in the same regular general election.                                     County political party  means, for each registered political party, all of the persons                           within a single county who, under definitions established by the county political party, are                           members of the registered political party.                                     Newly registered political party  means a statewide organization of voters that has                           complied with the petition and organizing procedures of this chapter to become a registered                           political party.                                     Registered political party  means an organization of voters that                                 a   i   participated in the last regular general election  and                                ii   in at least one of the last two regular general elections, polled a total vote for any of                           its candidates for any office equal to    or more of the total votes cast for all candidates for the                           United States House of Representatives in the same regular general election  or                                b   has complied with the petition and organizing procedures of this chapter.                                     State political party  means, for each registered political party, all of the persons in                           Utah who, under definitions established by the state political party, are members of the                           registered political party.                               Section  .    Section      A           is amended to read                                   A      .  Organization of newly registered political parties    Rights.                                    Any organization of voters whose organization did not participate in the last regular                           general election, or whose organization polled a total vote equivalent to less than    of the                           total vote cast for all  representatives in Congress  candidates for the United States House of                          Representatives for any of its candidates in both of the last two regular general  election                           elections shall comply with the requirements of this chapter to become a registered political                           party.                                    a   Unless an organization of registered voters is a registered political party under                           this chapter, it may not place the names of candidates representing that organization upon the                              primary and regular general election ballots under the common organization name.                                b   Nothing in this subsection prohibits an organization of voters from qualifying                           candidates as independent candidates or as write in candidates.

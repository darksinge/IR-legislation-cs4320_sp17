HIGHER EDUCATION PARKING ENFORCEMENT                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  A.  Lamont Tyler          AN ACT RELATING TO HIGHER EDUCATION  PROVIDING THAT THE STATE BOARD         OF REGENTS MAY AUTHORIZE HIGHER EDUCATION INSTITUTIONS TO ENACT         PARKING AND TRAFFIC REGULATIONS  AND PROVIDING FOR THE         ENFORCEMENT OF THOSE REGULATIONS THROUGH JUDICIAL PROCESS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           AMENDS                  B      , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                      B      , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section    B       is amended to read                  B      .  Power of board to enact regulations.                     The board may enact regulations governing the conduct of university and college          students, faculty, and employees.                   The  regulations  board may  include rules governing  enact and authorize higher          education institutions to enact traffic, parking, and related  matters upon  regulations governing          the conduct of all individuals on campuses and other facilities owned or controlled by the          institutions or the board.                   The board  has the power to  and institutions may enforce  its  these rules and          regulations in any reasonable manner, including the assessment of fees, fines, and forfeitures, the          collection of which may be by withholding from moneys owed the violator, the imposition of          probation, suspension, or expulsion from the institution, the revocation of privileges, the refusal          to issue certificates, degrees, and diplomas,  and  through judicial process or any reasonable          combination of these alternatives.                Section  .  Section    B       is amended to read                 B      .  Traffic violations    Notice of rule or regulation.                    It is a violation of this  chapter  section for any person , not a student, faculty member,         or employee of a state institution of higher education,  to operate or park a vehicle upon any         property owned or controlled by a state institution of higher education contrary to posted signs          and to  authorized by the published rules and regulations of the institution or to block or impede         traffic through or on any of these properties.                  Notice of a rule or regulation to all persons is sufficient if the rule or regulation is         published in one issue of a newspaper of general circulation in the county or counties in which the          institution and the campus or facility is located.       Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

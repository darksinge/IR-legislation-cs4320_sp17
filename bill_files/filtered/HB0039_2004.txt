SPECIAL FUNCTIONS OFFICERS FOR THE                           DIVISION OF OCCUPATIONAL AND                           PROFESSIONAL LICENSING                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor  J. Morgan Philpot                                                      LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill modifies provisions of the Division of Occupational and Professional                           Licensing Act related to employment of staff.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    allows the director, with approval of the executive director, to appoint investigators                           designated as special function officers  and                               .    makes certain technical changes.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            AMENDS                                        , as renumbered and amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section                 is amended to read                                         .  Employment of staff.                                       The director, with the approval of the executive director, may employ necessary                           staff, including investigators, specialists, and professionals, to assist  him  the director in                           performing the duties, functions, and responsibilities of the division.                                    An investigator employed under Subsection     is a special function officer, as                          defined in Section                  , but is not eligible for retirement benefits under the Public Safety                          Noncontributory Retirement System.      Legislative Review Note      as of                  AM   A limited legal review of this legislation raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

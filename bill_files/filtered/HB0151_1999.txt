Section      A           is amended to read                                   A      .  Commission s power to grant licenses    Limitations.                                    For purposes of this section                                 a    public space  means the space within the international airport s terminals and                          concourses that is used to provide public services                                 b    public space  includes                                 i   a circulation area                                 ii   a hold room                                 iii   a baggage claim area  and                                iv   a retail establishment  and                                c    public space  does not include                                 i   administrative office space  or                                ii   bag makeup space.                                             Before any airport lounge may sell or allow the consumption of liquor on its                           premises, it shall first obtain a license from the commission as provided in this part.                                          The commission may issue airport lounge liquor licenses for the purpose of                           establishing airport liquor outlets  at international airports  for the storage, sale, and consumption                           of liquor on premises operated as public airport lounges at an international airport with a United                          States customs office on the airport premises.                                          a   The total number of airport lounge liquor licenses may not exceed one airport                           lounge per  terminal plus one lounge per concourse located beyond the security point    ,                             square feet of public space at that international airport.                                b   A person seeking an airport lounge liquor license under this part shall obtain a letter                          from the operator of the international airport where the airport lounge will be located that certifies                          the square feet of public space that exists at the international airport at the time the application is                          filed with the department under Section       A          . All airport lounge liquor licenses expire on October    of each year. Persons desiring                           to renew their airport lounge liquor license shall submit a renewal fee of   ,    and a completed                           renewal application to the department no later than September   .  Failure to meet the renewal                           requirements shall result in an automatic forfeiture of the license, effective on the date the existing                           license expires.  Renewal applications shall be in a form as prescribed by the department.                                    If any airport liquor licensee does not immediately notify the department of any change                           in ownership of the licensee, or in the case of a Utah corporate owner of any change in the                           corporate officers or directors, the commission may suspend or revoke that license.

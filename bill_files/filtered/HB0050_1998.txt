LOCAL OPTION SALES TAX FOR           CONSERVATION EASEMENTS FOR           AGRICULTURAL USE                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Evan L. Olsen          AN ACT RELATING TO THE SALES AND USE TAX ACT  AUTHORIZING A COUNTY TO         IMPOSE A SALES AND USE TAX FOR THE ACQUISITION OF CONSERVATION         EASEMENTS ONLY FOR AGRICULTURAL USE  ESTABLISHING CONDITIONS FOR          THE IMPOSITION, USE, AND REPEAL OF THE TAX  PROVIDING PROCEDURES FOR          ADMINISTERING THE TAX  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           ENACTS                          , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  . Base    Rate    Imposition requirements    Limit on use    Administration              Repeal.                   Subject to the provisions of Subsections     through    , and in addition to any other          tax authorized under this chapter, a county may impose a sales and use tax of up to      upon the          sales and uses described in Subsection             , subject to the exemptions provided for in          Section          , to acquire conservation easements as provided in Title   , Chapter   , Land          Conservation Easement Act, only for agricultural use.                   a   A sales and use tax under Subsection     shall be imposed                i   upon sales and uses made in the county, including sales and uses made within          municipalities in the county  and               ii   beginning on the first day of January, April, July, or October.              b   A county imposing a sales and use tax under this section shall               i   establish a special revenue fund known as the  Acquisition of Agricultural         Conservation Easements Fund                ii   deposit all of the revenues generated by a tax under this section into the fund               iii   invest the revenues in the fund as provided in Title   , Chapter   , Uniform Fiscal         Procedures Act for Counties  and              iv   budget appropriations for acquiring conservation easements for agricultural use from         the fund.                   Before imposing a sales and use tax under this section, a county shall                a   obtain a majority vote of all members of the county legislative body                b   obtain voter approval during a regular general election                c   adopt by ordinance                i   a limit on the total amount of sales and use tax revenues that the county may collect          under this section  and               ii   a limit on the amount per acre the county may expend to acquire conservation          easements for agricultural use with the sales and use tax revenues collected under this section  and               d   notify the commission at least    days prior to the imposition of the ordinance          imposing the tax.                   Except as provided in Subsection    , a sales and use tax under this section shall be          imposed and administered in the same manner as a tax imposed under Part  , The Local Sales and          Use Tax Act, except that a sales and use tax imposed under this section is not subject to                a   the distribution provisions of Subsections              and               and               b   the provisions of Subsection             .                   If at any time after the day on which a county imposes a tax under this section the          county does not acquire a conservation easement for agricultural use for three consecutive years                a   the tax shall be repealed on the first day of January immediately following the          three year period  and               b   the county shall include the revenues as property tax revenues for the next year for          purposes of calculating the county s certified tax rate under Section         .                   If, after including a distribution of revenues as property tax revenues under Subsection                    , the property taxes distributed to the county exceed the amount of revenues levied by the         county for the year in which the revenues are distributed, for purposes of establishing the county s         certified tax rate in subsequent years, the county shall carry forward as property tax revenues the         excess revenues until the entire amount of remaining revenues have been carried forward as         property tax revenues.

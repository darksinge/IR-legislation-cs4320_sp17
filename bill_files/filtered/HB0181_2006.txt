EDUCATION REFORM                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Stephen H. Urquhart                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill provides stipends for supplemental instruction to students who have not passed                           the Utah Basic Skills Competency Test, bonuses for teachers based on the academic                           progress of their students, and modifications to the Utah Orderly School Termination                           Procedures Act.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    specifies eligibility requirements for students to receive a stipend for basic skills                           education                                .    establishes stipend amounts                                .    requires basic skills educators to accept students for instruction on a first come first                           served basis                                .    allows a basic skills educator to charge a stipend recipient an additional amount                           above the stipend amount                                .    provides that a basic skills educator shall receive payment in the amount of the                           stipend if the stipend recipient passes the subtest for which the basic skills educator                           provided instruction                                .    requires the State Board of Education to administer the Basic Skills Education                           Stipend Program and make rules                                .    requires the Legislature to annually appropriate money from the General Fund for                              stipends for basic skills education                                .    requires the State Board of Education to develop a uniform statewide plan to award                           bonuses to teachers based on the academic performance of their students                                .    requires the bonus plan to be implemented beginning in the         school year in                           Title I schools in kindergarten through grade six                                .    provides that kindergarten through grade three teachers shall be awarded bonuses                           based on student progress in reading, and grade four through six teachers shall be                           awarded bonuses based on student progress in mathematics                                .    directs the State Board of Education to make rules to administer the bonus plan and                           to make reports to the Education Interim Committee                                .    modifies provisions of the Utah Orderly School Termination Procedures Act  and                               .    appropriates money for teacher bonuses.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                This bill appropriates                                .    as an ongoing appropriation subject to future budget constraints,    ,   ,    from                           the Uniform School Fund for fiscal year         for teacher bonuses. Section      A           is enacted to read                                  A      .  Basic Skills Education Program.                                    As used in this section                                 a    Basic skills education  means individual or group instruction, including                          assessments, designed to develop the skills and knowledge necessary to pass the Utah Basic                          Skills Competency Test.                                b    Basic skills educator  means                                 i   a school district                                 ii   a charter high school                                 iii   an accredited public or private educational institution  or                                iv   other individual or entity that meets board requirements pursuant to Subsection                              .                                c    Program  means the Basic Skills Education Stipend Program.                                d    Stipend recipient  means a student who receives a stipend under this section.                                e    Utah Basic Skills Competency Test  or  UBSCT  means the basic skills                          competency test that students must pass to receive a regular high school diploma pursuant to                          Section       A          .                                    The Basic Skills Education Stipend Program is created to provide students who                          have not passed the UBSCT supplemental instruction in the skills and knowledge necessary to                          pass the test.                                    The State Board of Education shall administer the Basic Skills Education Stipend                          Program.                                    a   A student may receive a stipend for basic skills education if the student has                          scored below the midpoint of the partial mastery range on one or more subtests of the UBSCT.                                b   A student who meets the requirements of Subsection     a  may receive a stipend                          for basic skills education in the subject of each subtest failed.  Depending upon the number of                          subtests failed, a student may receive one, two, or three stipends.                                    Stipend amounts shall be based on a student s subtest score as follows                                 a       , if the student s subtest score was below the midpoint of the partial mastery                          range but above the minimal mastery range                                    b     ,   , if the student s subtest score was below the partial mastery range, but above                          or at the midpoint of the minimal mastery range  or                                c     ,   , if the student s subtest score was below the midpoint of the minimal                          mastery range.                                    A stipend recipient may apply for basic skills education from any basic skills                          educator. Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                             Part   .  Teacher Bonus Act                                   A       .  Title.                                This part is known as the  Teacher Bonus Act.                                 Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                                   A       .  Definitions.                                As used in this part                                       Board  means the State Board of Education.                                      Title I school  means a school receiving federal monies under Title I of the No                           Child Left Behind Act of     , Pub. L. No.        , for a schoolwide or targeted assistance                           program.                                Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                                   A       .  Bonus plan.                                     The board, in consultation with teachers, school and school district administrators,                              assessment specialists, and other interested individuals, shall develop a uniform statewide plan                           to award bonuses to teachers based on the academic progress of their students consistent with                           this part.                                     The bonus plan shall be implemented beginning in the         school year in Title                           I schools as follows                                  a   teachers in kindergarten through grade three shall be awarded bonuses based on                           student progress in reading  and                                 b   teachers in grades four through six shall be awarded bonuses based on student                           progress in mathematics.                                     A teacher shall be eligible for a bonus if the average progress of the teacher s                           students exceeds one year s growth in learning.                                     a   Student progress in reading shall be measured by a common assessment to be                           administered to all students in kindergarten through grade three in Title I schools.                                 b   Student progress in mathematics shall be measured by the mathematics                           criterion referenced tests administered statewide pursuant to Section       A          .                                     The bonus plan shall include the following components                                  a   how individual student progress shall be determined                                  b   how average student progress for a teacher shall be determined                                  c   how one year s growth in learning is defined                                  d   bonus amounts for increasing levels of average student progress in excess of one                           year s growth in learning  and                                 e   how students who are not in a class for a full year shall be counted for the purpose                           of determining a bonus.                                Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                                   A       .  Funding and administration.                                     Subject to future budget constraints, the Legislature shall annually appropriate                           money for teacher bonuses.                                     The board shall make rules as necessary to administer this part in accordance with                           Title   , Chapter   a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act.                                Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                                   A       .  Reporting.                                   By October   , beginning in      and through     , the board shall make an annual                           report on the implementation of the bonus plan to the Education Interim Committee.                                Section  .    Section      A           is amended to read                                    A      .  Definitions.                                As used in this chapter                                       Administrative disciplinary action  means any written statement provided to the                           school employee, signed by a school or district administrator, and retained in the employee s                           personnel file evidencing the employee s willful or intentional misconduct as defined in                           Subsection     .                                            Career employee  means an employee of a school district who has obtained a                            reasonable expectation of continued employment based upon Section       A           and an                            agreement with the employee or the employee s association, district practice, or policy.                                            Contract term  or  term of employment  means the period of time during                            which an employee is engaged by the school district under a contract of employment, whether                            oral or written.                                            Dismissal  or  termination  means                                  a   termination of the status of employment of an  employee                                  b   failure to renew or continue the employment contract of a career employee beyond                            the then current school year                                  c   reduction in salary of an employee not generally applied to all employees of the                            same category employed by the school district during the employee s contract term  or                                 d   change of assignment of an employee with an accompanying reduction in pay,                            unless the assignment change and salary reduction are agreed to in writing.                                            Employee  means a career or provisional employee of a school district, but                            does not include                                  a   the district superintendent, or the equivalent at the Schools for the Deaf and the                            Blind                                  b   the district business administrator or the equivalent at the Schools for the Deaf and                            the Blind  or                                 c   a temporary employee.                                            Provisional employee  means an individual, other than a career employee or                               a temporary employee, who is employed by a school district.                                            School board  or  board  means a district school board or its equivalent at                            the Schools for the Deaf and the Blind.                                            School district  or  district  means                                  a   a public school district  or                                 b   the Schools for the Deaf and the Blind.                                            Temporary employee  means an individual who is employed on a temporary                            basis as defined by policies adopted by the local board of education.  If the class of employees                            in question is represented by an employee organization recognized by the local board, the board                            shall adopt its policies based upon an agreement with that organization.  Temporary employees                            serve at will and have no expectation of continued employment.                                       Willful or intentional misconduct  means conduct that jeopardizes the health or                           safety of students or conduct that significantly impairs the employee s ability to safely or                           professionally fulfill the employee s responsibilities or assignments.

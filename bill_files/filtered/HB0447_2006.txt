EDUCATOR PHONICS REQUIREMENTS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   David N. Cox                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill establishes prerequisites for obtaining certain educator licenses.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    requires an applicant for a level   license with a concentration in elementary                           education or early childhood education to have successfully completed a course in                           the teaching of phonics  and                               .    defines terms.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            ENACTS                                  A      , Utah Code Annotated                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A           is enacted to read                                  A      .  Phonics course prerequisite for licensing.                                    As used in this section                                    a    Phonemic awareness  means the awareness of sounds that make up spoken words                          and the ability to use this awareness of sounds in reading.                                b    Phonics  means the techniques and strategies used to teach children to match,                          blend, and translate letters of the alphabet into the sounds they represent, which techniques and                          strategies are systematically integrated and thoroughly practiced in a developmentally                          appropriate instructional program to assist the child in learning to read, write, and spell.                                    Rules enacted by the board establishing criteria for obtaining a license pursuant to                          Section       A           shall require each applicant for a level   license with a concentration in                          elementary education or early childhood education to have successfully completed at least one                          separate course of at least three semester hours in the teaching of phonics in the context of                          reading, writing, and spelling that covers the following topics                                 a   phonological and morphological underpinnings of English spellings                                 b   the nature and role of word recognition in proficient reading                                 c   methods for the instruction of phonemic awareness, decoding, and spelling  and                                d   the relation of deficits in phonemic awareness, decoding, spelling, and word                          recognition to reading disabilities.      Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM   Based on a limited legal review, this legislation has not been determined to have a high  probability of being held unconstitutional.

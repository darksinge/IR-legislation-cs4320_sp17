DRUG ABUSE AMENDMENTS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Carol Spackman Moss                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill modifies the Criminal Code to impose a penalty for a person who fails to seek                           or provide assistance when another person appears to be seriously affected by a                           controlled substance.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    provides that if a person is in the presence of an individual who reasonably appears                           to be at risk of death or serious bodily injury due to ingestion of a controlled                           substance, gas, or inhalant, it is a criminal offense to fail to seek or provide                           treatment to mitigate or attempt to mitigate the apparent effects  and                               .    provides that the failure to act is a class B misdemeanor.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            ENACTS                                        . , Utah Code Annotated                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                   Section  .    Section            .      is enacted to read                                        . .  Reckless neglect    Penalty.                                    A person commits reckless neglect if, under circumstances not amounting to a                          felony offense                                 a   the person is in the presence of a second person whom the person knows or has                          reason to know has ingested a controlled substance, gas, or inhalant, and the second person is                          in physical condition that a reasonable person would perceive as creating a substantial risk of                          death or serious bodily injury, as exhibited by physical symptoms such as intoxication,                          unconsciousness, euphoria, dizziness, stupor, or inability to move  and                                b   the person fails to make reasonable efforts to obtain or provide emergency medical                          assistance for the second party to mitigate or attempt to mitigate the apparent effects of the                          controlled substance, gas, or inhalant.                                    Reckless neglect is a class B misdemeanor.

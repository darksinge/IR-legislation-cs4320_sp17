LIMITS OF MUNICIPAL LIABILITY                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Peter C. Knudson          AN ACT RELATING TO CITIES AND TOWNS  LIMITING THE LIABILITY OF A         MUNICIPALITY THAT MAKES FACILITIES AVAILABLE FOR CERTAIN SPORTS OR         RECREATIONAL ACTIVITIES  PROVIDING DEFINITIONS  PROVIDING AN         EXCEPTION  AND MAKING CONFORMING CHANGES TO IMMUNITY PROVISIONS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           AMENDS                       , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah                           , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah               ENACTS                        , Utah Code Annotated                            , Utah Code Annotated                            , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section           is enacted to read            Part  .  Limitation on Municipal Liability                        .  Definitions.              As used in this part                     Person  includes any person, regardless of age, maturity, ability, capability, or          experience.                   a    Recreational facilities  means land, structures, buildings, equipment, or          improvements of any kind owned or operated by a municipality and made available after May  ,              , to the public for use in a sport or recreational activity that inherently involves a relatively          high degree of risk of injury to persons engaged in the activity.               b   Examples of a sport or recreational activity that inherently involves a relatively high         degree of risk of injury to persons engaged in the activity include skateboarding, roller blading,         and snow sledding.                  a    Without charge  means that the municipality does not require a person to pay any         sum of money for the use of the recreational facilities.              b   The determination under Subsection     a  of whether the municipality provides the         use of recreational facilities without charge is made without regard to tax revenues that a         municipality collects and uses to acquire, develop, and maintain the recreational facilities.             Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Limitation on liability    Exception.                   Except as provided in Subsection     and notwithstanding Sections         and                 , a municipality that, after May  ,     , makes available recreational facilities for use by          the public without charge may not be held liable for any personal injury or property damage          resulting from a person s use of the recreational facilities.                   Nothing in Subsection     shall limit a municipality s liability that would, in the          absence of Subsection    , exist for                a   a willful or malicious failure to guard or warn against a dangerous condition, use,          structure, or activity  or               b   deliberate, willful, or malicious injury to a person or property.              Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  No affect on person s obligation to use due care.              Nothing in this part may be construed to relieve a person using the recreational facilities          from an obligation that the person would have in the absence of this part to exercise due care in          the use of the recreational facilities or from the legal consequences of a failure to use due care.              Section  .  Section          is amended to read                       .  Waiver of immunity for injury caused by defective, unsafe, or dangerous           condition of highways, bridges, or other structures.              Unless the injury arises out of one or more of the exceptions to waiver set forth in Section                   and except as provided in Section         , immunity from suit of all governmental          entities is waived for any injury caused by a defective, unsafe, or dangerous condition of any          highway, road, street, alley, crosswalk, sidewalk, culvert, tunnel, bridge, viaduct, or other structure                 located on them.             Section  .  Section          is amended to read                      .  Waiver of immunity for injury from dangerous or defective public building,          structure, or other public improvement    Exception.             Unless the injury arises out of one or more of the exceptions to waiver set forth in Section                  and except as provided in Section         , immunity from suit of all governmental         entities is waived for any injury caused from a dangerous or defective condition of any public         building, structure, dam, reservoir, or other public improvement.

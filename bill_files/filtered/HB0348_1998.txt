EQUIPMENT REQUIREMENTS ON LARGE           TRUCKS                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Carl R. Saunders          AN ACT RELATING TO MOTOR VEHICLES  AMENDING CERTAIN VEHICLE         SPLASHGUARD REQUIREMENTS  AND MAKING  TECHNICAL AMENDMENTS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          AMENDS                        .  , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section          .   is amended to read                        .  .  Splashguards or flaps at rear wheels of trucks, trailers, truck tractors,           or altered motor vehicles    Exemptions.                     The definitions in Section         .   apply to this section.                   a   Except as provided in Subsections     and    ,  when operated  a person may not          operate a vehicle on a highway , a  unless the vehicle is equipped with wheel covers, mudguards,          flaps, or splash aprons behind the rearmost wheels to prevent, as far as practicable, the wheels from          throwing dirt, water, or other materials on other vehicles if the vehicle is                i   a motor vehicle that has been altered from the original manufacturer s frame height or          in any other manner  so that the motor vehicle s wheels may throw dirt, water, or other materials          on other vehicles  that may increase spray on other vehicles                ii  any  truck  vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating of   ,    pounds or more                iii   any truck tractor   and  or               iv   any trailer or semitrailer with an unladen weight of     pounds or more  shall be          equipped with wheel covers, mudguards, flaps, or splash aprons behind the rearmost wheels to          prevent, as far as practicable, the wheels from throwing dirt, water, or other materials on other          vehicles .              b   The wheel covers, mudguards, flaps, or splash aprons shall               i   be at least as wide as the tires they are protecting               ii   be directly in line with the tires  and              iii   have a ground clearance of not more than     of the diameter of a rear axle wheel,         under any conditions of loading of the motor vehicle.                  Wheel covers, mudguards, flaps, or splash aprons are not required if the motor vehicle,         trailer, or semitrailer is designed and constructed so that the requirements of Subsection                  are accomplished by means of fenders, body construction, or other means of enclosure.                   Wheel covers, mudguards, flaps, or splash aprons are not required on a vehicle          operated or driven during fair weather on well maintained, hard surfaced roads if the motor          vehicle                a   was made in America prior to                     b   is any identifiable vintage or replica of a vintage that is titled as a vintage vehicle  or               c   is a street rod, which means a motor vehicle with a body that is or resembles the body          of a motor vehicle made prior to      for its original sale and which has a significant drive train          update.                    Rear  A person may not operate a vehicle on a highway unless rear wheels not covered          at the top by fenders, bodies, or other parts of the vehicle  shall be  are covered at the top by          protective means extending rearward at least to the center line of the rearmost axle.

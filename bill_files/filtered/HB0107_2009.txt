ECONOMIC IMPACT OF ILLEGAL ALIENS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Stephen D. Clark                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill provides for a study on the economic impact of illegal aliens residing within                           the state.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    provides for a study, under the direction and approval of the Executive                           Appropriations Committee, on the economic impact of illegal aliens residing within                           the state                                .    provides that the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall issue a                           request for proposal to conduct the study                                .    provides that the study shall identify and summarize state and local costs incurred                           from providing services to illegal aliens residing within the state as well as revenues                           received from illegal aliens residing within the state through the payment of taxes                           and fees to state and local government                                .    provides that the study shall include projections of costs and revenues for five, ten,                           and    years in the future and be a baseline for potential state legislation                                .    provides for a report of study findings to the Executive Appropriations Committee                           prior to the      General Session  and                               .    delays the effective date of S.B.   , Illegal Immigration, from the      General                           Session.                              Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                This bill appropriates                                .        ,    from the General Fund to the Office of Legislative Research and General                           Counsel for a request for proposal to conduct a study on the economic impact of                           illegal aliens residing within the state.                           Other Special Clauses                                This bill provides a split effective date.                           Uncodified Material Affected                            ENACTS UNCODIFIED MATERIAL                           AMENDS UNCODIFIED MATERIAL                            Laws of Utah     , Chapter   , Uncodified Section                                                       Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .   One time appropriation for study of economic impact of illegal aliens                           within the state.                                    a   There is appropriated     ,    from the General Fund for fiscal year                                  only to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel for a study on the economic                          impact of illegal aliens residing within the state.                                b   The study shall be made under the direction and approval of the Executive                          Appropriations Committee.                                c   As directed and approved by the Executive Appropriations Committee, the Office                          of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall issue a request for proposal  RFP  to                          conduct the study referred to in Subsection     a .                                    The study shall                                 a   identify, categorize, and summarize the costs incurred by the state and its political                          subdivisions for providing goods and services to illegal aliens residing within the state                                 b   look at a range of state and local public services received by illegal aliens residing                          within the state, but focus primarily on costs related to education, health care, and law                          enforcement, including incarceration                                 c   identify tax revenues and fees, such as income, property, and sales tax, collected at                          both the state and local levels from illegal aliens residing within the state                                    d   identify any restrictions placed on the state and its political subdivisions by the                          federal government or various court decisions that require the provision of certain state or local                          public services regardless of a person s lawful status in the United States                                 e   focus primarily on illegal alien costs incurred by the state and its local political                          subdivisions and revenues collected from illegal aliens during        , but also provide                          projections of costs and revenues for five, ten, and    years in the future  and                                f   be a baseline for potential state legislation related to illegal aliens residing within                          the state.                                    The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall coordinate with the                          entity awarded the study to establish a time line for reporting its study findings to the Executive                          Appropriations Committee of the Legislature prior to the      General Session.

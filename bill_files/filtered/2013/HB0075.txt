                            This bill modifies the Occupational and Professional Licensure Review Committee Act                            Title     Occupations and Professions  and the Legislative Oversight and Sunset Act  by                           limiting the use of the state s regulatory authority over lawful occupations to matters of                           public health or safety                            Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                     defines terms                                     modifies the membership of the Occupational and Professional Licensure Review                           Committee by requiring that the public members be selected by the director of the                           Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing                                     provides that the committee shall meet at least twice annually                                     requires that the committee conduct sunrise and sunset reviews for all occupations                           and professions proposed to be newly regulated or that are subject to their related                           statutes being terminated under Section       I                                               modifies the committee s reporting requirements                                     creates a statutory recognition of an individual s fundamental right to pursue any                           lawful occupation                                     provides that the state may not enact an occupational regulation that substantially                              burdens an individual s ability to engage in a lawful occupation unless the state demonstrates                           that the occupational regulation                                         addresses a compelling state interest in protecting against present  recognizable                            and significant harm to the health or safety of the public                                         furthers the state s compelling interest in protecting and maintaining the health                           or safety of the public  and                                        is the least restrictive means for furthering the state s compelling interest in                           protecting and maintaining the health or safety of the public                                     creates a private right of action for violation of this statute and establishes                           applicable burdens of proof and standards of judicial construction                                     requires that future legislation proposing licensing or regulation of a previously                           unregulated occupation or profession include a sunset date  and                                    makes technical changes                            Money Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            AMENDS CHAPTER  a   RIGHT TO PURSUE ANY LAWFUL OCCUPATION ACT                                     a       Title                                 This chapter is known as the  Right to Pursue any Lawful Occupation Act                                  Section       Section        a         is enacted to read                                     a       Definitions                                 For purposes of this chapter                                       Court  means a trial or appellate court                                      a    Lawful occupation  means a course of conduct  profession  or vocation that                           includes the sale of a good or the rendering of a service for profit                                  b    Lawful occupation  includes the sale of a good or the rendering of a service when                           the good or service itself is not illegal  irrespective of whether the individual selling the good or                           rendering the service is subject to an occupational regulation that restricts the individual s                           ability to sell the good or render the service                                  c    Lawful occupation  does not include a course of conduct  profession  or vocation                           of an individual selling a good or rendering a service when the good or service itself is illegal                                       Occupational regulation  means a statute  rule  practice  policy  or other                              state prescribed requirement related to the requirements of an individual to engage in a specific                           lawful occupation                                      a    State  means the government of the state                                  b    State  includes                                  i   a government office  department  division  bureau  or other body of government in                           the state  and                                 ii   a county  municipality  or political subdivision of the state                                      a    Substantial burden  means the imposition of a significant obstacle or cost on an                           individual seeking to enter into or continue in a lawful occupation                                  b    Substantial burden  does not include a burden that is incidental or negligible to the                           average person                                 Section        Section        a         is enacted to read                                     a       Right to engage in any lawful occupation                                      Each citizen of the state    years of age or older has a fundamental right to engage                           in any lawful occupation                                      The state may not adopt an occupational regulation that imposes a substantial                           burden on a citizen of the state    years of age or older unless the state demonstrates that the                           occupational regulation                                  a   addresses a compelling state interest in protecting against present  recognizable  and                           significant harm to the health or safety of the public                                  b   materially furthers the state s compelling interest in protecting and maintaining the                           health or safety of the public                                  c   is the least restrictive means for furthering the state s compelling interest in                           protecting and maintaining the health or safety of the public  and                                 d   is not primarily enacted to discourage competition or to confer economic advantage                           to a limited class                                 Section        Section        a         is enacted to read

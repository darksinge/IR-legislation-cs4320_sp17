        GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Blake D  Chard          AN ACT RELATING TO STATE AFFAIRS IN GENERAL  CREATING THE PRIVACY         TASK FORCE  PROVIDING FOR MEMBERSHIP  DELINEATING RESPONSIBILITIES         AND PROCEDURES  PROVIDING A REPORTING DATE  APPROPRIATING                 FROM THE GENERAL FUND  AND PROVIDING A REPEAL DATE          This act enacts uncodified material           Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section      Privacy Task Force    Creation    Membership    Quorum    Compensation              Staff                    There is created the Privacy Task Force consisting of the following    members          appointed by the speaker of the House and the president of the Senate as follows                a   three members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate  no more than          two of whom may be from the same political party                b   three members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the House          of Representatives  no more than two of whom may be from the same political party                c   the director of the Division of Archives and Records Services or the director s designee                d   two representatives of the media nominated by the Society of Professional Journalists                e   two representatives from state agencies which collect  use  and or share personal          information of individuals nominated by the Information Technology Commission chairs                f   one attorney who practices in the personal information privacy area nominated by the          State Bar Association                g   one representative from commercial or private business involved in the collection and          use of personal information of individuals whether that information comes from public records          and or other sources                h   one representative of a trade organization whose members obtain and use personal         information of individuals               i   two representatives from public interest organizations interested in privacy of         individuals  personal information nominated by the Information Technology Commission chairs          and              j   two representatives from the general public nominated by the Information Technology         Commission chairs                   The members in Subsections     g and     h  may be selected from the utility  banking          health  medical  publishing  marketing  and information technology industries                    a   The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed under          Subsection     a  as a cochair of the task force                b   The speaker of the House of Representatives shall designate a member of the House          of Representatives appointed under Subsection     b  as a cochair of the task force                    Attendance of ten or more members at a meeting is a quorum for transaction of          business   The action of a majority of a quorum constitutes the action of the task force                    a   Salaries and expenses of the members of the task force who are legislators shall be          paid in accordance with Section        and Legislative Joint Rule                      b   Members of the task force who are not legislators may not receive compensation for          their work associated with the task force  but may receive per diem and expenses incurred as a          member of the task force at the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections            A       and   A                          The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support          to the task force               Section      Duties    Interim report                    The task force shall review and make recommendations regarding individuals  right          to privacy including                a   defining personal privacy and what is a reasonable expectation of privacy of personal          information                b   determining what personal information about individuals is collected  used  sold  or          shared by business entities                c   determining whether changes are needed to any laws or administrative rules regarding                 individuals  privacy               d   any other personal privacy issue regarding individuals  right to privacy for personal         information               e   determining remedies against or penalties for those who infringe on personal privacy         or deliberately divulge private information               f   identifying existing state and federal statutes and self regulatory practices to ensure         consumer protection  individual rights  and fair practices  and              g   considering the impacts on electronic commerce  consumer choice  and citizen         protection                    A final report  including any proposed legislation  shall be presented to the Information          Technology Commission and the Public Utilities and Technology Interim Committee before          November                        Section      Appropriation               There is appropriated from the General Fund for fiscal year                                   to the Senate to pay for the compensation and expenses of senators on the task          force                           to the House of Representatives to pay for the compensation and expenses of          representatives on the task force  and                           to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel to pay for staffing          the task force               Section      Repeal date               This act is repealed November                Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns

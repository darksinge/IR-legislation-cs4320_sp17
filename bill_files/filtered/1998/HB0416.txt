FORMS           OF COUNTY GOVERNMENT                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Christine R  Fox Finlinson          AN ACT RELATING TO TASK FORCES  CREATING THE OPTIONAL FORMS OF         COUNTY GOVERNMENT TASK FORCE  ESTABLISHING MEMBERSHIP AND         DUTIES  APPROPRIATING         FOR TASK FORCE SALARIES AND EXPENSES          PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE  AND PROVIDING A REPEAL DATE           This act enacts uncodified material           Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section      Task force creation    Membership    Chairs                    There is created the Optional Forms of County Government Task Force consisting of                a   three members of the Senate  appointed by the president of the Senate  and               b   five members of the House of Representatives  appointed by the speaker of the House          of Representatives                    The president of the Senate shall appoint a senator appointed to the task force under          Subsection     a  as Senate chair of the task force  and the speaker of the House of Representatives          shall appoint a representative appointed to the task force under Subsection     b  as House chair          of the task force               Section      Task force duties               The task force shall                    review current statutes establishing the procedure for a county to adopt an optional          form of government and the optional forms available under current statutes                    study whether the currently available optional forms of county government are meeting          the needs of counties in the state                    explore the advisability of modifying the currently available optional forms of county          government or of providing in statute for other optional forms of county government                   determine whether current statutory procedures for adopting an optional form of county         government should be revised  and                  if the task force considers it to be appropriate  make recommendations to or propose         legislation for adoption by the Legislature              Section      Quorum    Meetings    Report to interim committee                   A majority of the task force constitutes a quorum  and action by a majority of a quorum         at any meeting constitutes action of the task force                   The task force may hold up to six meetings                    The task force shall give a report of its activities and recommendations to the Political          Subdivisions Interim Committee no later than November                        Section      Salaries and expenses               Salaries and expenses of legislators are paid in accordance with Section        and          Legislative Joint Rule                     Section      Staff support               The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support to the          task force               Section      Appropriation               There is appropriated from the General Fund for fiscal year                                   to the Senate for salary and expenses of senators who are members of the task          force                           to the House of Representatives for salary and expenses of representatives who          are members of the task force  and                           to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel for staffing          expenses associated with the task force               Section      Effective date               If approved by two thirds of all the members elected to each house  this act takes effect          upon approval by the governor  or the day following the constitutional time limit of Utah          Constitution Article VII  Section    without the governor s signature  or in the case of a veto  the          date of veto override               Section      Repeal date                      This act is repealed November                Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises

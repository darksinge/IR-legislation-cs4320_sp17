Section          is amended to read                     . Interference with peace officer.                 A person is guilty of a class B misdemeanor if  he has knowledge  the person       knows, or by the exercise of reasonable care should have  knowledge  known, that a peace       officer is seeking to effect a lawful arrest or detention of that person or another person and       interferes with the arrest or detention by                    a   use of force or any weapon                    b    the arrested person s refusal  refusing to perform any act required by lawful       order               a    i   necessary to effect the arrest or detention  and              b    ii   made by a peace officer involved in the arrest or detention  or                   c    the arrested person s or another person s refusal  refusing to refrain from       performing any act that would impede the arrest or detention.                 Recording the actions of a law enforcement officer with a camera, mobile phone, or       other photographic device, while the officer is performing official duties in plain view, does       not by itself constitute              a   interference with the officer              b   willful resistance              c   disorderly conduct  or             d   obstruction of justice.

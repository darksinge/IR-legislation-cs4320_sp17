ELECTION LAW COMMISSION                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Mary Carlson          AN ACT RELATING TO ELECTIONS  ESTABLISHING A STATE ELECTIONS         COMMISSION  DEFINING ITS MEMBERSHIP, ITS MEMBER SELECTION         PROCEDURES, AND THE COMMISSION S POWERS AND DUTIES  AND MAKING         TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           ENACTS                  A      , Utah Code Annotated                      A      , Utah Code Annotated                      A      , Utah Code Annotated                      A      , Utah Code Annotated                      A      , Utah Code Annotated                      A      , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section    A       is enacted to read                  A      .  Definition.              As used in this chapter,  total constitutional officer vote  means the total number of votes          cast statewide in the last gubernatorial election for governor, attorney general, state auditor, and          state treasurer, excluding the vote for any candidate who had no opposition.              Section  .  Section    A       is enacted to read                  A      .  Creation.                   There is created a State Elections Commission.                   The governor, with the advice and consent of the Senate, shall appoint members to the          State Elections Commission as provided in this section.                   a   Each political party that received at least     of the total constitutional officer vote         shall submit a list of at least four nominees to the governor.              b   The governor shall select two commission members from each list of nominees         submitted to the governor under this section.                  a   At the first commission meeting, the commission shall select one additional         commission member by a majority vote.              b   If the commission is unable to select an additional member within    days after the         first commission meeting, the governor shall replace one member of the commission from each         political party represented on the commission in accordance with the procedures established by          this chapter.               c    If the new commission fails to reach agreement upon an additional member, the          process in Subsection     b  shall continue until an additional commission member is selected.                   a   Except as provided in Subsection     b , the governor shall appoint commission          members to serve four year terms.               b   i   When selecting the initial commission members, the governor shall select     of           the commission members to serve two year terms and the remaining     of the commission          members to serve four year terms.               ii   The commission member selected by the commission at the first commission meeting          shall serve a four year term.               c   i  Commission members shall serve a four year term unless their position becomes          vacant as provided in Section   A       or unless they were appointed to a two year term under          Subsection  b .               ii  Commission members may not serve more than two four year terms.                   a  The commission shall elect a chair and vice chair from the commission to serve for          a two year period.               b  The chair and vice chair may not be members of the same political party.               c  The chair may not be a member of the same political party as the preceding chair.                  A simple majority of the commission is a quorum for the transaction of business.                   a  The commission shall meet at least once per quarter.               b  Additional meetings may be called by                i  the chair of the commission                       ii  the executive director of the commission  or              iii  a simple majority of the commission members communicating their request for a         meeting in writing to the chair.                  a   For each meeting that a commission member attends, the commission member shall         receive a per diem rate that is equal to the rate legislators receive under Subsection          .              b   Members may also be reimbursed for expenses incurred in the performance of their         official duties as established in this chapter.                   a   The commission shall hire an executive director and additional support staff as         needed.               b   In conjunction with the Department of Human Resource Management,  the commission          shall establish job classifications, job descriptions, and salary ranges for the executive director and          each staff member.               c   The executive director may be removed from office for any reason at any time by a          simple majority vote of the members of the commission.                    Commission members are not subject to the requirements of Title   , Chapter   ,          Utah Public Officers  and Employees  Ethics Act.              Section  .  Section    A       is enacted to read                  A      .  Commission vacancies.                   A vacancy occurs on the commission when                a   the political party of a commission member does not receive at least     of the total          constitutional officer vote                b   a commission member resigns from the commission or is otherwise unable to complete          his term  or               c   a commission member completes his or her term.                   a   Except as provided in Subsection     b , the governor, within    days after a          vacancy has occurred, shall appoint a member of the same political party as that of the member          creating the vacancy to fill the vacancy in accordance with the procedures established in this          chapter.               b   i   When the political party of a commission member does not receive more than              of the vote required by Subsection     a , the terms of the commission members of that political          party have expired, and those persons are no longer members of the commission.                      ii   The governor may not replace any vacant positions that occur under Subsection  b .             Section  .  Section    A       is enacted to read                 A      .  Adding members to the commission.             By the January    of the year after each gubernatorial election, the governor shall select         two additional nominees to serve on the commission from a list of at least four nominees submitted         by the state chair of a political party if the political party                   received at least     of the total constitutional officer vote  and                 does not already have a representative on the commission who was appointed by the         governor.

         JOINT RESOLUTION ON HOMELESS AND RUNAWAY      YOUTH           GENERAL SESSION      STATE OF UTAH      Chief Sponsor   Gage Froerer      Senate Sponsor    Luz Escamilla             LONG TITLE      General Description             This joint resolution of the Legislature designates the month of November as        Homeless and Runaway Youth Awareness Month.        Highlighted Provisions             This resolution                   designates the month of November as  Homeless and Runaway Youth Awareness       Month   and                  recognizes the efforts of those addressing the problem of youth homelessness.       Special Clauses             None               Be it resolved by the Legislature of the state of Utah             WHEREAS, between     and  ,    youth in Utah experience homelessness each year, youth are lured into prostitution within    hours of       leaving home and are susceptible to becoming victims of human trafficking             WHEREAS, homeless youth are typically too poor to secure basic needs, are unable to       access adequate medical or mental health care, and often engage in  survival sex  in exchange       for food, clothing, or shelter             WHEREAS, essential for programs involving businesses, families, law enforcement agencies,       schools, and community and faith based organizations to be effective at helping youth remain       off the streets, and for making youth homelessness prevention a local and state priority             NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of the state of Utah       designates the month of November as  Homeless and Runaway Youth Awareness Month  and       encourages businesses, organizations, and volunteers to continue to intensify their efforts to       address the needs of homeless and runaway youth.            BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature supports the values and efforts of       businesses, organizations, and volunteers who are dedicated to preventing youth homelessness       and to meeting the needs of homeless children and teens.            BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature applauds the initiative taken, and       the time and resources donated by businesses, organizations, and volunteers, to increase the       public s awareness of the problems of youth homelessness and increase awareness of the causes       and potential solutions for youth homelessness, and also applauds the work they have       performed through effective public and private partnerships to prevent homelessness among       children and teens.Legislative Review Note     as of                  AMOffice of Legislative Research and General Counsel

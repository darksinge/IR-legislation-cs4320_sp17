ACCESS TO MEDICAL RECORDS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  J. Brent Haymond          AN ACT RELATING TO HEALTH  REQUIRING THE PATIENT BE NOTIFIED IF HIS         HEALTH CARE TREATMENT IS SUBJECT TO REVIEW BY A HEALTH CARE         PROVIDER PRACTICE REVIEW ENTITY.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          ENACTS                       , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  Notification of patient whose treatment is subject to review.                   As used in this section,  health care provider  has the same meaning as defined in          Section        .                   When a division board, health care facility board or committee, professional society,          or any other entity acting under this chapter reviews or evaluates the quality or adequacy of a          health care provider s treatment of a patient, the entity shall notify that patient                a   that confidential records or information that would otherwise be protected by          patient physician privilege are being shared and reviewed by that entity                b   that the patient has the right to obtain from the reviewing entity a copy of each record          shared and reviewed by the entity                c   of the title and address of the reviewing entity, and the name and telephone number of          a person acting on behalf of the entity whom the patient may contact to request the records.                   a   The reviewing entity shall provide notice under Subsection     by certified mail,          if the address of the patient or the patient s guardian or representative is reasonably available to the          entity.               b   If an address is not available under Subsection  a , notice shall be provided by         publication, such as under Rule  , Utah Rules of Civil Procedure.              c   Notice under this subsection shall be posted or published as soon as reasonably         possible, but not later than three business days after the reviewing entity is aware the patient s         records will be subject to review.                  Costs of providing the records to the requesting patient shall be assumed by the         reviewing entity.      Legislative Review Note      as of                PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

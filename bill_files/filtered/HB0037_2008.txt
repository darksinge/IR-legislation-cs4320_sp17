UTAH BUSINESS RESOURCE CENTERS ACT                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   David Clark                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          Committee Note                               The Business and Labor Interim Committee recommended this bill.                           General Description                                This bill provides for the establishment and administration of business resource centers                           by the Governor s Office of Economic Development.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    enacts the Utah Business Resource Centers Act                                .    provides that the Governor s Office of Economic Development shall establish                           business resource centers as one stop resource and assistance centers to provide                           business support, education, sources of funding, training, and networking to Utah                           businesses                                .    provides for the composition and administration of the business resource centers                                                    .    provides duties and responsibilities for the centers  and                               .    creates the Utah Business Resource Centers Executive Board and allows the                           executive board to appoint an advisory board.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                This bill appropriates                                .    as an ongoing appropriation subject to future budget constraints,   ,   ,    from                           the General Fund for fiscal year         to the Governor s Office of Economic                              Development.                           Other Special Clauses                                This bill takes effect on July  ,     . Section         f          is enacted to read                            Part   .  Utah Business Resource Centers Act                                     f     .  Title.                               This part is known as the  Utah Business Resource Centers Act.                                Section  .    Section         f          is enacted to read                                     f     .  Purpose.                               The Legislature recognizes that                                     the development of and assistance to business in Utah is a state public purpose                          necessary to assure the growth of the state s economy and provide adequate employment                          opportunities for its citizens                                     many new and existing businesses in the state fail for lack of support and from not                          knowing where to go for support services that could be available to help them succeed through                          one stop business resource centers                                     the state s economy will be improved if these new and existing businesses receive                          assistance and support and grow into healthy tax paying entities  and                                    these businesses can receive needed assistance and support through the creation of                          business resource centers.                                  Section  .    Section         f          is enacted to read                                     f     .  Definitions.                               As used in this part,  business resource centers  means entities established by the                          Governor s Office of Economic Development as certified one stop resource centers that                          provide coordination of business support, education, tracking of clients, access to sources of                          funding, training, technical expertise, talent, and networking for new and existing businesses                          located throughout the state.                               Section  .    Section         f          is enacted to read                                     f     .  Establishment and administration of business resource centers                              Components.                                    The Governor s Office of Economic Development shall establish business resource                          centers throughout the state.                                    The office shall provide administrative oversight for the centers.                                    The office shall work with the Business Resource Centers Executive Board,                          established under Section          f          and hereafter referred to in this part as  the board,  to                          provide operational oversight of and coordination with the business resource centers.                                    a   A business resource center may be composed of state, local, and federal                          agencies and institutions, as well as private entities that                                 i   participate in the activities of the center  and                                ii   provide personnel or other appropriate links to the center.                                b   Other entities capable of providing supportive services to Utah businesses that are                          not initially involved in the establishment of a business resource center may apply to the center                          to become a provider of services at the center.                                c   Each business resource center established under this section shall provide the board                          with a service plan, to include funding and a one to one match unless exempted by the board,                          which would be made available or supplied to cover the expenses and services offered at the                          center.                                d   The board shall review each plan referred to in Subsection     c  for approval by                          the office as a precondition for providing the service being offered.                                    As components of a business resource center program, the center may                                 a   partner with the Governor s Office of Economic Development and other entities to                             develop and establish web based access to virtual business resource center services over the                          Internet to assist in establishing and growing businesses in the state, and particularly in those                          situations where traveling to the business resource center site is not possible or practical  and                                b   develop programs for outreach to entrepreneurs in rural areas of the state.                                    The Governor s Office of Economic Development shall make a report by November                            of each year to the Legislature s Business and Labor Interim Committee and the Workforce                          Services and Community and Economic Development Interim Committee on the performance                          of the business resource centers in carrying out their duties and responsibilities under Section                                  f         .                               Section  .    Section         f          is enacted to read                                      f     .  Duties and responsibilities.                                     A business resource center shall                                  a   be a focal point to provide businesses assistance in partnering with governmental                           entities and other business resources                                  b   initiate and encourage business education programs, including programs in                           coordination and collaboration with public, private, and governmental institutions  and                                 c   use existing physical facilities, in conjunction with local, state, or federal agencies,                           if appropriate, secured through leases, rentals, donations, shared facilities, or other                           arrangements through which they shall provide the services described in this part.                                     A business resource center may                                  a   provide research, development, or training programs for new businesses                                  b   provide needs assessment relating to new or existing businesses in conjunction with                           other public or private economic development programs or initiatives                                  c   provide business incubator space or services, or both, to businesses based on                           criteria established by the office in consultation with the board  and                                 d   work with local business leaders and government officials to help them formulate                           and implement sound economic development decisions for their communities.                                Section  .    Section         f          is enacted to read                                      f     .  Utah Business Resource Centers Executive Board    Creation                               Membership    Vacancies    Chairs.                                     There is created the Utah Business Resource Centers Executive Board, composed                              of seven members appointed by the executive director of the Governor s Office of Economic                           Development.                                     The executive director                                  a   may appoint up to five members from urban areas in the state  and                                 b   shall appoint at least two members from rural areas in the state.                                     Each board member shall have a background or expertise in any one or all or the                           following                                  a   state or local economic development                                  b   business networking, growth, or development                                  c   entrepreneurship                                  d   business management or administration  or                                 e   the establishment of partnerships or collaborative efforts with state, local, and                           federal agencies and institutions, as well as private entities.                                     a   Except as required by Subsection     b , the executive director shall appoint                           board members for four year terms.                                 b   Notwithstanding the requirements of Subsection     a , the board shall, at the time                           of appointment or reappointment, adjust the length of terms to ensure that the terms of these                           members are staggered so that approximately half of the seven members are appointed every                           two years.                                 c   When a vacancy occurs in the membership for any reason, the replacement shall be                           appointed by the executive director for the unexpired term in the same manner as the vacated                           member was chosen.                                     The board shall elect one of its members as a chair of the board for a two year term.                                     The board shall meet at the call of the chair, but at least quarterly.                                     a   A majority of the members of the board constitute a quorum.                                 b   The action of a majority of a quorum constitutes the action of the board.                                     a   A board member may not receive compensation or benefits for the member s                           service, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the performance of the member s                           official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections       A           and                                A          .                                 b   A member may decline to receive per diem and expenses authorized under Section                                  a .                                Section  .    Section         f          is enacted to read                                      f     .  Duties.                                The board shall                                      make recommendations to the office on requirements for the requisite certification                           of each business resource center and director                                      make recommendations to the office for approval of certified business resource                           centers and ongoing certification status of the centers                                     make recommendations to the office for certification of the business plans the                           board is required to review under Subsection          f             d                                       if requested by the executive director, assist the office in providing operational                           oversight to and coordination of the business resource centers established under this part                                      identify issues and make recommendations to the office regarding programs,                           policies, and procedures that could be implemented by business resource centers in fulfilling                           their duties and responsibilities under Section          f                                               make budget recommendations to the office regarding the operation and staffing of                           business resource centers established under this part  and                                     appoint an advisory board that shall include                                  a   at least one representative from each business resource center  and                                 b   other individuals as determined by the executive board.                                Section  .   Appropriation.                                As an ongoing appropriation subject to future budget constraints, there is appropriated                           from the General Fund for fiscal year        ,   ,   ,    to the Governor s Office of                           Economic Development to fund the Utah Business Resource Centers Act.                                Section  .   Effective date.                                This bill takes effect on July  ,     .

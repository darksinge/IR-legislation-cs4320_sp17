REGULATING SALE OF TOBACCO PRODUCTS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Wayne A. Harper          AN ACT RELATING TO COMMERCE  DEFINING TERMS  ALLOWING THE         REGULATION OF TOBACCO PRODUCTS BY LOCAL AUTHORITIES  REQUIRING         THE SUBMISSION OF A FEDERAL WAIVER BY THE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH          AND ALLOWING THE RESTRICTION OF TOBACCO SALES WITHIN THE PROXIMITY         OF A CHURCH OR SCHOOL UPON THE APPROVAL OF THE FEDERAL WAIVER.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           ENACTS                  B      , Utah Code Annotated                      B      , Utah Code Annotated                      B      , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section    B       is enacted to read            TITLE   B.  TOBACCO CONTROL ACT            CHAPTER  .  LOCAL REGULATION FOR TOBACCO SALES                  B      .  Definitions.              As used in this chapter                     Cigarette  means any roll for smoking made wholly or in part of tobacco, irrespective          of size or shape, and whether or not the tobacco is flavored, adulterated, or mixed with any other          ingredient, the wrapper or cover of which is made of paper or any other substance or material          except tobacco.                    Local authority  means                a   the county legislative body of the county if the premises are located in an          unincorporated area of a county  or               b   the governing body of the city or town if the premises are located in an incorporated         city or town.                   Retailer  means the holder of a permit issued by a local authority to allow the holder         to engage in the sale of tobacco products to consumers.                   School  means a public or private elementary or secondary school.                   Tobacco products  means all products made of, or containing tobacco, including         cigarettes, that are intended for smoking, inhaling, chewing, or any other form of ingestion.             Section  .  Section    B       is enacted to read                 B      .  Local permitting for sale of tobacco products.                   A local authority may                a   require a permit for the retail sale of tobacco products and may issue, renew, suspend,          and revoke the permit based on enacted local ordinances  and               b   otherwise regulate the retail sale of tobacco products consistent with regulations of the          federal Food and Drug Administration.                   A local authority s suspension or revocation of a permit issued under this section          prohibits the retailer whose permit is suspended or revoked from continuing to operate under any          other license, permit, or other authority granted by the local authority or the state.                   Upon the approval of a waiver application submitted in accordance with Subsection             , a local authority may not grant a permit under this chapter to sell tobacco products within the          proximity of any church or school.  The local authority shall define the proximity and shall apply          the definition in a uniform and consistent manner.                   The Department of Health shall submit an application before July  ,     , to the          federal Food and Drug Administration requesting the waiver of any federal regulation which may          prevent the state or a local authority from prohibiting the sale of tobacco within the proximity of          a church or school in the manner provided for in Subsection    .              Section  .  Section    B       is enacted to read                  B      .  Effect of chapter.              This chapter does not affect the state taxation, licensure, or regulation of tobacco products          under Title   , Chapter   , Cigarette and Tobacco Tax and Licensing Act, or Title   , Chapter   ,          Part  , Cigarettes and Tobacco and Psychotoxic Chemical Solvents.              Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

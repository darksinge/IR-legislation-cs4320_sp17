Wanton destruction of livestock    Penalties    Seizure and disposition of                            property.                                     As used in this section                                  a    Law enforcement officer  is as defined in Section                  .                                 b    Livestock  means a domestic animal or fur bearer raised or kept for profit,                           including                                  i   cattle                                  ii   sheep                                  iii   goats                                  iv   swine                                  v   horses                                  vi   mules                                  vii   poultry  and                                 viii   domesticated elk as defined in Section                 .                                     A person is guilty of wanton destruction of livestock if that person                                  a   injures, releases, or causes the death of livestock  and                                 b   does so                                     i   intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly       and                                 ii   without the permission of the owner of the livestock.                                     Wanton destruction of livestock is punishable as a                                  a   class B misdemeanor if the aggregate value of the livestock is      or less                                  b   class A misdemeanor if the aggregate value of the livestock is more than     , but                           does not exceed   ,                                     c   third degree felony if the aggregate value of the livestock is more than   ,   , but                           does not exceed   ,     and                                 d   second degree felony if the aggregate value of the livestock is more than   ,   .                                     a   A law enforcement officer may seize a material or device used for the wanton                           destruction of livestock.                                 b   Upon a finding by the court on the record that a material, device, or vehicle was                           used in the wanton destruction of livestock, the material, device, or vehicle is subject to                           criminal or civil forfeiture under Title   , Chapter  , Utah Uniform Forfeiture Procedures Act.                                     A law enforcement officer may seize and impound a vehicle used for the wanton                           destruction of livestock to                                  a   provide for the safekeeping of the vehicle, if the owner or operator is arrested  and                                 b   search the vehicle for evidence of the wanton destruction of livestock as provided                           by law.                                     A law enforcement officer seizing and impounding a vehicle under Subsection                               shall comply with the provisions of Section         a         .                                     The owner of a seized vehicle is not liable for the payment of an impound fee or, if                           the fee has been paid, is entitled to reimbursement of the fee paid, if                                  a   no charges are filed                                  b   all charges are dismissed that involve the use of the vehicle for the wanton                           destruction of livestock                                  c   the person charged with using the vehicle for the wanton destruction of livestock is                           found by a court to be not guilty  or                                 d   the vehicle was stolen at the time of the impoundment as shown by a copy of the                           stolen vehicle report presented within    days of the impoundment.

Hickman          AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC EDUCATION  CREATING A NEW SCHOOL BUILDINGS         MAINTENANCE AND OPERATION COSTS TRUST FUND TO PAY FOR FIRST YEAR         MAINTENANCE AND OPERATION COSTS RELATED TO THE OPENING OF NEW         SCHOOLS  PROVIDING A DISTRIBUTION PROCESS  AND PROVIDING A   ,   ,             APPROPRIATION TO THE FUND.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           ENACTS                  A       , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section    A        is enacted to read                  A       .  New School Buildings Maintenance and Operation Costs Trust Fund.                   There is created an expendable trust fund entitled the  New School Buildings          Maintenance and Operation Costs Trust Fund.                    The fund consists of appropriations made to the fund by the Legislature.                   a   The fund shall earn interest.               b   All interest earned on fund moneys shall be deposited into the fund.                   a   The State Board of Education shall obtain a statement of the fund balance as of July            of each calendar year from the Division of Finance.               b   The board may expend the July   fund balance during the fiscal year beginning July            as provided in Subsection    .               c   The board shall distribute money from the fund to qualified school districts for          first year maintenance and operation costs related to the opening of new schools within the          districts as follows               i       ,    for each new elementary school facility               ii       ,    for each new middle school with students in grades   through                 iii       ,    for each new junior high school with students in grades   through    and              iv     ,   ,    for each new high school with students in grades    through   .                  In order for a school district to qualify for fund money under Subsection     c , it must               a   have experienced significant student growth for a period of at least three consecutive         years, with an average annual increase in its student population of at least     students or                  b   justify to the satisfaction of the board that the opening of the new school was not to          replace an existing facility and that alternative housing methods such as portables, double sessions,          or year round schools had been employed prior to the construction of the new facility                c   demonstrate to the satisfaction of the board that the opening of the new facility          significantly impacts the existing funding of the district  and               d   have a student teacher ratio at or above the state average.              Section  .   Appropriation.              There is appropriated for fiscal year        ,   ,   ,    from the Uniform School Fund          to the New School Buildings Maintenance and Operations Costs Trust Fund.

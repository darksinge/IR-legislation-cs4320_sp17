AMENDMENTS TO STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Perry Buckner          AN ACT RELATING TO THE CRIMINAL CODE  CLARIFYING THAT THE STATUTE OF         LIMITATIONS DOES NOT RUN WHILE A DEFENDANT IS APPEALING A         CONVICTION OR AN INDICTMENT OR INFORMATION IS PENDING  ADDING         MISCONDUCT OF A PUBLIC OFFICER TO THE FOUR YEAR LIMITATION  AND         MAKING TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           AMENDS                        . , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                            , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section          .  is amended to read                        . .  Time limitations for prosecution of misusing public monies, falsification           or alteration of government records, and misconduct by a public officer or employee.                   A prosecution for misusing public monies  or , falsification or alteration of government          records, or any offense based upon misconduct in office by a public officer or employee shall be          commenced within four years after facts constituting the offense have been reported to a          prosecutor having responsibility and jurisdiction to prosecute the offense.                   Nothing in this section shall operate to shorten the limitation of actions under          Subsection            .              Section  .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Defendant out of state    Indictment, information, or appeal pending.                   The period of limitation does not run against any defendant during any period of time           he is  in which                a   the defendant is out of the state following the commission of an offense .                b   an indictment or an information is pending against the defendant  or              c   the defendant is appealing or collaterally attacking the conviction.                  As used in this section, the period of time during which an indictment or information         is pending against the defendant means that period of time beginning with the day the indictment         or information is filed in a court of competent jurisdiction and ending with the day the accusation         is, by order of a court of competent jurisdiction, determined to be invalid for any reason.                  When the period of limitation is suspended pursuant to this section, the suspension         includes any known charges which were not filed or were dismissed as a result of a plea          agreement.  Notwithstanding any other limitation, the prosecutor may commence a new          prosecution within one year after the order determining the invalidity of the prior prosecution.  The          new prosecution may include any known charges which were not filed or were dismissed as a          result of a plea agreement.                   When the issue is raised, the judge shall determine whether the prosecution is barred          by the limitations in this part by a preponderance of the evidence.

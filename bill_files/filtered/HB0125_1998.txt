Section          is enacted to read           CHAPTER   .  OUTFITTERS AND GUIDES ACT                      .  Title.             This chapter shall be known as the  Outfitters and Guides Act.              Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                      .  Definitions.             As used in this chapter                    Consideration  means something of value given or done in exchange for something          of value given or done by another.                   a    Guide  means an individual who, for consideration, accompanies a client of an          outfitter on an expedition for the purpose of taking or pursuing big game, cougar, or bear.               b    Guide  includes an individual who is an outfitter who personally accompanies a client          on an expedition for the purpose of taking big game, cougar, or bear.                    Outfitter  means any person who                a   for consideration, provides any of the following to enable a person to take or pursue          big game, cougar, or bear                i   a saddle or pack animal                ii   lodging or other accommodations                iii   camping equipment                iv   a vehicle, watercraft, or other conveyance  or               v   a personal service  and               b   accompanies the person, either part or all of the way, on an expedition for the purpose          of taking or pursuing big game, cougar, or bear or supervises a guide who accompanies the person.              Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  Certificate of registration required to act as an outfitter or guide.                   Except as provided in Subsection    , a person may not act as an outfitter or guide or          advertise or otherwise represent to the public that the person is an outfitter or guide without first          obtaining a certificate of registration in accordance with this chapter.                   A landowner is not required to obtain a certificate of registration to provide the services          of an outfitter or guide, as described in Section        , if those services are provided to assist a                 person in pursuing or taking big game, cougar, or bear on the landowner s land. Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                      .  Qualifications for guides.                  An individual may receive a certificate of registration to act as a guide if the individual               a   is    years of age or older                b    is not subject to a revocation order pertaining to the privilege of pursuing, possessing,          or taking protected wildlife or acting as an outfitter or guide, including a revocation order issued          by another state                c   attests in writing that he or she has read and understands the laws and rules relating to          the hunting of big game, cougar, and bear                d   has successfully completed an American Red Cross standard first aid training course          or has obtained equivalent training  and               e   has paid a fee of    . Section          is enacted to read                       .  Written information for clients    Remedy.                   An outfitter may not provide any services to a client unless the client has signed a          written contract with the outfitter that specifies                a   the type of services to be provided                b   the experience and qualifications of the guide                c   dates of service                d   transportation arrangements                e   fees for services and payment schedule  and               f   provisions, including related costs, for the recision of service arrangements.                   Upon the division s request, an outfitter must make a contract available to the division          for its inspection.  Provisions relating to fees for services need not be disclosed.                          A contract for the services of an outfitter shall be void and unenforceable by the         outfitter if the outfitter has not obtained a certificate of registration from the division.             Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                      .  Records    Annual report.                  Each outfitter shall maintain the following records until the report required by         Subsection     is filed with the division               a   the number and species of big game, cougar, or bear taken by each client               b   each client s license, permit, and tag number  and              c   the name, address, and certificate of registration number of each guide employed or          under contract by the outfitter.                   The records must be kept current and available for inspection by the division during          reasonable business hours after the division has provided    hours notice of its intent to inspect          the records.                   By January   , each outfitter shall submit a report for the previous calendar year          containing the information specified in Subsection    .                   If an outfitter is required to report the information provided in Subsection     to a          federal agency, a copy of that report may be submitted to the division to satisfy the requirements          of Subsection    .              Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  General duties.              Before accompanying a client on an expedition for the purpose of taking or pursuing big          game, cougar, or bear, an outfitter or guide must                    ensure that the appropriate licenses, permits, and tags required under this title and the          rules and proclamations of the Wildlife Board are in the client s possession                    procure the required permits if pursuing big game, cougar, or bear on land owned or          leased by the state or federal government  and                   obtain written permission from the landowner as provided in Section         , if          pursuing big game, cougar, or bear on private property.              Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  Outfitters  and Guides  Registration Review Committee created with power           to revoke certificates of registration   membership   appointment   terms   expenses.                          There is created the Outfitters  and Guides  Registration Review Committee which shall         have power to revoke the certificate of registration of an outfitter or guide in accordance with this         section and Section        .                  a   The committee shall consist of three members appointed by the division director.              b   Each member shall be qualified to perform the adjudicative functions provided in this         section and Section        .  The director may not appoint a division employee who investigates         or enforces wildlife violations.              c   The division director shall select               i   one member from a list of nominees submitted by the Utah Cooperative Wildlife          Management Unit Association  and               ii   one member from a list of nominees submitted by the Wildlife Board.                   a   Except as provided in Subsection     b , the members shall serve four year terms.               b   One of the initial appointments shall be for a term of two years.               c   If a vacancy occurs, the division director shall appoint a replacement for the unexpired          term in accordance with Subsection    .                   a   The committee shall elect a chair.               b   Meetings may be called by the chair.               c   Two members shall constitute a quorum.  A quorum is necessary for the committee to          act.               d   Approval of any motion requires a favorable vote by at least two members.                   The division shall provide staff support to the committee.                   a   i   Committee members who are not government employees shall receive no          compensation or benefits for their services, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the          performance of the member s official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance          under Sections   A       and   A      .               ii   Members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their service.               b   i   State government officer and employee members who do not receive salary, per          diem, or expenses from their agency for their service may receive per diem and expenses incurred          in the performance of their official duties from the committee at the rates established by the          Division of Finance under Sections   A       and   A      .               ii   State government officer and employee members may decline to receive per diem and                 expenses for their service.             Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Revocation of an outfitter s or guide s certificate of registration.                  The certificate of registration of an outfitter or guide shall be revoked in accordance         with Section        , except the duties and powers of the hearing officer referred to in Section                 shall be assumed by the Outfitters  and Guides  Registration Review Committee created         in Section        .                  The Outfitters  and Guides  Registration Review Committee               a   shall revoke the certificate of registration of an outfitter or guide for the reasons          specified in Section          and               b   may revoke the certificate of registration of an outfitter or guide for a violation of Title            , Chapter   , Utah Consumer Sales Practices Act, or rules adopted under the act.                   a   A person whose certificate of registration has been revoked may not continue to act          as an outfitter or guide.               b   A violation of Subsection     a  is a class A misdemeanor.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Rulemaking authority.              The Wildlife Board may make and enforce rules consistent with this chapter specifying                    application procedures and the form of applications for outfitters  and guides           certificates of registration  and                   recordkeeping and reporting requirements.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  List of registered outfitters and professional guides.              The division shall make a list available to the public of all registered outfitters and guides          without any preference indicated.              Section   .   Effective date.

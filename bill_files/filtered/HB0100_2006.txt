ENVIRONMENTAL LITIGATION BOND                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Aaron Tilton                                Senate Sponsor             Curtis S. Bramble                                                         LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill requires entities that do business in the state to file a bond with the Division of                           Corporations and Commercial Code when it begins the environmental litigation.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    defines terms                                .    requires an entity doing business in Utah to file a bond with the division when it                           begins environmental litigation                                .    authorizes the division to                                    .    establish a fee for the bond filing                                    .    hold a hearing to establish the bond amount                                    .    make rules for posting the bond  and                                   .    revoke a certificate of existence for failure to post a bond  and                               .    requires the division to revoke a certificate of existence if another state with a                           similar law has revoked an entity s certificate of existence. The division shall require an entity registered with the division to post a corporate                          surety bond                                 a   in an amount that will cover the payment of the reasonably foreseeable costs and                          damages suffered in Utah by any person because of the environmental litigation, including                                 i   employees  lost wages, salaries, and benefits                                 ii   lost net revenue  and                                iii   consequential damages, including increased construction costs, because of the                          litigation                                  b   written by a surety licensed to do business within the state                                  c   in favor of the state, for the benefit of any person injured in Utah by the                           environmental litigation  and                                 d   conditioned upon                                  i   the payment of court costs and reasonable attorney s fees to the prevailing party                           incident to any suit upon the bond  and                                 ii   the unsuccessful environmental litigation.                                     The division may establish a fee to be paid when posting the bond by following the                           procedures and requirements of Section            .     .                                     By following the procedures and requirements of Title   , Chapter   a, Utah                           Administrative Rulemaking Act, the division shall make rules governing the procedures for                           posting the bond.                                     The division shall hold a formal adjudicative proceeding to determine the bond                           amount by following the procedures and requirements of Title   , Chapter   b, Administrative                           Procedures Act.                                     If an entity fails to file the bond required by Subsection                                      a   the division shall revoke the certificate of existence or license  and                                 b   the entity is no longer authorized to transact business in Utah.                                     A person, as defined in Section                , claiming damages as a result of any                           environmental litigation pursued by an entity registered with the division may file a claim upon                           the bond for damages against both the principal and the surety.                                        The total aggregate liability on the bond to all persons making claims may not                           exceed the amount of the bond.                                     If any other state has a provision of law substantially similar to this section, and the                           other state has revoked an entity s certificate of existence or license or revoked the entity s                           authority to transact business in that state                                  a   the division shall revoke any existing certificate of existence or license  and                                 b   the entity is no longer authorized to transact business in Utah.

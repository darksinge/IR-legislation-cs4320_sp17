CHILDREN S READING SKILLS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  J. Brent Haymond          AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC EDUCATION  PROVIDING FOR THE         ADMINISTRATION OF A READING PROFICIENCY TEST IN THE FIFTH GRADE          REQUIRING STUDENTS TO PASS THE TEST IN ORDER TO ADVANCE TO THE SIXTH         GRADE  PROVIDING FOR EXEMPTIONS  PROVIDING FOR AN ASSESSMENT OF         EMERGING AND EARLY READING SKILLS OF CHILDREN ENTERING          KINDERGARTEN AND THE FIRST GRADE  PROVIDING THAT SCHOOL DISTRICTS          MAKE AVAILABLE MATERIALS TO PARENTS TO ASSIST THEM IN HELPING THEIR          CHILDREN TO MASTER EMERGING READING SKILLS AND EARLY READING          SKILLS  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           AMENDS                  A      , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                      A      , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               ENACTS                  A      . , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section    A       is amended to read                  A      .  Conducting of testing programs.                   a   Each local school board shall, in accordance with rules adopted by the State Board          of Education, conduct a testing program within the school district.               b   i   In conjunction with the testing program required in grade five, each school shall          administer a reading proficiency test involving basic skills courses.               ii   A student must pass the reading proficiency test in order to advance from grade five          to grade six.              iii   The local school board may provide exceptions from Subsection     b  ii  for students         with disabilities and students with hardships, such as residing in a nonEnglish speaking household.                  Each board shall provide for district evaluation of the test results and use the         evaluations in setting goals and establishing programs for the district and schools within the         district.                  The local board may also administer other tests.             Section  .  Section    A       is amended to read                 A      .  Construction of part.               Nothing  Except as otherwise provided in Subsection   A         , nothing in this part          shall be construed to mean or represented to require that graduation from a high school or          promotion to another grade is in any way dependent upon successful performance of any test          administered as a part of the testing program established under this part.              Section  .  Section    A      .  is enacted to read                  A      . .  Assessment of emerging and early reading skills    Resource provided           by school districts.                   The Legislature recognizes that                a   well developed reading skills help to form the basis of children who have positive          self esteem, young adults who have the necessary tools for developing learning independence, and          adults who are productive members of a rapidly advancing technology based society                b   elementary teachers in the lower grades cannot bear the sole responsibility for teaching          basic reading skills  and               c   parents represent the most fundamental resource to constructively model and reinforce          the emerging and early reading processes to their children, even though many parents may not feel          sufficiently knowledgeable or skilled to help their children learn to read.                   a   Therefore, each local board of education shall offer free of charge to parents of each          child beginning kindergarten within the school district an assessment of the child s emerging          reading skills.               b   Except for the time constraints of the first year of the program, the board shall provide          the service three months prior to the child entering kindergarten.               c   As used in this section,  emerging reading skills  means a child s ability to understand                 that               i   print forms letters, words, and sentences               ii   letters represent sounds, sounds form words, and words form ideas               iii   books are read from front to back and left to right               iv   questions are sentences needing answers  and              v   reading and being read to is an enjoyable activity.              d   i   Based upon available resources, the school district shall provide parents with         materials to assist them in helping their child master the emerging reading skills in the child s own         home in the months prior to the child s first day in Kindergarten.               ii   The board shall make the materials fun for the children and easy to use for all parents,          especially for those parents who                A   are functionally illiterate                B   do not speak English as their primary language  or               C   are unsure of how to assist their child in developing emerging reading skills.                   a   Each local school board shall offer free of charge to parents of each child entering          first grade within the school district an assessment of the child s early reading skills.               b   Except for the time constraints of the first year of the program, the board shall provide          the service three months prior to the child entering the first grade.               c   As used in this section,  early reading skills  means a child s ability to                i   recognize and verbally produce and sustain short vowel sounds and simple sustainable          consonant sounds                ii   perform the blending process for two and three letter words which are phonetically          pure                iii   read simple sight words                iv   read simple sentences made of simple decodable words and sight words                v   read and understand questions made of simple decodable words and sight words  and               vi   understand that reading and being read to is an enjoyable, enriching activity that leads          to the discovery of new ideas and opens the door to independent learning.               d   i   Based upon available resources, the school district shall provide the parents with          materials to assist them in helping their child master early reading skills in the child s own home          in the months prior to the child s first day in first grade.                      ii   The board shall make the materials fun for the children and easy to use for all parents,         especially those parents who               A   are functionally illiterate               B   do not speak English as their primary language  or              C   are unsure of how to assist their child in reinforcing early reading skills.             Section  .   Effective date.             This act takes effect on July  ,     .

CONSUMER FOCUSED HEALTH DELIVERY AND PAYMENT                           DEMONSTRATION PROJECT                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor  Stewart Barlow                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill directs the Insurance Department to develop a demonstration project on the                           subject of consumer focused health care delivery and payment reform.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    instructs the Insurance Department to work with health insurers to develop a                           demonstration project for consumer focused health care delivery and payment                           reform and to apply for available health care innovation grants to implement and                           expand the demonstration project  and                               .    requires the Insurance Department to report to the Health System Reform Task                           Force regarding the progress towards consumer focused health delivery and                           payment reform by January  ,     .                           Money Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            ENACTS                                  A       , Utah Code Annotated                                                            Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                                  A       .  Consumer focused health care delivery and payment reform                           demonstration project.                                    The Legislature finds that                                 a   current health care delivery and payment systems do not provide system wide                          incentives for the competitive delivery and pricing of health care services to consumers                                 b   there is a compelling state interest to encourage consumers to seek high quality, low                          cost care and educate themselves about health care options                                 c   some health care providers and health care payers have developed                          consumer focused ideas for health care delivery and payment system reform, but lack the                          critical number of patient lives and payer involvement to accomplish system wide                          consumer focused reform  and                                d   there is a compelling state interest to encourage as many health care providers and                          health care payers to join together and coordinate efforts at consumer focused health care                          delivery and payment reform that would provide to consumers enrolled in a high deductible                          health plan                                 i   greater choice in health care options                                 ii   improved services through competition  and                                iii   more affordable options for care.                                    a   The insurance department shall convene a meeting of health care providers and                          health care payers for the purpose of coordinating a demonstration project for                          consumer focused health care delivery and payment reform.                                b   Participation in the coordination efforts by health care providers and health care                          payers is voluntary, but encouraged.                                    The commissioner shall facilitate a coordinated demonstration project for                          consumer focused health care delivery reform and consumer focused health care payment                          reform between one or more health care providers and one or more health care payers that elect                          to participate in the demonstration project by                                 a   consulting with health care providers and health care payers who elect to join                             together in a consumer based reform demonstration project                                 b   applying for health care innovation grants and other assistance that may be                          available for demonstrating consumer focused improvements to health care delivery and                          payment  and                                c   adopting administrative rules in accordance with Title   G, Chapter  , Utah                          Administrative Rulemaking Act, as necessary, to develop, oversee, and implement the                          demonstration project.                                    The commissioner shall report to the Health System Reform Task Force by January                           ,     , regarding the progress toward coordination of consumer focused health care system                          payment and delivery reform.

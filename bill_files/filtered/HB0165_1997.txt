CHILD SUPPORT GUIDELINES AMENDMENTS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Byron L. Harward          AN ACT RELATING TO THE JUDICIAL CODE  PROVIDING FOR A     CREDIT         TOWARD A NONCUSTODIAL PARENT S MONTHLY CHILD SUPPORT OBLIGATION          DELINEATING WHEN THE CREDIT MAY APPLY  AND ESTABLISHING CONDITIONS         THAT MUST BE MET BEFORE THE CREDIT MAY APPLY.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           ENACTS                       .  , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section         .   is enacted to read                       .  .  Noncustodial parent s credit toward monthly child support obligation.                   A noncustodial parent is entitled to take up to a     credit toward the monthly child          support obligation each January and one month other than January of each year if the noncustodial          parent                a   provides notice to the custodial parent, and an employer, or other income withholding          entity if applicable, at least    days in advance of the due date for the monthly child support          obligation in the month the credit will apply                b   provides a monetary benefit to the child which is at least equal to the credit claimed           and               c   provides an accounting of amounts expended on the child, including receipts or other          documentation to                i  the custodial parent on the due date for the monthly child support obligation in the          month the credit will apply  and               ii  the employer, or other income withholding entity if applicable,    days before the          income withholding date in the month the credit will apply.                  Expenditures applied toward the     credit in Subsection     may be accumulated         during the period between months in which the credit is claimed, however, claims shall be honored         only during January and the one other month of each year as provided in Subsection    .      Legislative Review Note      as of                 AM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.   Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel    Committee Note    The Judiciary Interim Committee recommended this bill.

ETHICS PROVISIONS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Sheryl L. Allen                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill creates the Legislative Ethics Commission.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    establishes a legislative code of conduct, and provides criminal penalties for its                           violation                                .    defines terms                                .    creates the Legislative Ethics Commission                                .    provides for membership and other requirements for the commission                                .    prescribes the commission s duties                                .    addresses the filing of an initial complaint against a legislator for violating certain                           rules and statutes                                .    requires the commission staff to conduct an investigation of an initial complaint and                           recommend action to the commission                                .    requires the commission to act on an initial complaint                                .    provides for the filing of a formal complaint against a legislator                                .    addresses pre hearing proceedings after a formal complaint is filed                                .    outlines hearing procedures                                .    addresses commission deliberations and recommendations                                .    requires the commission to forward any recommendation concerning a legislator to                              the Senate or House of Representatives, respectively  and                               .    provides for notifying certain county attorneys of criminal conduct. The following rules apply to a legislator                                 a   While in session, a member of the Senate and House shall disclose any conflict of                          interest on any audit provided for by law or legislative rule prepared by the Office of                          Legislative Research and General Counsel, the Office of the Legislative Fiscal Analyst, or the                          Office of the Legislative Auditor General, except to the extent the law or legislative rule                          authorizes the legislator to influence the preparation. Section                  is enacted to read                               CHAPTER   .  LEGISLATIVE ETHICS COMMISSION ACT                            Part  .  General Provisions                                         .  Title.                               This chapter is known as the  Legislative Ethics Commission Act.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Definitions.                               As used in this chapter                                      Commission  means the Legislative Ethics Commission created in this chapter.                                     Executive director  means the executive director appointed in accordance with                          Section                  .                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                             Part  .  Legislative Ethics Commission                                          .  Legislative Ethics Commission    Creation    Membership    Chairs                               Per diem and expenses    Staff    Chair    Quorum    Commission action.                                     There is created the Legislative Ethics Commission composed of five members as                           follows                                  a   one member appointed by the president of the Senate, who may not be a legislator,                           registered lobbyist, or employee of an entity that hires a registered lobbyist                                  b   one member appointed by the minority leader of the Senate, who may not be a                           legislator, registered lobbyist, or employee of an entity that hires a registered lobbyist                                  c   one member appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives, who may                           not be a legislator, registered lobbyist, or employee of an entity that hires a registered lobbyist                                  d   one member appointed by the minority leader of the House of Representatives, who                           may not be a legislator, registered lobbyist, or employee of an entity that hires a registered                           lobbyist  and                                 e   one member appointed jointly by the president of the Senate and the speaker of the                           House of Representatives.                                     a   Members who are not government employees shall receive no compensation or                           benefits for their services, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the performance                           of the members  official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance under                           Sections       A           and       A          .                                    b   Members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their service.                                     a   State government officer and employee members who do not receive salary, per                           diem, or expenses from their agency for their service may receive per diem and expenses                           incurred in the performance of their official duties from the commission at the rates established                           by the Division of Finance under Sections       A           and       A          .                                 b   State government officer and employee members may decline to receive per diem                           and expenses for their service.                                     a   The commission shall employ an executive director, who shall be licensed to                           practice law in Utah.                                 b   The commission may employ additional staff within budgetary constraints.                                     A majority of the commission constitutes a quorum for the conduct of business.                                     The action of a majority of a quorum present constitutes the action of the                           commission.                                     The commission shall elect a chair from among its members.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Duties.                                The commission shall                                      receive complaints concerning legislators                                      investigate any complaint concerning a legislator that it receives                                      conduct hearings in accordance with this chapter                                      issue a recommendation concerning discipline to the Senate or House of                           Representatives, respectively, in accordance with this chapter                                      issue advisory opinions concerning a legislator s ethical duties upon request by a                           legislator or legislative committee                                      create and administer a training program for legislators in accordance with Section                                                                                 investigate, study, and make recommendations for improving matters of ethical                           concern to the Legislature                                      make policies to carry out its duties  and                                     meet as necessary.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                             .  Ethics training.                                     The commission shall create an ethics training program for legislators that                           addresses the following topics                                  a   the Code of Official Conduct for legislators, as found in Section                                                  b   provisions of federal and state criminal law that may be applicable to legislators                                  c   campaign finance and gift disclosure requirements                                  d   harassment and discrimination provisions of federal and state law  and                                 e   any other matters the commission considers appropriate for an ethics training                           program.                                     A legislator shall complete the commission provided ethics training                                  a   in person before the end of the legislator s first legislative general session as a                           legislator  and                                 b   in person or electronically in each calendar year after the calendar year in which the                           legislator s first legislative general session as a legislator occurs.                                     The commission shall make an online training program that satisfies this section                           available to legislators no later than July  ,     .                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                             Part  .  Commission Procedures                                          .  Initial complaints    Investigation.                                     a   Any person, except a member of the commission or its staff, may file an initial                           complaint against a legislator for a violation of a legislative rule or criminal statute.                                 b   An initial complaint may not be filed more than four years after the conduct                           complained of occurs or reasonably should have been discovered.                                     a   Upon filing, an initial complaint is                                  i   confidential  and                                 ii   classified as a protected record under Title   G, Chapter  , Government Records                           Access and Management Act.                                 b   The commission and its staff shall keep an initial complaint confidential, except to                           the extent disclosure is necessary to investigate the complaint, including disclosure to the                           legislator who is the subject of the initial complaint.                                     a   Upon receiving an initial complaint, the executive director and commission staff                              shall investigate the complaint to determine if it merits further consideration by the                           commission.                                 b   i   In investigating an initial complaint under this section, the executive director or                           the commission may issue a subpoena to compel the production of documents or other                           evidence, or to compel the appearance of a witness.                                 ii   A subpoena issued in accordance with this section may be enforced in the manner                           provided by Section                .                                 c   Within    days after receiving the initial complaint, the executive director shall                           make a recommendation to the commission concerning whether the initial complaint should be                           dismissed or whether the commission should proceed with further investigation of the matter.                                 d   Notwithstanding Subsection     c , the commission may, for good cause, grant the                           commission staff additional time to investigate the initial complaint and make a                           recommendation for good cause.                                     After receiving a recommendation from the executive director under Subsection                               c , the commission shall determine whether to dismiss a complaint or to proceed with                           further investigation of a matter that is the subject of an initial complaint.                                     If the commission determines there is reasonable suspicion justifying further                           investigation of a matter that is the subject of an initial complaint, it shall order the executive                           director to prepare a formal complaint, in accordance with Section                  , against the                           legislator who was the subject of the initial complaint.                                     Any commission proceeding concerning an initial complaint or matters concerning                           the investigation of an initial complaint shall be closed in accordance with Title   , Chapter  ,                           Open and Public Meetings Act.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Formal complaint    Response    Subpoenas    Evidence    Hearing.                                     a   Except as provided in Subsection     b , within    days after being ordered to                           prepare a formal complaint against a legislator under Section                     , the executive                           director shall file the formal complaint, which shall include                                  i   the name of the legislator who is accused of violating the Code of Official Conduct                           in Section                 or a criminal statute                                  ii   the facts surrounding the complaint, to the extent they are known from the initial                              complaint and the investigation of the initial complaint                                  iii   the identity of the initial complainant  and                                 iv   an identification of the provision of Section                 or criminal statute alleged to                           have been violated.                                 b   Except upon a two thirds vote of the commission, a formal complaint may not be                           filed against a legislator within    days before a primary or general election in which the                           legislator is a candidate for any office.                                     Upon filing the formal complaint with the commission, the formal complaint is a                           public document.                                     The executive director shall cause a copy of the formal complaint to be served on                           the legislator who is the subject of the formal complaint.                                     a   A legislator who is the subject of a formal complaint may submit a response,                           which may include any motion to dismiss, to the formal complaint within    days after the day                           on which the formal complaint is served on the legislator.                                 b   The commission may act on a motion to dismiss before adjudicating the matters                           contained in the formal complaint.                                     a   The commission shall begin a hearing to adjudicate the matters contained in the                           formal complaint                                  i   within    days after receiving a legislator s response to a formal complaint under                           Subsection      or                                 ii   within    days after the day on which a legislator could last submit a response                           under Subsection    , if no response is submitted.                                 b   The legislator who is the subject of the formal complaint may waive the    day                           requirement of Subsection     a .                                     The commission and the executive director may issue subpoenas to compel the                           production of documents or other evidence, or to compel the appearance of a witness.                                     a   Documentary evidence in the possession of the commission, the commission                           staff, or the legislator who is the subject of the formal complaint shall be served on or mailed to                           the commission, the commission staff, and the legislator at least    days before the day on                           which the hearing under this section is scheduled to begin.                                 b   The commission may refuse to consider evidence not provided to the commission,                              the commission staff, or the legislator in accordance with Subsection     a .                                     A legislator who is the subject of a formal complaint may stipulate to facts with the                           concurrence of the commission.                                     a   A hearing held under this chapter is an open meeting under Title   , Chapter  ,                           Open and Public Meetings Act.                                 b   A hearing held under this chapter is not subject to Title   G, Chapter  ,                           Administrative Procedures Act.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Hearing Procedure.                                     a   At a hearing to adjudicate a formal complaint held in accordance with this                           chapter, the chair shall make rulings concerning the admissibility of evidence and testimony.                                 b   The commission may overrule the chair s decision on the admissibility of evidence                           or testimony by a majority vote.                                 c   The chair and the commission are not required to follow the Utah Rules of                           Evidence in making decisions concerning the admissibility of evidence or testimony.                                     a   The legislator who is the subject of the formal complaint, the complainant, and                           any witness who is subpoenaed to testify at the hearing are entitled to have counsel present.                                 b   Counsel for the legislator who is the subject of the formal complaint and counsel                           for the complainant may question any witness.                                 c   Counsel for a witness                                  i   may not question any witness  and                                 ii   may not assist the witness unless the witness requests counsel s assistance.                                 d   The executive director may question witnesses and make oral argument.                                 e   In a formal hearing, the legislator s guilt must be proved by a preponderance of the                           evidence.                                     At the conclusion of the presentation of evidence in the hearing, the commission                           may permit counsel for the legislator who is the subject of the formal complaint and for the                           complainant to make oral arguments.                                     a   At the conclusion of the presentation of evidence and any oral argument, the                           commission shall deliberate.                                 b   The commission s deliberations concerning a formal complaint are a closed meeting                              under Title   , Chapter  , Open and Public Meetings Act.                                     The commission may                                  a   dismiss a formal complaint after the hearing                                  b   recommend discipline against the legislator who is the subject of the formal                           complaint  or                                 c   make other recommendations it considers appropriate.                                     If the commission finds probable cause that a legislator who is the subject of a                           formal complaint violates Section                 or any criminal provision, the commission shall                                  a   notify the county attorney for the county in which the conduct occurred and a                           county attorney affiliated with a different political party of the conduct for potential                           prosecution  and                                 b   share any evidence in the commission s possession with the county attorneys                           notified under Subsection     a .                                Section   .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Commission disposition of complaints.                                     Within    days after the conclusion of a hearing under Section                  , the                           commission shall forward any recommendation, including any findings of fact or conclusions                           of law, to the Senate or the House of Representatives, respectively, for further consideration.                                     A recommendation of the commission is a public document under Title   G,                           Chapter  , Government Records Access and Management Act, when the commission forwards                           the recommendation to the Senate or House of Representatives under Subsection    .

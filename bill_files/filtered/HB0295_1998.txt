METROPOLITAN PUBLIC SAFETY TASK FORCE                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Perry Buckner          AN ACT RELATING TO LAW ENFORCEMENT  ESTABLISHING A TASK FORCE TO         STUDY CREATING METROPOLITAN POLICE DEPARTMENTS AND FIRE         DEPARTMENTS  PROVIDING FOR MEMBERSHIP  DELINEATING RESPONSIBILITIES         AND PROCEDURES  PROVIDING A REPORTING DATE  APPROPRIATING    ,            FROM THE GENERAL FUND  AND PROVIDING A REPEAL DATE.          This act enacts uncodified material.          Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .       Metropolitan Police Task Force    Creation    Membership    Quorum              Compensation    Staff.                      There is created the Metropolitan Public Safety Task Force, consisting of the following             members                a   two members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate, no more than one          of whom may be from the same political party                b   four members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the House          of Representatives, no more than two of whom may be from the same political party                c   the Commissioner of Public Safety or his designee                d   a representative of the Utah Sheriffs  Association, appointed by the association                e   a representative of the Association of Chiefs of Police, appointed by the association                f   one representative of the League of Cities and Towns, appointed by the league                g   one representative of the Association of Counties, appointed by the association                h   one representative who is an active member of the United Association of Community          Councils, who is appointed by the association  and               i   one representative of the Utah State Fire Chiefs  Association, appointed by the          association.                  a   The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed under         Subsection     a  as a cochair of the task force.              b   The speaker of the House of Representatives shall designate a member of the House         of Representatives appointed under Subsection     b  as a cochair of the task force.                  A majority of the members of the task force constitute a quorum.  The action of a         majority of a quorum constitutes the action of the task force.                  a   Salaries and expenses of the members of the task force who are legislators shall be         paid in accordance with Section        and Legislative Joint Rule   .  .               b   Members of the task force who are not legislators may not receive compensation for          their work associated with the task force, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred as a          member of the task force at the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections            A       and   A      .                   The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support          to the task force.              Section  .       Duties    Interim report.                      The task force shall review and make recommendations regarding the merging of          municipal and county                a   law enforcement agencies into one metropolitan law enforcement agency  and               b   firefighting agencies into one metropolitan firefighting agency.                   As part of its work, the task force shall                a   actively seek comment and input from various affected parties, including, for example,          county commission members, leadership of local municipalities, peace officers, firefighters, and          citizens                b   evaluate other states  experiences regarding the creation of metropolitan law          enforcement and firefighting agencies, including any enabling legislation or guidelines those states          may have created  and               c   consider the following issues regarding metropolitan police forces or firefighting          agencies                i   the effect on the quality of law enforcement and firefighting services provided to the          citizens served by the affected agencies                       ii   the costs, savings, and any other financial impacts upon affected agencies and         municipalities  and              iii   considerations and recommendations regarding the feasibility of creating a         metropolitan law enforcement agency or firefighting agency in any of the urban counties along the         Wasatch Front, including Salt Lake County, and in other areas of the state, including rural areas.                  A final report, including any proposed legislation, shall be presented to the Law         Enforcement and Criminal Justice Interim Committee before November   ,     .             Section  .   Appropriation.             There is appropriated from the General Fund for fiscal year                              ,    to the Senate to pay for the compensation and expenses of senators on the task          force                      ,    to the House of Representatives to pay for the compensation and expenses of          representatives on the task force  and                      ,    to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel to pay for staffing          the task force.              Section  .   Repeal date.              This act is repealed November   ,     .

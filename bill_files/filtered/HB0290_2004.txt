STATE EMPLOYEE COMPENSATION STUDY                           TASK FORCE                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor  Merlynn T. Newbold                                                      LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill creates the State Employee Compensation Study Task Force.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    establishes the membership of the task force and provides for compensation of                           legislative members                                .    outlines the duties of the task force                                .    prescribes the number of times the task force may meet                                .    provides for staffing the task force  and                               .    requires the task force to give a final report to the Retirement and Independent                           Entities Interim Committee.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                This bill appropriates for fiscal year         only                                .      ,    to the Senate                                .      ,    to the House of Representatives  and                               .       ,    to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel.                           Other Special Clauses                                This bill is repealed on November   ,     .                           Uncodified Material Affected                            ENACTS UNCODIFIED MATERIAL                                                       Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .   State Employee Compensation Study Task Force    Creation                              Membership    Quorum    Compensation    Staff.                                    There is created the State Employee Compensation Study Task Force consisting of                          the following    members, including    voting members and three nonvoting members                                 a   five members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate, no more than                          three of whom may be from the same political party, as voting members                                 b   seven members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the                          House of Representatives, no more than four of whom may be from the same political party, as                          voting members                                 c   the executive director of the Department of Human Resource Management or a                          designee, as a nonvoting member                                 d   the executive director of the Utah State Retirement Office or a designee, as a                          nonvoting member  and                                e   the director of the Public Employee Health Plan or a designee, as a nonvoting                          member.                                    a   The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed                          under Subsection     a  as a cochair of the task force.                                b   The speaker of the House of Representatives shall designate a member of the House                          of Representatives appointed under Subsection     b  as a cochair of the task force.                                    In conducting its business, the task force shall comply with the rules of legislative                          interim committees.                                    a   Salaries and expenses of the members of the task force who are legislators shall                          be paid in accordance with Section                and Legislative Joint Rule   .  .                                b   A member of the task force who is not a legislator may not receive compensation                          for the member s work associated with the task force, but may receive per diem and                          reimbursement for travel expenses incurred as a member of the task force at the rates                          established by the Division of Finance under Sections       A           and       A          .                                c   i   State government officer and employee members who do not receive salary, per                          diem, or expenses from their agency for their service may receive per diem and expenses                             incurred in the performance of their official duties from the committee at the rates established                          by the Division of Finance under Sections       A           and       A          .                                ii   State government officer and employee members may decline to receive per diem                          and expenses for their service.                                    The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support                          to the task force.                               Section  .   Duties    Interim report.                                    a   The task force shall review and make recommendations on the following issues                          concerning state employees  total compensation package                                 i   salaries                                 ii   retirement benefits                                 iii   health care benefits                                 iv   private and public sector market salary comparisons  and                                v   other issues that may be recommended by the task force, including the merits of                          prior and ongoing studies and reports done by others.                                b   The task force may review salaries and benefits offered by other private and                          governmental entities both within and outside Utah.                                    The task force may meet up to eight times during the      interim.                                    A final report, including any proposed legislation, shall be presented to the                          Retirement and Independent Entities Interim Committee before November   ,     . Section  .   Repeal date.                               This bill is repealed on November   ,     .

ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE CONTROL ACT                           MODIFICATIONS                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Gregory H. Hughes                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill modifies the Alcoholic Beverage Control Act.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    modifies definition provisions                                .    provides for electronic verification of proof of age by social on premise liquor                           licensees                                .    addresses a bar structure in restaurants, including access by minors                                .    creates a new social on premise liquor license, which includes two types of classes,                           including                                    .    authorizing the commission to grant the license                                    .    providing application and renewal requirements                                    .    establishing qualifications                                    .    establishing the commission and department duties before granting a license                                    .    requiring a bond                                    .    imposing operational requirements  and                                   .    providing a transition from private club licenses or restaurant liquor licenses to                           the new social on premise liquor license                                .    modifies the provisions related to a private club licensee, including                                       .    modifying the number and requirements for classes of licenses                                    .    changing renewal fees  and                                   .    changing operational requirements                                .    establishes requirements for renting or leasing a social on premise liquor licensee                           premises or private club premises                                .    expands licenses subject to protections for employees who exercise judgment  and                               .    makes technical and conforming amendments.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                This bill provides an effective date. A person may not operate an association, restaurant, private club, social on premise                          liquor licensee, or similar business that allows customers, members, guests,  visitors,  or other                           persons to possess or consume alcoholic beverages on the private club, association, restaurant,                          social on premise liquor license, or similar business premises without a business license.                                    Any local authority may issue a business license to any person who owns or                           operates an association, restaurant, private club, social on premise liquor license, or similar                           business that allows the customers, members, guests,  visitors,  or other persons to hold, store,                           possess, or consume alcoholic beverages on the premises. Each local authority shall set policy by written rules that establish criteria and                           procedures for granting, denying, suspending, or revoking licenses issued under this chapter.                                    A license issued under this section constitutes consent of the local authority within                           the meaning of Title   A,  the  Alcoholic Beverage Control Act.

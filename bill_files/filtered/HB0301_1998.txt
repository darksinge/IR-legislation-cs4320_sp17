MINIMUM AGE FOR MARRIAGE                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Carl R. Saunders          AN ACT RELATING TO HUSBAND AND WIFE  INCREASING THE MINIMUM AGE FOR         MARRIAGE TO     REQUIRING JUDICIAL CONSENT FOR    TO    YEAR OLDS TO         MARRY  AND MAKING TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          AMENDS                      , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah     , Second Special Session                     , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                          , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section         is amended to read                      .  Marriages prohibited and void.              The following marriages are prohibited and declared void                    when there is a husband or wife living, from whom the person marrying has not been          divorced                    when the male or female is under    years of age unless consent is obtained as          provided in Section                           when the male or female is under         years of age                    between a divorced person and any person other than the one from whom the divorce          was secured until the divorce decree becomes absolute, and, if an appeal is taken, until after the          affirmance of the decree  and                   between persons of the same sex.              Section  .  Section         is amended to read                      .  Application for license    Contents.                   A marriage license may be issued by the county clerk only after an application has         been filed in his office, requiring the following information               a   the full names of the parties, including the maiden name of the female               b   the Social Security numbers of the parties, unless the party has not been assigned a         number               c   the current address of each party               d   the date and place of birth  town or city, county, state or country, if possible                e   the names of their respective parents, including the maiden name of the mother               f   the birthplaces of fathers and mothers  town or city, county, state or country, if          possible   and               g   the distinctive race or nationality of each of the parents.                   If the female is a widow, her maiden name shall be shown in brackets.                   If one or both of the parties is under         years of age, the clerk shall provide them          with a standard petition on a form approved by the Judicial Council to be presented to the juvenile          court to obtain the authorization required by Section       .                   a   The Social Security numbers obtained under the authority of this section may not          be recorded on the marriage license, and are not open to inspection as a part of the vital statistics          files.               b   The Department of Health, Bureau of Vital Records and Health Statistics  shall, upon          request, supply those Social Security numbers to the Office of Recovery Services within the          Department of Human Services.               c   The Office of Recovery Services may not use any Social Security numbers obtained          under the authority of this section for any reason other than the administration of child support          services.              Section  .  Section         is amended to read                      .  Marriage by minors    Consent of parent or guardian    Juvenile court           authorization.                   If at the time of applying for a license the male or the female is under    years of age,          and not before married,  a license may not be issued without                a   the consent of his or her father, mother, or guardian personally given or certified in          writing to the clerk over his or her signature  and                      b    if under    years of age,  the written authorization to marry from a judge of the court         exercising juvenile jurisdiction in the county where either party to the marriage resides.  The         written authorization may also be obtained from a court commissioner as permitted by rule of the         Judicial Council.                  Before issuing written authorization for a minor to marry, the judge shall ascertain that         the minor is entering into the marriage voluntarily.                  The determination of voluntariness shall be made on the record. Any inquiry conducted         by the judge may be conducted in chambers.      Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

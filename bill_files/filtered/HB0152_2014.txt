HIGHWAY SPONSORSHIP PROGRAM ACT                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   John Knotwell                                Senate Sponsor             J. Stuart Adams                                                         LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill modifies the Transportation Code by authorizing the Department of                           Transportation to establish a sponsorship program.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    provides definitions                                .    authorizes the Department of Transportation to establish a sponsorship program to                           allow for private sponsorship of certain department operational activities or other                           highway related services or programs                                .    requires revenues generated from a sponsorship to be deposited into the                           Transportation Fund to be used for certain transportation purposes                                .    requires the Department of Transportation to adopt a policy on sponsorship                           agreements that is applicable to certain department operational activities or other                           highway related services or programs                                .    grants the Department of Transportation rulemaking authority to make and enforce                           rules regarding size, placement, and content restrictions for sponsorship                           advertisements  and                               .    establishes restrictions for certain sponsorship advertisements.                           Money Appropriated in this Bill                                None                              Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            ENACTS                                            , Utah Code Annotated                                                , Utah Code Annotated                                                , Utah Code Annotated                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  . This part is known as the  Highway Sponsorship Program Act.                                Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                        .  Definitions.                               As used in this section                                      Acknowledgment sign  means a sign that                                 a   is intended to inform the traveling public that a highway related service, product, or                          monetary contribution has been sponsored by a person, firm, or entity  and                                b   meets all design and placement guidelines for acknowledgment signs as set forth in                          the most recent edition of the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices for Streets and                          Highways adopted by the department in accordance with Section        a    .                                         Sponsorship agreement  means an agreement or contract between the department                          or its contractors and a person, firm, or entity that includes a provision authorizing an                          acknowledgment of the person, firm, or entity that is providing                                 a   the highway related service or product  or                                b   a monetary contribution to pay for a portion of the highway related service or                          product. The department may establish a sponsorship program to allow for private                          sponsorship of the following department operational activities or other highway related                          services or programs                                 a   traveler information                                 b   rest areas  and                                c   incident management services.                                    All revenue generated from a sponsorship authorized by this section shall be                          deposited into the Transportation Fund created by Section                  to be used to                                 a   offset costs associated with providing the service being sponsored  and                                b   support costs associated with operation and maintenance of the state highway                          system.                                    a   The department shall adopt a policy on sponsorship agreements that is                          applicable to all department operational activities or other highway related services within the                          state described in Subsection    .                                b   The policy described in Subsection     a  shall                                 i   include language requiring the department to terminate a sponsorship agreement if it                          determines the sponsorship agreement or acknowledgment sign                                 A   presents a safety concern                                 B   interferes with the free and safe flow of traffic  or                                C   is not in the public interest  and                                ii   describe the sponsors and sponsorship agreements that are acceptable and                          consistent with applicable state and federal laws.                                    A sponsorship authorized by this section                                 a   may not contain                                 i   promotion of any substance or activity that is illegal for minors, such as alcohol,                          tobacco, drugs, or gambling                                 ii   promotion of any political party, candidate, or issue  or                                iii   sexual material                                 b   may not resemble a traffic control device as defined in Section        a           and                                c   shall comply with federal outdoor advertising regulations in accordance with                             U.S.C. Sec.    .                                       a   In accordance with Title   G, Chapter  , Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act,                          the department shall make and enforce rules governing                                 i   the placement and size restrictions for acknowledgment signs at rest areas                                 ii   the placement and size restrictions for sponsorships placed on department incident                          management vehicles  and                                iii   other size, placement, and content restrictions that the department determines are                          necessary.                                b   The rules made under Subsection     a  shall limit the size of an advertisement to                          no more than     of the total area of an incident management vehicle.                                    a   A sponsorship advertisement shall be painted or affixed by decal on an incident                           management vehicle in a manner that complies with rules adopted under Subsection    .                                 b   A commercial advertiser that enters a sponsorship agreement with the department                           for the use of space for a sponsorship shall pay                                  i   the cost of placing the sponsorship advertisement on a sign or an incident                           management vehicle  and                                 ii   for the removal of the sponsorship advertisement after the term of the sponsorship                           agreement has expired.

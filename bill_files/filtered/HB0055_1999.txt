Rowan                           AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC EDUCATION  ESTABLISHING A MIDDLE SCHOOL                          TECHNOLOGY BASED SCIENCE PILOT PROGRAM TO PROVIDE                          TECHNOLOGY BASED SUPPORT FOR AN INTEGRATED SCIENCE CURRICULUM                           PROVIDING FOR A     ,    APPROPRIATION  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.                           This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                            ENACTS                                  A       , Utah Code Annotated                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                            Part  .  Middle School Technology Based Science Pilot Program                                  A       .  Middle School Technology Based Science Pilot Program    Components                              Evaluation.                                    a   There is established a Middle School Technology Based Science Pilot Program to                          provide technology based support for an integrated science curriculum at the middle school level.                                b   The program shall use technology to connect science curriculum components at                          selected middle schools to  real world  science research being done around the world.                                    a   The State Board of Education, through the State Superintendent of Public                          Instruction, shall                                 i   establish application procedures and deadlines for participation in the program  and                                ii   select ten middle schools to participate in the program.                                b   Participation in the program is on a voluntary basis and subject to the following                          requirements                                    i   a commitment on the part of each applicant school that at least three but no more than                          four of its science teachers shall participate in an intense teacher development course focused on                          implementing the program, the duration of which shall be from three to five days  and                                ii   an agreement by each school with the board to an evaluation of its program based on,                          at least, criterion referenced testing against a base control group of similar middle schools.                                    The State Board of Education, through the State Superintendent of Public Instruction,                          shall closely monitor the pilot program and make an annual report on its effectiveness to the                          Legislature s Education Interim Committee and the governor.                               Section  .   Appropriation.                                    Except as provided in H.B.  , Appropriations Coordination Act, there is appropriated                          from the Uniform School Fund for fiscal year          ,     ,    to the State Board of                          Education to fund the Middle School Technology Based Science Pilot Program authorized under                          Section       A           .                                   a   Each of the ten selected middle schools shall receive    ,    of the appropriation                          to help implement the program at each school.                               b   The board shall use     ,    of the appropriation for equipment needed to implement                          the program.                                   The appropriation is nonlapsing.                               Section  .   Effective date.                               This act takes effect on July  ,     .

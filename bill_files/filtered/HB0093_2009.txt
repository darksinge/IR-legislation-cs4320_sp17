Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    establishes a state ethics commission                                .    provides that certain commission proceedings are not subject to the Open and                           Public Meetings Act                                .    requires that records provided to the commission be classified as protected records                           under the Government Records Access and Management Act unless all or part of                           the record is publicly released by the commission                                 .    provides that the commission shall hear certain ethics complaints filed against the                           governor, lieutenant governor, state attorney general, state treasurer, state auditor,                           and members of the Legislature                                .    provides for membership of the commission                                .    permits the commission to hire staff                                .    requires the Department of Administrative Services to provide physical facilities                           and equipment for the commission and commission staff                                .    provides for procedures for filing ethics complaints, responding to ethics                           complaints, and holding hearings in response to ethics complaints                                   .    provides for the consolidation of multiple complaints                                .    provides that the commission may not schedule hearings or meetings in relation to                           an ethics complaint filed immediately preceding an election under certain                           circumstances                                .    provides that an ethics hearing is public                                 .    permits the commission to issue private or public reprimands if it determines that an                           ethics complaint is substantiated                                .    permits the commission to publicly release ethics complaints, responses to ethics                           complaints, and records and documents related to ethics hearings in some                           circumstances  and                               .     requires the commission to issue an annual report of its activities. Section  .    Section      G            is enacted to read                                   G       .  Definitions.                                   As used in this chapter                                       Respondent  means the individual against whom an ethics complaint is filed.                                      Witness  means any of the following persons when offering testimony at an ethics                           hearing held in response to an ethics complaint filed under this chapter                                  a   the person who filed the ethics complaint                                  b   the respondent  or                                 c   any other person offering testimony during the ethics hearing.                                Section  .    Section      G            is enacted to read                             Part  .  State Ethics Commission                                   G       .  State Ethics Commission    Creation    Membership.                                     There is created a State Ethics Commission composed of five members as follows                                  a   a member designated by the governor                                  b   a member designated by the president of the Senate                                  c   a member designated by the minority leader of the Senate                                  d   a member designated by the speaker of the House of Representatives  and                                 e   a member designated by the minority leader of the House of Representatives.                                     a   Each member of the commission shall be registered to vote in the state.                                 b   A member of the commission may not, during the member s term of office on the                           commission, act or serve as                                  i   an officeholder as defined in Section       A                                             ii   an agency head as defined in Section                                                  iii   a lobbyist as defined in Section                    or                                 iv   a principal as defined in Section                  .                                     a   i   Except as required by Subsection     a  ii , each member shall serve a                           four year term.                                 ii   At the time the commission is created                                  A   the members selected by the governor, the president of the Senate, and the minority                           leader of the                       appointed for the unexpired term.                                 c   A member may not be appointed for or serve more than two full terms.                                     The commission shall annually elect, by a majority vote, a commission chair from                           its membership.                                     The commission shall meet                                  a   upon the request of the chair of the commission                                  b   upon the written request of a majority of the members of the commission  or                                 c   in response to an ethics complaint filed and accepted under Section       G           . Section  .    Section      G            is enacted to read                                   G       .  Response to ethics complaint.                                     Within    calendar days after the date the commission sends notice of the filing of                           an ethics complaint, the respondent may file a written response to the complaint, which must                           be signed by the respondent or the respondent s counsel.                                        The respondent shall limit the response to the following                                  a   an admission or denial of each count, under oath, with any supportive evidence or                           relevant information                                  b   an objection to any or all counts on the grounds that the count fails to state facts                           that constitute a violation of any law  and                                 c   an objection to the jurisdiction of the commission in considering the allegations                           contained in the complaint.                                     If the respondent fails to submit a response to the ethics complaint or to any count                           contained in it, the commission may determine that the failure to respond constitutes an                           admission of the alleged violation.                                     Within three business days after receipt of a response filed under this section, staff                           for the commission shall provide a copy of the response to                                  a   each commission member  and                                 b   the person who filed the ethics complaint.

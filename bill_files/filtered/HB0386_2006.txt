PERSONAL PROPERTY TAX AMENDMENTS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   John Dougall                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill amends the Property Tax Act relating to personal property.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    defines terms  and                               .    requires the State Tax Commission to apply certain percentages of value to personal                           property determined on the basis of other states  percentages of value of personal                           property.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            ENACTS                                        , Utah Code Annotated                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                        .  Personal property valuation.                                    As used in this section                                    a    Acquisition cost  means all costs required to put an item of personal property into                          service, including                                 i   the purchase price of the personal property                                 ii   the freight and shipping costs of the personal property                                 iii   the installation, engineering, erection, or assembly costs of the personal property                                 iv   an excise tax imposed on the personal property  or                                v   a sales and use tax imposed on the personal property.                                b    Cost new  means the actual cost of personal property on the date the personal                          property is purchased.                                c    Percent good  means an estimate of value, expressed as a percentage that is                                 i   determined on the basis of acquisition cost or cost new of an item of personal                          property  and                                ii   adjusted for any appreciation or depreciation.                                    As part of its rulemaking process to develop an official schedule defining any class                          or item as personal property as required by Section                 , the commission shall                                 a   review each personal property valuation guide or schedule                                 i   of each state within the United States that has a property tax valuation system that                          requires tangible personal property to be assessed on the basis of fair market value                                 ii   utilized by a state described in Subsection     a  i  within the one year period                          before the commission begins its rulemaking process  and                                iii   that is available to the commission before the commission begins its rulemaking                          process  and                                b   adopt the lowest percent good as follows                                 i   if one or more valuation guides or schedules described in Subsection     a  that are                          adopted by a state other than this state lists a percent good for a particular year and type or class                          of personal property, determine the lowest percent good of those valuation guides or schedules                          for that particular type or class of personal property  and                                ii   adopt the lowest percent good described in Subsection     b  i .                                    If a property tax valuation system described in Subsection     a  i  allows for the                          classification of property, the commission shall include that property tax valuation for a type of                          personal property in the review required by Subsection     a  if that property tax valuation                             system determines the percent good of that type of personal property on the basis of full fair                          market value with no deductions or exemptions.

LICENSING OF VOCATIONAL                           REHABILITATION COUNSELORS                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Ronda Rudd Menlove                                Senate Sponsor             Peter C. Knudson                                                         LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill provides for the licensing of vocational rehabilitation counselors by the                           Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    enacts the Vocational Rehabilitation Counselors Licensing Act                                .    provides definitions regarding a licensed vocational rehabilitation counselor and the                           practice of providing vocational rehabilitation services                                .    creates the Vocational Rehabilitation Counselors Licensing Board and provides for                           its duties and responsibilities                                .    provides for licensing qualifications, for licensure by credential, for the term of the                           license and its renewal, and for exemptions from licensure                                .    provides grounds for the denial, suspension, or revocation of a license or restrictions                           placed on a license issued under the act  and                               .    defines unlawful and unprofessional conduct as related to licensed vocational                           rehabilitation counselors.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                   None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            ENACTS                                         , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                             , Utah Code Annotated                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                            CHAPTER   .  VOCATIONAL REHABILITATION COUNSELORS                            LICENSING ACT                            Part  .  General Provisions                                         .  Title.                               This chapter is known as the  Vocational Rehabilitation Counselors Licensing Act.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Definitions.                               In addition to the definitions in Section                 , as used in this chapter                                      Board  means the Vocational Rehabilitation Counselors Licensing Board created                          in Section                  .                                     Licensed vocational rehabilitation counselor  means an individual licensed under                          this chapter who is engaged in the practice of providing vocational rehabilitation services.                                     Practice of providing vocational rehabilitation services  means rendering or                          offering to an individual, group, organization, or the general public vocational rehabilitation                          services                                    a   in private practice                                 b   for compensation  and                                c   involving the application of principles, methods, or procedures of the vocational                          rehabilitation counseling profession, which include                                 i   vocational assessment                                 ii   vocational rehabilitation counseling and guidance                                 iii   vocational and other training services                                 iv   self employment plans                                 v   job analysis  and                                vi   job placement.                               Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                            Part  .  Board                                         .  Board.                                    There is created the Vocational Rehabilitation Counselors Licensing Board                          consisting of four licensed vocational rehabilitation counselors and one individual representing                          the general public.                                    The board shall be appointed and serve in accordance with Section                 .                                    a   The duties and responsibilities of the board shall be in accordance with Sections                                          and                 .                                b   The board may also designate one of its members on a rotating or permanent basis                          to                                 i   assist the division in reviewing complaints concerning unlawful or unprofessional                          conduct of a licensee  and                                ii   advise the division in its investigation of these complaints.                                    A board member who has, under Subsection    , reviewed a complaint or advised                          in its investigation may be disqualified from participating with the board when the board serves                          as a presiding officer in an adjudicative proceeding concerning that complaint.                               Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                            Part  .  Licensing                                         .  License required.                                    Beginning January  ,     , and except as provided in Sections                  and                                             , a license is required to engage in the practice of vocational rehabilitation                          counseling.                                    The division shall issue to an individual who qualifies under this chapter a license                          to practice vocational rehabilitation counseling.                               Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Qualifications for licensure    Licensure by credential.                                    Except as provided in Subsection    , an applicant for licensure as a vocational                          rehabilitation counselor under this chapter shall                                 a   submit an application in a form as prescribed by the division                                 b   pay a fee determined by the department under Section       J                                            c   be of good moral character                                  d   provide satisfactory evidence of having earned a master s degree in rehabilitation                           counseling or a related field and  ,    hours of work experience under the supervision of a                           licensed vocational rehabilitation counselor  and                                 e   after January  ,     , meet the examination requirement established by rule by the                           division in collaboration with the board.                                     The division may issue a license under this chapter to an individual who is licensed                           in another state or jurisdiction to practice vocational rehabilitation counseling if the division                           finds that the other state or jurisdiction has substantially the same or higher licensure                           requirements as this state.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Term of license    Expiration    Renewal.                                     The division shall issue each license under this chapter in accordance with a                           two year renewal cycle established by rule.                                     The division may extend or shorten a renewal cycle by as much as one year to                           maintain an established renewal cycle or to change an established renewal cycle.                                     Each license automatically expires on the expiration date shown on the license                           unless renewed by the licensee in accordance with Section                 .                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Exemption from licensure.                                In addition to the exemptions from licensure in Section                 , the following may                              engage in the practice of vocational rehabilitation counseling subject to the stated                           circumstances and limitations, without being licensed under this chapter if they do not use the                           title of vocational rehabilitation counselor or are not directly represented or held out to the                           public to be legally qualified to engage in the practice of vocational rehabilitation counseling                           before the public in this state                                      an individual employed or supervised by a licensed vocational rehabilitation                           counselor while carrying out specific tasks under the licensee s supervision  and                                     a priest, rabbi, or minister of the gospel of any religious denomination.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                             Part  .  License Denial and Discipline                                          .  Grounds for denial of license    Disciplinary proceedings.                                Grounds for the division to refuse to issue a license to an applicant under this chapter,                           to refuse to renew the license of a licensee, to revoke, suspend, restrict, or place on probation                           the license of a licensee, to issue a public or private reprimand to a licensee, and to issue a                           cease and desist order to a licensee shall be in accordance with Section                 .                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                             Part  .  Unlawful and Unprofessional Conduct                                          .  Unlawful conduct.                                In addition to the definition in Section                 ,  unlawful conduct  includes using the                           title  vocational rehabilitation counselor  or any other title or designation tending to indicate                           that the individual is a vocational rehabilitation counselor unless the individual has a current                           license as a vocational rehabilitation counselor issued under this chapter.                                Section   .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Unprofessional conduct.                                 Unprofessional conduct  includes, in addition to the definition in Section                  and                           as may be further defined by rule, engaging in an act or practice in a professional capacity                           which the licensee is not competent to perform through training or experience.

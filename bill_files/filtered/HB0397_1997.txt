Section  .  Section    A        is amended to read                 A       .  Housing funds in agencies under redevelopment plans adopted before          July  ,     .                  Tax increment paid to an agency under a redevelopment plan adopted before July  ,             , under Section   A        or   A       .  from one project area may be used to pay all         or part of the value of the land for and the cost of installation, construction, and rehabilitation of         any building, facility, structure, or other housing improvement, including infrastructure          improvements related to housing located in one or more project areas.                   Notwithstanding any provisions of this part, a maximum of     of tax increment          under Sections   A        and   A       . , under a redevelopment plan adopted before July           ,     , may be used by an agency outside of project areas for the purpose of replacing housing          units lost by redevelopment and economic development, or increasing, improving, and preserving          the community s supply of affordable housing generally.                   The funds allocated under this section shall be held by the agency in a separate account          of the special fund of the redevelopment agency designated as the housing fund until used. The          housing fund, together with any interest earned by the fund, and any payments or repayments made          to the agency for loans, advances, or grants of any kind from the fund shall accrue to and be          deposited in the housing fund to be used to increase, improve, and preserve the supply of housing          within project areas and affordable housing within the boundaries of the community or used to          effectuate any purposes of redevelopment or economic development in the project area from which          the funds originated.                   Expenditures or obligations incurred by the agency under this section shall constitute          an indebtedness incurred by an agency.                   An agency may lend, grant, or contribute funds from the housing fund to a person,          public body, housing authority, private entity or business, or nonprofit corporation for housing          purposes as defined in this section.                   For purposes of this section,  affordable housing  means housing to be owned or          occupied by persons and families of low or moderate income as determined by resolution of the          agency.

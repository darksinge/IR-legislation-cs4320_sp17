PROTECTION OF SPORTS OFFICIALS FROM                           VIOLENT ABUSE                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor   David L. Hogue                             This act modifies the Criminal Code by providing a specific criminal penalty for committing                          an assault against a sports official and provides related definitions.                          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                           ENACTS                                        . , Utah Code Annotated                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section            .      is enacted to read                                        . .  Assault against a sports official.                                    As used in this section                                 a    Sports event  means                                 i   any interscholastic or intramural athletic activity in a primary or middle school, junior                          high or high school, college, or university                                 ii   any organized athletic activity sponsored by a community, business, or nonprofit                          organization                                 iii   any athletic activity that is a professional or semiprofessional event  or                                iv   any other organized and lawful athletic activity in the state.                                b    Sports official  means a person at a sports event who                                 i   serves as a referee, umpire, linesperson, timekeeper, coach, or any person who serves                          in a similar capacity but who may be known by other titles, and is a member of or is registered with                          a local, state, regional, or national organization that is engaged in providing education and training                          for sports officiating  or                                ii   supervises the participants, such as a coach, trainer, team attendant, game manager,                             athletic director, assistant athletic director, president, dean, headmaster, principal, or assistant                          principal of a school, college, or university.                                    Any person who assaults a sports official is guilty of a class A misdemeanor if                                 a   i   the assault is committed against a sports official immediately prior to, during, or                          immediately after a sports event during which the victim is acting as a sports official                                 ii   the person knew or reasonably should have known that the victim was acting as a                          sports official                                 iii   the assault is an act not subject to a greater penalty under another provision of state                          law  and                                iv   the assault is committed by a person who was attending, participating in, or involved                          in the event  or                                b   the assault is committed in retaliation for any act or decision of the victim committed                          while acting as a sports official.       Legislative Review Note      as of                  AM     A limited legal review of this legislation raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

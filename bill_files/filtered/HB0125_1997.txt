NATURAL GAS TECHNICIAN           CERTIFICATION ACT                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Richard M. Siddoway          AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC SAFETY  CREATING A CERTIFICATION PROGRAM         FOR NATURAL GAS INSTALLER AND SERVICE TECHNICIANS  CREATING A         NATURAL GAS CERTIFICATION BOARD  AUTHORIZING CERTAIN FEES AND         RULEMAKING  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE. Section           is enacted to read             Part  .  Natural Gas Installer and Service Technician Certification                       .  Title.             This part is known as the  Natural Gas  Installer and Service Technician Certification Act.              Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Definitions.             As used in this part                    Board  means the Natural Gas Installer and Service Technician Certification Board         created in Section         .                   Distributor  means any person engaged in the distribution of natural gas who          transports natural gas that is to be distributed or sold within this state.                    Division  means the Division of the State Fire Marshall.                    Downstream piping  means an assembly consisting of components with a means for          conveying natural gas from the outlet of the distributor s meter to the burner and includes          combustion air supply and venting systems, either continuously or intermittently, and that          incorporates components intended to achieve control of quantity, flow, and pressure.                    Enforcing authority  means the division.                    Gas appliance  means any device that uses natural gas to produce light, heat, power,          steam, hot water, refrigeration, or air conditioning.                    Installer  means any person who has satisfactorily passed an examination under the          supervision of the board, testing his knowledge and ability to install or properly repair domestic          or commercial gas appliances and downstream piping, and who holds an installer s certificate          under this part.                    Natural gas  means a naturally occurring fluid mixture of hydrocarbons composed          primarily of methane containing one grain or less hydrogen sulfide per     standard cubic feet,          and    grains or less total sulfur per     standard cubic feet, produced in geologic formations          beneath the earth s surface, and maintaining a gaseous state at standard atmospheric temperature          and pressure under ordinary conditions, or such fluid mixture produced by manufacturing          processes such as coal gasification and which is conveyed through a piping system.                    Service technician  means any person who has satisfactorily passed an examination          under the supervision of the board, testing his knowledge and ability to install or properly repair          domestic or commercial natural gas appliances and downstream piping and who holds a service                 technicians certificate under this part.             Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Exclusions from part.             This part does not apply to any of the following                   the production, refining, or manufacture of natural gas                   the sale, storage, transportation, or distribution of natural gas by a producer, pipeline         company, distributor, refiner, or manufacturer                   equipment used by a producer, pipeline company, refiner, or manufacturer in a         producing, distribution, refining, transportation, or manufacturing process  and                   compressed natural gas systems for vehicles.              Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Board    Creation    Composition    Appointment    Terms of officers              Meetings    Compensation.                   There is created within the division the Natural Gas Installer and Service Technician          Certification Board consisting of the following seven members                a   two licensed contractors whose employees are affected by this act                b   one member from the fire service                c   one building code official                d   one representative of the natural gas industry who has been actively engaged in the          industry for at least five years                e   one licensed general building contractor  and               f   one member of the general public.                   The governor shall appoint all board members, on a nonpartisan basis, from a list of          at least five nominees for each appointment, submitted by the division as directed by the board.                   All board members shall have been legal residents of the state for at least one year          immediately preceding the date of appointment.                   a   Except as required by Subsection  b , as terms of current board members expire,          the governor shall appoint each new member or reappointed member to a four year term.               b   Notwithstanding the requirements of Subsection     a , the governor shall, at the time          of appointment or reappointment, adjust the length of terms to ensure that the terms of board          members are staggered so that approximately half of the board is appointed every two years.                      c   Members serve from the date of appointment until a replacement is appointed.                  When a vacancy occurs in the membership for any reason, the replacement shall be         appointed for the unexpired term.                  a   The board shall elect its own chair and vice chair at its first regular meeting each         calendar year.              b   All meetings of the board shall be held on a prescribed date, at least quarterly, and at         any time a majority of the board members makes a request in writing to the board chair.              c   Any four members constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.                  a   i   Members who are not government employees shall receive no compensation or          benefits for their services, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the performance of          the member s official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections            A       and   A      .               ii   Members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their service.               b   i   Government officer and employee members who do not receive salary, per diem,          or expenses from their agency for their service may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the          performance of their official duties from the board at the rates established by the Division of          Finance under Sections   A       and   A      .               ii   Government officer and employee members may decline to receive per diem and          expenses for their service.              Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Board rulemaking    Notice.                   The board shall make rules as reasonably necessary for                a   the training and certification of installers and service technicians for the protection of          the health, welfare, and safety of the public and persons using natural gas  and               b   governing natural gas installers and service technicians and their installation and          maintenance of natural gas appliances and downstream piping in accordance with manufacturer s          instructions and codes.                   The rules shall be in substantial conformity with the generally accepted standards of          safety concerning natural gas.                   a   When a proposed rule is filed, the board shall give reasonable notice to natural gas          installers and service technicians of the proposed new, revised, or amended rule together with a                 hearing date.              b   Any person affected by rulemaking under this part may submit written comment on         the rule.              c   After the adoption and effective date of the rule, the board shall give reasonable notice         of the newly adopted rule to natural gas installers and service technicians.             Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Duties and powers of the board    Fee setting.                  The board may               a   conduct hearings on the issuance or revocation of any certificate                i   compel the attendance of witnesses by subpoena                ii   require the production of any records or documents determined by it to be pertinent          to the subject matter of the hearing  and               iii   apply to the district court of the county where the hearing is held for an order citing          any applicant or witness for contempt, and for failure to attend, testify, or produce required          documents                b   suspend or revoke certificates and refuse renewals of certificates if the applicant or          certificate holder has been guilty of conduct harmful to either the safety or protection of the public           and               c   adopt bylaws for its procedures and methods of operation.                   The board shall, in accordance with Section        . , establish certification fees to          cover the cost of administering this section.              Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Duties of the division.              The division shall                    prescribe the method and form to apply for a natural gas installer or service technician          certificate, with the approval of the board                    investigate the experience, reputation, and background of applicants as directed by the          board                    recommend to the board the issuing, suspending, revoking, and denying of certificates                    assist the board in conducting hearings in connection with the applications for, or          revocation of, certificates                           keep accurate records and minutes of all meetings, which shall be open to public         inspection at all reasonable times, and keep a public record of all applications for certification and         certificates issued by the board                   conduct examinations of every certificate applicant to determine the responsibility,         ability, knowledge, experience, or other qualifications of the applicant for a certificate                   prepare and administer examinations, collect fees, and issue certificates  and                  complete other duties as assigned by the board.             Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Certificate required.              A person may not engage in the service, installation, or repair of natural gas appliances or          downstream piping unless                    the person has obtained the appropriate certificate  or                   is continually working under the direction of a certified installer or service technician          as provided in Section         .              Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Classification of applicants and certificates.              To administer this part, the board shall classify all applicants and certificate holders as          either                    an installer engaged in the business of installing natural gas appliances or downstream          piping  or                   a service technician engaged in the business of service, installation, or repair of natural          gas appliances or downstream piping.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Certification specifications and limits.              A certificate issued under this part                    shall state the name of the person to whom it is issued  and                   is not transferable by its holder to any other person, and is valid only for the particular          person described on the certificate.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Certification for certain activities.                   A person who installs, repairs, or services appliances or downstream piping for the use                 of natural gas shall be certified by the division by passing an appropriate examination based on the         requirements of the board.                  A person is exempt from this examination for up to one year, and until examined by         a representative of the board, provided during the one year period, he shall be continually         supervised by a certified installer or service technician.                  Any person hiring an uncertified employee shall, within    days of the commencement         of employment, notify the division.  If the uncertified employee fails to pass the examination,         within    days, he may retake the examination after additional instruction.  Prior to retaking the         exam, he shall again be continually supervised by a certified installer or service technician.                   a   The board shall establish a reasonable fee in accordance with Section          to          cover the costs of administering the examination.               b   All examinations shall be administered by the division.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Fees    Setting    Deposit    Use.                   The board shall establish fees authorized in this part in accordance with the procedures          specified in Section        . , but the fees shall be deposited as provided in Subsection    .                   Fees collected by the division under this part, shall be deposited with the state treasurer          as a dedicated credit, to be used for the implementation of this part.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Enforcement of part and rules.                   This part, the rules made under it, and orders issued by the board are enforced by                a   the enforcing authority, unless otherwise provided by the board  and               b   the board.                   a   A person who knowingly violates or fails to comply with this part is guilty of a          class B misdemeanor and is punishable by a fine of not less than     nor more than     .               b   A person previously convicted under Subsection     a  who knowingly violates or fails          to comply with this part is guilty of a class B misdemeanor and is punishable by a fine of not less          than      nor more than   ,   .               c   Each day the violation or failure to comply continues constitutes a separate offense.                   The enforcing authority may enter any building or other premises open to the public,          at any reasonable time, for the purpose of determining and verifying compliance with this part and                 the rules and orders of the board.                  a   The board may establish and collect a fee for any services required by this part, the         rules made under it, and orders issued by the board.  The fee shall be reasonable and may not         exceed the amount of the cost of service or inspection provided.              b   Fees collected under this Subsection      may be retained by the enforcing authority,         and shall be applied to the expenses of providing these services.             Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Effect of part on state and local provision.                  Except as provided in Title   , Chapter   , Uniform Building Standards Act, this part          supersedes all other conflicting state laws or rules concerning natural gas as regulated under this          part.                   A municipality or other political subdivision may not adopt or enforce any ordinance          or rule in conflict with this part, or with the rules made under this part.              Section   .   Effective date.              This act takes effect on July  ,     .

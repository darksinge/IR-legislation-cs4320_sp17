ASSERTIVE COMMUNITY MENTAL HEALTH                           TREATMENT PILOT PROGRAM                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Carol Spackman Moss                                Senate Sponsor             Gregory S. Bell                                                         LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill establishes a pilot program within the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Act                           for the provision of assertive community treatment services to people with a severe                           mental disorder, who are not receiving or are transitioning out of other mental health                           services, and to their families.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    establishes a pilot program, beginning on July  ,     , and ending on July  ,     ,                           for the provision of assertive community treatment mental health services to eligible                           people, who are not receiving or are transitioning out of other mental health                           services                                .    grants rulemaking authority to the division                                .    describes a person who is eligible to receive services under this bill                                .    provides that a local mental health authority shall establish criteria to determine the                           order of priority for receiving services under this bill                                .    provides that the services provided under the pilot program described in this bill do                           not constitute an entitlement and may be withdrawn from a person at any time                                .    requires the director of the division to report, for consideration and decision, to the                           Health and Human Services Interim Committee during the      interim, regarding                              whether the pilot program created by this bill should be modified or converted into an ongoing                           program                                .    provides, under the Legislative Oversight and Sunset Act, that the pilot program                           created by this bill will be repealed on July  ,       and                               .    makes technical changes.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            AMENDS                                  I      , as last amended by Laws of Utah     , Chapter     and renumbered and                           amended by Laws of Utah     , Chapter                               ENACTS                                  A       , Utah Code Annotated                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                                  A       .  Pilot program for assertive community mental health treatment.                                    There is established a pilot program for assertive community mental health                          treatment, beginning on July  ,      and ending on July  ,     .                                    The purpose of the pilot program described in Subsection     is to provide mental                          health services to a person with a severe mental disorder in order to reduce                                 a   the use of emergency and inpatient medical and psychiatric services by the person                                 b   the person s involvement in the criminal justice system  and                                c   the person s use of illegal substances.                                    The pilot program shall                                 a   primarily focus on a person who is transitioning from inpatient treatment, outpatient                          treatment, or incarceration                                 b   be substantially in accordance with the Assertive Community Treatment model of                          the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, within the United States                             Department of Health and Human Services  and                                c   utilize fidelity scales for evidenced based practices.                                    The division may make rules, in accordance with Title   G, Chapter  , Utah                          Administrative Rulemaking Act, as necessary for the implementation and administration of this                          section.                                    In accordance with Subsection    , within funds appropriated by the Legislature for                          the pilot program described in this section, the division shall, through a local mental health                          authority, provide assertive community treatment as described in this section to a person with a                          severe mental disorder, and that person s family, if that person                                 a   is eligible to receive services from a local mental health authority                                 b   is not receiving, or is transitioning out of, other mental health services  and                                c   is determined by a local mental health authority to be a person who would                          substantially benefit from the provision of assertive community treatment.                                    A local mental health authority described in Subsection     shall establish criteria to                          determine the priority, between persons eligible for services under this section, for receiving                          services under this section.                                    It is the intent of the Legislature that the services provided under the pilot program                          described in this section                                 a   do not constitute an entitlement of any kind  and                                b   may be withdrawn from a person at any time.                                    The services provided under the pilot program described in this section may be                          provided in connection with an existing assertive community treatment program or programs to                          increase the level of the program to a fidelity scale model.                                    The director of the division shall report to the Health and Human Services Interim                          Committee during the      interim regarding                                 a   the operation and accomplishments of the pilot program described in this section                                 b   whether the Legislature should convert the pilot program to an ongoing program                          within the division  and                                c   recommendations for changes, if any, related to the pilot program.                                     During the      interim, the Health and Human Services Interim Committee                          shall                                    a   hear or review the report described in Subsection      and                                b   determine whether the pilot program described in this section should be converted                          into an ongoing program within the division.

EDUCATION CAPITAL OUTLAY FOUNDATION                           AMENDMENTS                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                           Sponsor   A. Lamont Tyler                           AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC EDUCATION  PROVIDING THAT A SCHOOL DISTRICT                          LEVYING LESS THAN A .     TAX RATE FOR CAPITAL OUTLAY AND DEBT                          SERVICE SHALL RECEIVE PROPORTIONAL FUNDING UNDER THE CAPITAL                          OUTLAY FOUNDATION PROGRAM BASED UPON THE PERCENTAGE OF THE .                               TAX RATE LEVIED BY THE DISTRICT  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.                           This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                            AMENDS                                  A       , as repealed and reenacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A            is amended to read                                   A       .   Qualifications for participation in the foundation program                              Distribution of monies    Distribution formulas.                                    In order for a school district to qualify for monies under the capital outlay foundation                           program established in Subsection       A              , a local school board must levy a tax rate of up                          to .     per dollar of taxable value for capital outlay and debt service.                                     If the local school board levies the .     tax rate during one year, but due to increased                          assessed valuations and a corresponding reduction in the certified tax rate is prevented from                          levying the full .     tax rate during the subsequent year, the district will considered to be in                          compliance with Subsection     if it levys the maximum allowable under these circumstances and                          shall continue to receive support under the foundation program for a period of up to two years                          without loss of state funding.                                     The State Board of Education shall adopt a rule in accordance with Title   , Chapter                               a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, that allows a school district levying less than the full                          .     tax rate to receive proportional funding under the foundation program based upon the                          percentage of the .     tax rate levied by the district.                                    a   Through June   ,     ,     of the monies in the capital outlay foundation program                           shall be used in an emergency school building needs program.                                b   Beginning July  ,     , the emergency school building needs program shall be                           terminated and the monies otherwise spent in that program shall be used for the general purposes                           of the capital outlay foundation program.                                    The State Board of Education shall distribute monies in the capital outlay foundation                           program and the emergency school building needs program in accordance with formulas developed                           by the state superintendent of public instruction.                                a   The board shall distribute capital outlay foundation monies on the basis of a minimum                           guarantee per average daily membership as computed by the state superintendent of public                           instruction using                                 i   available monies  and                                ii   the assessed valuation per average daily membership in each school district.                                b   The formula for the emergency school building needs distribution shall include the                           following  components                                 i   a school district s  ability to raise money for school building needs as related to the                            assessed valuation per student for real property within the school district                                 ii   need as reflected by                                 A   the current number of students in the school district who are in alternative housing                            and                                B   growth, both within the district and compared to the state as a whole  and                                iii   the school district s effort to raise money based on                                 A   the district s total tax rate  and                                B   the district s bond and bond interest payments compared to its ability to raise revenue.

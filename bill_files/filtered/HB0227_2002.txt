ELECTRONIC MONITORING OF                           PROBATIONERS   CRIMINAL                           INTERFERENCE                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor   Katherine M. Bryson                             This act modifies the Criminal Code by providing for the offense of interfering with an                          electronic device used in the supervision of a person on probation, parole, or other form of                          detention.  The act provides definitions, a penalty, and an affirmative defense for persons                           acting in their capacity as employees of the supervising agency.                           This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                            ENACTS                                        . , Utah Code Annotated                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section            .      is enacted to read                                        . .  Interference with an electronic supervision device    Definitions                              Penalties    Defense.                                    As used in this section                                 a    Detention  means any diversion agreement, plea in abeyance agreement, condition of                          probation or parole, pretrial supervision, work release, home confinement, sentence, or other                          release, court order, or agreement that requires the monitoring of the location of a person.                                b    Electronic supervision device  or  device  means any device used to monitor the                          location of a person on detention, including any                                 i   sending or receiving device                                 ii   wire, radio, or telephone device or connection                                 iii   antenna                                 iv   mechanical device  or                                   v   computer equipment, including hardware or software.                                c   i    Interferes with  means to                                 A   remove, disconnect, damage, destroy, disable, or tamper with any electronic                          supervision device  or                                B   interfere with the signal transmitted or received by a device.                                ii    Interferes with  includes the theft of a device.                                    Any person who interferes with an electronic supervision device is guilty of a class A                          misdemeanor.                                    Any sentence imposed under this section shall be consecutive to any other sentence,                          unless the court states in writing or on the record the reasons why a consecutive sentence would                          be unjust.                                    It is an affirmative defense to a charge under this section that the person charged was                                 a   an employee of the agency or private contractor providing supervision for the                          detention  and                                b   acting within the scope of his employment in the removal, disconnection, or handling                          of the electronic supervision device.                                    If the act committed as a violation of this section amounts to an offense subject to a                          greater penalty under another provision of state law than is provided under this section, the section                          with the greater penalty applies.

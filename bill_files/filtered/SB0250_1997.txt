Enrolled                DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS            EMERGENCY RELEASE                 GENERAL SESSION         STATE OF UTAH          Sponsor   Lyle W. Hillyard         AN ACT RELATING TO STATE INSTITUTIONS  PROVIDING A CHANGE IN        TERMINOLOGY TO PHYSICAL CAPACITY FROM OPERATIONAL CAPACITY        FOR EMERGENCY RELEASE OF INMATES.        This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows         AMENDS                      , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah             Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah             Section  .  Section           is amended to read                      .  Emergency release due to overcrowding.                 When the executive director of the department finds that the inmate population of the        Utah State Prison has exceeded  operational  physical capacity for at least    calendar days, he        may              a   notify the governor that an overcrowding emergency exists and provide him with        information relevant to that determination  and             b   notify the Board of Pardons and Parole of the existence of the overcrowding        emergency so that the board may commence emergency releases pursuant to Subsection    .                 Upon the governor s receipt of notification of the existence of an emergency release,        the department shall              a   notify the board of the number of inmates who need to be released in order to eliminate        the overcrowding emergency              b   in cooperation and consultation with the board, compile a list of inmates by        chronological order according to their existing parole release dates, sufficient to eliminate the        overcrowding emergency  and             c   for each inmate listed in accordance with Subsection     b , notify the board if the          department has any reason to believe that the inmate has violated a disciplinary rule or for some        other reason recommends that the inmate s existing parole date be rescinded.                 Unless the board has identified a reason to believe that the inmate s existing parole date        should be rescinded, the parole release date of each inmate identified in Subsection     b  may be        advanced a sufficient number of days to allow for release.                 When the process described in Subsections     and     has been completed, the board        may order the release of the eligible inmates.                 The department shall              a   send to the Commission on Criminal and Juvenile Justice a list of names of the inmates        released under this section  and             b   provide the name and address of each inmate to the local law enforcement agency for the        political subdivision in which the inmate intends to reside.                 The department shall inform the governor when the emergency release has been        completed.                 The board shall make rules in accordance with Title   , Chapter   a, Utah        Administrative Rulemaking Act, to carry out the  provisions of this section.

Continuing education requirements    Regulatory authority.                  The commissioner shall by rule prescribe the continuing education requirements for         each class of agent s license under Subsection   A          , except that no continuing education         requirement may be imposed upon holders of licenses under Subsection   A           or other         license classifications recognized by the commissioner by rule as provided in Subsection           A          .                  a   Educational requirements may not be stated in terms of formal education.   These          requirements may be stated in terms of classroom hours, or their equivalent, of insurance related          instruction received, for which insurance related formal education may be a substitute, in whole          or in part.                b   Notwithstanding Subsection     a , the commissioner may count formal education          related to insurance toward a licensee s educational requirements.               c   i   No more than one required course for a professional insurance designation and six          classroom hours, or their equivalent, of continuing education may be required for a two year          period regardless of the number of lines for which an agent or broker may be licensed.                    a    ii   No more than    classroom hours, or their equivalent, of continuing education          may be required for a two year period regardless of the number of lines for which an agent or          broker may be licensed if the agent or broker has at least one professional insurance designation.                iii   Licensees who, as of April  ,     , have completed    years of licensure in good          standing shall be exempt from the requirement of continuing education.               b   The rules shall                i   provide for home study, video tapes, and experience credits in addition to other          provisions .  However, not less than half of the required hours shall be met through actual class          attendance.                 ii   require a licensee to attend at least half of the required continuing education classroom          hours in person  and               iii   allow a licensee to satisfy the professional insurance designation course requirement          through                A   attending at least     of the classes in person  or                      B   passing the final examination for the course  and              iv   allow a licensee to take a professional insurance designation course to satisfy the         required continuing education classroom hours if the licensee is subject to               A   Subsection     c  i  and has completed the professional insurance designation course         requirement within the current two year educational cycle  or              B   Subsection     c  ii .              c   The rules shall authorize professional agent associations to offer qualified programs         and courses for all classes of licenses on a geographically accessible basis and to collect reasonable         fees for funding and administration of the continuing education program, subject to the review and          approval of the commissioner.  The fees charged to fund and administer the  program  programs          and courses shall reasonably relate to the costs of  administering the program  administration.           Nothing in this section prohibits a provider of continuing education programs or courses from          charging fees for attendance at courses offered for continuing education credit.               d   The fees charged for attendance at a professional agent association program may be          less for an association member, based on the member s affiliation expense, but shall preserve the          right of a nonmember to attend without affiliation.                         The commissioner shall designate programs and courses, including those          presented by insurers, which satisfy the requirements of this section.                         The requirements of this section apply only to applicants who are natural persons.                         The commissioner may waive the requirements of this section as to any person          who has been an active insurance agent or broker in another state for two years immediately prior          to applying for a license in this state, but only if the applicant s state of residence  has imposed          upon  currently requires the applicant to satisfy education requirements which are substantially as          rigorous as those of this state.

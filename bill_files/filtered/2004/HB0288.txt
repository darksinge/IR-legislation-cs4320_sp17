                 a       Definitions                                As used in this chapter                                      Election official  means the county clerk or municipal clerk                                      Opinion question  means a nonbinding question submitted to the voters of Utah                          according to the procedures established in this chapter                                      Originating house  means the Utah House of Representatives if the resolution is a                          House joint resolution or the Utah Senate if the resolution is a Senate joint resolution                                      Regular general election  means the election held throughout the state on the first                          Tuesday after the first Monday in November of each even numbered year                                Section       Section         a         is enacted to read                                     a       Submission of opinion questions to Utah voters                                     The Legislature may submit opinion questions to legal voters by complying with                          the procedures contained in this section                                     Any legislator may file a joint resolution proposing that an opinion question be                          submitted to the legal voters of Utah                                     The resolution shall include                                 a   the language of the opinion question as it will appear on the ballot                                 b   a statement directing that the lieutenant governor submit the language of the                          opinion question to the legal voters of Utah for their approval or rejection  and                                c   language designating the regular general election date for the election or language                             calling a special election and setting the date for that special election                                     After passage by both houses of the Legislature  the originating house shall submit                          the resolution to the lieutenant governor with instructions that the opinion question specified in                          the resolution be submitted to the legal voters on the election date specified in the resolution                                Section       Section         a         is enacted to read                                     a       Lieutenant governor s duties                                     After receipt of an opinion question resolution the lieutenant governor shall                                 a   submit the opinion question to the legal voters of Utah as required by the                          resolution                                 b   comply with the procedures of Section          a                                         c   if the election is not held as part of a regular general election  reimburse each                          election official for the expenses of conducting the election according to a formula determined                          by the lieutenant governor  and                                d   comply with all relevant provisions of Title   A  Election Code  relating to the                          conduct of general and municipal elections                                     The lieutenant governor may establish additional requirements for election officials                          to facilitate the conduct of the election                                Section       Section         a         is enacted to read                                     a       Election official duties                                Each election official shall comply with the requirements of Title   A  Election Code                           relating to general and municipal elections  the requirements of Section          a          and any                          other requirements imposed by the lieutenant governor                                Section       Section         a         is enacted to read                                     a       Exemption                                Title   A  Chapter    Issues Submitted to the Voters  does not apply to opinion                          questions submitted to the legal voters under the authority of this chapter                                Section       Section         a         is enacted to read                                     a       Procedures for submission of opinion questions to popular vote                                     The lieutenant governor  the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel                           and each election official shall comply with the procedures contained in this section whenever                          the Legislature submits an opinion question to the voters                                        Not later than    days before the date of the election  the lieutenant governor shall                          cause the full text of the opinion question to be published in at least one newspaper in every                          county of the state where a newspaper is published                                     The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall                                 a   designate the opinion questions by number and order of presentation on the ballot                                 b   draft and designate a ballot title that summarizes the subject matter of the                          amendment or question  and                                c   deliver them to the lieutenant governor                                     Not later than    days before the election  the lieutenant governor shall certify the                          number and ballot title of each opinion question to each election official                                      Each election official shall cause both the number and title of the opinion question                           to be printed on the ballot to be used on election day  and on the sample ballots  and publish                           them as provided by law                                 Section       Section         a         is enacted to read                                      a       Ballot form    Manner of voting                                      The lieutenant governor shall ensure that the ballots containing opinion questions                           submitted to the people include                                  a   a printed number and ballot title                                  b   the printed text of the opinion question  and                                 c   the words  YES  and  NO   each followed by a square in which the voter may                           place a mark to indicate his vote                                      Voters desiring to vote  yes  on the opinion question shall mark in the appropriate                           square with a mark opposite the word  YES   and voters desiring to vote  no  shall mark in the                           appropriate square with a mark opposite the word  NO                                  Section       Section         a         is enacted to read                                      a       Canvass of returns                                      The county legislative body shall conduct a public canvass of the returns from the                           opinion question election no later than    days after the election                                      Each election official shall                                  a   make a certified abstract of the record of the canvassers detailing the votes given for                           and against the opinion question  and                                    b   seal the transcript  endorse it  Election Returns   and transmit it to the lieutenant                           governor s office so that the lieutenant governor receives it by the fifth day before the day                           designated for the meeting of the state board of canvassers                                      The state board of canvassers established by Section       A           shall meet to                           compute and determine the vote on the opinion question                                      After consulting existing statutes governing other general and municipal elections                            the lieutenant governor may determine any issues relating to the

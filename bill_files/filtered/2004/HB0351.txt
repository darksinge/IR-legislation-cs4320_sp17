CURRENT APPROACHES                           TO COVERING THE UNINSURED AND                           UNDERINSURED TASK FORCE                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor  Steven R  Mascaro                                                      LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill creates the Gaps Analysis of Current Approaches to Covering the Uninsured                           and Underinsured Task Force                            Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                     creates a task force to analyze gaps in current approaches to providing                           comprehensive  affordable  accessible  and quality health care for the uninsured and                           the underinsured  and                                    focuses on the extent to which each approach reduces the use of uncompensated                           health care  contributes to improved health outcomes  and how successful each                           program is in leveraging both public and private resources                            Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                This bill appropriates for fiscal year         only                                             to the Senate                                            to the House of Representatives  and                                            to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel                            Other Special Clauses                                This bill is repealed on November                                                                 Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section      Gaps Analysis of Current Approaches to Covering the Uninsured and                           Underinsured Task Force    Creation    Membership    Quorum    Compensation    Staff                                     There is created the Gaps Analysis of Current Approaches to Covering the                          Uninsured and Underinsured Task Force consisting of the following six Legislative members                          and nine ex officio members                                 a   two members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate  no more than                          one of whom may be from the same political party                                 b   four members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the                          House of Representatives  no more than two of whom may be from the same political party                                 c   nine ex officio members appointed by the president of the Senate and the speaker of                          the House of Representatives including                                 i   four members from the Department of Health  including                                 A   the executive director or designee                                 B   the director or designee of the Division of Health Care Financing                                 C   the director or designee of the Children s Health Insurance Program and Access                          Initiative  and                                D   the director or designee of the Health Systems Improvement Program                                 ii   the executive director or a designee of the Comprehensive Health Insurance Pool                                 iii   a member representing a federally qualified health center located in the state  and                                iv   three members from the general public with demonstrated expertise and interest in                          private and public sector approaches to covering the uninsured and underinsured                                     a   The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed                          under Subsection     a  as a cochair of the task force                                 b   The speaker of the House of Representatives shall designate a member of the House                          of Representatives appointed under Subsection     b  as a cochair of the task force                                     A majority of the legislative members of the task force constitute a quorum   The                          action of a majority of a legislative quorum constitutes the action of the task force                                     a   Salaries and expenses of the members of the task force who are legislators shall                          be paid in accordance with Section                and Legislative Joint Rule                                       b   A member of the task force who is not a legislator may not receive compensation                             for the member s work associated with the task force  but may receive per diem and                          reimbursement for travel expenses incurred as an ex officio member of the task force at the                          rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections       A           and       A                                               The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support                          to the task force                                Section      Duties    Interim report                                     The task force shall review and make recommendations on the following issues                                 a   review the current approaches to covering the uninsured and the underinsured and                          addressing the need for comprehensive  affordable  accessible  and quality health care                           including                                 i   the Utah Comprehensive Health Insurance Pool                                 ii   the Primary Care State Network                                 iii   the state Primary Care Grants Program  and                                iv   other relevant approaches identified by the task force                                 b   the cost effectiveness and public health impact of the programs in Subsection     a                           in terms of the following                                 i   the extent to which each program reduces the use of uncompensated care                                 ii   the extent to which each contributes to improved health outcomes  and                                iii   the success of each program in leveraging federal and private sector financial                          resources for covering the uninsured and underinsured  and                                c   other issues that may be recommended by the task force including a review of                          available studies and reports                                     A final report  including any proposed legislation shall be presented to the Health                          and Human Services Interim Committee before November                                         Section      Appropriation                                There is appropriated from the General Fund for fiscal year         only                                            to the Senate to pay for the compensation and expenses of senators on the                          task force                                            to the House of Representatives to pay for the compensation and expenses                          of representatives on the task force  and                                            to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel to pay for                             staffing the task force                                Section      Repeal date                                This bill is repealed

TASK FORCE ON PRIVATIZATION OF           STATE ADOPTION SERVICES                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Nora B. Stephens          AN ACT RELATING TO THE LEGISLATURE  CREATING A TASK FORCE ON THE         PRIVATIZATION OF STATE ADOPTION SERVICES  DEFINING ITS MEMBERSHIP         AND DUTIES  APPROPRIATING    ,    FROM THE GENERAL FUND  PROVIDING         AN EFFECTIVE DATE  AND PROVIDING A REPEAL DATE.          This act enacts uncodified material.          Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .   Task Force on Privatization of State Adoption Services    Creation              Membership    Interim rules followed    Compensation    Staff.                   There is created a legislative Task Force on Privatization of State Adoption Services          consisting of the following    members                a   three members from the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate, two from the          majority party and one from the minority party                b   three members from the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the          House, two from the majority party and one from the minority party                c   the executive director of the Department of Human Services or designee  and               d   five members appointed by the president of the Senate and the speaker of the House          as follows                i   one representative of the Division of Child and Family Services within the Department          of Human Services                ii   one foster parent                iii   one adoptive parent                iv   one representative of licensed child placing agencies  and               v   one attorney whose practice includes private adoption placements.                  a   The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed under         Subsection     a  as a cochair of the task force.              b   The speaker of the House shall designate a member of the House appointed under         Subsection     b  as a cochair of the task force.                  A majority of the members of the task force constitutes a quorum, and the action of a         majority of a quorum constitutes the action of the task force.                  a   Members of the task force who are legislators shall be paid salaries and expenses         in accordance with Section        and Legislative Joint Rule   .  .               b   Members of the task force who are not legislators and are not state employees may not          receive compensation from the state for their work associated with the task force.                   The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support          to the task force.              Section  .   Duties    Reports to interim committee and Privatization Policy Board.                   The task force shall review and make recommendations regarding whether adoption          services provided by the Division of Child and Family Services for children in the custody of the          division should be privatized.  The task force shall consider                a   whether private providers exist or could exist to provide adoption services currently          being provided by the division for children in its custody                b   whether fee for service or managed care models could be used                c   whether privatized adoption services would                i   reduce state adoption costs                ii   increase the number and quality of available adoptive families                iii   improve adoptive families  satisfaction with the system for adopting children in the          custody of the Division of Child and Family Services  or               iv   increase the speed and number of adoptions of children in the custody of the Division          of Child and Family Services, including, but not limited to                A   minority children                B   older children                C   sibling groups  and               D   medically fragile children                       d   the effects of privatization in other states  and              e   whether the provision of ongoing medical, mental health, family support, and other         services by the Department of Human Services and the Department of Health to adoptive families         and adopted children previously in the custody of the Division of Child and Family Services         should be privatized.                  If the task force determines it to be necessary, it shall draft legislation to implement         the task force recommendations.                  A final report, including any proposed legislation, shall be presented to the Health and         Human Services Interim Committee and the Privatization Policy Board by November   ,     .              Section  .   Appropriation.              There is appropriated from the General Fund for fiscal year                              ,    to the Senate to pay for the compensation and expenses of senators on the task          force                      ,    to the House of Representatives to pay for the compensation and expenses of          representatives on the task force  and                      ,    to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel to pay costs of the          task force staff.              Section  .   Effective date.              If approved by two thirds of all the members elected to each house, this act takes effect          upon approval by the governor, or the day following the constitutional time limit of Utah          Constitution Article VII, Section  , without the governor s signature, or in the case of a veto, the          date of veto override.              Section  .   Repeal date.              This act is repealed January  ,     .

GIVING FALSE INFORMATION TO POLICE                           OFFICER                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor   Karen W. Morgan                             This act modifies the Criminal Code by amending the offense of giving false information to                          a peace officer to include the specific offense of giving the name or identification of another                          person.                          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                            AMENDS                                        , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section                 is amended to read                                         .  False personal information to peace officer.                                    A person commits a class C misdemeanor if, with intent of misleading a peace officer                           as to  his  the person s identity, birth date, or place of residence,  he  the person knowingly gives                           a false name, birth date, or address to a peace officer in the lawful discharge of  his  the peace                          officer s official duties.                                    A person commits a class A misdemeanor if, with the intent of misleading a peace                          officer as to the person s identity, birth date, or place of residence, the person knowingly or                          intentionally gives the name, birth date, or address of another person to a peace officer in the                          lawful discharge of the peace officer s official duties.        Legislative Review Note      as of                 AM     A limited legal review of this legislation raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

Olsen          AN ACT RELATING TO THE SALES AND USE TAX ACT  AUTHORIZING A COUNTY TO         IMPOSE A SALES AND USE TAX FOR THE ACQUISITION OF CONSERVATION         EASEMENTS  ESTABLISHING CONDITIONS FOR THE IMPOSITION, USE, AND         REPEAL OF THE TAX  PROVIDING PROCEDURES FOR ADMINISTERING THE TAX           AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE. Subject to the provisions of Subsections     through    , and in addition to any other          tax authorized by this chapter, a county may impose a county option sales and use tax of up to               upon the sales and uses described in Subsection             , subject to the exemptions          provided for in Section          , to acquire conservation easements as provided in Title   ,          Chapter   , Land Conservation Easement Act.                   A sales and use tax under Subsection     shall be imposed                a   upon sales and uses made in the county, including sales and uses made within          municipalities in the county  and               b   beginning on the first day of January, April, July, or October.                   Before imposing a sales and use tax under this section, a county shall               a   obtain a majority vote of all members of the county legislative body               b   obtain voter approval during a regular general election               c   adopt by ordinance               i   a limit on the total amount of sales and use tax revenues that the county may collect         under this section  and              ii   a limit on the amount per acre the county may expend to acquire conservation         easements with the sales and use tax revenues collected under this section  and              d   notify the commission at least    days prior to the imposition of the ordinance          imposing the tax.                   Except as provided in Subsection    , a sales and use tax under this section shall be          imposed and administered in the same manner as a tax imposed under Part  , The Local Sales and          Use Tax Act, except that a sales and use tax imposed under this section is not subject to                a   the distribution provisions of Subsections              and               and               b   the provisions of Subsection             .                   If after imposing a tax under this section a county does not acquire a conservation          easement for three consecutive years                a   the tax shall be repealed on the first day of the calendar quarter immediately following          the three year period  and               b   the revenues generated by the tax during the three year period shall be distributed to          the county and the municipalities within the county in proportion to the sales and use tax          distributed to the county and each municipality within the county under Part  , The Local Sales          and Use Tax Act, during the calendar year immediately preceding the end of the three year period.                   a   If a county or municipality receives a distribution of revenues under Subsection              b  on or before September   , the county or municipality shall include the revenues as property          tax revenues for the current year for purposes of calculating the county s or municipality s certified          tax rate.               b   If a county or municipality receives a distribution of revenues under Subsection     b           after September   , the county or municipality shall include the revenues as property tax revenues          for the next year for purposes of calculating the county s or municipality s certified tax rate.                   If, after including a distribution of revenues as property tax revenues under Subsection                     a  or  b , the property taxes distributed to the county or municipality exceed the amount of         revenues levied by the county or the municipality for the year in which the revenues are         distributed, for purposes of establishing the county s or municipality s certified tax rate in         subsequent years, the municipality shall carry forward as property tax revenues the excess         revenues until the entire amount of remaining revenues have been carried forward as property tax         revenues.

ACCESSIBLE PARKING SPACES                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                           Sponsor   Perry L. Buckner                           AN ACT RELATING TO MOTOR VEHICLES  PROVIDING DEFINITIONS  AMENDING                          PROVISIONS RELATED TO ACCESSIBLE PARKING SPACES  AND MAKING                          TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS.                          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                           AMENDS                                    a    , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                                           . , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section        a         is amended to read                                     a    .  Parking privileges for persons with disabilities.                                    As used in this section,  accessible parking space  means a parking space that is clearly                          identified as reserved for use by a person with a disability and includes                                 a   vertical signage, including the international symbol of accessibility, that is visible from                          a passing vehicle  and                                b   a clearly marked access aisle that is adjacent to and considered part of the parking                          space.                                          Except in parking areas designated for emergency use, a person with a disability,                           qualifying under rules made in accordance with Section         a        , may park an appropriately                           marked vehicle for reasonable periods without charge in metered parking zones and restricted                           parking areas, in a manner that allows proper access to the vehicle by the person with a disability.                                          Only those vehicles carrying a person with a disability special group license plate,                           temporary removable windshield placard, or removable windshield placard and transporting a                           qualifying person with a disability may park in an accessible parking space  that is clearly                             identified as reserved for use by the person with a disability .                                          This section applies to and may be enforced on public property and on private                           property that is used or intended for use by the public.                                          The parking privileges granted by this section also apply to vehicles displaying                           a person with a disability special group license plate, temporary removable windshield placard, or                           removable windshield placard issued by another jurisdiction if displayed on a vehicle being used                           by a person with a disability.                               Section  .    Section           .      is amended to read                                        . .  Volunteers may be authorized to enforce certain parking provisions.                                    Any law enforcement agency authorized to enforce parking laws in this state may                           appoint volunteers to issue citations for violations of                                 a   the provisions of Subsections         a             and      and     related to parking for a                           person with a disability                                 b   any municipal or county accessible parking privileges ordinance for a person with a                           disability  or                                c   the provisions of Subsection             .         related to parking in a school bus parking                           zone.                                    A volunteer appointed under this section must be at least    years of age.                                    The law enforcement agency appointing a volunteer may establish any other                           qualification for the volunteer that the agency finds desirable.                                    A volunteer may not issue citations until the volunteer has received training from the                           appointing law enforcement agency.                                    A citation issued by a volunteer under this section has the same force and effect as a                           citation issued by a peace officer for the same offense.

HIGHER EDUCATION FACULTY   GRADUATE           ENROLLMENT                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Perry Buckner          AN ACT RELATING TO HIGHER EDUCATION  PROVIDING THAT FULL TIME         FACULTY AT HIGHER EDUCATION INSTITUTIONS MAY ENROLL IN GRADUATE         CLASSES WITHOUT PAYING TUITION IF SPACE IS AVAILABLE WITHOUT         INCREASING INSTRUCTIONAL COSTS  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           ENACTS                  B      , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section    B       is enacted to read                  B      .  Tuition waivers for full time faculty.                   Full time faculty at the state s institutions of higher education may enroll in graduate          level classes for which they are qualified at any institution within the state system of higher          education without paying tuition and any other fee associated with those classes after                a   regularly enrolled students have been assigned and admitted to available classroom          space for the graduate level classes in accordance with regular procedures and normal teaching          loads for that space within the approved budget  and               b   the institution has determined that space is available for the enrollment of full time          faculty without increased instructional costs.                   The board shall adopt rules upon which the institutions shall base their space          availability and instructional cost determinations under Subsection    .                   a   Institutional enrollment reports shall show full time faculty enrolled in graduate          classes under this section.               b   Institutions may not count faculty as full time students under this section.             Section  .   Effective date.             This act takes effect on July  ,     .      Legislative Review Note      as of                 AM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

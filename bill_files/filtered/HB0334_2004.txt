SYSTEM OF CARE PILOT PROGRAM                                GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                           Sponsor  Michael E. Noel                                                    LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill creates the System of Care Pilot Program to be administered by the                          Department of Human Services.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    provides definitions                                .    provides wrap around services to    children and their families in select counties                           throughout the state                                .    requires the Division of Child and Family Services and the Division of Youth                           Corrections to pay for certain mental health costs associated with the wrap around                           services                                .    requires the Department of Human Services, the juvenile court, and school district                           personnel who are involved to participate in the development and implementation                           of the wrap around service plan                                .    requires the mental health centers involved to work cooperatively with the                           Department of Human Services, the juvenile court, school districts, and local family                           organizations to implement a local assessment team to monitor the wrap around                           service plan  and                                .    requires participating families to complete a family team survey.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                                 Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            ENACTS                                  A  a    , Utah Code Annotated                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A  a         is enacted to read                                  A  a    .  System of Care Pilot Program.                                    There is established the System of Care Pilot Program to be administered by the                          Division of Child and Family Services beginning on July  ,      and ending on June   ,     .                                    As used in this section                                 a    Family facilitator  means a family member who has had experience in the family                          member s own family with a child with a serious emotional disturbance and has been trained to                          support care givers and youth who have a serious emotional disturbance.  The family                          facilitator                                 i   ensures that the family voice is heard at                                 A   family team meetings  and                                B   public agency meetings                                 ii   assists the parents and family members to partner with agencies  and                                iii   coordinates family team meetings.                                b    Family member  means a parent or family member who has in the past or is                          currently acting as care giver to a child with a serious emotional disturbance.                                c   i    Family team  means a team composed of individuals chosen by the family                          which may include                                 A   the youth or child care giver                                 B   a family facilitator                                 C   an agency partner  or                                D   a support person from the community.                                ii   The family team shall meet regularly to develop and implement service plans and                          to develop and implement crisis and safety plans as needed.                                    iii   The goals of the family team are to help families understand and negotiate the                          system of care, including agency services and community resources.                                d    Family team survey  means a tool that monitors                                 i   agency participation                                 ii   informal and formal supports                                 iii   services delivered in a manner that meet the cultural needs of the family  and                                iv   family satisfaction.                                e    Wrap around service plan  means a single individualized service plan that can be                          understood by the family and, when appropriate, the children, that coordinates all the services                          provided and legal mandates that agencies are required to complete for funding or legislative                          compliance.                                f    Wrap around services  means a family centered, team based, coordinated service                          plan that focuses on the strengths, needs, and cultures of the child and family using formal,                          informal, and natural supports to develop and implement the service plan with the child and                          family.                                    a   Under the pilot program,    children and their families shall receive wrap                          around services from the mental health agency responsible for services in the county where the                          children and their families reside.  The program shall serve the following number of children in                          the following counties                                 i   five children from Washington County                                 ii   five children from Iron County                                 iii   five children from Beaver, Kane, and Piute Counties combined  and                                iv   ten children from Carbon, Emery, and Garfield Counties combined.                                b   The mental health agency will use a family facilitator to work with a family to                          coordinate across agencies to develop and implement a service plan that provides community                          based services or out of home placements that meet the mental health needs of the child and                          provide for community safety.                                    a   The Division of Child and Family Services and the Division of Youth                          Corrections shall pay the mental health center a mutually agreed upon daily rate for the wrap                          around services.                                b   The daily rate shall be paid monthly and shall cover the complete cost of care for                             the child.                                c   The wrap around services purchased at the daily rate must meet the child s clinical                          needs and provide for community safety.                                    The Department of Human Services, the juvenile court, and school district                          personnel directly involved in service delivery to the child and parent or other family members                          who may be direct care givers for the child, shall participate in the wrap around service plan                          development and service implementation.                                    The mental health centers involved shall work cooperatively with the Department                          of Human Services, the juvenile court, school districts, and local family organizations to                          implement a local assessment team to monitor the wrap around service plan to ensure the                           following are being met by the wrap around service plan                                  a   that the child s clinical needs are being met                                  b   that community safety is a primary consideration                                  c   that legislative and funding mandates are being met  and                                 d   that the Department of Human Services, the juvenile court, and school district                           personnel are participating in the planning and delivery of wrap around services.                                     Each family involved in the program will complete a family team survey to                           determine whether they feel their family s needs are being met by the wrap around team and                           wrap around service plan.

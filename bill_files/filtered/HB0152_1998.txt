Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section       a     is amended to read                    a    .  Title.             This chapter is known as the   Cosmetologist Barber  Cosmetologist Barber Esthetician         Licensing Act.              Section  .  Section       a     is amended to read                    a    .  Definitions.                   Approved cosmetologist barber apprenticeship  means an apprenticeship that meets         the requirements of Section      a        and the requirements established by rule.                   Approved esthetician apprenticeship  means an apprenticeship that meets the          requirements of Section      a        and the requirements established by rule.                          Board  means the  Cosmetology Barbering  Cosmetology Barbering  Esthetics          Licensing Board created in Section      a    .                          Cosmetologist barber  means a person who is licensed under this chapter to          engage in the practice of cosmetology barbering.                          Cosmetologist barber apprentice  means a person who is licensed under this          chapter to engage in the practice of cosmetology barbering in an approved cosmetologist barber          apprenticeship.                          Cosmetologist barber instructor  means a cosmetologist barber who is licensed          under this chapter to teach cosmetology barbering at a licensed cosmetology barber school.                          Cosmetology barber shop  means a place, shop, or establishment in which          cosmetology, barbering, or both, are practiced.                          Direct supervision  means that the supervisor of the cosmetology barber          apprentice or esthetician apprentice or instructor of a cosmetology barber student or esthetician          student is immediately available for consultation, advice, instruction, and evaluation.                          Electrologist  means a person who is licensed under this chapter to engage in          the practice of electrology.                     Esthetician  means a person who is licensed under this chapter to engage in the          practice of esthetics.                     Esthetician apprentice  means a person who is licensed under this chapter to engage          in practice of esthetics in an approved esthetician apprenticeship.                     Esthetician instructor  means an esthetician who is licensed under this chapter to                 teach esthetics at a licensed esthetics school.                    Esthetics shop  means a place, shop, or establishment in which esthetics is practiced.                          Licensed cosmetology barber school  means a cosmetology barber school         licensed under this chapter.                    Licensed esthetics school  means an esthetics school licensed under this chapter.                           Practice of cosmetology barbering  means               a   styling, arranging, dressing, curling, waving, permanent waving, cleansing, singeing,         bleaching, dyeing, tinting, coloring, or similarly treating the hair of the head of a person               b   cutting, clipping, or trimming the hair by the use of scissors, shears, clippers, or other          appliances                c   arching eyebrows, or tinting eyebrows or eyelashes, or both                d   removing hair from the face, neck, shoulders, arms, or legs of a person by the use of          depilatories or shaving equipment                e   cutting, curling, styling, fitting, measuring, or forming caps for wigs or hairpieces or          both on the human head  or               f   practicing hair weaving or hair fusing or servicing previously medically implanted hair.                            Practice of cosmetology barbering instruction  means instructing          cosmetology barbering in a licensed cosmetology barber school.                            Practice of electrology  means the removal of superfluous hair from the body          of a person by the use of electricity.                     Practice of esthetics  means any one of the following skin care procedures done on          the face, neck, torso, abdomen, back, arms, hands, legs, feet, nails, eyebrows, or eyelashes                a   cleansing, stimulating, manipulating, exercising, or applying oils, antiseptics, clays,          masks, exfolients, depilatories, or other preparations, or procedures including waxes, sanding,          tweezing, whether by hand massage or by mechanical or electrical appliances on the skin or nails           or               b   removing superfluous hair by means other than electrolysis.                     Practice of esthetics instruction  means instructing esthetics in a licensed esthetics          school.                            Recognized cosmetology barber school  means a cosmetology barber school          located in another state, whose students, upon graduation, are recognized as having completed the                 educational requirements for licensure in that state.                           Recognized electrology school  means an electrology school               a   located in this state whose curriculum consists of     hours of instruction approved         by rule  or              b   located in another state               i   whose curriculum consists of     hours of instruction  and              ii   whose students, upon graduation, are recognized as having completed the educational         requirements for licensure in that state.                    Recognized esthetics school  means an esthetics school located in another state,          whose students, upon graduation, are recognized as having completed the educational requirements          for licensure in that state.                            Unlawful conduct  is as defined in  Section  Sections          and               a    .                            Unprofessional conduct  is as defined in Sections          and      a              and as may be further defined by rule.              Section  .  Section       a     is amended to read                     a    .  Board.                   There is created the  Cosmetology Barbering  Cosmetology Barbering Esthetics          Licensing Board consisting of                a   three cosmetologists barbers                b   one cosmetologist barber instructor                c   one representative of a licensed cosmetology barber school                d   two estheticians                e   one representative of a licensed esthetics school                 d    f   one electrologist  and                e   one member   g   two members from the general public.                   The board shall be appointed and serve in accordance with Section         .                   The duties and responsibilities of the board are in accordance with Sections                   and         .  In addition, the board shall designate one of its members on a permanent or rotating          basis to                a   assist the division in reviewing complaints concerning the unlawful or unprofessional                 conduct of a licensee  and              b   advise the division in its investigation of these complaints.                  A board member who has, under Subsection    , reviewed a complaint or advised in         its investigation may be disqualified from participating with the board when the board serves as         a presiding officer in an adjudicative proceeding concerning the complaint. Section  .  Section       a     is amended to read                     a    .  Terms of license    Expiration    Renewal.                   a   The division shall issue each license under this chapter in accordance with a          two year renewal cycle established by rule, except that a cosmetology barber or esthetician          instructor license is a one time certificate and does not expire unless the licensee fails to maintain          a current cosmetology barber license.               b   The division may by rule extend or shorten a renewal period by as much as one year                 to stagger the renewal cycles it administers.                  At the time of renewal a cosmetology barber school shall               a   show satisfactory evidence of accreditation with the National Accrediting Commission         of Cosmetology Arts and Sciences or other accrediting commissions recognized by the State Board         of Regents for postsecondary schools  and              b   provide satisfactory documentation that the applicant meets the standards for         cosmetology barber schools established by rule.                  At the time of renewal an esthetics school shall show satisfactory evidence that the         applicant meets the standards for esthetics schools established by rule by the division, in          collaboration with the board, including any rules regarding accreditation.                         Each license expires on the expiration date shown on the license unless the          licensee renews it in accordance with Section         .              Section  . In addition to the exemptions from licensure in Section         , the following          persons may engage in the practice of cosmetology barbering without being licensed under this          chapter                      a   persons licensed under the laws of this state to engage in the practice of medicine,          surgery, osteopathy, or chiropractic when engaged in the practice of the profession for which they          are licensed                      b   commissioned physicians and surgeons serving in the armed forces of the United          States or another federal agency                      c   registered nurses, undertakers, and morticians licensed under the laws of this state          when engaged in the practice of the profession for which they are licensed                      d   persons who visit the state to engage in instructional seminars or competitions of          a limited duration                      e   persons who engage in the practice of cosmetology barbering without          compensation                      f   persons instructing adult education classes and other educational programs          directed toward persons who are not licensed in cosmetology barbering and that are not intended          to train persons to become licensed to engage in cosmetology barbering, provided                        a    i   each instructor is licensed as a cosmetologist barber  and               b    ii   attendees receive no credit toward the educational requirement for licensure  and                    g   persons instructing in workshops, seminars, training meetings, and other         educational programs whose purpose is to provide continuing professional development to licensed         cosmetologist barbers or electrologists.                  In addition to the exemptions from licensure in Section         , the following         individuals may engage in the practice of esthetics, without being licensed under this chapter               a   persons licensed under the laws of this state to engage in the practice of medicine,         surgery, osteopathy, or chiropractic when engaged in the practice of the profession for which they          are licensed                b   commissioned physicians and surgeons serving in the armed forces of the United States          or another federal agency                c   persons who visit the state to engage in instructional seminars, advanced esthetics          classes, trade shows, or competitions of a limited duration                d   persons instructing adult education classes and other educational programs directed          toward people who are not licensed in esthetics and that are not intended to train persons to          become licensed to practice esthetics, provided                i   each instructor is licensed as an esthetician  and               ii   attendees receive no credit toward the educational requirement for licensure                e   persons who engage in the practice of esthetics without compensation  and               f   persons employed by a licensed esthetic school to teach theory classes in anatomy,          physiology, pathology, nutrition, or chemistry, who are licensed or certified in their own          profession. Section   .  Section       a     is enacted to read                    a    .  Unlawful conduct.               Unlawful conduct  includes touching, or applying an instrument or device to the following          areas of the body                    the genitals or the anus  and                   the breast of a female patron, except in cases in which the female patron states to a          licensee that the patron requests breast skin procedures and signs a written consent form          authorizing the licensee to perform breast skin procedures.              Section   .   Study by the board.              The board shall study the number of course hours in a recognized or licensed esthetics          school required for licensure as an esthetician.  The board shall report the results of its study and          make a recommendation regarding the appropriate number of course hours to the Business, Labor          and Economic Development Interim Committee of the Legislature at or before the November,              , meeting of the committee.

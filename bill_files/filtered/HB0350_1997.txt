Short          AN ACT RELATING TO STATE AFFAIRS IN GENERAL  ESTABLISHING A UNIFORM         PROCESS FOR AWARDING STATE AGENCIES TO USE WHEN SOLICITING,         EVALUATING, AND AWARDING GRANTS. Section       d     is enacted to read            CHAPTER   d.  STATE GRANT PROCEDURES                     d    .  Title.              This chapter is known as  State Grant Procedures.               Section  .  Section       d     is enacted to read                     d    .  Definitions.              As used in this chapter                    a    Agency  means each department, commission, board, council, agency, institution,          officer, corporation, fund, division, office, committee, authority, laboratory, library, unit, bureau,         panel, or other administrative unit of the state that is authorized to award a grant.              b    Agency  does not include the legislative branch, the board of regents, or the         institutional councils of each higher education institution.                  a    Grant  means a gift of money that an agency is statutorily authorized to award         from money appropriated by the Legislature to an identifiable class of persons without any         requirement that the money be repaid.              b    Grant  does not include loans or discretionary funds.                   Grant committee  means the committee created by Section      d    .                    Substantial interest  means the ownership, either legally or equitably, by an          individual, his spouse, or his minor children, of at least     of the outstanding capital stock of a          public or private corporation or a     interest in any other business entity.              Section  .  Section       d     is enacted to read                     d    .  Application of statute.                   Except as provided in Subsection    , each agency shall comply with this chapter when          awarding grants.                   If the provisions of this chapter conflict with the provisions of the statute granting an          agency the authority to disburse grants, the provisions of the statute granting an agency the          authority to disburse grants prevails.              Section  .  Section       d     is enacted to read                     d    .  Grant committee    Qualifications    Conflicts of interest.                   When directed by the Legislature to disburse grants, an agency shall form a grant          committee composed of at least three but not more than five persons who meet the qualifications          and requirements of this chapter.                   a   The agency may select state officers, state employees, members of the public, or          some combination of those to be members of the grant committee.               b   The agency may not select any person to be a member of the grant committee who has          a substantial interest in, or is an officer, employee, or volunteer serving, a public corporation,          private corporation, or other business entity or governmental entity that might apply for or be a          recipient of a grant from the committee.                   a   A person selected to be a member of the grant committee may not                       i   disclose or improperly use private, controlled, or protected information acquired by         reason of his official position or in the course of official duties in order to further substantially the         member s personal economic interest or to secure special privileges or exemptions for himself or         others               ii   use or attempt to use his official position to               A   further substantially the member s personal economic interest  or              B   secure special privileges for himself or others.              b   Each person who violates this Subsection     is guilty of a class B misdemeanor.             Section  .  Section       d     is enacted to read                     d    .  Request for grant applications.                   The grant committee shall begin the grant process by issuing a request for grant          applications.                   a   The request for grant applications shall include the following                i   instructions and information to potential grant applicants concerning the grant          application submission requirements, including the time and closing date for submission of grant          applications, the address of the office to which grant applications are to be delivered, and any other          special information                ii   the evaluation factors, distribution schedule, and any acceptance requirements                iii   a statement that discussions may be conducted with grant applicants who submit grant          applications that the committee determines to be reasonably susceptible of being selected for          award, but that grants may be made without discussions  and               iv   the terms and conditions of the grant, including any requirements imposed by state          or federal law.               b   The request for grant applications may incorporate documents by reference provided          that the request specifies where the documents can be obtained.               c   The manner in which proposals are to be submitted, including any forms for that          purpose, may be designated as a part of the request for grant applications.                   a   The grant committee shall mail or otherwise furnish copies of the request for grant          applications to a sufficient number of potential grant applicants to secure reasonable competition.                b   The committee shall make a copy of the request for grant applications available for          public inspection at the agency office.                     Section  .  Section       d     is enacted to read                    d    .  Opening and registering requests for grant applications.             The committee shall                   ensure that the agency               a   time stamps the grant applications upon receipt  and              b   keeps each grant application confidential and in a secure place until the date for         opening the applications                   open grant applications in a public meeting and announce only the name of each grant         applicant                    prepare a register of grant applications that identifies the name of the grant applicant,          the date upon which the grant application was submitted, and the use to which the grant applicant          will put the grant                    make the register of grant applications open to public inspection                    make the grant application of the successful applicants open to public inspection for          a period of    days after selection  and                   ensure that grant applications of unsuccessful applicants are classified protected.              Section  .  Section       d     is enacted to read                     d    .  Evaluation of grant applications.                   a   The committee shall evaluate each grant application based upon the grant          requirements imposed by statute and the evaluation factors set forth in the request for grant          applications.               b   The committee may use a numerical rating system.               c   The committee may not use factors not specified in the request for grant applications          in determining award of grant.                   a   The committee may for the purpose of conducting discussions under this          Subsection    , perform a preliminary evaluation of grant applications and classify each          application as                i   acceptable                ii   potentially acceptable, that is, reasonably susceptible of being made acceptable  or               iii   unacceptable.               b   The committee may hold discussions with grant applicants whose grant applications                 were classified  acceptable  or  potentially acceptable  to facilitate and encourage an adequate         number of grant applicants to offer their best grant applications, by amending their original         applications, if needed.              c   The committee shall               i   ensure that grant applicants are accorded fair and equal treatment with respect to any         opportunity for discussions and revisions of grant applications               ii   establish procedures and schedules for conducting discussions  and              iii   require the grant applicant to submit any oral clarification or change of a grant         application in writing to the committee.               d   The committee may not use auction techniques or disclose any information derived          from competing grant applications.                   a   The committee shall establish a common time and date for submission of best and          final offers.               b   The committee may not discuss the best and final offers with grant applicants or allow          changes in grant applications before grants are awarded.              Section  .  Section       d     is enacted to read                     d    .  Award.                   The committee shall apply the grant requirements imposed by statute and the factors          set forth in the request for grant applications in deciding whether or not to award a grant.                   The committee shall make a written determination showing the basis on which grant          was made, identifying how the grant applicant meets the grant requirements imposed by statute          and the factors set forth in the request for grant applications.                   If the committee receives only one grant application in response to a request for grant          applications, the committee may either                a   award the grant to that applicant  or               b   if time permits, resolicit for the purpose of obtaining additional grant applications.                   After grants are awarded, the committee shall give notice of the award to each          successful applicant and make the notice of award available for public inspection in the agency          office.              Section  .  Section       d     is enacted to read                     d    .    Appeals.                          a   Each grant applicant aggrieved by a decision of a grant committee may appeal that         decision to the chief executive officer to the agency.              b   The chief executive officer shall decide the appeal within    calendar days after it is         received.                  If the chief executive officer of the agency does not rule on the appeal within            calendar days, or if the grant applicant is aggrieved by the chief executive officer s decision, the         grant applicant may appeal the decision to the Legislature s interim Executive Appropriations         Committee.

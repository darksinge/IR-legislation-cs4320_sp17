FOSTER CARE CITIZEN REVIEW BOARD           AMENDMENTS                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Nora B. Stephens          AN ACT RELATING TO THE JUDICIAL CODE, REGARDING ABUSE, NEGLECT, AND         DEPENDENCY PROCEEDINGS  MAKING PILOT PROJECT REGARDING FOSTER         CARE CITIZEN REVIEW BOARDS PERMANENT AND STATEWIDE          REESTABLISHING THE RESPONSIBILITIES OF THOSE BOARDS AND OF THE          STEERING COMMITTEE  GRANTING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY  AND PROVIDING          AN EFFECTIVE DATE.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           AMENDS                    a    , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                        g    , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                        g    , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               ENACTS                    g    , Utah Code Annotated               REPEALS AND REENACTS                    g    , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section      a     is amended to read                    a    .  Periodic review hearings    Foster care citizen review boards.               Following the dispositional review hearing, periodic  Periodic review hearings shall be          held by the court or by a  court approved administrative body within the Division of Family          Services  foster care citizen review board, as provided in Chapter  g, at least every six months, in          accordance with federal law.                Section  .  Section      g     is amended to read                   g    .  Definitions.             As used in this chapter                           Panel  Board  means a foster care citizen review  Panel  board created in         accordance with Section     g    .                         Committee  means the Foster Care Citizen Review  Panel Pilot Project  Board         Steering Committee created in  this section  accordance with Section     g    .                         Division  means the Division of Child and Family Services within the         Department of Human Services.                    Plan   or  plans   means the same as that term is defined in Subsection     a       .              Section  .  Section      g     is amended to read                    g    .  Foster Care Citizen Review Board Steering Committee    Membership              Chair    Equipment and staff.                   There is created within state government the Foster Care Citizen Review  Panel Pilot          Project  Board Steering Committee composed of the following members                 a   a member of the Legislature appointed by the president of the Senate and the speaker          of the House of Representatives                  b    a   a member of the Board of Child and Family Services, within the Department of          Human Services, appointed by the chair of that board                 c    b   the director of the division, or his designee                 d    c   a juvenile court judge, appointed by the presiding officer of the Judicial Council                 e    d   a juvenile court administrator, appointed by the administrator of the courts                 f    e   a representative of the Utah Foster Parents Association, appointed by the president          of that organization                 g    f   a representative of a statewide advocacy organization for children, appointed by          the chair of the committee                 h    g   a representative of an agency or organization that provides services to children          who  are  have been adjudicated to be under the jurisdiction of the juvenile court, appointed by          the chair of the committee                  i   a   h   the guardian ad litem , appointed by the chair of the committee  director,          appointed pursuant to Section     a    , or the director s designee                       i   the director or chief of the child protection unit within the Office of the Attorney         General, or his designee               j   one person from each region who is a member of a  citizen review panel  board,         appointed by the chair of the committee  and              k   a private citizen, appointed by the chair of the committee.                  The persons described in Subsection     shall annually elect a chair of the committee         from among themselves.                   Six  A majority of the members of the committee  constitute  constitutes a quorum.          The action of the majority of a quorum represents the action of the committee.                   a   Members of the committee who are not government employees shall receive no          compensation or benefits for their services, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the          performance of the member s official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance          under Sections   A       and   A      .               b   State government officer and employee members who do not receive salary, per diem,          or expenses from their agency for their service may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the          performance of their official duties from the board at the rates established by the Division of          Finance under Sections   A       and   A      .               c   Local government members who do not receive salary, per diem, or expenses from the          entity that they represent for their service may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the          performance of their official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance under          Sections   A       and   A      .               d   Members of the committee may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their          services.                         The committee shall                a   appoint members of  panels  boards in  the  each juvenile court  districts and locations          chosen by the committee  district                b   supervise the recruitment, training, and retention of  panel  board members                 c   develop methods for assigning plans to panels boards for review, including the          methodology to be followed in randomly assigning plans                  d    c   supervise  the evaluation of the pilot project  and evaluate the boards                 e    d   establish and approve  the  policies for  citizen review panels  the boards  and                       f    e   submit a report  on  detailing the results of the  pilot project  boards to the         Legislative Human Services and Judiciary Interim Committees and the Board of Juvenile Court         Judges, on or before December    ,        of each year.                        a   The  division  department shall provide  the following assistance to the         committee   i  office and conference space   ii  equipment and telephones  and  iii   fiscal         management services, including payroll and accounting services, to the committee.              b   Within appropriations from the Legislature, the committee may hire professional and         clerical staff as it considers necessary and appropriate.                   The Legislative Auditor General shall conduct a sunset review of the Citizen Review          Panel Pilot Project and shall report the results of that review to the Legislative Human Services          and Judiciary Interim Committees before November   ,     .                    In accordance with Title   , Chapter   a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the          committee may make rules necessary for                a    recruitment, appointment, and training of board members                b   supervision and evaluation of boards  and               c   establishment of policy for boards.                   The committee may receive gifts, grants, devises, and donations.  If the donor          designates a specific purpose or use for the gift, grant, devise, or donation, it shall be used solely          for the designated purpose.              Section  .  Section      g     is repealed and reenacted to read                    g    .   Foster care citizen review boards    Membership    Responsibilities              Periodic Reviews.                   Foster care citizen review boards shall be established in each juvenile court district in          the state.                   a   The committee shall appoint seven members to each board. Five of those members          shall be parents.               b   Five members of a board constitute a quorum, and an action of a majority of the          quorum constitutes the action of the board.               c   A board member may not be an employee of the division or the juvenile court.               d   Board members are to be representative of the ethnic, cultural, religious,          socio economic, and professional diversity found in the community.                      e   A board may elect its own chair, vice chair, and other officers as it considers         appropriate.              f   The division may designate a representative to provide technical advice to the board         regarding division policy and procedure.                  a   With regard to each child in its custody who has not achieved permanency, the         division shall submit plans and case files to the appropriate boards for their review.  Either a board         or the court shall periodically review each of those cases no less frequently than once every six         months, in accordance with the provisions of Section     a     and    U.S.C. Section    .  In         cases where the court has conducted review hearings, a foster care citizen review board hearing          shall also be conducted within    months from the date of the child s removal from his home.               b   Boards may review additional abuse, neglect, or dependency cases and plans at the          request of the court.                   A board s review and report regarding a child s case and plan shall be consistent with          the provisions of Title   A, Chapter  a, Family Services, and Title   , Chapter  a, Part  , Abuse,          Neglect, and Dependency Proceeding, and shall include at least the following considerations                a   the extent to which the plan s objectives have been implemented or accomplished by          the parent, the child, and the division                b   whether revisions to the plan are needed, and if so, how the plan should be revised                c   the extent to which the division has provided the services and interventions described          in the plan, and whether those services and interventions are assisting, or will assist, the parent and          child to achieve the plan s objectives within the statutory time limitations                d   the extent to which the parent and child have willingly and actively participated in the          interventions described in the plan                e   the continuing necessity for and appropriateness of the child s placement                f   the extent of progress that has been made toward alleviating or mitigating the causes          necessitating the child s removal or continued placement                g   a recommended permanency plan for the child and, if one has been established, an          opinion regarding the appropriateness of that permanency plan  and               h   a determination regarding whether the statutory time limitations described in Title   ,          Chapter  a, Part  , have been met, specifically, whether the    month limitation on reunification          services required by Section     a     has been complied with.  The board shall also render an                 opinion regarding when it estimates that the child will achieve permanency.                  a   Each board shall report its findings and recommendations to the court and to the         division within    days after a case is reviewed by the board.              b   The board s report and recommendation shall be filed with the court, and shall be made         a part of the court s case file.  The report and recommendation shall be presented to the appropriate         judge and, within the parameters of the Rules of Evidence, shall be considered by the judge in the         child s abuse, neglect, or dependency proceedings.                  Members of boards shall receive no financial compensation or benefits for their         services.  Members may not receive per diem or expenses for their service, except that                a   members may be reimbursed for mileage on days that they are involved in training, at          rates established by the Division of Finance  and               b   members may be provided with a meal on days that they serve on a board.                   Boards are authorized to receive funds from public and private grants and donations.              Section  .  Section      g     is enacted to read                    g    .  Foster Care Citizen Review Board Steering Committee Restricted           Account.                   There is created in the General Fund a restricted account known as the  Foster Care          Citizen Review Board Steering Committee Account.                    The account shall be funded from undesignated gifts, grants, devises, or donations to          the committee, pursuant to Subsection     g       .                   The account shall be used to pay necessary expenses of the committee and of boards,          as described in Sections     g     and     g    .              Section  .   Effective date.              This act takes effect on April  ,     .      Legislative Review Note      as of                  PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

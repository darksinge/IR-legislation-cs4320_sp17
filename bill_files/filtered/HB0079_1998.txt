PRIVATE KINSHIP CARE                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Patricia B. Larson          AN ACT RELATING TO UNIFORM PROBATE CODE  AUTHORIZING THE USE OF A         POWER OF ATTORNEY TO REGISTER A CHILD IN SCHOOL AND OBTAIN         INSURANCE  AND PROVIDING LEGAL FORMS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          AMENDS                        , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Delegation of powers by parent or guardian.                     As used in this section                a    Responsible adult  means a person    years of age or older who is a resident of this          state and is willing and able to provide adequate food, clothing, shelter, and supervision for a          minor child.               b    State benefits  include cash assistance and food stamps pursuant to Title   A, Chapter           , Employment Support Act, and Medicaid pursuant to Title   , Chapter   , Medical Assistance          Act.                   A parent or a guardian of a minor or incapacitated person, by a properly executed          power of attorney, may delegate to another person, for a period not exceeding six months, any of          his powers regarding care, custody, or property of the minor child or ward, except his power to          consent to marriage or adoption of a minor ward.                   a   A power of attorney executed in accordance with Subsection     may be used for          a specific purpose listed in Subsection     b  if                i   the parent or guardian of the minor child has delegated all parental powers to a          responsible adult, except the power to consent to marriage or adoption               ii   the minor child resides with the responsible adult  and              iii   the responsible adult agrees, in a sworn notarized statement, to be held legally         responsible for the minor child in all respects and to the same extent as a parent or guardian of the         minor child while the power of attorney is in effect.              b   In addition to its general uses, a power of attorney executed in accordance with         Subsection     a  may be used by the designated responsible adult to               i   register the minor child for school in the school district in which the responsible adult         resides if the requirements of Section   A       are otherwise satisfied  and               ii   add the minor child as a dependent on the responsible adult s health, motor vehicle, or          other insurance policy governed by Title   A to the same extent as is permitted for a natural or          adopted child of the responsible adult and subject to the same terms that apply to minor          dependents, including preexisting conditions and increased premiums, if                A   the minor child is not covered by an insurance policy of the type being sought  and               B   the parent or guardian of the minor child does not have an insurance policy of the type          being sought for which the child would be eligible for coverage.               c   A power of attorney executed in accordance with this section may be renewed in          writing in six month increments for up to    months.               d   If a power of attorney is used for a specific purpose described in Subsection     b , the          responsible adult shall notify each affected school district and insurance company within    days          of the renewal of the power of attorney.               e   An affected school district or insurance company may request information regarding          the status of a power of attorney from the responsible adult or the parent or guardian.                   A power of attorney executed in accordance with this section does not affect                a   the ability of a responsible adult to apply for state benefits as a specified relative on          behalf of a minor child                b   the familial status of the minor child or ward, except that a minor child shall have the          status of a dependent of the responsible adult under the terms of an insurance policy to which the          minor child has been added in accordance with Subsection     b                 c   the status of a child in the custody of the Division of Child and Family Services or          involved in any proceeding under Title   , Chapter  a, Juvenile Courts  or                      d   the rights and responsibilities of the parent or guardian for the minor child, which         continue while the power of attorney is in effect.                  A power of attorney for a specific purpose described in Subsection     a  shall be in         substantially the following form and notarized          POWER OF ATTORNEY  PARENTAL RIGHTS           Pursuant to Utah Code Section                         .  I,               , being of sound mind, willfully and voluntarily delegate to                       , a responsible adult    years of age or older, all of the parental powers I have         as the parent or legal guardian of               , a minor child, with the exception of the power          to consent to the child s marriage or adoption.               .  The minor child shall reside with the responsible adult to whom parental powers have          been delegated while this power of attorney is in effect.               .  The responsible adult shall agree, in a sworn notarized statement, to be held legally          responsible for the minor child in all respects and to the same extent as a parent or guardian of the          minor and to provide adequate food, clothing, shelter, and supervision for the minor child while          this power of attorney is in effect.               .  The responsible adult may use this power of attorney to, in addition to its general          purposes, register the minor child for school in the school district in which the responsible adult          resides as long as the requirements of state law are otherwise satisfied and add the minor child as          a dependent on the responsible adult s health, motor vehicle, or other insurance policy governed          by state law.               .  This power of attorney automatically terminates six months from the date it is executed          or upon the minor child residing with me or upon my oral or written declaration of termination.               .  This power of attorney may be renewed in writing in six month increments for up to             months.               .  The responsible adult shall give each affected school district and insurance company          notice within    days of the renewal of this power of attorney and information regarding its status          upon request.               .  My rights and responsibilities for the minor child continue while this power of attorney          is in effect.

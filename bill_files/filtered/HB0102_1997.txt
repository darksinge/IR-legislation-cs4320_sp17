INCOME TAX CREDIT FOR EDUCATIONAL CHOICE                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Evan L. Olsen          AN ACT RELATING TO INCOME TAX  PROVIDING A TAX CREDIT FOR TUITION         COSTS INCURRED IN SATISFYING THE STATE S COMPULSORY EDUCATION LAW          PROVIDING FOR AN ANNUAL REPORT ON THE NUMBER AND TOTAL DOLLAR         AMOUNT OF CREDITS CLAIMED  AND PROVIDING FOR RETROSPECTIVE         OPERATION.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           ENACTS                         , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section            is enacted to read                         .  Education tax credit.                   a   For the tax year beginning January  ,     , and for each year thereafter, a taxpayer          may claim as a credit against the tax otherwise due under this chapter an amount not to exceed               for each dependent in grades kindergarten through   , for tuition costs in attending an          elementary or secondary school in the state.               b   As used in this section                i    School  means a public, private, or home school, attendance at which satisfies the          state s compulsory education law and which is not operated for profit.               ii    Tuition  means any charges for the expenses of personnel, buildings, equipment and          materials, other than textbooks, and other expenses of elementary and secondary schools which          relate to the teaching only of those subjects legally and commonly taught in the public and          secondary schools in this state.  It includes instruction provided by the taxpayer or the taxpayer s          spouse in a home school.  It does not include charges related to                A   the teaching of religious tenets, doctrine, or worship, the purpose of which is to         inculcate those tenets, doctrines, or worship  and              B   extracurricular activities, including sporting events, musical or dramatic events, speech         activities, driver s education, or programs of a similar nature.                  Taxpayers filing a joint return must jointly request the tax credit.                  The tax credit provided for in Subsection     a  is a partial offset against the state         monies that would otherwise be required to educate private and home school students in the public         schools.                  Prior to each annual legislative session, the State Tax Commission shall report to the          Legislature on the number and total dollar amount of credits approved under this section for the          previous tax year to enable the Legislature to determine if the credit limit should be modified.              Section  .   Retrospective operation.              This act has retrospective operation for taxable years beginning on or after January  ,    .

Section   .  Section           is enacted to read            CHAPTER   .  DOMESTICATED ELK ACT            Part  .  General Provisions                        .  Title.              This chapter is known as the  Domesticated Elk Act.               Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Definitions.                      As used in this chapter                    Domesticated elk  means elk of the genus and species cervus elaphus, held in         captivity and domestically raised for commercial purposes.                   Domesticated elk facility  means a facility where domesticated elk are raised.                   Domesticated elk product  means any carcass, part of a carcass, hide, meat, meat food         product, antlers, or any part of a domesticated elk.             Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Department s responsibilities.             The department is responsible for enforcing laws and rules relating to                    the importation, possession, or transportation of domesticated elk into the state or          within the state                    the inspection of domesticated elk facilities                    preventing the outbreak and controlling the spread of disease causing pathogens among          domesticated elk in domesticated elk facilities                    preventing the spread of disease causing pathogens from domesticated elk to wildlife,          other animals, or humans  and                   if necessary, quarantining any domesticated elk pursuant to Title  , Chapter   ,          Livestock Inspection and Quarantine.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Advisory council.                   The department shall establish an advisory council to give advice and make          recommendations on policies and rules adopted pursuant to this chapter.                   The advisory council shall consist of eight members appointed by the commissioner          of agriculture to four year terms as follows                a   two members, recommended by the executive director of the Department of Natural          Resources, shall represent the Department of Natural Resources                b   two members shall represent the Department of Agriculture, one of whom shall be the          state veterinarian                c   two members shall represent the livestock industry, one of whom shall represent the          domesticated elk industry  and               d   two members, recommended by the executive director of the Department of Natural                  Resources from a list of candidates submitted by the Division of Wildlife Resources, shall         represent wildlife interests.                  Notwithstanding the requirements of Subsection    , the commissioner shall, at the         time of appointment or reappointment, adjust the length of terms to ensure that the terms of council         members are staggered so that approximately half of the council is appointed every two years.                  When a vacancy occurs in the membership for any reason, the replacement shall be         appointed for the unexpired term.                  A majority of the advisory council constitutes a quorum.  A quorum is necessary for         the council to act.                   a   i   Members who are not government employees shall receive no compensation or          benefits for their services, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the performance of          the member s official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections            A       and   A      .               ii   Members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their service.               b   i   State government officer and employee members who do not receive salary, per          diem, or expenses from their agency for their service may receive per diem and expenses incurred          in the performance of their official duties from the department at the rates established by the          Division of Finance under Sections   A       and   A      .               ii   State government officer and employee members may decline to receive per diem and          expenses for their service.              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Prohibited activities.                   Except as provided in this title or in the rules of the department made pursuant to this          title, a person may not                a   acquire, import, or possess domesticated elk intended for use in a domesticated elk          facility                b   transport domesticated elk to or from a domesticated elk facility                c   propagate domesticated elk in a domesticated elk facility  or               d   harvest, transfer, possess, or sell domesticated elk or domesticated elk products from          a domesticated elk facility.                   Hunting of domesticated elk is prohibited.                           Releasing domesticated elk into the wild is prohibited.             Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Department to make rules.                  The department shall make rules in accordance with Title   , Chapter   a, Utah         Administrative Rulemaking Act, after considering the recommendations of the advisory council               a   specifying procedures for the application and renewal of licenses for operating a         domesticated elk facility               b   governing the disposal or removal of domesticated elk from a domesticated elk facility         for which the license has lapsed or been revoked                c   setting standards and requirements for operating a domesticated elk facility  and               d   setting health requirements and standards for health inspections.                   The department may make other rules consistent with its responsibilities set forth in          Section         .              Section   .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Powers of state veterinarian.              The state veterinarian shall                    set up periodic or ongoing surveillance programs considered necessary for                a   the recognition, control, monitoring, and elimination of infectious diseases and          parasites  and               b   monitoring genetic purity  and                   quarantine or make any disposition of diseased animals that he or she considers          necessary for the control or eradication of that disease. Section           is enacted to read            Part  .  Escape of Domesticated Elk   Removal of Captive Elk                        .  Escape of domesticated elk.                   It is the owner s responsibility to try to capture any domesticated elk that may have          escaped.                   The escape of a domesticated elk shall be reported immediately to the state veterinarian          or a brand inspector of the Department of Agriculture who shall notify the Division of Wildlife          Resources.                   If the domesticated elk is not recovered within    hours of the escape, the Department          of Agriculture, in conjunction with the Division of Wildlife Resources, shall take whatever action          is necessary to resolve the problem.                   The owner shall reimburse the department, or the Division of Wildlife Resources for          any reasonable recapture costs that may be incurred in the recapture or destruction of the animal.                           Any escaped domesticated elk taken by a licensed hunter in a manner which complies         with the provisions of Title    and the rules of the Wildlife Board shall be considered to be a legal         taking and neither the licensed hunter, the department, nor the Division of Wildlife Resources shall         be liable to the owner for the killing.

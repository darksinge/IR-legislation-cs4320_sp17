Section         b         is amended to read                                      b    .  Definitions.                               In addition to the definitions in Section                 , as used in this chapter                                      Board  means the Utah Board of Massage Therapy created in Section          b        .                                     Homeostasis  means maintaining, stabilizing, or returning to equilibrium the muscular                           system.                                     Massage apprentice  means an individual licensed under this chapter as a massage                           apprentice to work under the direct supervision of a licensed massage technician.                                     Massage therapist  means an individual licensed under this chapter as a massage                           therapist.                                     Minor  means a person under the age of    who                                 a   is not married  and                                   b   has not been declared emancipated by a court of law.                                           Practice of massage therapy  means                                 a   the examination, assessment, and evaluation of the soft tissue structures of the body                           for the purpose of devising a treatment plan to promote homeostasis                                 b   the systematic manual or mechanical manipulation of the soft tissue of the body for the                           therapeutic purpose of                                 i   promoting the health and well being of a client                                 ii   enhancing the circulation of the blood and lymph                                 iii   relaxing and lengthening muscles                                 iv   relieving pain                                 v   restoring metabolic balance  and                                vi   achieving homeostasis                                  c   the use of the hands or a mechanical or electrical apparatus                                   d    c   the use of rehabilitative procedures involving the soft tissue of the body                                  e    d   range of motion or movements without spinal adjustment as set forth in Section                                                                           f    e   oil rubs, heat lamps, salt glows, hot and cold packs, or tub, shower, steam, and                           cabinet baths                                  g    f   manual traction and stretching exercise                                  h    g   correction of muscular distortion by treatment of the soft tissues of the body                                  i    h   counseling, education, and other advisory services to reduce the incidence and                           severity of physical disability, movement dysfunction, and pain  and                                 j    i   similar or related activities and modality techniques. Section  .    Section         b         is amended to read                                      b    .  Unlawful conduct.                                     Unlawful conduct  includes                                       a   practicing, engaging in, or attempting to practice or engage in massage therapy                              without holding a current license as a massage therapist or a massage apprentice under this chapter                                       b   advertising or representing himself as practicing massage therapy when not                           licensed to do so  and                                      c   massaging, touching, or applying any instrument or device  to the following areas                          of the body  by a licensee in the course of practicing or engaging in massage therapy to                                  a    i   the genitals or anus of a patron at the request or with the consent of the patron                             and                                  b    ii   the breasts of a female patron, except in cases in which the female patron states                           to a licensee that the patron requests breast massage and signs a written consent form authorizing                           the licensee to perform breast massage before the procedure .   and                                iii   a patron who is a minor unless the licensee secured both the verbal and written                          consent of the minor s parent or legal guardian.                                   It is an affirmative defense to Subsection     c  iii  if a licensee proves by a                          preponderance of the evidence that, prior to any massage, touching, or application of an instrument                          or device, the licensee reviewed, recorded, and maintained a personal identification number taken                          from an apparently valid driver s license or other picture identification card that clearly indicated                          that the minor was    years of age or older.                                    Notwithstanding Section          b        , the massaging, touching, or application of an                          instrument or device to the genitals or anus of a patron that is nonconsenual or involves a minor,                          while constituting unlawful conduct for purposes of this section, is subject to the applicable                          provisions and penalties in Title   , Criminal Code.                               Section  .    Section         b         is amended to read                                      b    .  Penalties.                                    Each violation of this chapter, other than a violation of Section          b        , is a class                           B misdemeanor.                                     Any  Unless Subsection          b            applies, an individual who commits an act of                           unlawful conduct under Section          b         is guilty of a class A misdemeanor.

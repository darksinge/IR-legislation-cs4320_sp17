PUBLIC OFFICE MISCONDUCT AMENDMENTS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Susan J. Koehn          AN ACT RELATING TO THE CRIMINAL CODE  REMOVING CRIMINAL SANCTIONS         FOR A PUBLIC OFFICIAL S FAILURE TO TAKE THE OATH OF OFFICE AND         EXECUTE THE BOND  AND PROVIDING A CIVIL REMEDY.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          AMENDS                        , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah               ENACTS                        , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Unofficial misconduct.                  A person is guilty of unofficial misconduct if he  exercises or attempts to exercise any          of the functions of a public office when                  a   he has not taken and filed the required oath of office                  b   he has failed to execute and file the required bond                  c    a   exercises or attempts to exercise any of the functions of a public office when  he          has not been elected or appointed to that office                 d   he   b  exercises any of the functions of his office after his term has expired and the          successor has been elected or appointed and has qualified, or after his office has been legally          removed  or                e    c   he knowingly withholds or retains from his successor in office or other person          entitled to the official seal or any records, papers, documents, or other writings appertaining or          belonging to his office or mutilates or destroys or takes away the same.                    Unofficial misconduct is a class B misdemeanor.             Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                       .  Action regarding incomplete qualification for office.             If any elected or appointed official has failed to take, and file, the required oath of office,         or has failed to execute and file the bond required for that office, a voter within that official s         district may                   deliver to the official a written notice of the failure to fulfill the requirements  and                  if the official has not completed the requirements within    days after receipt of the         written notice required under Subsection    , the voter may file a writ of mandamus against the          official.      Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

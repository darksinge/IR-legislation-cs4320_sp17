Section      A  a         is enacted to read                            Part  .  Recognition of Academic Excellence                                  A  a    .  Recognition of Academic Excellence Pilot Program    Purpose                              Termination date    Application process    Requirements    Report.                                    a   There is established a Recognition of Academic Excellence Pilot Program to                          provide financial rewards to educators in schools demonstrating enhanced academic excellence                          as provided in this part.                                b   The pilot program shall last for three years and terminate on June   ,      unless                          continued by the Legislature.                                    a   The State Board of Education, through the state superintendent of public                          instruction, shall                                    i   establish application deadlines for schools to participate in the pilot program  and                                ii   select a representative sample of schools with regard to socioeconomic status and other                          factors it considers significant.                                b   The local school board of the district in which the applicant school is located shall                          review and approve the school s application prior to its submission to the state board.                                c   The application process shall allow for individual schools to participate in the pilot                          program, but total participation in the pilot program may not exceed   ,    students and a single                          district s participation may not exceed   ,    students.                                d   Charter schools and schools with less than    students per grade are not eligible for                          the pilot program.                                    Participation in the pilot program is on a voluntary basis and subject to the following                          requirements                                 a   current usage of and agreement to the annual administration of end of level                          criterion referenced tests to students at the participant school                                 b   a commitment by the school districts in which participant schools are located to a                          dollar for dollar match of money allocated by the state according to each district s percentage of                          students initially in the pilot program                                 c   agreement to assistance by an intervention team of highly effective teachers from the                          district in which the school is located if a school, grade, or grades are consistently performing                          below established expectations under the program  and                                d   agreement by the district in which the school is located to provide press announcements                          of schools given awards under this section or reaching other benchmarks of excellence established                          by the State Board of Education.                                    The State Board of Education shall structure the pilot program to provide for the                          following                                 a   alignment of tests given under Subsection     a  with the state core curriculum                                 b   use of end of level test scores to give equal financial recognition to each teacher in a                          given grade at a participant school based on                                 i   a progress recognition award that recognizes the extent by which the grade s median                          score on the end of level criterion referenced tests exceeded the average of the two previous years,                          except that the first year awards for the school year ending in      may be based upon                             improvement of the median score from the previous school year if the school has only been using                          those tests for a single year                                 ii   an excellence recognition award based on how much the median score exceeded the                          expected score  and                                iii   weighting of excellence awards under Subsection     b  ii  so that     of the money                          awarded under the pilot program is for these awards                                 c   calculation of expected scores under Subsection     b  ii  by the State Office of                          Education which takes into account such proven factors as student mobility, socioeconomic status,                          limited English proficiency, and the overall nature or status of the school, such as alternative                          schools or magnet schools  and                                d   financial recognition awards for the school s principal and assistant principals equal                          to the average award given to teachers at the school.                                    The state superintendent of public instruction shall provide a report to the Education                          Interim Committee on the progress made under the pilot program by September   of each year, the                          first report due by September  ,     .                                    a   Except as provided in H.B.  , Appropriations Coordination Act, there is                          appropriated from the Uniform School Fund for the fiscal year          ,   ,   ,    to the State                          Board of Education to fund the pilot program established under this section.                                b   The board shall establish a distribution formula for the appropriation to include at least                          the following considerations                                 i   the number of educators, including principals and assistant principals, who are involved                          in the pilot program  and                                ii   the nature and weighting of the awards as referred to in Subsections     b  and  d .                                c   The appropriation is nonlapsing.                                d   The appropriation for the second and third year of the pilot program shall include                          funding for intervention teams referred to in Subsection     c .

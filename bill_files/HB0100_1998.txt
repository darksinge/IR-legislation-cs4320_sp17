1     UTAH SUMMER GAMES APPROPRIATION




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: DeMar Bud Bowman


5    AN ACT RELATING TO APPROPRIATIONS; MODIFYING PROVISIONS OF THE

6    GOVERNOR'S BUDGET SUBMISSION; APPROPRIATING $50,000 FROM THE

7    GENERAL FUND TO THE OFFICE OF UNIVERSITY ADVANCEMENT OF SOUTHERN

8    UTAH UNIVERSITY FOR THE UTAH SUMMER GAMES; MAKING TECHNICAL

9    CORRECTIONS; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.

10    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

11    AMENDS:

12         63-38-2, as last amended by Chapter 136, Laws of Utah 1997

13    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

14        Section 1.  Section  63-38-2 is amended to read:

15         63-38-2.  Governor to submit budget to Legislature -- Contents -- Preparation --

16     Appropriations based on current tax laws and not to exceed estimated revenues.

17        (1) (a)  The governor shall, within three days after the convening of the Legislature in the

18    annual general session, submit a budget for the ensuing fiscal year by delivering it to the presiding

19    officer of each house of the Legislature together with a schedule for all of the proposed

20    appropriations of the budget, clearly itemized and classified.

21        (b)  The budget message shall include a projection of estimated revenues and expenditures

22    for the next fiscal year.

23        (2)  At least 34 days before the submission of any budget, the governor shall deliver a

24    confidential draft copy of his proposed budget recommendations to the Office of the Legislative

25    Fiscal Analyst.

26        (3) (a)  The budget shall contain a complete plan of proposed expenditures and estimated

27    revenues for the next fiscal year based upon the current fiscal year state tax laws and rates.


1        (b)  The budget may be accompanied by a separate document showing proposed

2    expenditures and estimated revenues based on changes in state tax laws or rates.

3        (4)  The budget shall be accompanied by a statement showing:

4        (a)  the revenues and expenditures for the last fiscal year;

5        (b)  the current assets, liabilities, and reserves, surplus or deficit, and the debts and funds

6    of the state;

7        (c)  an estimate of the state's financial condition as of the beginning and the end of the

8    period covered by the budget;

9        (d)  a complete analysis of lease with an option to purchase arrangements entered into by

10    state agencies;

11        (e)  the recommendations for each state agency for new full-time employees for the next

12    fiscal year; which recommendation should be provided also to the State Building Board under

13    Subsection 63A-5-103(2);

14        (f)  any explanation the governor may desire to make as to the important features of the

15    budget and any suggestion as to methods for the reduction of expenditures or increase of the state's

16    revenue; and

17        (g)  the information detailing certain regulatory fee increases required by Section

18    63-38-3.2.

19        (5)  The budget shall include an itemized estimate of the appropriations for:

20        (a)  the Legislative Department as certified to the governor by the president of the Senate

21    and the speaker of the House;

22        (b)  the Executive Department;

23        (c)  the Judicial Department as certified to the governor by the state court administrator;

24        (d)  payment and discharge of the principal and interest of the indebtedness of the state of

25    Utah;

26        (e)  the salaries payable by the state under the Utah Constitution or under law for the lease

27    agreements planned for the next fiscal year;

28        (f)  other purposes that are set forth in the Utah Constitution or under law; and

29        (g)  all other appropriations.

30        (6)  Deficits or anticipated deficits shall be included in the budget.

31        (7) (a) (i)  For the purpose of preparing and reporting the budget, the governor shall require

10    Department, the Judicial Department, and those providing for the payment of principal and interest

11    to the state debt and for the salaries and expenditures specified by the Utah Constitution or under

12    the laws of the state.

13        (8)  The total appropriations requested for expenditures authorized by the budget may not

14    exceed the estimated revenues from taxes, fees, and all other sources for the next ensuing fiscal

15    year.

16        (9)  If any item of the budget as enacted is held invalid upon any ground, the invalidity

17    does not affect the budget itself or any other item in it.

18        (10) (a)  In submitting the budget for the Departments of Health and Human Services, the

19    governor shall consider a separate recommendation in his budget for funds to be contracted to:

20        (i)  local mental health authorities under Section 17A-3-606;

21        (ii)  local substance abuse authorities under Section 62A-8-110.5;

22        (iii)  area agencies on aging under Section 62A-3-104.2;

23        (iv)  programs administered directly by and for operation of the Divisions of Mental

24    Health, Substance Abuse, and Aging and Adult Services; and

25        (v)  local health departments under Title 26A, Chapter 1, Local Health Departments.

28    authorities, local substance abuse authorities, and area agencies on aging the same percentage

29    increase for wages and benefits that he includes in his budget for persons employed by the state.

30        (c)  If the governor does not include in his budget an amount sufficient to grant the increase

9    for not including that amount.

11    uniform fee and other motor vehicle fees under Section 59-2-406 in the 1996-97 fiscal year budget.

12        (13) (a)  The Families, Agencies, and Communities Together Council may propose to the

14    delivery systems operated under Section 63-75-6.5.

15        (b)  The Legislature may, through a specific program schedule, designate funds

16    appropriated for collaborative service delivery systems operated under Section 63-75-6.5.

17        (14)  The governor shall include in his budget the state's portion of the budget for the Utah

18    Communications Agency Network established in Title 63C, Chapter 7, Utah Communications

19    Agency Network Act.

20        (15)  The governor shall include in the budget at least  $50,000 to the Office of University

21    Advancement of Southern Utah University to help defray the costs of staging the event known as

22    the Utah Summer Games.

23        Section 2.   Appropriation.

24        There is appropriated $50,000 from the General Fund for fiscal year 1998-99 to the Office

25    of University Advancement of Southern Utah University to help defray the costs of staging the

26    event known as the Utah Summer Games.

27        Section 3.   Effective date.

28        This act takes effect on July 1, 1998.

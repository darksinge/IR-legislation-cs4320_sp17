H.B. 346





1     CHILD CARE LICENSING REGULATION




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor:  David M. Jones


5    AN ACT RELATING TO CHILD CARE LICENSING; DELAYING THE CODIFICATION OF

6    CHILD CARE LICENSING REGULATIONS UNTIL THE 1999 GENERAL SESSION;

7    REQUIRING THE REPORTING OF COMMUNICABLE DISEASES; AND CLARIFYING

8    TERMS OF APPOINTMENT FOR CHILD CARE LICENSING ADVISORY COMMITTEE.

9    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

10    AMENDS:

11         26-6-6, as repealed and reenacted by Chapter 211, Laws of Utah 1996

12         26-39-103, as enacted by Chapter 196, Laws of Utah 1997

13         26-39-104, as enacted by Chapter 127, Laws of Utah 1997

14    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

15        Section 1.  Section  26-6-6 is amended to read:

16         26-6-6.  Duty to report individual suspected of having communicable disease.

17        The following shall report to the department or the local health department regarding any

18    individual suffering from or suspected of having a disease that is communicable, as required by

19    department rule:

20        (1)  health care providers as defined in Section 78-14-3;

21        (2)  facilities licensed under Title 26, Chapter 21, Health Care Facility Licensure and

22    Inspection Act;

23        (3)  health care facilities operated by the federal government;

24        (4)  mental health facilities;

25        (5)  care facilities licensed by the Department of Human Services;

26        (6)  nursing homes and other care facilities;

27        (7)  dispensaries, clinics, or laboratories that diagnose, test, or otherwise care for


1    individuals who are suffering from a disease suspected of being communicable;

2        (8)  individuals who have knowledge of others who have a communicable disease; [and]

3        (9)  individuals in charge of schools having responsibility for any individuals who have a

4    disease suspected of being communicable; and

5        (10)  child care programs, as defined in Section 26-39-102.

6        Section 2.  Section  26-39-103 is amended to read:

7         26-39-103.  Child Care Licensing Advisory Committee.

8        (1)  There is established the Child Care Licensing Advisory Committee to advise the

9    department on rules promulgated by the department pursuant to this chapter.  It shall be composed

10    of nine members who shall be appointed by the executive director.  Two members shall represent

11    consumers of child care programs licensed pursuant to this chapter, four members shall be

12    providers licensed pursuant to this chapter at least two of whom shall be facility owner-operators,

13    two members shall be health care providers, and one member shall be an individual with expertise

14    in early childhood development.

15        (2)  Of the initial members of the Child Care Licensing Advisory Committee, the executive

16    director shall appoint three to four-year terms, [two] three to three-year terms, and [two] three to

17    two-year terms.  Thereafter, appointments shall be for four-year terms, except for those members

18    who have been appointed to complete an unexpired term.  The committee shall annually elect a

19    chairman from its membership.

20        (3)  The advisory committee shall meet at least quarterly, or more frequently as determined

21    by the executive director, the chairman, or three or more members of the committee.  Five

22    members constitute a quorum and a vote of the majority of the members present constitutes an

23    action of the committee.

24        (4)  Advisory committee members shall be reimbursed for their actual and necessary

25    expenses incurred in the performance of their duties as established by the director of the Division

26    of Finance, pursuant to Section 63A-3-107.  Advisory committee members may decline

27    reimbursement.

28        Section 3.  Section  26-39-104 is amended to read:

29         26-39-104.  Duties of the department.

30        (1)  With regard to child care programs licensed pursuant to this chapter, the department

31    may:

- 2 -


1        (a)  make and enforce rules to implement the provisions of this chapter and, as necessary

2    to protect children's common needs for a safe and healthy environment, to provide for:

3        (i)  adequate facilities and equipment; and

4        (ii)  competent caregivers considering the age of the children and the type of program

5    offered by the licensee;

6        (b)  make and enforce rules necessary to carry out the purposes of this chapter, in the

7    following areas:

8        (i)  requirements for applications, the application process, and compliance with other

9    applicable statutes and rules;

10        (ii)  documentation and policies and procedures that providers shall have in place in order

11    to be licensed, in accordance with Subsection (1)(a);

12        (iii)  categories, classifications, and duration of initial and ongoing licenses;

13        (iv)  changes of ownership or name, changes in licensure status, and changes in operational

14    status;

15        (v)  license expiration and renewal, contents, and posting requirements;

16        (vi)  procedures for inspections, complaint resolution, disciplinary actions, and other

17    procedural measures to encourage and assure compliance with statute and rule; and

18        (vii)  guidelines necessary to assure consistency and appropriateness in the regulation and

19    discipline of licensees; and

20        (c)  set and collect licensing and other fees in accordance with Section 26-1-6.

21        (2)  The department may not regulate educational curricula, academic methods, or the

22    educational philosophy or approach of the provider.  The department shall allow for a broad range

23    of educational training and academic background in certification or qualification of child day care

24    directors.

25        (3)  In licensing and regulating child care programs, the department shall, by rule, provide

26    for a range of licensure, depending upon the needs and different levels and types of child care

27    provided.  The department shall prepare language for the [1998] 1999 General Session of the

28    Legislature to codify as much licensing regulation implemented pursuant to this subsection as the

29    department determines to be practicable.

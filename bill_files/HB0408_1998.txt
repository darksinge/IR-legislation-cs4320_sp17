H.B. 408





1     STATUTORY PRESUMPTIONS




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: David Ure


5    AN ACT RELATING TO STATUTORY CONSTRUCTION; PROVIDING PRESUMPTIONS

6    FOR STATUTORY CONSTRUCTION.

7    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

8    ENACTS:

9         68-3-12.5, Utah Code Annotated 1953

10    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

11        Section 1.  Section  68-3-12.5 is enacted to read:

12         68-3-12.5.  Statutory presumptions.

13        (1)  Unless clearly indicated to the contrary in the text of a statute, because of the

14    constitutional duty of the state to protect water rights, forestry, and public lands under Articles

15    XVII, XVIII, and XX of the Utah Constitution, and powers guaranteed to the state under Article

16    IV, Section 4 and the Tenth Amendment of the United States Constitution, it shall be presumed

17    that the statutes adopted by the Legislature are intended to protect the beneficial use of the natural

18    resources of the state and to preserve or expand tax bases within the boundaries of the state.

19        (2)  Unless clearly indicated to the contrary in the text of a statute, because of governmental

20    duties to protect the rights and responsibilities guaranteed pursuant to Article I, Sections 1, 2, 3,

21    25, and 27 of the Utah Constitution and Article IV, Section 4 and the Tenth Amendment of the

22    United States Constitution, it shall be presumed that the statutes adopted by the Legislature are

23    intended to protect the exercise of inherent and inalienable rights of all residents under the Utah

24    Constitution, fulfill the federally mandated guarantee of a republican form of government, and

25    strengthen families in the state.

HEALTH CARE PROVIDER ABUSIVE WORK                           ENVIRONMENT PROHIBITION ACT                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Stephen E. Sandstrom                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill enacts the Health Care Provider Abusive Work Environment Prohibition Act. Section                  is enacted to read                            CHAPTER   .  HEALTH CARE PROVIDER ABUSIVE WORK ENVIRONMENT                            PROHIBITION ACT                            Part  .  General Provisions                                         .  Title.                               This chapter is known as the  Health Care Provider Abusive Work Environment                          Prohibition Act.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                         .  Definitions.                               As used in this chapter                                     a    Abusive conduct  means conduct                                 i   of an employer or employee in the workplace                                 ii   with malice  and                                iii   that a reasonable person would find is                                 A   hostile                                 B   offensive  and                                   C   unrelated to an employer s legitimate business interests.                                b    Abusive conduct  includes                                 i   repeated infliction of verbal abuse such as the use of a derogatory remark, insult, or                          epithet                                 ii   verbal or physical conduct that a reasonable person would find threatening,                          intimidating, or humiliating  or                                iii   the gratuitous sabotage or undermining of a person s work performance.                                     Abusive work environment  means a workplace where an employee is subjected to                          abusive conduct that is so severe that it causes physical or psychological harm to the employee.                                     Agent  means a person who directs the activities of an employee under a contract                          with the employee s employer.                                     Aggrieved employee  means an employee who brings an action under this chapter                          alleging that the employee is or has been subject to an unlawful employment practice under this                          chapter.                                     Conduct  is a form of behavior, including an act or an omission of an act.                                     Constructive discharge  means abusive conduct that causes an employee to resign                          when before the employee resigns                                 a   the employee brings to the employer s attention the existence of the abusive                          conduct  and                                b   the employer fails to take reasonable steps to eliminate the abusive conduct.                                    a    Employee  means an individual hired by an employer if                                 i   the individual s labor is controlled by the employer  or                                ii   the individual is economically dependent upon the employer in return for labor                          rendered.                                b    Employee  includes                                 i   an independent contractor  or                                ii   a medical intern or resident.                                    a    Employer  means a health care provider who compensates an individual in                          return for performing labor.                                b    Employer  includes a health care provider of                                 i   the state                                    ii   a subdivision of the state                                 iii   a county, city, or town                                 iv   a school district  and                                v   an institution of higher education as defined in Section       B          .                                     Health care provider  means a person or governmental entity that is described as a                           health care provider  in Section       B          .                                      Malice  means the desire to see another person suffer psychological, physical, or                          economic harm, without legitimate cause or justification.                                      Negative employment decision  means                                 a   termination                                  b   constructive discharge                                  c   demotion                                  d   unfavorable reassignment                                  e   refusal to promote  or                                 f   a disciplinary action.                                       Physical harm  means the material impairment of a person s physical health or                           bodily integrity                                  a   as documented by a competent physician  or                                 b   supported by competent expert evidence in a civil action.                                       Psychological harm  means the material impairment of a person s mental health                                  a   as documented by a competent psychologist, psychiatrist, or psychotherapist  or                                 b   supported by competent expert evidence in a civil action.                                       Retaliate  means an adverse action against an employee in a term, privilege, or                           condition of employment.                                       Workplace  means the location at which an employee engages in the labor for                           which the employee is employed.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Scope of chapter.                                This chapter may not be considered to exempt or relieve a person from a liability, duty,                           penalty, or punishment provided by another law of this state.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                Part  .  Abusive Conduct Prohibited                                          .  Abusive conduct prohibited.                                     It is an unlawful employment practice under this chapter for one or more of the                           following to subject an employee to an abusive work environment                                  a   an employer                                  b   an agent of an employer  or                                 c   an employee of an employer.                                     An employer is vicariously liable for an unlawful employment practice in violation                           of this chapter committed by the employer s                                  a   agent  or                                 b   employee.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Retaliation.                                It is an unlawful employment practice under this chapter for a person described in                           Subsection                      to retaliate against an employee because the employee                                      opposes an unlawful employment practice under this chapter  or                                     makes a charge, testifies, assists, or participates in an investigation or proceeding                           under this chapter including                                  a   an internal proceeding                                  b   an arbitration or mediation proceeding  or                                 c   a legal action.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                             Part  .  Private Enforcement                                          .  Private right of action.                                     a   This chapter may be enforced only by a private right of action filed by an                           aggrieved employee against                                  i   an employer                                  ii   an agent of the employer                                  iii   an employee of the employer  or                                 iv   a combination of persons listed in Subsections     a  i  through  iii .                                 b   Civil liability under this chapter is joint and several for a violation committed by                              two or more persons.                                     An aggrieved employee may commence an action under this chapter by filing a                           civil action with a court of competent jurisdiction.                                     An aggrieved employee may not commence an action under this chapter later than                           five years after the day on which occurs the last act that comprises the alleged unlawful                           employment practice.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Factors in determining the existence of abusive conduct.                                     In considering whether abusive conduct is present, a court shall weigh the severity,                           nature, and frequency of the one or more acts that comprise the alleged abusive conduct.                                     A single act normally does not constitute abusive conduct, unless the court finds                           that the act is especially severe and egregious.                                     A court may infer malice from the presence of a factor such as                                  a   an outward expression of hostility                                  b   harmful conduct inconsistent with an employer s legitimate business interests                                  c   a continuation of harmful, illegitimate conduct after the aggrieved employee                                  i   requests that the conduct cease  or                                 ii   demonstrates outward signs of emotional or physical distress in the face of the                           conduct  or                                 d   attempts to exploit an aggrieved employee s known psychological or physical                           vulnerability.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Affirmative defenses.                                     a   It is an affirmative defense to an action for an abusive work environment that                                  i   an employer exercises reasonable care to prevent and promptly correct the abusive                           conduct  and                                 ii   the aggrieved employee unreasonably fails to take advantage of appropriate                           preventive or corrective opportunities provided by the employer.                                 b   The affirmative defense described in Subsection     a  is not available if the                           abusive conduct culminates in a negative employment decision.                                     It is an affirmative defense to an action for an abusive work environment that the                              complaint is grounded primarily on                                  a   a negative employment decision made consistent with an employer s legitimate                           business interests  or                                 b   an employer s reasonable investigation of potentially illegal or unethical activity.                                Section  .    Section                  is enacted to read                             Part  .  Remedies                                          .  Remedies.                                     If a court finds that a person described in Subsection                      has committed                           an unlawful employment practice under this chapter, the court may                                  a   enjoin the person from engaging in the unlawful employment practice  and                                 b   order any other relief that the court considers appropriate, including                                  i   reinstatement                                  ii   removal of the person who engages in the abusive conduct from the aggrieved                           employee s work environment                                  iii   back pay                                  iv   front pay                                  v   medical expenses                                  vi   subject to Subsection    , compensation for emotional distress                                  vii   punitive damages  and                                 viii   attorney fees.                                     a   If an employer is found to have committed an unlawful employment practice                           under this chapter that does not result in a negative employment decision                                  i   the employer s liability for damages for emotional distress may not exceed    ,                               and                                 ii   the employer may not be held liable for punitive damages.                                 b   This Subsection     does not apply to an individual named as a defendant in the                           action who is not an employer.                                Section   .    Section                  is enacted to read                                          .  Relationship to workers  compensation.                                     The remedies in this chapter are in addition to a remedy under Title   A, Chapter  ,                           Workers  Compensation Act, and Chapter  , Utah Occupational Disease Act.                                        a   Notwithstanding Subsection    , an employee who believes that the employee is                           subjected to an unlawful employment practice under this chapter may elect to in lieu of                           bringing an action under this chapter accept workers  compensation benefits in connection with                           the underlying unlawful employment practice to the extent allowed under Title   A, Chapter  ,                           Workers  Compensation Act, and Chapter  , Utah Occupational Disease Act.                                 b   An employee who elects to accept workers  compensation may not bring an action                           under this chapter for the same underlying unlawful employment practice.                                 c   The Labor Commission may make rules in accordance with Title   G, Chapter  ,                           Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, defining for purposes of this Subsection     what                           constitutes an election to accept workers  compensation benefits in connection with an                           underlying unlawful employment practice.

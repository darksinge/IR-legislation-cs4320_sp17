 LONG TITLE
            
7

    
 General Description:
            
8

    
    This bill amends certain provisions related to water conservation plans.
            
9

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
10

    
    This bill:
            
11

    
    .    requires that water conservation plans contain existing and proposed water
            
12

    
conservation measures;
            
13

    
    .    requires that water conservation plans contain an evaluation of the extent to which a
            
14

    
retail provider will use certain measures to achieve its conservation goals;
            
15

    
    .    requires that water conservation plans contain a clearly stated water use reduction
            
16

    
goal and implementation plan for each conservation measure, including a timeline
            
17

    
for action and an evaluation process to measure progress; and
            
18

    
    .    requires that the Board of Water Resources' report be presented to the interim
            
19

    
committee at its November 2004 meeting.
            
20

21

    
    None

 Other Special Clauses:

    None

     73-10-32.  Definitions -- Water conservation plan required.

    (1)  As used in this section:

    (a)  "Board" means the Board of Water Resources created under Section 

73-10-1.5

.

73-10-18

.

    (c)  "Retail" means the level of distribution of culinary water that supplies culinary
            
35

    
water directly to the end user.
            
36

    
    (d)  "Retail water provider" means a person who:
            
37

    
    (i)  supplies culinary water to end users; and
            
38

    
    (ii)  has more than 500 service connections.
            
39

    
    (e) (i)  "Water conservation plan" means a written document that contains [ideas,
            
40

    
suggestions, or recommendations as to] existing and proposed water conservation measures
            
41

    
describing what [can] will be done by state and local governments, retail water providers, and
            
42

    
the end user of culinary water to help conserve water and limit or reduce its use in the state in
            
43

    
terms of per capita consumption so that adequate supplies of water are available for future
            
44

    
needs.
            
45

    
    (ii)  Each "water conservation plan" shall contain [recommendations for] water saving
            
46

    
measures that [may] shall include an evaluation of the extent to which the retail water provider
            
47

    
will utilize the following measures to achieve its conservation goals:
            
48

    
    (A)  the installation and use of water efficient fixtures and appliances, including toilets,
            
49

50

    
    (B)  residential and commercial landscapes and irrigation that require less water to

52

    
    (C)  more water efficient industrial and commercial processes involving the use of
            
53

    
water;
            
54

    
    (D)  water reuse systems, both potable and not potable;
            
55

    
    (E)  distribution system leak repair;
            
56

    
    (F)  dissemination of public information regarding more efficient use of water,
            
57

    
including public education programs, customer water use audits, and water saving
            
58

    
demonstrations;

            
59


    
    (G)  water rate structures designed to encourage more efficient use of water;
            
60

    
    (H)  statutes, ordinances, codes, or regulations designed to encourage more efficient use
            
61

    
of water by means such as water efficient fixtures and landscapes;
            
62

    
    (I)  incentives to implement water efficient techniques, including rebates to water users
            
63

    
to encourage the implementation of more water efficient measures; [and]

    (J)  a clearly stated water use reduction goal and an implementation plan for each

    [(J)] (K)  other measures designed to conserve water.

78

79

    
notice.
            
80

81

districts and the retail water providers;

    (ii)  develop recommendations on how to implement those plans; and
            
84

    
    (iii)  report its recommendations to the Natural Resources, Agriculture, and
            
85

    
Environment Interim Committee of the Legislature at its meeting in November [1999] 2004.

    (b)  The board's report to the Natural Resources, Agriculture, and Environment Interim
            
87

    
Committee [may] shall include a [recommendation] provision:

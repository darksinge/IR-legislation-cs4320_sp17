TRAFFIC CHECKPOINT AMENDMENTS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Nora B. Stephens          AN ACT RELATING TO CRIMINAL PROCEDURE  ELIMINATING REQUIRING         MAGISTRATE AUTHORITY TO ESTABLISH ADMINISTRATIVE TRAFFIC         CHECKPOINTS  AND AMENDING THE REQUIREMENTS FOR ADMINISTRATIVE         TRAFFIC CHECKPOINT PLANS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           AMENDS                         , as enacted by Chapter   , Laws of Utah                             , as enacted by Chapter   , Laws of Utah                             , as enacted by Chapter   , Laws of Utah                Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section            is amended to read                         .  Circumstances permitting an administrative traffic checkpoint.              A motor vehicle may be stopped and the occupants detained by an enforcement officer          when the enforcement officer                    is acting pursuant to a duly authorized search warrant or arrest warrant                    has probable cause to arrest or search                    has reasonable suspicion that criminal activity has occurred or is occurring                    is acting under emergency circumstances  or                   is acting pursuant to duly authorized administrative traffic checkpoint authority           granted by a magistrate  in accordance with Section          .              Section  .  Section            is amended to read                         .  Written plan    Approval of commissioner of public safety.                    An administrative traffic checkpoint may be established and operated upon written          authority of a magistrate.                    A magistrate       The commissioner of public safety or the commissioner s delegate         may issue written authority to establish and operate an administrative traffic checkpoint if               a    a  the command level officer submits to the  magistrate  commissioner of public safety         or the delegate a written plan signed by the command level officer describing               i   the location of the checkpoint including geographical and topographical information               ii   the date, time, and duration of the checkpoint               iii   the sequence of traffic to be stopped               iv   the purpose of the checkpoint, including the inspection or inquiry to be conducted                v   the  names  number of  the  personnel to be employed in operating the checkpoint,          including the  name  rank of the officer or officers in charge at the scene                vi   the configuration and location of signs, barriers, and other means of informing          approaching motorists that they must stop and directing them to the place to stop                vii   any advance notice to the public at large of the establishment of the checkpoint  and               viii   the instructions to be given to the enforcement officers operating the checkpoint  and               b   the  magistrate  commissioner of public safety or the delegate makes an independent           judicial  determination that the plan appropriately                i   minimizes the length of time the motorist will be delayed                ii   minimizes the intrusion of the inspection or inquiry                iii   minimizes the fear and anxiety the motorist will experience                iv   minimizes the degree of discretion to be exercised by the individual enforcement          officers operating the checkpoint  and               v   maximizes the safety of the motorist and the enforcement officers.                         Upon determination by the  magistrate  commissioner of public safety or the          delegate that the plan meets the requirements of Subsection           b , the  magistrate           commissioner of public safety or the delegate shall sign the authorization and issue it to the          command level officer, retaining a copy for the  court s  Department of Public Safety s file.                         A copy of the plan and signed authorization shall be  issued to  kept by the          checkpoint command level officer participating in the operation of the checkpoint.                         Any enforcement officer participating in the operation of the checkpoint shall          conform his activities as nearly as practicable to the procedures outlined in the plan.                                The checkpoint command level officer shall be available to exhibit a copy of the         plan and signed authorization to any motorist who has been stopped at the checkpoint upon request         of the motorist.             Section  .  Section            is amended to read                        .  Failure to stop    Criminal liability.             Any person who intentionally and knowingly passes, without stopping as required, any         administrative traffic checkpoint operated  under the authority of a magistrate as provided  in         accordance with Section           is guilty of a class B misdemeanor.

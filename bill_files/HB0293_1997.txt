MAGISTRATE AUTHORITY                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Byron L. Harward          AN ACT RELATING TO JUDICIAL CODE  LIMITING SEARCH WARRANT AUTHORITY         TO THE JUDICIAL DISTRICT  CLARIFYING THAT ARREST WARRANT AUTHORITY         IS STATEWIDE  AND MAKING TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          AMENDS                       . , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section         .  is amended to read                       . .  Authority of magistrate.                   Except as otherwise provided by law, a magistrate as defined in Section        shall          have the authority to                a   commit a person to incarceration prior to trial                b   set or deny bail under Section         and release upon the payment of bail and          satisfaction of any other conditions of release                c   issue to any place in the state summonses and warrants of  search and  arrest                d   authorize administrative traffic checkpoints under Section           and issue search          warrants within the judicial district                 d    e   conduct an initial appearance in a felony                 e    f   conduct arraignments                 f    g   conduct a preliminary examination to determine probable cause                 g    h   appoint attorneys and order recoupment of attorney fees                 h    i   order the preparation of presentence investigations and reports   and                 i    j   issue temporary orders as provided by rule of the Judicial Council  and               k   perform any other act or function authorized by statute.                  A judge of the justice court may exercise the authority of a magistrate specified in         Subsection     with the following limitations               a   a judge of the justice court may conduct an initial appearance, preliminary examination,         or arraignment in a felony case as provided by rule of the Judicial Council  and              b   a judge of the justice court may not set bail in a capital or first degree felony nor deny         bail in any case.      Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

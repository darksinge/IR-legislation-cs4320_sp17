EMERGENCY INJECTIONS FOR           ANAPHYLACTIC REACTION                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Margaret Dayton          AN ACT RELATING TO HEALTH  PROVIDING FOR TRAINING OF SCHOOL         PERSONNEL IN THE USE OF AN AUTO INJECTOR FOR EMERGENCY         ADMINISTRATION OF MEDICATION IN CASE OF ANAPHYLACTIC SHOCK  AND         PROVIDING IMMUNITY FOR LIABILITY.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           ENACTS                       , Utah Code Annotated                           , Utah Code Annotated                           , Utah Code Annotated                           , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section          is enacted to read            CHAPTER   .  ADVERSE REACTION EMERGENCY TREATMENT ACT                       .  Title.              This chapter is known as the  Adverse Reaction Emergency Treatment Act.               Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  Definitions.              As used in this chapter                     Adverse reaction  means a life threatening physical reaction often referred to as          anaphylaxis.               a   Symptoms of an adverse reaction include asthma, wheezing, difficulty breathing,          difficulty talking or swallowing, hives, itching, shock, or abdominal pain.               b   Causes of an adverse reaction may include insect sting, food allergy, drug reaction, or         exercise.                   Auto injector  means a disposable drug delivery system with a spring activated         concealed needle that is designed for emergency self administration of epinephrine to provide         rapid, convenient first aid for persons sensitive to potentially fatal adverse reactions.             Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                      .  School personnel use of auto injector    Training.             Each primary and secondary school in the state, both public and private, may designate one         or more school personnel to receive training from the school nurse or other qualified person          regarding the emergency administration of an auto injector and may maintain auto injectors at the          school.  The training shall include                    techniques for recognizing symptoms of adverse reactions                    standards and procedures for management and administration of an auto injector  and                   emergency procedures, including calling the emergency     number, contacting the          student s parents or doctor if this information is available, and timing the adverse reaction.              Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  Immunity from liability.                   A person who has received training under Section         is not liable in any civil or          criminal action for action taken or not taken under the authority of this chapter regarding an          emergency adverse reaction, if the person acts in good faith.                   a   A person who has been trained under Section         and who is acting in good          faith is not subject to Section   A       , regarding administration of an auto injector to a          student, unless the parent or guardian of the student has filed a written request with the school          regarding the administration of or prohibition of administration of medication, and the person          administering an auto injector under this chapter has been advised by the school administrator in          writing of this request.               b   It is the responsibility of the school administrator to ensure staff at the school who          receive training under Section         are advised of any student regarding whom authorization          for use of an auto injector has been submitted under Section   A        or regarding whom any          written request under Subsection     a  has been submitted to the school.

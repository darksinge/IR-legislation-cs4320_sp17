 LONG TITLE
            
9

    
 Committee Note:
            
10

    
    The Natural Resources, Agriculture, and Environment Interim Committee
            
11

    
recommended this bill.
            
12

    
 General Description:
            
13

    
    This bill eliminates the presumption that agricultural operations are conducted in
            
14

    
accordance with sound agricultural practices and provides that agricultural operations
            
15

    
are not nuisances.
            
16

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
17

    
    This bill:
            
18

    
    .    states that agricultural activity is not a nuisance if conducted in accordance with
            
19

    
sound agricultural practices.
            
20

    
 Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
            
21

    
    None
            
22

    
 Other Special Clauses:
            
23

    
    None
            
24

    
 Utah Code Sections Affected:
            
25

    
AMENDS:
            
26

    
     76-10-803, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2008, Chapter 3
            
27

    
     78B-6-1101, as renumbered and amended by Laws of Utah 2008, Chapter 3

            
28


    
     78B-6-1104, as renumbered and amended by Laws of Utah 2008, Chapter 3
            
29

    

            
30

    
Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
            
31

    
    Section 1.  
Section  
76-10-803

 is amended to read:
            
32

    
     76-10-803.  "Public nuisance" defined -- Agricultural operations.
            
33

    
    (1)  A public nuisance is a crime against the order and economy of the state and consists
            
34

    
in unlawfully doing any act or omitting to perform any duty, which act or omission:
            
35

    
    (a)  annoys, injures, or endangers the comfort, repose, health, or safety of three or more
            
36

    
persons;
            
37

    
    (b)  offends public decency;
            
38

    
    (c)  unlawfully interferes with, obstructs, or tends to obstruct, or renders dangerous for
            
39

    
passage, any lake, stream, canal, or basin, or any public park, square, street, or highway;
            
40

    
    (d)  is a nuisance as defined in Section 

78B-6-1107

; or
            
41

    
    (e)  in any way renders three or more persons insecure in life or the use of property.
            
42

    
    (2)  An act which affects three or more persons in any of the ways specified in this
            
43

    
section is still a nuisance regardless of the extent to which the annoyance or damage inflicted
            
44

    
on individuals is unequal.
            
45

    
    (3) (a)  [Agricultural operations that are consistent] Activities conducted in the normal
            
46

    
and ordinary course of agricultural operations, as defined in Subsection 

78B-6-1101

(7), or
            
47

    
conducted in accordance with sound agricultural practices are presumed to be reasonable and
            
48

    
do not constitute a public nuisance under Subsection (1) [unless the agricultural operation has a
            
49

    
substantial adverse effect on the public health and safety].
            
50

    
    (b)  Agricultural operations undertaken in conformity with federal, state, and local laws
            
51

    
and regulations, including zoning ordinances, are presumed to be operating within sound
            
52

    
agricultural practices.
            
53

    
    Section 2.  
Section  
78B-6-1101

 is amended to read:
            
54

    
     78B-6-1101.  Definitions -- Nuisance -- Right of action -- Judgment.
            
55

    
    (1)  A nuisance is anything which is injurious to health, indecent, offensive to the
            
56

    
senses, or an obstruction to the free use of property, so as to interfere with the comfortable
            
57

    
enjoyment of life or property.  A nuisance may be the subject of an action.
            
58

    
    (2)  A nuisance may include the following:

            
59


    
    (a)  drug houses and drug dealing as provided in Section 

78B-6-1107

;
            
60

    
    (b)  gambling as provided in Title 76, Chapter 10, Part 11;
            
61

    
    (c)  criminal activity committed in concert with two or more persons as provided in
            
62

    
Section 

76-3-203.1

;
            
63

    
    (d)  party houses which frequently create conditions defined in Subsection (1); and
            
64

    
    (e)  prostitution as provided in Title 76, Chapter 10, Part 13.
            
65

    
    (3)  A nuisance under this part includes tobacco smoke that drifts into any residential
            
66

    
unit a person rents, leases, or owns, from another residential or commercial unit and the smoke:
            
67

    
    (a)  drifts in more than once in each of two or more consecutive seven-day periods; and
            
68

    
    (b)  creates any of the conditions under Subsection (1).
            
69

    
    (4)  Subsection (3) does not apply to:
            
70

    
    (a)  residential rental units available for temporary rental, such as for vacations, or
            
71

    
available for only 30 or fewer days at a time; or
            
72

    
    (b)  hotel or motel rooms.
            
73

    
    (5)  Subsection (3) does not apply to any unit that is part of a timeshare development, as
            
74

    
defined in Section 

57-19-2

, or subject to a timeshare interest as defined in Section 

57-19-2

.
            
75

    
    (6)  An action may be brought by any person whose property is injuriously affected, or
            
76

    
whose personal enjoyment is lessened by the nuisance.
            
77

    
    (7)  "Agricultural operation" means any [facility for the] activity engaged in the
            
78

    
commercial production [for commercial purposes] of crops, orchards, aquaculture, livestock,
            
79

    
poultry, livestock products, [or] poultry products, and the facilities, equipment, and property
            
80

    
used to facilitate the activity.
            
81

    
    (8)  "Manufacturing facility" means any factory, plant, or other facility including its
            
82

    
appurtenances, where the form of raw materials, processed materials, commodities, or other
            
83

    
physical objects is converted or otherwise changed into other materials, commodities, or
            
84

    
physical objects or where such materials, commodities, or physical objects are combined to
            
85

    
form a new material, commodity, or physical object.
            
86

    
    Section 3.  
Section  
78B-6-1104

 is amended to read:
            
87

    
     78B-6-1104.  Agricultural operations -- Nuisance liability.
            
88

    
    (1)  [Agricultural operations that are consistent] Activities conducted in the normal and
            
89

    
ordinary course of agricultural operations or conducted in accordance with sound agricultural

            
90


    
practices are presumed to be reasonable and do not constitute a nuisance [unless the
            
91

    
agricultural operation has a substantial adverse effect on the public health and safety].
            
92

    
    (2)  Agricultural operations undertaken in conformity with federal, state, and local laws
            
93

    
and regulations, including zoning ordinances, are presumed to be operating within sound
            
94

    
agricultural practices.

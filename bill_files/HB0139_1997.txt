Allen          AN ACT RELATING TO OCCUPATIONS AND PROFESSIONS  REQUIRING AN         EMPLOYEE LEASING COMPANY TO SUBMIT AN ANNUAL CERTIFIED AUDIT AND         PROVIDE CERTAIN INFORMATION  ALLOWING THE DEPARTMENT OF         COMMERCE TO SEEK JUDICIAL INTERVENTION IF THE PUBLIC INTEREST IS          THREATENED  MAKING EMPLOYEE LEASING COMPANIES LEGALLY          RESPONSIBLE FOR WORKERS  COMPENSATION  REQUIRING A CLIENT COMPANY          TO BE NAMED AS AN ADDITIONAL INSURED ON A WORKERS  COMPENSATION          POLICY  REQUIRING CERTAIN STATE AGENCIES TO INFORM THE DEPARTMENT          OF COMMERCE IF AN EMPLOYEE LEASING COMPANY FAILS TO COMPLY WITH          CERTAIN LEGAL REQUIREMENTS  MAKING TECHNICAL AND CONFORMING          AMENDMENTS  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE. Section  .  Section            is amended to read                        .  Definitions.             In addition to the definitions in Section         , as used in this chapter                    Board  means the Employee Leasing Company  Licensing  Board created in Section                  .                   Client company  means a person or entity that leases any or all of its permanent         employees from an employee leasing company.                   Employee leasing company  or  leasing company  means an individual or business          that, under an agreement between the client company and the leasing company ,  and for a fee ,                 a   places all or substantially all of the regular, full time employees of the client company          on the leasing company s payroll  and                 b   leases  them  the employees to the client company on an ongoing basis with no          restriction or limitation on the duration of employment .   and               c   receives funds from a client company or leasing company employees from which the          leasing company is obligated to pay taxes, insurance, or benefits on behalf of the employees.                    Employment agreement  means the written agreement between an employee leasing          company and each of its employees who are employed for the purpose of being leased as          permanent employees to client companies.                    Engage in practice as an employee leasing company  means to hold oneself out as an          employee leasing company, to lease an employee to another person, and to receive any          consideration for providing employee leasing services or to expect payment of any consideration          for providing employee leasing services.                   a    Financial responsibility  means a demonstration of a current and expected future          condition of financial solvency evidencing a reasonable expectation to the board that an applicant          or licensee can successfully engage in business as an employee leasing company without          jeopardizing                i   the interests of the employees of the leasing company who are leased to a client          company                ii   the interests of the client company  and               iii   the interests of the public.                      b   Financial responsibility may be determined by an evaluation of the total history         concerning the licensee or applicant for licensure, including past, present, and expected condition         and record of financial solvency and business conduct.                   Lease agreement  means the written agreement between an employee leasing         company and a client company in accordance with which the leasing company leases employees         to the client company and the client company leases individuals from the leasing company.                   Regular employee  means an individual who is an employee of an employee leasing         company for the purpose of being placed by the employee leasing company as a regular full time         or regular part time employee of a client company.                    Represent oneself as an employee leasing company  means to hold oneself out by any          means as an employee leasing company.                     Temporary employee,  as may be further defined by rule, means an individual who          is an employee of, registered for temporary assignment by, or otherwise associated with a          temporary help company that engages in the assignment of individuals as temporary full time or          part time personnel to fill assignments with a finite ending date to another independent entity.                     Temporary help company,  as may be further defined by rule, means a person or          entity that provides temporary employees to fill assignments with a finite ending date to another          independent entity in special, unusual, seasonal, or temporary skill shortage situations. Section  .  Section            is amended to read                         .  Financial responsibility.                    Each applicant for licensure as  To maintain a license in good standing, an employee          leasing company , and each licensee applying for renewal of a license as an employee leasing          company,  shall demonstrate financial responsibility  as a condition precedent to licensure or          renewal of license.  The burden to demonstrate financial responsibility is upon the applicant or          licensee .                    If there is presented to the division and the board sufficient information to cause a          reasonable belief that the financial responsibility of a licensee is impaired to the extent that there                 is a threat to the public interest, the division or the board may order the licensee to provide current         audited financial information prepared by an independent certified public accountant and other         supporting information upon which to make a competent evaluation of the licensee s financial         responsibility.                         Financial responsibility shall be demonstrated on               a   a quarterly basis by providing the division and the board with               i   evidence from an independent certified public  accounting firm  accountant that all         federal, state, and local withholding taxes, unemployment taxes, FICA taxes, workers          compensation premium, and employee benefit plan premiums have                  Upon a showing of reasonable cause, the division and board may inquire into the          financial responsibility of the corporation s officers and directors as a factor in determining the          corporation s financial responsibility.                b   an annual basis by providing the division and the board with                i   a certified audit performed by an independent certified accountant that all federal, state,          and local withholding taxes, unemployment taxes, FICA taxes, workers  compensation premium,          and employee benefit plan premiums have been paid  and               ii   any other relevant information the division or board considers appropriate.                   a   In addition to quarterly evidence or an annual audit

FUGITIVE BAIL AMENDMENTS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Bryan D. Holladay          AN ACT RELATING TO THE CODE OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE  PROVIDING         PRESUMPTIONS REGARDING THE SETTING OF BAIL  AND SETTING STANDARDS         FOR SETTING BAIL IN FUGITIVE PROCEEDINGS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          AMENDS                        , as last amended by Chapter  , Laws of Utah     , Second Special Session          Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Amount of bail.               Unless the prisoner is not entitled to bail under Section                            Except as provided in Subsection    , a judge or magistrate in this state may admit the          person arrested to bail by bond with sufficient sureties and in an amount he considers proper,          conditioned for his appearance before him at a time specified in the bond and for his surrender, to          be arrested upon the warrant of the governor of this state.                   A person arrested under Section          shall be admitted to bail as a matter of right,          except the court has discretion to deny bail as provided in Utah Constitution Article I, Section  ,          and when a judge or magistrate in the demanding state has ordered that the person charged be held          without bail or the person has waived extradition.                   There is a rebuttable presumption that the bail set by the court or magistrate in the          demanding state is the proper amount of bail in this state.      Legislative Review Note      as of                 AM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

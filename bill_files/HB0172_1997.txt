CHILDREN AND YOUTH TASK FORCE           REAUTHORIZATION                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Beverly Ann Evans          Lloyd W. Frandsen             AN ACT RELATING TO CHILDREN  CREATING THE CHILDREN AND YOUTH  TASK         FORCE  PROVIDING FOR MEMBERSHIP  DELINEATING RESPONSIBILITIES AND         PROCEDURES  PROVIDING A REPORTING DATE  APPROPRIATING    ,    FROM          THE GENERAL FUND  AND PROVIDING A REPEAL DATE.          This act enacts uncodified material.          Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .   Children and Youth Task Force     Creation    Membership    Quorum              Staff.                   There is created the Children and Youth Task Force consisting of the following             members                a   four members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate, no more than          three of whom may be from the same political party                b   four members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the House          of Representatives, no more than three of whom may be from the same political party                c   the chair of the Families, Agencies, and Communities Together Steering Committee          appointed under Subsection             and               d   the following members jointly appointed by the president of the Senate and the Speaker          of the House of Representatives                i   four persons who are members of  county governing bodies, at least one of whom shall          represent a rural area of the state  and               ii   two persons who are mayors of a city or town, at least one of whom shall represent a         rural area of the state.                  a   The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed under         Subsection     a  as a cochair of the task force.              b   The speaker of the House of Representatives shall designate a member of the House         of Representatives appointed under Subsection     b  as a cochair of the task force.                  A majority of the members of the task force constitute a quorum.  The action of a         majority of a quorum constitutes the action of the task force.                  a   Salaries and expenses of the members of the task force who are legislators shall be          paid in accordance with Section        and Legislative Joint Rule   .  .               b   Members of the task force who are not legislators may not receive compensation for          their work associated with the task force.                   The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support          to the task force.              Section  .   Duties    Interim report.                   The task force shall review and make recommendations on the following issues          regarding children, youth, and their families who experience problems due to poverty, dependency,          disability, abuse, neglect, or low educational achievement                a   ways to revise government policies that inadvertently encourage or lead to the          dissolution of or prevent the formation of two parent families                b   a process to systematically determine the necessity and effectiveness of programs and          to eliminate ineffective or obsolete programs to ensure accountability for tax dollars                c   ways to reallocate funds to programs that are committed to collaborative efforts and          systems change                d   a description of a management information system that allows the appropriate flow of          information between service delivery agencies while ensuring the  privacy of families who receive          services                e   implementation of collaborative programs for these children and their families created          under Title   , Chapter                   f   involvement of local governments, including ways to facilitate increased support of          these children and their families by local governments                       g   budgeting processes that promote collaboration between service agencies at both the         state and local level, including an assessment of all state and local funding expended on these         children and their families               h   a process to document emerging problems facing at risk children, youth, and families         and a method to convey these problems and possible responses to policy makers               i   developing program outcomes and reviewing the effectiveness of programs that serve         at risk children and youth and their families  and              j   other relevant issues regarding at risk children and youth and their families that the task         force considers appropriate.                   The task force shall periodically report on its activities to the Families, Agencies, and          Communities Together Steering Committee.                   A final report, including any proposed legislation, shall be presented to the Education,          Health and Environmental Quality, Human Services, and Judiciary Interim Committees before          November   ,     .              Section  .   Appropriation.              There is appropriated from the General Fund for fiscal year                              ,    to the Senate to pay for the compensation and expenses of the senators on the          task force                      ,    to the House of Representatives to pay for the compensation and expenses of          the representatives on the task force  and                      ,    to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel to pay for staffing          the task force.              Section  .   Repeal date.              This act is repealed November   ,     .      Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

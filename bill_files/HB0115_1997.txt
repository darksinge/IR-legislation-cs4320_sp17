Section  .  Section          is amended to read                      .  Purposes.              Posted hunting  Cooperative wildlife management units are established to                   provide income to landowners                   create satisfying hunting opportunities                   increase wildlife resources                   provide adequate protection to landowners who open their lands for hunting  and                   provide access to public and private lands for hunting  big game .              Section  .  Section          is amended to read                       .  Definitions.              As used in this chapter                            Posted hunting  Cooperative wildlife management unit  or  unit  means a          generally contiguous area of  private  land  open for hunting small game, waterfowl, or big game           by permit  which is registered in accordance with this chapter and rules of the Wildlife Board.                         a     Posted hunting  Cooperative wildlife management unit agent  means a          person appointed by a landowner  or , landowner association, or landowner association operator          to perform the functions described in Section        .               b   For purposes of this chapter, a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit          agent may not                 a    i   be appointed by the division or the state                 b    ii   be an employee or agent of the division or the state                 c    iii   receive compensation from the division or the state  or                d    iv   act as a peace officer or perform any duties of a peace officer without qualifying          as a peace officer under Title   , Chapter  a, Peace Officer Designation.                           Posted hunting  Cooperative wildlife management unit  permit  authorization           means a card, label, ticket, or other identifying document authorizing the possessor to hunt small          game or waterfowl in a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit.                    Cooperative wildlife management unit permit  means a permit authorizing the          possessor to hunt big game in a cooperative wildlife management unit.                                 Division  means the Division of Wildlife Resources.                    General public  includes all persons except                 a   a landowner association member and a spouse or dependent child of a landowner         association member  and                b   any person who pays a fee, in addition to a permit fee, to hunt on a posted hunting         unit.                          Landowner association  means a landowner or an organization of owners of         private lands  or its designated agent that  who operates a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife         management unit.                   a    Landowner association operator  means a person designated by a landowner          association to operate the cooperative wildlife management unit.               b   For purposes of this chapter, a landowner association operator may not                i   be appointed by the division                ii   be an employee or agent of the division  or               iii   receive compensation from the division.              Section  .  Section          is amended to read                       .  Rulemaking authority of Wildlife Board.                The Wildlife Board is authorized to make and enforce rules applicable to  posted hunting           cooperative wildlife management units organized for the hunting of small game, waterfowl, or big          game that in its judgment are necessary to administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter.              Section  .  Section          is amended to read                       .  Operation by landowner association.              A landowner association shall operate a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management          unit as prescribed by this chapter and the rules of the Wildlife Board.              Section  .  Section          is amended to read                       .  Certificate of registration    Renewal.                   A landowner association may not establish or operate a  posted hunting  cooperative          wildlife management unit without first obtaining a  posted hunting unit  certificate of registration          from the Wildlife Board.                    The Wildlife Board may renew annually certificates of registration if the landowner          association has previously complied with this chapter and the rules of the Wildlife Board.                     Section  .  Section          is repealed and reenacted to read                      .  Season dates    Boundaries.                  The Wildlife Board shall establish season dates and boundaries for each cooperative         wildlife management unit.                  Season dates may differ from general statewide season dates.                  No hunting season may exceed    days.             Section  .  Section          is amended to read                      .  Permits    Acreage and lands that may be included    Posting of boundaries.                  The division shall provide  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit           permits  authorizations for hunting small game or waterfowl to the  posted hunting  cooperative          wildlife management unit, free of charge.                   At least     of the  permits  cooperative wildlife management unit authorizations for          hunting small game or waterfowl provided to a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management          unit shall be offered for sale to the general public at the times and places designated on the          application for a certificate of registration.                   a   Cooperative wildlife management units organized for hunting small game or          waterfowl shall consist of private land.               b   At least     of the acreage within the boundaries of each  posted hunting  cooperative          wildlife management unit organized for the hunting of small game or waterfowl shall be open to          hunting by holders of valid  permits  authorizations.                   a   The division may issue  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit          permits for hunting big game to permittees                 a    i   qualifying through a public drawing  or                b    ii   named by the  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit operator.               b   The Wildlife Board may specify by rule those persons who are eligible to draw a          cooperative wildlife management unit permit in a public drawing.                   a   Cooperative wildlife management units organized for hunting big game shall          consist of private land to the extent practicable.  Public land may be included within a cooperative          wildlife management unit if                i   the public land is completely surrounded by private land or is otherwise inaccessible          to the general public                       ii   including public land is necessary to establish a readily identifiable boundary  or              iii   including public land is necessary to achieve big game management objectives.              b   If any public land is included within a cooperative wildlife management unit               i   the landowner association shall meet applicable federal or state land use requirements          and              ii   the Wildlife Board shall increase the number of permits or hunting opportunities made         available to the general public to reflect the proportion of public lands to private lands within the         cooperative wildlife management unit.                        Each landowner association shall                a   clearly post all boundaries of the unit and all corners, roads, trails, gates, and          rights of way entering the unit with signs provided by the division  and               b   provide a written copy of its guidelines to each  permittee  holder of an authorization          or permit.              Section  .  Section         .  is enacted to read                       . .  Landowner association to provide comparable hunting opportunities.              A landowner association shall provide each holder of an authorization or permit a          comparable hunting opportunity in terms of hunting area and number of days.              Section   .  Section          is amended to read                       .  Compensation for damage    Claims.              A landowner participating in a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit who          incurs damages caused by a hunter on his or her land may submit a claim and receive          compensation for the claim from money received for cooperative wildlife management unit          authorization or permit fees collected by the landowner association.                   These claims shall                      a   be paid first and have priority over all other obligations of the landowner          association                      b   be reviewed, investigated, and paid by  a board of claims selected and authorized          by  the landowner association  and                     c   not exceed annual revenues of a unit.                   A landowner participating in a cooperative wildlife management unit who incurs          damages caused by a hunter on his or her land may not hold the state liable for compensation.                     Section   .  Section          is amended to read                      .  Agents    Appointment    Identification    Refusal of entry by agent.                  A landowner association may appoint  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife         management unit agents to protect private property of the  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife         management unit.                  Each  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit agent shall wear or have         in his or her possession a form of identification prescribed by the Wildlife Board which indicates         he or she is a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management  unit agent.                  A  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit agent may refuse entry into          private lands within a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit to any person, except           owners  an owner of land within the unit and  their  his or her employees, who                a   does not have in his or her possession a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife          management unit authorization or permit                b   endangers or has endangered human safety                c   damages or has damaged private property within a  posted hunting  cooperative          wildlife management unit  or               d   fails or has failed to comply with reasonable rules of a landowner association.                   In performing the functions described in this section, a  posted hunting  cooperative          wildlife management unit agent shall comply with the relevant laws of this state.              Section   .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Possession of permits and licenses by hunter    Restrictions.                   A person may not hunt in a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit          without having in his or her possession                a   a valid  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit authorization or permit           and               b   the necessary hunting licenses, tags, and stamps.                   A  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit authorization or permit                a   entitles the holder to hunt only in the unit specified on the authorization or permit          pursuant to rules and proclamations of the Wildlife Board and does not entitle the holder to hunt          on any other private or public land  and               b   constitutes written permission for trespass as required under Section         .                     Section   .  Section           is amended to read                       .  Failure to comply with rules and requirements.             A person must leave  a posted hunting  private property within a cooperative wildlife         management unit immediately, upon request of a landowner, landowner association operator, or          posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit agent, if that person                   does not have in his or her possession a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife         management unit authorization or permit                   endangers or has endangered human safety                   damages or has damaged private property within a  posted hunting  cooperative          wildlife management unit  or                   fails or has failed to comply with reasonable rules of a landowner association.              Section   .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Landowner protection under Landowner Liability Act.              Landowners who participate in  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management units          shall have the full protection afforded under Title   , Chapter   ,  the  Limitation of Landowner          Liability  Act   Public Recreation.              Section   .  Section          is amended to read                       .  Liability not limited where willful or malicious conduct involved or           admission fee charged.                   Nothing in this act shall limit any liability which otherwise exists for                a   willful or malicious failure to guard or warn against a dangerous condition, use,          structure, or activity                b   deliberate, willful, or malicious injury to persons or property  or               c   an injury suffered where the owner of land charges a person to enter or go on the land          or use the land for any recreational purpose, except that where land is leased to the state or a          subdivision of the state, any consideration received by the owner for the lease is not a charge          within the meaning of this section.                   Any person who hunts upon a  posted hunting  cooperative wildlife management unit,          as authorized by Title   , Chapter   , Cooperative Wildlife Management Units is not considered          to have paid a fee within the meaning of this section.

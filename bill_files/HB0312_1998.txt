1     WEAPONS IN MOTOR VEHICLES




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: Richard L. Walsh


5    AN ACT RELATING TO CRIMINAL CODE; CLARIFYING THAT THE EXEMPTION OF A

6    CONCEALED FIREARM PERMIT HOLDER HAS FROM CERTAIN PROVISIONS IS FOR

7    A HANDGUN AND DOES NOT EXTEND TO OTHER FIREARMS.

8    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

9    AMENDS:

10         76-10-523, as last amended by Chapters 57 and 144, Laws of Utah 1997

11    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

12        Section 1.  Section  76-10-523 is amended to read:

13         76-10-523.  Persons exempt from weapons laws.

14        (1)  This part and Title 53, Chapter 5, Part 7, Concealed Weapon Act, do not apply to any

15    of the following:

16        (a)  a United States marshal while engaged in the performance of his official duties;

17        (b)  a federal official required to carry a firearm while engaged in the performance of his

18    official duties;

19        (c)  a peace officer of this or any other jurisdiction while engaged in the performance of

20    his official duties;

21        (d)  a law enforcement official as defined and qualified under Section 53-5-711;

22        (e)  a judge as defined and qualified in Section 53-5-711;

23        (f)  a common carrier while engaged in the regular and ordinary transport of firearms as

24    merchandise; or

25        (g)  a nonresident traveling in or through the state, provided that any firearm is:

26        (i)  unloaded; and

27        (ii) securely encased as defined in Section 76-10-501.


1        (2)  [The provisions of Subsections 76-10-504(1)(a), (1)(b), and Section 76-10-505 do not

2    apply to any] Any person [to whom a permit] licensed to carry a concealed firearm [has been

3    issued: (a)  pursuant to Section 53-5-704;] under Title 53, Chapter 5, Part 7, Concealed Weapon

4    Act, or [(b)]  by another state whose requirements for issuance of a concealed firearm permit have

5    been determined annually by the Department of Public Safety to meet or exceed the requirements

6    for issuance of a concealed firearm permit in this state[.] is exempt from the following provisions

7    if the firearm is a handgun:

8        (a)  carrying a concealed weapon in Subsections 76-10-504(1)(a) and (1)(b); and

9        (b)  carrying a loaded firearm in Section 76-10-505.

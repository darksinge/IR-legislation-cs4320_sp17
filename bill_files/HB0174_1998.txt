H.B. 174









1     WINDOW TINTING AMENDMENTS




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: Don E. Bush


5    AN ACT RELATING TO MOTOR VEHICLES; AMENDING CERTAIN MOTOR VEHICLE

6    WINDSHIELD AND WINDOW TINTING PROVISIONS; AND PROVIDING AN

7    EFFECTIVE DATE.

8    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

9    AMENDS:

10         41-6-149, as last amended by Chapter 39, Laws of Utah 1994

11    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

12        Section 1.  Section  41-6-149 is amended to read:

13         41-6-149.  Windshields and windows -- Obstructions reducing visibility -- Wipers --

14     Prohibitions.  

15        (1)  Except as provided in Subsections (2) and (3), a person may not operate a motor

16    vehicle with:

17        (a)  a windshield that allows less than 70% light transmittance;

18        (b)  a front side window that allows less than 43% light transmittance;

19        (c)  any window, other than the windshield or a front side window, that allows less than

20    28% light transmittance;

21        (d)  any windshield or window that is composed of, covered by, or treated with any

22    material or component that presents a metallic or mirrored appearance; or

23        (e) any sign, poster, or other nontransparent material on the windshield, sidewings, side or

24    rear windows of the motor vehicle other than a certificate or other paper required to be so

25    displayed by law.

26        (2)  Nontransparent materials may be used:

27        (a)  along the top edge of the windshield in an area not to exceed three inches to right or


1    left of center of the windshield if the materials do not extend downward more than four inches

2    from the top edge of the windshield; or

3        (b)  in the lower left-hand corner of the rear window provided they do not extend more than

4    three inches to the right of the left edge or more than four inches above the bottom edge of the

5    window.

6        (3)  A windshield or other window is considered to comply with the requirements of

7    Subsection (1) if the windshield or other window meets the federal statutes and regulations for

8    motor vehicle window composition, covering, light transmittance, and treatment [at the time the

9    windshield or other window is manufactured if the amount of light the windshield or other window

10    transmits is not altered after manufacture].

11        (4)  Except for material on the windshield or rear window used in compliance with

12    Subsection (2), a motor vehicle with tinting on any window shall be equipped with rear-view

13    mirrors mounted on the left side and on the right side of the motor vehicle to reflect to the driver

14    a view of the highway to the rear of the motor vehicle.

15        (5) (a)  The windshield on every motor vehicle shall be equipped with a device for cleaning

16    rain, snow, or other moisture from the windshield. The device shall be constructed to be operated

17    by the driver.

18        (b)  Every windshield wiper on a motor vehicle shall be maintained in good working order.

19        (6) (a)  A person may not have for sale, sell, offer for sale, install, cover, or treat a

20    windshield or window in violation of this section.

21        (b)  A person who violates this section is guilty of a class C misdemeanor.

22        (7)  Notwithstanding this section, any person subject to the federal Motor Vehicle Safety

23    Standards, including motor vehicle manufacturers, distributors, dealers, importers, and repair

24    businesses, shall comply with the federal standards on motor vehicle window tinting. 

25        Section 2.   Effective date.

26        If approved by two-thirds of all the members elected to each house, this act takes effect

27    upon approval by the governor, or the day following the constitutional time limit of Utah

28    Constitution Article VII, Section 8, without the governor's signature, or in the case of a veto, the

29    date of veto override.

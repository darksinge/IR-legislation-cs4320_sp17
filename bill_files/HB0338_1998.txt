5    AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC CONSTRUCTION PROCUREMENT; ALLOWING THE

6    DIVISION OF FACILITIES CONSTRUCTION AND MANAGEMENT CERTAIN

7    INCREASED FLEXIBILITY WHEN BIDS EXCEED AVAILABLE FUNDS; AND MAKING

11         63-56-20, as enacted by Chapter 75, Laws of Utah 1980

13    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

14        Section 1.  Section  63-56-20 is amended to read:

15         63-56-20.  Contracts awarded by sealed bidding -- Procedure.  

16        (1) Contracts shall be awarded by competitive sealed bidding except as otherwise provided

17    by this chapter.

18        (2) (a)  An invitation for bids shall be issued when a contract is to be awarded by

19    competitive sealed bidding.

20        (b)  The invitation shall include a purchase description and all contractual terms and

21    conditions applicable to the procurement.

22        (3) (a)  Public notice of the invitation for bids shall be given a reasonable time [prior to]

23    before the date set forth [therein] in the invitation for the opening of bids, in accordance with rules

24    [and regulations].

25        (b)  The notice may include publication in a newspaper of general circulation a reasonable

26    time [prior to] before bid opening.

27        (4) (a)  Bids shall be opened publicly in the presence of one or more witnesses at the time


1    and place designated in the invitation for bids.

2        (b)  The amount of each bid and any other relevant information specified by rules [and

3    regulations], together with the name of each bidder, shall be recorded.

4        (c)  The record and each bid shall be open to public inspection.

5        (5) (a)  Bids shall be unconditionally accepted without alteration or correction, except as

6    authorized in this chapter.

7        (b) (i)  Bids shall be evaluated based on the requirements set forth in the invitation for bids,

8    which may include criteria to determine acceptability such as inspection, testing, quality,

9    workmanship, delivery, and suitability for a particular purpose.

10        (ii)  Those criteria that will affect the bid price and be considered in evaluation for award

11    shall be objectively measurable.

12        (iii)  The criteria may include discounts, transportation costs, and total or life cycle costs.

13        (c)  No criteria may be used in bid evaluation that are not set forth in the invitation for bids.

14        (6) (a)  Correction or withdrawal of inadvertently erroneous bids before or after award, or

15    cancellation of awards or contracts based on [such] the bid mistakes, shall be permitted in

16    accordance with rules [and regulations].

17        (b)  After bid opening, no changes in bid prices or other provisions of bids prejudicial to

18    the interest of the state or fair competition [shall] may be permitted.

19        (c)  Except as otherwise provided by [rules and regulations] rule, all decisions to permit

20    the correction or withdrawal of bids or to cancel awards or contracts based on bid mistakes shall

21    be supported by a written determination made by the chief procurement officer or the head of a

22    purchasing agency.

23        (7) (a)  The contract shall be awarded with reasonable promptness by written notice to the

24    lowest responsive and responsible bidder whose bid meets the requirements and criteria set forth

25    in the invitation for bids. [In the event all]

26        (b) (i)  If all bids for a construction project exceed available funds as certified by the

27    appropriate fiscal officer, and the low responsive and responsible bid does not exceed [such] those

28    funds by more than 5%, the chief procurement officer or the head of a purchasing agency [is

29    authorized] may, in situations where time or economic considerations preclude resolicitation of

30    work of a reduced scope, to negotiate an adjustment of the bid price, including changes in the bid

31    requirements, with the low responsive and responsible bidder, in order to bring the bid within the

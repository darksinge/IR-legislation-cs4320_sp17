5    AN ACT RELATING TO REAL ESTATE AND TO CITIES AND TOWNS; REQUIRING A

6    MUNICIPALITY'S LEGISLATIVE BODY TO APPROVE AND TO RECORD A PLAT OR

7    MAP FOR A NEWLY INCORPORATED CITY OR TOWN OR AN ORDINANCE

8    APPROVING ANNEXATION OF AN UNINCORPORATED AREA ALLOWING THE

9    RERECORDING OF DOCUMENTS UNDER SPECIFIED CIRCUMSTANCES; AND

10    MAKING TECHNICAL CHANGES.

11    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

18         10-2-125.  Incorporation of a town.

19        (1)  A contiguous area of a county not within a municipality, with a population of at least

17         57-3-2.  Record imparts notice -- Change in interest rate -- Validity of document --

18     Notice of unnamed interests -- Conveyance by grantee.

20    this title, each original document or certified copy of a document complying with Section 57-4a-3,

22    and each financing statement complying with Section 70A-9-402, whether or not acknowledged

23    shall, from the time of [filing] recording with the appropriate county recorder, impart notice to all

24    persons of their contents.

26    accordance with the terms of an agreement pertaining to the underlying secured obligation does

27    not affect the notice or alter the priority of the document provided under Subsection (1).

28        (3)  This section does not affect the validity of a document with respect to the parties to the

29    document and all other persons who have notice of the document.

1    terms of the trust does not charge any third person with notice of any interest of the grantor or of

5    document recorded in accordance with this title that sets forth the names of the beneficiaries,

8         57-3-11.  Original documents required -- Captions -- Legibility -- Rerecording of

9     documents -- Corrections or amendments to recorded documents.

10        (1) Unless otherwise provided, documents presented for recording in the office of the

11    county recorder shall be originals and shall contain a brief caption stating the nature of the

12    document.  Documents presented for recording shall also be sufficiently legible for the recorder

13    to make certified copies.

14        (2) (a)  A document which is of record in the office of the appropriate county recorder in

15    compliance with this chapter, may not be recorded again in that same county recorder's office

16    unless the original document has been reexecuted by all parties who executed the document. 

17    Unless exempt by statute, original documents which are reexecuted must also contain the

18    appropriate acknowledgment, proof of execution, jurat or other notarial certification for all parties

19    who are reexecuting the document as required by Title 46, Chapter 1, Notaries Public Reform Act,

20    and Title 57, Chapter 2, Acknowledgments.  Documents submitted for rerecording shall contain

21    a brief statement explaining the reason for rerecording.

22        (b)  A county recorder may refuse to accept a document for rerecording if that document

23    does not conform to the requirements of this section.

24        (c)  Subsection (2) of this section applies only to documents executed after July 1, 1998.

25        (3)  Documents of record in the office of a county recorder may be augmented, amended,

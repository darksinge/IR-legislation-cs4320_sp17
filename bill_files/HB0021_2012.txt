 Committee Note:

 General Description:
            
11

    
    This bill modifies the Uniform Driver License Act by creating an emergency contact
            
12

    
database for driver license and identification card holders.
            
13

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
14

    
    This bill:
            
15

    
    .    requires the Driver License Division to create an emergency contact database for
            
16

    
license certificate and identification card holders;
            
17

    
    .    provides that a law enforcement officer may share information contained in the
            
18

    
emergency contact database with other public safety workers on the scene of a
            
19

    
motor vehicle accident or other emergency situation;
            
20

    
    .    provides that a person holding a license certificate or identification card may
            
21

    
provide the Driver License Division the names, addresses, and telephone numbers
            
22

    
of no more than two emergency contact persons;
            
23

    
    .    requires the Driver License Division to make rules establishing the procedures to
            
24

    
implement the emergency contact database;
            
25

    
    .    provides that if a person is involved in a motor vehicle accident or other emergency
            
26

    
situation and is unable to communicate with the contact person or persons specified
            
27

    
in the database, a law enforcement officer shall make a good faith effort to notify

            
28


    
the contact person or persons of the situation; and
            
29

    
    .    specifies that the Driver License Division, a law enforcement officer, or a law
            
30

    
enforcement agency that employs a law enforcement officer does not incur liability
            
31

    
in connection with the use of the emergency contact database in certain
            
32

    
circumstances.
            
33

    
 Money Appropriated in this Bill:
            
34

35

    
 Other Special Clauses:

 Utah Code Sections Affected:

Section

 is enacted to read:
            
43

    
     53-3-205.6.  Emergency contact database.
            
44

    
    (1) (a)  The division shall establish a database of the emergency contacts of a person
            
45

    
who holds a license certificate, learner permit, identification card, or any other type license or
            
46

    
permit issued under this chapter.
            
47

    
    (b)  Information in the database created under this section may only be accessed by:
            
48

    
    (i)  employees of the division;
            
49

    
    (ii)  law enforcement officers employed by a law enforcement agency; and
            
50

    
    (iii)  employees or authorized agents of a law enforcement agency.
            
51

    
    (c)  A law enforcement officer may share information contained in the emergency
            
52

    
contact database with other public safety workers on the scene of a motor vehicle accident or
            
53

    
other emergency situation, as needed to conduct official law enforcement duties.
            
54

    
    (2)  A person holding a license certificate, learner permit, identification card, or any
            
55

    
other type of license or permit issued under this chapter may provide the division, in a manner
            
56

    
specified by the division, the name, address, telephone number, and relationship to the holder
            
57

    
of no more than two emergency contact persons whom the holder wishes to be contacted by a
            
58

    
law enforcement officer if the holder:

            
59


    
    (a)  is involved in a motor vehicle accident or other emergency situation; and
            
60

    
    (b)  is unable to communicate with the contact person or persons.
            
61

    
    (3)  In accordance with Title 63G, Chapter 3, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the
            
62

    
division shall make rules establishing the procedures to implement this section including:
            
63

    
    (a)  specifying the method for a license certificate, instruction permit, or identification
            
64

    
card holder to provide the division with emergency contact information;
            
65

    
    (b)  specifying the method for a license certificate, learner permit, or identification card
            
66

    
holder to change the emergency contact information; and
            
67

    
    (c)  other rules required for the implementation of the database or its operation that the
            
68

    
division determines necessary to implement the provisions of this section.
            
69

    
    (4) (a)  If a person involved in a motor vehicle accident or other emergency situation is
            
70

    
unable to communicate with the contact person or persons specified in the database, a law
            
71

    
enforcement officer shall make a good faith effort to notify the contact person or persons of the
            
72

    
situation.
            
73

    
    (b)  A law enforcement officer or a law enforcement agency that employs a law
            
74

    
enforcement officer may not incur any liability if the law enforcement officer is not able to
            
75

    
make contact with a designated emergency contact person.
            
76

    
    (c)  Except for willful or wanton misconduct, a law enforcement officer or a law
            
77

    
enforcement agency that employs a law enforcement officer may not incur any liability relating
            
78

    
to the reporting or use of the database during a motor vehicle accident or other emergency
            
79

    
situation.
            
80

    
    (5)  The division is not liable for any damages, costs, or expenses arising or resulting
            
81

    
from any inaccurate or incomplete data or system unavailability.
            
82

    
    Section 2.   Effective date.
            
83

    
    This bill takes effect on July 1, 2012.

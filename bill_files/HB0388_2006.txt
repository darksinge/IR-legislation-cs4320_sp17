Peterson                                                         LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill creates the Traumatic Brain Injury Fund and a board to administer it.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    creates the Traumatic Brain Injury Board within the Department of Human                           Services                                .    creates a Traumatic Brain Injury Fund                                .    increases the surcharge on DUIs and other offenses to provide monies for the fund                                .    directs the state treasurer to deposit    of the surcharge into the fund  and                               .    specifies duties and responsibilities for the Traumatic Brain Injury Board. Section      A            is enacted to read                            CHAPTER   .  TRAUMATIC BRAIN INJURY FUND                            Part  . General Provisions                                  A       .  Title.                                    This chapter is known as the  Traumatic Brain Injury Fund.                                Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                                  A       .  Definitions.                               As used in this chapter                                      Board  means the board created in Section       A           .                                     Department  means the Department of Human Services.                                     Executive director  means the executive director of the Department of Human                          Services.                                     Fund  means the Traumatic Brain Injury Fund created in Section       A           .                               Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                            Part  .  Traumatic Brain Injury Fund                                  A       .  Traumatic Brain Injury Fund.                                    There is created a restricted special revenue fund entitled the Traumatic Brain                          Injury Fund.                                    The fund shall consist of                                 a   amounts collected in accordance with Section          a                                        b   gifts, grants, donations, or any other monies that may be made to the fund from                          private sources  and                                c   legislative appropriations.                                    The Traumatic Brain Injury Board shall administer the fund.                                    The board may use fund monies to                                 a   educate the general public and professionals regarding understanding, treatment,                             and prevention of traumatic brain injury                                 b   coordinate short term care to assist individuals in identifying services or support                          needs, resources, and benefits for which they may be eligible                                 c   develop and support an information and referral system for persons with traumatic                          brain injuries and their families  and                                d   provide grants to persons or organizations to provide the services in Subsections                              a ,  b , and  c .                                    The board may                                 a   use up to    of the total fund revenues for the actual and necessary operating                          expenses of the board                                 b   seek outside donations from public and private entities to offset operating expenses                           and                                c   contract with public and private organizations to provide the services in Subsection                             .                                    An individual who receives services either directly from the board or through a                          public or private organization under contract with the board shall                                 a   be a resident of Utah                                 b   have been diagnosed by a qualified professional with a traumatic brain injury that                          results in impairment of cognitive or physical function  and                                c   have a need that can be met within the requirements of this chapter.                                    The board may not duplicate any services or support mechanisms being provided to                          an individual by any government or private agency.                                    The board may not provide short term, long term, or acute care.                               Section  .    Section      A            is enacted to read                                  A       .  Traumatic Brain Injury Board.                                    There is created within the Department of Human Services, the Traumatic Brain                          Injury Board.                                    The board shall consist of the following nine members appointed by the executive                          director                                 a   one person with a traumatic brain injury                                 b   one family member of a person with a traumatic brain injury                                    c   a representative of an association that advocates for persons with traumatic brain                          injury                                 d   a specialist in a profession that works with brain injury patients                                 e   a representative from the department  and                                f   four members at large appointed by the executive director from a list of                          recommendations compiled by members in Subsections     a  through  e .                                    The board shall                                 a   provide a centralized resource for                                 i   persons with traumatic brain injury and their families  and                                ii   professionals that work with persons with traumatic brain injuries                                  b   recommend and approve expenditures from the Traumatic Brain Injury Fund                           created in Section       A             and                                 c   make recommendations to state agencies, private organizations, and the Legislature                           regarding services and assistance for persons with traumatic brain injuries.                                     a   Members shall receive no compensation or benefits for their services, but may                           receive per diem and expenses incurred in the performance of the member s official duties at                           the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections       A           and       A          .                                 b   Members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their service.                                     Not later than November    of each year the board shall                                  a   provide a written report to                                  i   the executive director of the department  and                                 ii   the Health and Human Services Interim Committee  and                                 b   in the report, summarize the activities of the board and identify each disbursement                           from the fund and its purposes.                                Section  .    Section         a       is amended to read                                       a  .  Surcharge    Application and exemptions.                                     a   A surcharge shall be paid on all criminal fines, penalties, and forfeitures                            imposed by the courts.                                 b   The surcharge shall be                                  i             upon conviction of a                                  A   felony                                     B   class A misdemeanor                                  C   violation of Title   , Chapter  a, Part  , Driving Under the Influence and Reckless                            Driving  or                                 D   class B misdemeanor not classified within Title   , Motor Vehicles, including                            violation of comparable county or municipal ordinances  or                                 ii       upon conviction of any other offense, including violation of county or                            municipal ordinances not subject to the           surcharge.                                     The surcharge may not be imposed                                  a   upon nonmoving traffic violations                                  b   upon court orders when the offender is ordered to perform compensatory service                            work in lieu of paying a fine  and                                 c   upon penalties assessed by the juvenile court as part of the nonjudicial adjustment                            of a case under Section         a        .                                     a   The surcharge and the exceptions under  Subsections     and     also apply to                            all fines, penalties, and forfeitures imposed on juveniles for conduct that would be criminal if                            committed by an adult.                                 b   However, the surcharge does not include amounts assessed or collected separately                            by juvenile courts for the Juvenile Restitution Account, which is independent of this chapter                            and does not affect the imposition or collection of the surcharge.

CHILD SUPPORT REDUCTION FOR           EXTENDED VISITATION                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Margaret  Dayton          AN ACT RELATING TO JUDICIAL CODE  ALTERING REQUIREMENTS FOR REDUCING         CHILD SUPPORT OBLIGATION DURING EXTENDED VISITATION.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          AMENDS                       .  , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section         .   is amended to read                       .  .  Reduction for extended visitation.                   For purposes of this section                a    Off track  means the period of time a child is on year round school break.               b    On track  means the period of time a child is not on break from year round school.                    The       a   Except as provided in Subsection    , the child support order shall          provide that the base child support award be reduced by     for each child for a time  periods           period during which the child is with the noncustodial parent by order of the court or by written          agreement of the parties for at least    of any    consecutive days.               b   Normal visitation and holiday visits to the custodial parent shall not be considered an          interruption of the consecutive day requirement.                   a   For each child enrolled in year round school, the base child support shall be          reduced by     for one month for each child if the child is with the noncustodial parent for at least             days of off track time in any   month period.               b   The    days are not required to be consecutive and shall include winter recess for          Christmas and New Year s day, but not normal visitation or other holiday visitation days during          the time the child is on track.              c   No day the child is with the noncustodial parent may be counted towards the day         requirement of Subsections     a  and  b  at the same time.                  If the dependent child is a recipient of Aid to Families with Dependent Children, any         agreement by the parties for reduction of child support during extended visitation shall be         approved by the administrative agency.   However, normal visitation and holiday visits to the         custodial parent shall not be considered an interruption of the consecutive day requirement.                         For purposes of this section, the per child amount to which the abatement applies         shall be calculated by dividing the base child support award by the number of children included          in the award.

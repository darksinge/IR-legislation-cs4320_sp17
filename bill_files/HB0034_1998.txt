1     DINOSAUR DIAMOND PREHISTORIC


2     HIGHWAY DESIGNATION




3    1998 GENERAL SESSION


4    STATE OF UTAH


5     Sponsor:  Jack A. Seitz


6    AN ACT RELATING TO HIGHWAYS; ESTABLISHING THE DINOSAUR DIAMOND

7    PREHISTORIC HIGHWAY AND DESIGNATING ITS ROUTE.

8    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

9    ENACTS:

10         27-12-163, Utah Code Annotated 1953

11    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

12        Section 1.  Section  27-12-163 is enacted to read:

13         27-12-163.  Dinosaur Diamond Prehistoric Highway.

14        (1)  There is established the Dinosaur Diamond Prehistoric Highway comprising the

15    existing highways beginning on Route 191 in Blanding north to Route 70 at Crescent Junction,

16    then west on Route 70 to Green River, then northerly on Route 191 and Route 6 to Route 10 near

17    Price, then:

18        (a)  south on Route 10 to Route 155, then south on Route 155 to the Cleveland Lloyd

19    Dinosaur Quarry via its access road; and

20        (b)  northerly on Route 191 and Route 6 to Helper and the State of Utah Indian Canyon

21    Scenic Byway, then northeasterly on Route 191 to Route 40 in Duchesne, then east on Route 40

22    and Route 191 to Vernal, and then east on Route 40 to Route 149, then northeasterly on Route 149

23    to the Dinosaur National Monument, then southwesterly on Route 149 to Route 40, then east on

24    Route 40 to the Utah and Colorado state boundary line.

25        (2)  In addition to other official designations, the Department of Transportation shall

26    designate and highlight the portions of the highways identified in Subsection (1) as the Dinosaur

27    Diamond Prehistoric Highway on all future state highway maps.

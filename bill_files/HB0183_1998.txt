1     QUICK COURT AMENDMENTS




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: Sheryl L. Allen


5    J. Brent Haymond
Patrice  Arent



6    AN ACT RELATING TO JUDICIAL CODE; AMENDING THE UTAH QUICK COURT

7    PROGRAM TO PERMIT ELECTRONIC FILING THROUGH THE INTERNET; AND

8    MAKING TECHNICAL AMENDMENTS.

9    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

10    AMENDS:

11         78-28-1, as last amended by Chapter 68, Laws of Utah 1995

12         78-28-2, as last amended by Chapter 243, Laws of Utah 1996

13    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

14        Section 1.  Section  78-28-1 is amended to read:

15         78-28-1.  Utah Quick Court -- Purpose of program -- Methods to access electronic

16     filing system -- User's fee.  

17        (1) (a)  There is established the Utah Quick Court as a program to be administered by the

18    Administrative Office of the Courts to begin on January 1, 1995, designed to assist the pro se civil

19    litigant in the preparation of pro se court documents through [computer use] an electronic filing

20    system in:

21        (i)  uncontested divorces;

22        (ii)  enforcement of orders in the divorce decree including visitation, child custody, and

23    property division; and

24        (iii)  landlord and tenant actions.

25        (b)  The pro se documents prepared in uncontested divorces shall include a calculation of

26    child support payments if applicable.

27        (c)  The electronic filing system of the program may be facilitated through computer


1    terminals and the Internet.

2        [(c)] (d)  The [computer terminal] electronic filing system shall also provide information

3    to users about:

4        (i)  the procedures of the district court;

5        (ii)  the procedures of the small claims court;

6        (iii)  landlord and tenant rights and responsibilities;

7        (iv)  alternative dispute resolution;

8        (v)  child support collection procedures;

9        (vi)  how to collect court judgments; and

10        (vii)  how to settle minor disputes without legal action.

11        (2)  The statewide purpose of the Utah Quick Court program shall be to:

12        (a)  minimize the costs of civil litigation;

13        (b)  improve access to the courts; and

14        (c)  provide for informed use of the courts and the law by pro se litigants.

15        (3)  The computer terminals shall be installed at four judicial districts with locations to be

16    selected by the policy board.

17        (4) (a)  The program shall provide for public access to computer terminals for a $10  fee

18    with the intent that the program shall become self-sufficient.

19        (b)  The fee shall be charged for the preparation of documents under Subsections (1)(a) and

20    (1)(b), but not for information under Subsection (1)(c).

21        (c)  The user fee shall cover the costs of providing the services and maintaining the

22    computer terminals.

23        (d)  Any excess monies generated by user fees shall be returned to the General Fund, not

24    the Administrative Office of the Courts. 

25        Section 2.  Section  78-28-2 is amended to read:

26         78-28-2.  Creation of policy board -- Membership -- Terms -- Chair -- Quorum --

27     Expenses.

28        (1)  There is created a 13 member policy board to be known as the "Utah Quick Court

29    Policy Board" which shall provide technical assistance and practical knowledge to the

30    Administrative Office of the Courts to:

31        (a)  prepare the legal forms and legal documents to be generated by the [computer

- 2 -


1    terminals] electronic filing system;

2        (b)  assure that the legal forms and legal documents are in conformity with the rules of

3    procedure and evidence; and

4        (c)  prepare relevant and accurate information on subjects listed in Subsection

5    78-28-1[(c)](d).

6        (2)  The voting membership shall consist of:

7        (a)  two members of the House of Representatives to be designated by the speaker, with

8    one member from each party;

9        (b)  two members of the Senate designated by the president, with one member from each

10    party;

11        (c)  two attorneys actively practicing in domestic relations designated by the Family Law

12    Section of the Utah State Bar;

13        (d)  one attorney actively practicing in civil litigation designated by the Civil Litigation

14    Section of the Utah State Bar;

15        (e)  one court commissioner designated by the chief justice of the Utah Supreme Court;

16        (f)  one district court judge designated by the chief justice of the Utah Supreme Court;

17        (g)  one attorney from Utah Legal Services designated by its director;

18        (h)  one attorney from Legal Aid designated by its director; and

19        (i)  two persons from the Administrative Office of the Courts designated by the state court

20    administrator.

21        (3) (a)  The terms of the members shall be four years and staggered so that approximately

22    half of the board expires every two years.

23        (b)  The board shall meet as needed.

24        (4)  The board shall select one of its members to serve as chair.

25        (5)  A majority of the members of the board constitutes a quorum.

26        (6) (a) (i)  Members who are not government employees shall receive no compensation or

27    benefits for their services, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in the performance of

28    the member's official duties at the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections

29    63A-3-106 and 63A-3-107.

30        (ii)  Members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their service.

31        (b) (i)  State government officer and employee members who do not receive salary, per

- 3 -


1    diem, or expenses from their agency for their service may receive per diem and expenses incurred

2    in the performance of their official duties from the board at the rates established by the Division

3    of Finance under Sections 63A-3-106 and 63A-3-107.

4        (ii)  State government officer and employee members may decline to receive per diem and

5    expenses for their service.

6        (c)  Legislators on the committee shall receive compensation and expenses as provided by

7    law and legislative rule.

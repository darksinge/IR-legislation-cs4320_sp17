 is amended to read:

     62A-4a-202.  Preventive services -- Family preservation services.
            
26

    
    (1) (a)  Within appropriations from the Legislature and monies obtained under
            
27

    
Subsection (5), the division shall provide preventive, in-home services and family preservation

            
28


    
services for families whose children are at immediate risk of being removed from the home and
            
29

    
for families in crisis, if:
            
30

    
    (i)  the child's welfare is not immediately endangered; and
            
31

    
    (ii)  the division determines that it is possible and appropriate.
            
32

    
    (b)  In determining whether preventive or family preservation services are reasonable
            
33

    
and appropriate, in keeping with the provisions of Subsection 

62A-4a-201

(1) the child's health,
            
34

    
safety, and welfare shall be the paramount concern.  The division shall consider whether those
            
35

    
services will be effective within a six-month period, and whether they are likely to prevent
            
36

    
reabuse or continued neglect of the child.
            
37

    
    (2)  The division shall maintain a statewide inventory of early intervention, preventive,
            
38

    
and family preservation services available through public and private agencies or individuals
            
39

    
for use by caseworkers. The inventory shall include:
            
40

    
    (a)  the method of accessing each service;
            
41

    
    (b)  eligibility requirements for each service; and
            
42

    
    (c)  the geographic areas and the number of families that can be served by each service,
            
43

    
and information regarding waiting lists for each service.
            
44

    
    (3)  As a part of its preventive services, the division shall provide family preservation
            
45

    
services that are short-term, intensive, crisis intervention programs, and that address:
            
46

    
    (a)  the safety of children;
            
47

    
    (b)  the physical and emotional needs of parents and children[; the division shall also
            
48

    
evaluate], including evaluating specific needs of the family, including depression, addiction,
            
49

    
and mental illness;
            
50

    
    (c)  the child's physical surroundings, including cleaning and repairing physical
            
51

    
housing, and addressing needs for necessities such as food, heat, and electricity;
            
52

    
    (d)  personal cleanliness, nutrition, and provision of personal grooming supplies and
            
53

    
clothing;
            
54

    
    (e)  budgeting, money management, and employment; and
            
55

    
    (f)  parenting skills, including nonviolent discipline, nurturing, and structure, and
            
56

    
teaching responsibility, respect for others, cooperation, and moral values.
            
57

    
    (4) (a)  The division may use only specially trained caseworkers or private providers to
            
58

    
provide the family preservation services described in Subsection (3).

            
59

60

61

62

63

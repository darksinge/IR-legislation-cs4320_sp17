1     ACUPUNCTURE PRACTICE AMENDMENTS




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: Afton B. Bradshaw


5    AN ACT RELATING TO OCCUPATIONS AND PROFESSIONS; AMENDING

6    DEFINITIONS; MODIFYING QUALIFICATIONS FOR LICENSURE; REQUIRING

7    ENGLISH LANGUAGE PROFICIENCY; REPEALING CERTAIN SECTIONS; AND

8    MAKING TECHNICAL AND CONFORMING AMENDMENTS.

9    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

10    AMENDS:

11         58-72-101, as renumbered and amended by Chapter 253, Laws of Utah 1996

12         58-72-102, as renumbered and amended by Chapter 253, Laws of Utah 1996

1    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

2        Section 1.  Section  58-72-101 is amended to read:

3         58-72-101.  Title.

4        This [act] chapter shall be known [and may be cited] as the "Acupuncture Licensing Act."

5        Section 2.  Section  58-72-102 is amended to read:

6         58-72-102.  Acupuncture licensing -- Definitions.

7        In addition to the definitions in Section 58-1-102, as used in this chapter:

8        [(1)  "Acupuncture" means the stimulation of a certain point or points on or near the surface

9    of the body by the insertion of needles to prevent or modify the perception of pain or to normalize

10    physiological functions, including pain control, for the treatment of certain diseases or

11    dysfunctions of the body, and includes the techniques of electroacupuncture, cupping, and

12    moxibustion.]

13        [(2)] (1)  "Acupuncturist" means a person who has been licensed under this chapter to

14    practice acupuncture.

15        [(3)] (2)  "Board" means the Acupuncture Licensing Board created in Section 58-72-201.

16        (3)  "Moxibustion" means a heat therapy that uses the herb moxa to heat acupuncture points

17    of the body.

18        (4)  "Practice of acupuncture" means the insertion of acupuncture needles and application

19    of moxibustion to specific areas of the human body based on traditional oriental medical diagnosis

20    and concepts as a primary mode of therapy.  Adjunctive therapies within the scope of acupuncture

21    may include:

22        (a)  manual, mechanical, thermal, electrical, and electromagnetic treatments based on

23    traditional oriental medical diagnosis and concepts; and

24        (b)  the recommendation of dietary guidelines and therapeutic exercise based on traditional

25    oriental medical diagnosis and concepts.

26        [(4)] (5)  "Unprofessional conduct" is as defined in [Section] Sections 58-1-501 and

27    58-72-503, and as may be further defined by rule [includes:].

28        [(a)  performing, procuring, or agreeing to perform or procure, or advising, aiding in or

29    abetting, or offering to perform, procure, advise, aid in or abet a criminal abortion;]

30        [(b)  willfully betraying or disclosing a professional secret, or violating a privileged

31    communication, except:]

- 2 -


1        [(i)  as required by law; or]

2        [(ii)  to assist the division in fully and freely exchanging information concerning applicants

3    or licenses with the licensing or disciplinary board of other states or foreign countries and

4    professional associations;]

5        [(c)  fraudulently representing that an incurable disease can be cured, or that a curable

6    disease, sickness, or injury can be cured in a stated time, or knowingly making any false statement

7    in connection with the practice of acupuncture, except as the making of the statement may be

8    necessary for accepted therapeutic reasons;]

9        [(d)  refusing to divulge to the division upon demand the means, methods, device, or

10    instrumentality used in the treatment of a disease, injury, ailment, or infirmity;]

11        [(e)  practicing acupuncture as a partner, agent, or employee of, or in joint venture with any

12    person who does not hold a license to practice acupuncture or medicine within this state;]

13        [(f)  using or administering to himself cocaine, opium, morphine, codeine, heroin, alpha

14    eucaine, beta eucaine, chloralhydrate, or any of the salts, derivatives, or compounds of the

15    foregoing substances or the using of paraldehyde or barbituric acid, or their salts, compounds or

16    derivatives, or amphetamines, hallucinogens or related drugs, or controlled substances, or any

17    narcotic or dangerous drug regulated by the statutes of this state or any alcoholic beverages to the

18    extent, or in a manner, dangerous or injurious to the person holding the license under this chapter,

19    or any other person or the public, or to the extent that its use impairs the ability of persons holding

20    licenses to conduct with safety to the public the practice of acupuncture, or being convicted of a

21    felony involving the use, consumption, or self-administration of any of the substances referred to

22    in this section or any combination thereof;]

23        [(g)  knowingly selling, furnishing, giving away or administering directly or indirectly, or

24    offering to sell, furnish, give away, or administer any of the drugs or compounds mentioned in

25    Subsection (f) to a habitue or addict unless permitted by law; or]

26        [(h)  willfully and intentionally making any false statement in a report or claim form to a

27    governmental agency or insurance company with the intent to secure payment not rightfully due.]

28        Section 3.  Section  58-72-201 is amended to read:

29         58-72-201.  Acupuncture Licensing Board.

30        (1)  There is created an Acupuncture Licensing Board consisting of:

31        (a)  two acupuncturists[,];

- 3 -


1        (b)  one physician with knowledge of and experience in acupuncture[,];

2        (c)  one other licensed health care practitioner with knowledge of and experience in

3    acupuncture[,]; and

4        (d)  one member from the general public.

5        (2)  The board shall be appointed and serve in accordance with Section 58-1-201.

6        (3)  The duties and responsibilities of the board shall be in accordance with Sections

7    58-1-202 and 58-1-203.  In addition, the board shall designate one of its members on a permanent

8    rotating basis to:

9        (a)  assist the division in reviewing complaints concerning the unlawful or unprofessional

10    conduct of a licensee; and

11        (b)  advise the division in its investigation of these complaints.

12        (4)  A board member who has, under Subsection (3), reviewed a complaint or advised in

13    its investigation may be disqualified from participating with the board when the board serves as

14    a presiding officer in an adjudicative proceeding concerning the complaint.

15        Section 4.  Section  58-72-301 is repealed and reenacted to read:

16         58-72-301.  License required -- License classification.

17        (1)  A license is required to engage in the practice of acupuncture, except as specifically

18    provided in Section  58-1-307 or 58-72-304.

19        (2)  The division shall issue to a person who qualifies under this chapter a license in the

20    classification of acupuncturist.

21        Section 5.  Section  58-72-302 is repealed and reenacted to read:

22         58-72-302.  Qualification for licensure.

23        Notwithstanding Section 58-1-302, all applicants for licensure as an acupuncturist shall:

24        (1)  submit an application in a form prescribed by the division;

25        (2)  pay a fee determined by the department under Section 63-38-3.2;

26        (3)  be of good moral character;

27        (4)  meet the requirements for current active certification in acupuncture under guidelines

28    established by the National Commission for the Certification of Acupuncturists (NCCA) as

29    demonstrated through a current certificate or other appropriate documentation;

30        (5)  pass the examination required by the division by rule;

31        (6)  be able to read, write, speak, understand, and be understood in the English language

- 4 -


1    as demonstrated by passing the Test of English as a Foreign Language, including the test for

2    speaking English and the test for writing English; and

3        (7)  meet with the board, if requested, for the purpose of evaluating the applicant's

4    qualifications for licensure.

5        Section 6.  Section  58-72-303 is repealed and reenacted to read:

6         58-72-303.  Terms of license -- Expiration -- Renewal.

7        (1)  Each license issued under this chapter shall be issued in accordance with a two-year

8    renewal cycle established by rule.  A renewal period may be extended or shortened by as much as

9    one year to maintain established renewal cycles or to change an established renewal cycle.

10        (2)  Each license automatically expires on the expiration date shown on the license unless

11    renewed by the licensee in accordance with Section 58-1-308.

12        Section 7.  Section  58-72-304 is repealed and reenacted to read:

13         58-72-304.  Exceptions from licensure.

14        In addition to the exemptions from licensure set forth in Section 58-1-307, the following

15    persons may engage in the practice of acupuncture subject to the stated circumstances and

16    limitations without being licensed under this chapter:

17        (1)  an individual licensed as a physician and surgeon or osteopathic physician and surgeon

18    under this title; and

19        (2)  a commissioned physician or surgeon serving in the armed forces of the United States

20    or other federal agency.

21        Section 8.  Section  58-72-401 is amended to read:

22         58-72-401.  Grounds for denial of license -- Disciplinary proceedings -- Resumption

23     of practice.

24        [(1)]  Grounds for refusal to issue a license to an applicant, for refusal to renew the license

25    of a licensee, to revoke, suspend, restrict, or place on probation the license of a licensee, to issue

26    a public or private reprimand to a licensee, and to issue cease and desist orders shall be in

27    accordance with Section 58-1-401.

28        [(2)  The terms of revocation, suspension, or probation under this chapter may include the

29    following:]

30        [(a)  suspending, limiting, or restricting the license to practice acupuncture for up to five

31    years, including limiting the practice of the person to, or excluding from the practice of the person,

- 5 -


1    one or more specific branches of acupuncture practice, including any limitation on practice;]

2        [(b)  requiring the licensee to submit to care, counseling, or treatment by physicians

3    approved or designated by the board, as a condition for licensure;]

4        [(c)  requiring the licensee to participate in a program of education prescribed by the board;

5    or]

6        [(d)  requiring the licensee to practice under the direction of a physician or acupuncturist

7    designated by the board for a specified period of time.]

8        [(3)  An acupuncturist whose license is suspended under Subsection 58-1-401(2) shall, at

9    reasonable intervals, be afforded the opportunity to demonstrate that he can resume the competent

10    practice of acupuncture with reasonable skill and safety to patients.]

11        Section 9.  Section  58-72-501 is amended to read:

12         58-72-501.  Acupuncture licensee -- Restriction on titles used.

13        (1)  A person practicing as an acupuncturist may not display or in any way use any title,

14    words, or insignia in conjunction with his name or practice except the word "acupuncturist." When

15    used in conjunction with the person's practice the term "acupuncturist" shall be displayed next to

16    the name of the acupuncturist.

17        (2)  An acupuncturist may not use the term "physician," "physician or surgeon," or "doctor"

18    in conjunction with his name or practice.  "Doctor of acupuncture[,]" ["constitutional

19    acupuncturist,"] or "oriental medical doctor" may be used if the term is commensurate with the

20    degree in acupuncture received by the practitioner.

21        (3)  Medical doctors who choose to practice acupuncture shall represent themselves as

22    medical doctors practicing acupuncture and not as acupuncturists.

23        Section 10.  Section  58-72-503 is enacted to read:

24         58-72-503.  Unprofessional conduct.

25        Unprofessional conduct includes keeping patient records in any language other than the

26    English language.

27        Section 11.  Section  58-73-501 is amended to read:

28         58-73-501.  Unprofessional conduct.

29        Unprofessional conduct is as defined in Section 58-1-501, as defined by division rule, and

30    also includes:

31        (1)  engaging in practice as a chiropractic physician after electing to place his license on

- 6 -


1    inactive status, without having established with the board that he has initiated or completed

2    continuing education necessary to reinstate active status of his license;

3        (2)  failing to complete required continuing professional education;

4        (3)  violating any of the scope of practice standards set forth in Section 58-73-601;

5        (4)  failing to maintain patient records in sufficient detail to clearly substantiate a diagnosis,

6    all treatment rendered to the patient in accordance with the recognized standard of chiropractic

7    care, and fees charged for professional services;

8        (5)  refusing to divulge to the division on demand the means, methods, device, or

9    instrumentality used in the treatment of a disease, injury, ailment, or infirmity, unless that

10    information is protected by the physician-patient privilege of Utah and the patient has not waived

11    that privilege;

12        (6)  refusing the division or its employees access to his office, instruments, laboratory

13    equipment, appliances, or supplies at reasonable times for purposes of inspection;

14        (7)  fraudulently representing that curable disease, sickness, or injury can be cured in a

15    stated time, or knowingly making any false statement in connection with the practice of

16    chiropractic;

17        (8)  offering, undertaking, or agreeing to cure or treat a disease, injury, ailment, or infirmity

18    by a secret means, method, device, or instrumentality;

19        (9)  willfully and intentionally making any false statement or entry in any chiropractic

20    office records or other chiropractic records or reports;

21        (10)  knowingly engaging in billing practices which are abusive and represent charges

22    which are fraudulent or grossly excessive for services rendered;

23        (11)  performing, procuring, or agreeing to procure or perform, or advising, aiding in or

24    abetting, or offering or attempting to procure or aid or abet in the procuring of a criminal abortion;

25        (12)  willfully betraying or disclosing a professional confidence or violation of a privileged

26    communication, except:

27        (a)  as required by law; or

28        (b)  to assist the division by fully and freely exchanging information concerning applicants

29    or licensees with the licensing or disciplinary boards of other states or foreign countries, the Utah

30    chiropractic associations, their component societies, or chiropractic societies of other states,

31    countries, districts, territories, or foreign countries;

- 7 -


1        [(13)  knowingly selling, giving away, or directly or indirectly administering, or offering

2    to sell, furnish, give away, or administer any of the drugs or compounds referred to in Subsection

3    58-72-102(4)(f) to a known addict unless permitted by law;]

4        [(14)] (13)  directly or indirectly giving or receiving any fee, commission, rebate, or other

5    compensation for professional services not actually rendered or supervised, but this subsection

6    does not preclude the legal relationships within lawful professional partnerships, corporations, or

7    associations; and

8        [(15)] (14)  knowingly failing to transfer a copy of pertinent and necessary medical records

9    or a summary of them to another physician when requested to do so by the subject patient or his

10    designated representative.

11        Section 12.  Repealer.

12        This act repeals:

13        Section  58-72-305, Exceptions from licensure.

14        Section  58-72-402, Reissuance of license after revocation.

15        Section  58-72-601, Acupuncture license not authorizing medical practice -- Insurance

16     payments.

 LONG TITLE
            
8

    
 General Description:
            
9

    
    This bill requires students' reading and numeric skills to be assessed upon entering
            
10

    
kindergarten.
            
11

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
12

    
    This bill:
            
13

    
    .    requires each kindergarten student, the student's parent, and kindergarten personnel
            
14

    
at the student's school to participate in an assessment of the student's reading and
            
15

    
numeric skills.
            
16

    
 Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
            
17

    
    None

 Other Special Clauses:
            
19

    
    None

 Utah Code Sections Affected:
            
21

    
AMENDS:
            
22

    
     53A-3-402.9, as last amended by Chapter 254, Laws of Utah 2002
            
23

    

            
24

    
Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
            
25

    
    Section 1.  
Section  
53A-3-402.9

 is amended to read:
            
26

    
     53A-3-402.9.  Assessment of students entering kindergarten.
            
27

    
    (1)  The Legislature recognizes that well-developed reading skills help:

            
28


    
    (a)  children to succeed in school, develop self esteem, and build positive relationships
            
29

    
with others;
            
30

    
    (b)  young adults to become independent learners; and
            
31

    
    (c)  adults to become and remain productive members of a rapidly changing
            
32

    
technology-based society.
            
33

    
    (2) (a) Each kindergarten student, the student's parent or guardian, and kindergarten
            
34

    
personnel at the student's school [may] shall participate in an assessment of the student's
            
35

    
reading and numeric skills.
            
36

    
    (b)  The assessment[, if made, should] shall take place no later than during the first
            
37

    
[two] four weeks of the school year.
            
38

    
    (c)  The State [Office] Board of Education, in cooperation with the state's school
            
39

    
districts, may develop the assessment instrument and any additional materials needed to
            
40

    
implement and supplement the assessment program.
            
41

    
    (3)  The kindergarten student's teacher [may] shall use the assessment in planning and
            
42

    
developing an instructional program to meet the student's identified needs.
            
43

    
    (4)  Based on the assessment under Subsection (2), the school shall provide the
            
44

    
student's parent or guardian with appropriate resource materials to assist them at home in the
            
45

    
student's literacy development.

H.B. 77









1     FILLING MIDTERM VACANCIES IN THE


2     LEGISLATURE




3    1998 GENERAL SESSION


4    STATE OF UTAH


5     Sponsor: David Ure


6    AN ACT RELATING TO ELECTIONS; MODIFYING PROCEDURES FOR FILLING

7    MIDTERM VACANCIES IN THE LEGISLATURE; AND MAKING TECHNICAL

8    CORRECTIONS.

9    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

10    AMENDS:

11         20A-1-503, as enacted by Chapter 1, Laws of Utah 1993

12    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

13        Section 1.  Section  20A-1-503 is amended to read:

14         20A-1-503.  Midterm vacancies in the Legislature.

15        (1)  As used in this section[,]:

16        (a)  "Central committee" means:

17        [(a)] (i)  the state central committee, when the legislative district encompasses more than

18    one county; and

19        [(b)] (ii)  the county central committee, when the legislative district is entirely within one

20    county.

21        (b) "Legislative party leader" means:

22        (i)  the speaker of the House, for a representative who is a member of the majority political

23    party;

24        (ii)  the House minority leader, for a representative who is a member of the minority

25    political party;

26        (iii)  the Senate president, for a senator who is a member of the majority political party; or

27        (iv)  the Senate minority leader, for a senator who is a member of the minority political


1    party.

2        (2)  When a vacancy occurs for any reason in the office of representative in the Legislature,

3    the [governor] legislative party leader shall fill the vacancy by appointing a person who meets the

4    qualifications for the office from three persons nominated by the central committee of the same

5    political party as the prior officeholder.

6        (3) (a)  When a vacancy occurs for any reason in the office of senator in the Legislature,

7    it shall be filled for the unexpired term at the next regular general election.

8        (b)  The [governor] legislative party leader shall fill the vacancy until the next regular

9    general election by appointing a person who meets the qualifications for the office from three

10    persons nominated by the central committee of the same political party as the prior officeholder.

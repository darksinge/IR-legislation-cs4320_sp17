AMENDMENTS TO WHISTLE BLOWERS                           STATUTE                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor   Neil A. Hansen                             This act modifies the Utah Protection of Public Employees Act by removing the requirement                          that the employee establish that the adverse action by an employer was taken because of the                          protected conduct of the employee and creating an affirmative defense for the employer to                          establish that the adverse action against the employee was the result of separate conduct by                           the employee.                           This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                            AMENDS                                       , as last amended by Chapter    , Laws of Utah                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section                is amended to read                                        .  Remedies for employee bringing action    Proof required.                                    As used in this section,  damages  means damages for injury or loss caused by each                           violation of this chapter.                                    An employee who alleges a violation of this chapter may bring a civil action for                           appropriate injunctive relief or actual damages, or both, within     days after the occurrence of                           the alleged violation of this chapter.                                    An action begun under this section may be brought in the district court for the county                           where the alleged violation occurred, the county where the complainant resides, or the county                           where the person against whom the civil complaint is filed resides or has his principal place of                           business.                                    To prevail in an action brought under the authority of this section, the employee shall                           establish, by a preponderance of the evidence, that the employee has  suffered an adverse action                             because the employee, or a person acting on his behalf engaged or intended to engage                                  a   engaged in an activity protected under Section                 .   and                                b   subsequently suffered an adverse action by the employer of the employee.                                    Upon a showing by the employee that the elements of Subsection     have been                          satisfied, an employer may assert as an affirmative defense that the adverse action taken against                          the employee was                                 a   not connected to the activity by the employee protected under Section                  and                                b   was taken because of separate actions or conduct of the employee that warranted the                          adverse action taken by the employer.

Section  .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Designation of economically disadvantaged rural area targeted industries.                   For purposes of this part, the board shall determine annually which industries or groups          of industries shall be targeted industries as defined in Subsection            .                   In designating an economically disadvantaged rural area                a   the  board  Rural Resettlement Board shall consider the average agricultural and          nonagricultural wage, personal income, unemployment, and employment in the area  and               b   the  board  Rural Resettlement Board may use an econometric cost benefit model or          models adopted by the Governor s Office of Planning and Budget.              Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Creation and duties of Rural Resettlement Board.                   a   There is created within the department the Rural Resettlement Board consisting of          five members appointed by the governor to four year terms.               b   Notwithstanding Subsection     a , the governor shall, at the time of the appointment          or reappointment, adjust the length of terms to ensure that the terms of board members are          staggered so that approximately half of the board is appointed every two years.               c   The members may not service more than two full consecutive terms unless the          governor determines that an additional term is in the best interest of the state.                          The members shall be employed in agriculture or other forms of private enterprise and         reside in an economically disadvantaged rural area with at least one member employed by a         financial institution.                  When a vacancy occurs in the membership for any reason, the replacement shall be for         the unexpired term.                  Three members of the board constitute a quorum for conducting board business and         exercising board power.                  The governor shall select one of the board members as its chair.                  a   Members shall receive no compensation or benefits for their service, but may          receive per diem and expense incurred in the performance of the member s official duties at rates          established by the Division of Finance under Section   A       and   A      .               b   Members may decline to receive per diem and expenses for their service.                   The board shall provide direction to the administrator on the use of the fund in          economically disadvantaged rural areas.              Section  .   Effective date.

CRIME REDUCTION TASK FORCE                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                           Sponsor   John E. Swallow                           AN ACT RELATING TO TASK FORCES  CREATING THE CRIME REDUCTION TASK                          FORCE  DEFINING ITS MEMBERSHIP  DELINEATING RESPONSIBILITIES AND                          PROCEDURES  PROVIDING A REPORTING DATE  APPROPRIATING    ,    FROM THE                          GENERAL FUND  PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE  AND PROVIDING A REPEAL                          DATE.                           This act enacts uncodified material.                          Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .   Crime Reduction Task Force    Creation    Membership    Quorum                              Compensation    Staff.                                    There is created the Crime Reduction Task Force, consisting of the following                             members                                 a   two members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate, no more than one                          of whom may be from the same political party                                 b   four members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the House                          of Representatives, no more than two of whom may be from the same political party                                 c   the commissioner of the Department of Public Safety or designee                                 d   the executive director of the Department of Corrections or designee                                 e   a representative of the Utah Sheriffs  Association, appointed by the association                                 f   a representative of the Utah Police Chief s Association, appointed by the association                                 g   the executive director of the Department of Human Services                                 h   the director of the Division of Youth Corrections                                 i   the chair of the Board of Juvenile Court Judges or designee                                 j   the chair of the Board of District Court Judges or designee                                    k   one citizen representative appointed by the governor                                 l   one citizen representative appointed by the president of the Senate  and                                m   one citizen representative appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives.                                    a   The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed under                          Subsection     a  as a cochair of the task force.                                b   The speaker of the House of Representative shall designate a member of the House of                          Representatives appointed under Subsection     b  as a cochair of the task force.                                    A majority of the members of the task force constitutes a quorum.  The action of a                          majority of a quorum constitutes the action of the task force.                                    a   Salaries and expenses of the members of the task force who are legislators shall be                          paid in accordance with Section                and Legislative Joint Rule   .  .                                b   Members of the task force who are not legislators may not receive compensation for                          their work associated with the task force, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred as a                          member of the task force at the rates established by the Division of Finance under Sections                               A           and       A          .                                    The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support to                          the task force.                               Section  .   Duties    Interim report.                                    The task force shall review and make recommendations regarding ways to reduce crime                          victimization in Utah.                                    As part of its work, the task force shall                                 a   study the organizational structure of crime fighting and responsibility                                 b   study the feasibility of creating the position of a statewide crime director                                 c   evaluate other states  organizational structures of crime fighting and crime reduction                          strategies  and                                d   create a statewide strategy for reducing crime victimization.                                    A final report, including any proposed legislation, shall be presented to the Law                          Enforcement and Criminal Justice Interim Committee before November   ,     .                               Section  .   Appropriation.                               Except as provided in H.B.   , Appropriations Coordination Act, there is appropriated from                          the General Fund for fiscal year                                                   ,    to the Senate to pay for the compensation and expenses of senators on the task                          force                                      ,    to the House of Representatives to pay for the compensation and expenses of                          representatives on the task force  and                                      ,    to the Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel to pay for staffing                          the task force.                               Section  .   Effective date.                               If approved by two thirds of all the members elected to each house, this act takes effect                          upon approval by the governor, or the day following the constitutional time limit of Utah                          Constitution Article VII, Section  , without the governor s signature, or in the case of a veto, the                          date of veto override.                               Section  .   Repeal date.                               This act is repealed November   ,     .

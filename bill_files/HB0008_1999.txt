CHILD LITERACY PROGRAMS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                           Sponsor   Sheryl L. Allen                           AN ACT RELATING TO EDUCATION  PROVIDING FOR A THREE LEVEL CHILD                          LITERACY PROGRAM TO ASSIST CHILDREN IN ACQUIRING LITERACY SKILLS                           ESTABLISHING LITERACY GOALS AT EACH LEVEL  ESTABLISHING ROLES FOR                          SCHOOLS, FAMILIES, AND COMMUNITIES  PROVIDING FOR COLLABORATION                           PROVIDING FOR AN ANNUAL REPORT  AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.                           This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                            ENACTS                                  A      , Utah Code Annotated                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A           is enacted to read                            Part  .  Child Literacy Programs                                  A      .  Child literacy programs    Levels    Goals    Collaboration    School,                           family, and community roles    Annual report.                                    The Legislature recognizes that effective, collaborative child literacy programs have                          a dramatic long term impact on each child s ability to succeed in school, to successfully compete                          in a global society, and to become a productive, responsible citizen.                                    The State Board of Education, through the state superintendent of public instruction,                          shall implement a three level literacy program.                                    a   Level one of the literacy program shall                                 i   focus on children from birth to age five                                 ii   involve the families of children in this age group, public elementary schools, and                          communities in a collaborative effort  and                                iii   have as its goal to assist families in understanding the need for and establishing an                             environment to support literacy, which would include reading aloud to children on a regular basis.                                b   i   The role of the family at level one of the program is to create an environment that                          supports literacy learning.                                ii   The role of elementary schools and communities at level one is to collaborate with                          each other to identify children up to age five, to facilitate high quality literacy training and to                          provide literacy support and resources for parents of children within this age group.                                    a   Level two of the program shall                                 i   focus on children ages five through eight  and                                ii   have as its goal to provide research based instruction and support so that all children                          read on or above grade level by the end of the third grade.                                b   i   The role of the family at level two is to                                 A   collaborate and communicate closely with school and community resources on                          childhood literacy  and                                B   maintain an environment that supports literacy learning.                                ii   The role of elementary schools at level two is to                                 A   provide high quality research based instruction to each student                                 B   provide additional resources to English as a second language students and other at risk                          students  and                                C   collaborate and communicate closely with families and community resources on                          childhood literacy.                                iii   The role of communities at level two is to                                 A   identify key players and community groups to develop and implement plans for                          literacy projects that impact children ages five through eight                                 B   implement high quality training programs and provide resources to support literacy                          projects for this age group  and                                C   coordinate the collaboration of literacy programs for children ages five through eight                          among families, communities, and elementary schools.                                    a   Level three of the program shall                                 i   focus on children age nine and above  and                                ii   have as its goal to provide high quality literacy intervention and support for nonreaders                          and those reading below grade level.                                   b   i   The role of the family at level three is to collaborate closely with school and                          community resources and to implement high quality practices for extra literacy support at home.                                ii   The role of elementary and secondary schools at level three is to                                 A   identify nonreaders and those reading below grade level                                 B   provide research based instruction to those identified under Subsection  b  ii  A   and                                C   collaborate and communicate closely with family and community resources.                                iii   The role of communities at level three is to                                 A   identify key players and community groups to develop and implement literacy projects                          that will assist nonreaders in learning to read or improve the reading level of those who are reading                          below level or do both                                 B   coordinate training and resources for literacy intervention programs required at level                          three                                 C   work with schools and families to identify those individuals needing additional support                          in acquiring or developing literacy skills  and                                D   coordinate the collaboration required among communities, schools, and families to                          successfully implement level three.                                    The State Board of Education shall work closely with the Department of Workforce                          Services as to the involvement of communities, including the training of child care providers, in                          the programs established under this section.                                    The State Board of Education shall provide the Legislature and the governor with an                          annual report on the effectiveness of the program, together with any recommendations for                          modifications in or additions to each level.                                    Implementation of the program authorized under this section is contingent upon an                          appropriation made to the State Board of Education in the General Appropriations Act or in the                          Minimum School Finance Act to fund the program.                               Section  .   Effective date.                               This act takes effect on July  ,     .

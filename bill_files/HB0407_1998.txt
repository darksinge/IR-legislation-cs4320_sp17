11    AQUACULTURE.

24    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

25        Section 1.  Section  4-37-103 is amended to read:

26         4-37-103.  Definitions.  

27        As used in this chapter:


1        (1)  "Aquaculture" means the controlled cultivation of aquatic animals.

2        (2) (a)  "Aquaculture facility" means any tank, canal, raceway, pond, off-stream reservoir,

3    or other structure used for aquaculture. "Aquaculture facility" does not include any public

4    aquaculture facility or fee fishing facility.

5        (b)  Structures that are separated by more than 1/2 mile, or structures that drain to or are

6    modified to drain to, different drainages, are considered separate aquaculture facilities regardless

7    of ownership.

8        (3) (a)  "Aquatic animal" means a member of any species of fish, mollusk, crustacean, or

9    amphibian.

10        (b)  "Aquatic animal" includes a gamete of any species listed in Subsection (3)(a).

11        (4)  "Fee fishing facility" means a body of water used for holding or rearing fish for the

12    purpose of providing fishing for a fee or for pecuniary consideration or advantage.

13        (5) (a)  "Private fish pond" means a body of water where privately owned fish are

14    propagated or kept.

15        (b)  "Private fish pond" does not include any aquaculture facility or fee fishing facility.

16        [(5)] (6)  "Public aquaculture facility" means a tank, canal, raceway, pond, off-stream

17    reservoir, or other structure used for aquaculture by the Division of Wildlife Resources, U.S.  Fish

18    and Wildlife Service, [a school,] or an institution of higher education.

19        [(6)] (7)  "Public fishery resource" means fish produced in public aquaculture facilities and

20    wild and free ranging populations of fish in the surface waters of the state.

24        (c)  stock or propagate aquatic animals in an aquaculture or fee fishing facility; or

25        (d)  harvest, transfer, or sell aquatic animals from an aquaculture or fee fishing facility.

26        (2)  If a person commits an act in violation of Subsection (1) and that same act constitutes

27    wanton destruction of protected wildlife as provided in Section 23-20-4, the person is guilty of a

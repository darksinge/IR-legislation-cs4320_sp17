H.J.R. 24





            
1

    
 JOINT RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING WEBER STATE

            
2

    
 UNIVERSITY'S 125TH ANNIVERSARY



            
3

    
2014 GENERAL SESSION

            
4

    
STATE OF UTAH

            
5

    


 Chief Sponsor:  Brad L. Dee



            
6

    



Senate Sponsor: 






  Jerry W. Stevenson





            
7

    

            
8

    
 LONG TITLE
            
9

    
 General Description:
            
10

    
    This joint resolution of the Legislature recognizes the 125th anniversary of Weber State
            
11

    
University.
            
12

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
13

    
    This resolution:
            
14

    
    .     recognizes the 125th anniversary of Weber State University.
            
15

    
 Special Clauses:
            
16

    
    None
            
17

    

            
18

    
Be it resolved by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
            
19

    
    WHEREAS, Weber State University first opened its doors in downtown Ogden as
            
20

    
Weber Stake Academy on January 7, 1889, and became a college during the first two decades
            
21

    
of the 20th century;
            
22

    
    WHEREAS, Weber Stake Academy was founded by the Church of Jesus Christ of
            
23

    
Latter-day Saints;
            
24

    
    WHEREAS, in the 1920s, the school changed its name to Weber College;
            
25

    
    WHEREAS, the students became Wildcats and Waldo the mascot was born after a
            
26

    
sportswriter described Weber athletes as a "scrappy bunch of wildcats";
            
27

    
    WHEREAS, in 1933, Weber College was established as a junior state college and its

            
28


    
ownership was transferred to the state of Utah;
            
29

    
    WHEREAS, after World War II, the college moved its campus to Harrison Boulevard
            
30

    
and, thanks to an outpouring of public support, its enrollment and physical campus grew
            
31

    
significantly;
            
32

    
    WHEREAS, this growth helped the college to become Weber State College, a four-year
            
33

    
institution, in 1962;
            
34

    
    WHEREAS, in 1991, Weber State College became recognized as a university and was
            
35

    
renamed Weber State University;
            
36

    
    WHEREAS, Weber State University experienced significant growth during the 1990s
            
37

    
and, recognizing the need to serve the growing number of students from Davis County, opened
            
38

    
Weber State University Davis in 2003;
            
39

    
    WHEREAS, in 2007, Weber State University established the Center for Community
            
40

    
Engaged Learning;
            
41

    
    WHEREAS, the Center for Community Engaged Learning has enjoyed a partnership
            
42

    
between the community and students who have contributed more than 765,000 combined hours
            
43

    
of community service;
            
44

    
    WHEREAS, for this service, Carnegie Classification for Community Engagement
            
45

    
recognized Weber State University in 2008;
            
46

    
    WHEREAS, Weber State University has made education more accessible by continuing
            
47

    
to open doors to learning through its affordable tuition, scholarship programs, online classes,
            
48

    
and flexible schedules;
            
49

    
    WHEREAS, it is these aspects of Weber State University that have provided increased
            
50

    
opportunities to many in the state of Utah who otherwise would not have access to a college
            
51

    
education;
            
52

    
    WHEREAS, Weber State University now serves more than 25,000 students, primarily
            
53

    
from Weber and Davis counties, and offers more than 225 degree programs consisting of
            
54

    
associate's degrees, bachelor's degrees, and master's degrees;
            
55

    
    WHEREAS, Weber State University consistently receives national recognition for
            
56

    
providing quality programs in business, nursing, teaching, and many other fields;
            
57

    
    WHEREAS, Weber State University has been a consistent contributor to the
            
58

    
community for the past 125 years;

            
59


    
    WHEREAS, through its academic programs and technology outreach center, Weber
            
60

    
State University has become a critical economic driver in northern Utah and a partner in
            
61

    
economic development and business growth; and
            
62

    
    WHEREAS, Weber State University alumni number over 100,000 across the globe,
            
63

    
including national and international leaders in business, education, government, military,
            
64

    
religion, sports, and entertainment:
            
65

    
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of the state of Utah
            
66

    
recognizes the 125th anniversary of Weber State University.
            
67

    
    BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this resolution be sent to the President of
            
68

    
Weber State University.

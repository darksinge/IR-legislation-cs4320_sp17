ADDRESSING MAJOR DISRUPTIONS BY                           PUBLIC SCHOOL STUDENTS                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Eric K. Hutchings                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                                This bill modifies the State System of Public Education Code by authorizing and                           requiring school districts and charter schools to address disruptive student behavior.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    clarifies that school discipline and conduct plan provisions are applicable to school                           districts and charter schools                                .    authorizes a school district or charter school to                                    .    issue notices of disruptive student behavior to certain school age minors  and                                   .    issue a habitual disruptive student behavior citation to certain school age                           minors  and                               .    makes technical corrections. Section      A            is amended to read                                   A       .  School discipline policies    Basis of the policies    Enforcement.                                    The Legislature recognizes that every student in the public schools should have the                           opportunity to learn in an environment which is safe, conducive to the learning process, and                           free from unnecessary disruption.                                    a   To foster such an environment, each local school board or governing board of a                          charter school, with input from  district  school employees, parents and guardians of students,                           students, and the community at large, shall adopt conduct and discipline policies for the public                           schools  within the district .                                b   Each district or charter school shall base its policies on the principle that every                           student is expected                                 i   to follow accepted rules of conduct  and                                ii   to show respect for other people and to obey persons in authority at the school.                                c   i   The State Superintendent of Public Instruction shall develop conduct and                           discipline policy models for elementary and secondary public schools.                                ii   Each district or charter school shall use the models, where appropriate, in                           developing its conduct and discipline policies under this chapter.                                d   The policies shall emphasize that certain behavior, most particularly behavior                           which disrupts, is unacceptable and may result in disciplinary action.                                    The local superintendent and designated employees of the district or charter school                              shall enforce the policies so that students demonstrating unacceptable behavior and their                           parents or guardians understand that such behavior will not be tolerated and will be dealt with                           in accordance with the district s conduct and discipline policies. Section  .    Section      A            is amended to read                                    A       .  Alternatives to suspension or expulsion.                                     Each local school board or governing board of a charter school shall establish                                  a   policies providing that prior to suspending or expelling a student for repeated acts                            of willful disobedience, defiance of authority, or disruptive behavior which are not of such a                            violent or extreme nature that immediate removal is required, good faith efforts shall be made                            to implement a remedial discipline plan that would allow the student to remain in school  and                                 b   alternatives to suspension, including policies that allow a student to remain in                            school  under an in school suspension program or under a program allowing the parent or                            guardian, with the consent of the student s teacher or teachers, to attend class with the student                            for a period of time specified by a designated school official.                                     If the parent or guardian does not agree or fails to attend class with the student, the                            student shall be suspended in accordance with the conduct and discipline policies of the district                            or the school.                                      The parent or guardian of a suspended student and the designated school official                            may enlist the cooperation of the Division of Child and Family Services, the juvenile court, or                            other appropriate state agencies, if necessary, in dealing with the student s suspension. Section      A            is enacted to read                                   A       .  Notice of disruptive student behavior    Failure to cooperate with                            school authorities    Habitual disruptive student behavior citation.                                     As used in this section,  disruptive student behavior  includes the conduct                           described in Subsection       A               b  and the grounds for suspension or expulsion                           described in Section       A           .                                     A local school board, school district, governing board of a charter school, or charter                           school may impose administrative penalties on a school age minor who violates this part.                                     A local school board or governing board of a charter school shall                                  a   authorize a school administrator or a designee of a school administrator to issue                           notices of disruptive student behavior to school age minors who are at least    years old  and                                 b   establish a procedure for a school age minor, or a school age minor s parents, to                           contest a notice of disruptive student behavior.                                     The notice of disruptive student behavior described in Subsection                                      a   may not be issued until the school age minor has committed disruptive student                           behavior at least five times during the school year                                  b   may not be issued to a school age minor who is less than    years old                                  c   shall request that the school age minor and the parent of the school age minor                                  i   meet with school authorities to discuss the school age minor s disruptive student                           behavior  and                                 ii   cooperate with the school board or school district in correcting the school age                           minor s disruptive behavior  and                                 d   shall be mailed to, or served on, the school age minor s parent.                                     A habitual disruptive student behavior citation may be issued to a habitual                              disruptive student if                                  a   the local school board, school district, governing board of a charter school, or                           charter school has made reasonable efforts to resolve the student s disruptive behavior                           problems                                  b   the efforts to resolve the student s disruptive behavior problems, described in                           Subsection     a , have not been successful  and                                 c   the school age minor has committed disruptive student behavior at least ten times                           during the school year.                                     A disruptive student to whom a habitual disruptive student citation is issued under                           Subsection                                      a   shall be referred to the juvenile court for violation of Subsection      and                                 b   is subject to the jurisdiction of the juvenile court.                                     A notice of disruptive student behavior or a habitual disruptive student behavior                           citation may only be issued by                                  a   a school administrator, or a truancy specialist, who is authorized by a local school                           board or governing board of a charter school  or                                 b   a designee of a school administrator described in Subsection     a .                                     Nothing in this part prohibits a local school board, school district, governing board                           of a charter school, or charter school from taking action to resolve a disruptive student behavior                           problem with a school age minor who has been given notice of disruptive student behavior less                           than five times, provided that the action does not conflict with the requirements of this part.

H.B. 282









1     AVOIDING CONFLICTS OF INTEREST FOR


2     MEMBERS OF SCHOOL BOARDS




3    1998 GENERAL SESSION


4    STATE OF UTAH


5     Sponsor:  Lloyd W. Frandsen


6    AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC EDUCATION; PROVIDING THAT STATE AND LOCAL

7    SCHOOL BOARD MEMBERS MAY NOT SERVE AS EMPLOYEES OF THEIR

8    RESPECTIVE BOARDS DURING THEIR TERMS OF OFFICE; PROVIDING THAT

9    STATE BOARD MEMBERS MAY NOT SERVE AS EMPLOYEES OR BOARD MEMBERS

10    OF OTHER STATE EDUCATION ENTITIES DURING THEIR TERMS OF OFFICE; AND

11    PROVIDING AN EXCEPTION TO THE CURRENT TERM FOR THOSE SERVING AS

12    BOARD MEMBERS ON THE EFFECTIVE DATE OF THE ACT.

13    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

14    AMENDS:

15         20A-14-103, as enacted by Chapter 1, Laws of Utah 1995

16         20A-14-202, as enacted by Chapter 1, Laws of Utah 1995

17    This act enacts uncodified material.

18    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

19        Section 1.  Section  20A-14-103 is amended to read:

20         20A-14-103.  State Board of Education members -- When elected -- Qualifications.

21        (1) (a)  In 1994 and every four years thereafter, one member each shall be elected from

22    Districts 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 10, and 13 to serve a four-year term.

23        (b)  In 1992 and every four years thereafter, one member each shall be elected from

24    Districts 2, 4, 6, 8, 11, 12, 14, and 15 to serve a four-year term.

25        (2)  A member shall:

26        (a)  be and remain a registered voter in the board district from which the member was

27    elected or appointed; and


1        (b)  maintain his primary residence within the board district from which the member was

2    elected or appointed.

3        (3)  A member of the State Board of Education may not, during the member's term of

4    office, also serve as an employee of the board, the Utah State Office of Education, or the Utah

5    State Office of Rehabilitation, or as an employee or board member for an applied technology

6    center or the Utah Schools for the Deaf and the Blind.

7        Section 2.  Section  20A-14-202 is amended to read:

8         20A-14-202.  Local Boards of Education -- Membership -- When elected --

9     Qualifications -- Avoiding conflicts of interest.  

10        (1) (a)  Unless otherwise required by this part, the Salt Lake City Board of Education shall

11    consist of seven members and the boards of education of all other local school districts shall

12    consist of five members.

13        (b) (i)  Members of a local board of education shall be elected at each regular general

14    election.

15        (ii)  Except as provided in Subsection (1)(b)(iii), no more than three members of a local

16    board of education may be elected to a five-member board, nor more than four members elected

17    to a seven-member board, in any election year.

18        (iii)  More than three members of a local board of education may be elected to a

19    five-member board and more than four members elected to a seven-member board in any election

20    year only when required by reapportionment or to fill a vacancy.

21        (c)  One member of the local board of education shall be elected from each local school

22    board district.

23        (2)  A member of a local school board shall:

24        (a)  be and remain a  registered voter in the local school board district from which the

25    member is elected or appointed; and

26        (b)  maintain his primary residence within the local school board district from which the

27    member is elected or appointed.

28        (3)  A member of a local school board may not, during the member's term in office, also

29    serve as an employee of that board.

30        Section 3.   Exceptions.

31        Subsections 20A-14-103(3) and 20A-14-202(3) of this act do not apply to the current term

1    in office of a person who is serving as a member of the State Board of Education or a local board

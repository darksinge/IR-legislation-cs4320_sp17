1     LIABILITY FOR MINOR WHO DRIVES




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor:  John E. Swallow


5    AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC SAFETY; AMENDING PROVISIONS RELATING TO THE

6    LIABILITY OF CERTAIN ADULTS FOR MINORS WHO DRIVE.

7    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

8    AMENDS:

9         53-3-211, as last amended by Chapter 122, Laws of Utah 1995

10         53-3-212, as renumbered and amended by Chapter 234, Laws of Utah 1993

11    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

12        Section 1.  Section  53-3-211 is amended to read:

13         53-3-211.  Application of minors -- Liability of person signing application --

14     Cancellation of cosigning adult's liability.  

15        (1)  As used in this section, "minor" means any person younger than 18 years of age who

16    is not married or has not been emancipated by adjudication.

17        (2) (a)  The application of a minor for a learner permit or provisional license shall be signed

18    by the parent or guardian of the applicant and verified before a person authorized to administer

19    oaths.

20        (b)  If no person has custody of the minor applicant, then a responsible adult who is willing

21    to assume the obligation imposed under this chapter may sign the application.

22        (3) (a)  Any negligence or willful misconduct of a minor when driving a motor vehicle

23    upon a highway is imputed to the person who has signed the application of the minor under

24    Subsection (2).

25        (b)  Except as provided under Subsection (4), the person who has signed the application

26    under Subsection (2), is jointly and severally liable with the minor for any damages caused by the

27    minor's negligence or willful misconduct.


1        (c)  This liability provision is in addition to the liability provisions in Section 53-3-212.

2        [(4) (a)  If a minor deposits, or there is deposited on his behalf, proof of financial

3    responsibility for driving a motor vehicle in form and in amounts as required under Title 41,

4    Chapter 12a, Financial Responsibility of Motor Vehicle Owners and Operators Act, the division

5    may accept the application of the minor when signed before a person authorized to administer

6    oaths by a parent, guardian of the minor, or a responsible adult.]

7        [(b)  While the proof is maintained, the parent, guardian, or responsible adult is not subject

8    to the liability imposed under Subsection (3).]

9        (4)  If owner's or operator's security for the minor's operation of the motor vehicle is in

10    effect in amounts as required under Title 41, Chapter 12a, Financial Responsibility of Motor

11    Vehicle Owners and Operators Act, the parent, guardian, or responsible adult who signed the

12    minor's application is not subject to the liability imposed under Subsection (3).

13        (5) (a)  A person who has signed the application of a minor under Subsection (2) may file

14    with the division a verified written request that the license of the minor be cancelled.

15        (b)  The division shall then cancel the license of the minor, and the person who signed the

16    application of the minor under Subsection (2) is relieved from the liability imposed under this

17    chapter regarding any subsequent negligence or willful misconduct of the minor in driving a motor

18    vehicle.

19        (6) (a)  The division upon receipt of satisfactory evidence of the death of the person who

20    signed the application of a minor under Subsection (2) shall cancel the license and may not issue

21    a new license until a new application, signed and verified, is made under this chapter.

22        (b)  This subsection does not apply to an application of a person who is no longer a minor.

23        Section 2.  Section  53-3-212 is amended to read:

24         53-3-212.  Owner giving permission and minor liable for damages caused by minor

25     driving a motor vehicle.  

26        (1) (a)  The owner of a motor vehicle causing or knowingly permitting a person younger

27    than 18 years of age to drive the motor vehicle on a highway, or a person who gives or furnishes

28    a motor vehicle to the minor, are each jointly and severally liable with the minor for any damages

29    caused by the negligence of the minor in driving the motor vehicle.

30        [(2)  This] (b)  The liability provision in Subsection (1)(a) is in addition to the liability

31    provisions in Section 53-3-211.

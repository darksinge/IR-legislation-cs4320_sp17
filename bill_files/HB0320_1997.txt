ACTION FOR LOSS OF CONSORTIUM                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Christine R. Fox          AN ACT RELATING TO HUSBAND AND WIFE  ENACTING PROVISIONS RELATING TO         AN ACTION FOR LOSS OF CONSORTIUM DUE TO PERSONAL INJURY.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          ENACTS                      , Utah Code Annotated               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section          is enacted to read                       .  Action for consortium due to personal injury.                   For purposes of this section                a    injury  or  injured  means a significant permanent injury to a person that substantially          changes that person s lifestyle and includes the following                i   a partial or complete paralysis of one or more of the extremities                ii   significant disfigurement  or               iii   incapability of the person of performing the types of jobs the person performed before          the injury  and               b    spouse  means the legal relationship                i   established between a man and a woman as recognized by the laws of this state  and               ii   existing at the time of the person s injury.                   The spouse of a person injured by a third party on or after May  ,     , may maintain          an action against the third party to recover for loss of consortium.                   A claim for loss of consortium begins on the date of injury to the spouse.  The statute          of limitations applicable to the injured person shall also apply to the spouse s claim of loss of          consortium.                   A claim for the spouse s loss of consortium shall be               a   made at the time the claim of the injured person is made and joinder of actions shall         be compulsory  and              b   subject to the same defenses, limitations, immunities, and provisions applicable to the         claims of the injured person.                  The spouse s action for loss of consortium               a   shall be derivative from the cause of action existing in behalf of the injured person  and              b   may not exist in cases where the injured person would not have a cause of action.                  Fault of the spouse of the injured person, as well as fault of the injured person, shall          be compared with the fault of all other parties, pursuant to Sections          through         ,          for purposes of reducing or barring any recovery by the spouse for loss of consortium.                   Damages awarded for loss of consortium, when combined with any award to the          injured person for general damages, may not exceed any applicable statutory limit on noneconomic          damages, including Section        . .

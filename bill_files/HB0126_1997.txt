Section  .  Section          .  is amended to read                        . .  Election of municipal officials.                   If the incorporation measure passes, the county clerk shall hold an election consistent          with this section to fill the elective offices                a   appropriate to the form of municipal government chosen by the voters at the          incorporation election  and               b   consistent with the voters  decision about whether some or all members of the city          commission or council should be elected by district.                   If there are    or more days between the incorporation election and the November                 election, the county clerk shall hold a primary election    days before the November election and         shall hold a general election on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November.                  If there are fewer than    days between the incorporation election and the November         election, the county clerk shall not hold a primary election but shall hold an election on the first         Tuesday after the first Monday in November.                  If there are fewer than    days between the incorporation election and the November         election, the county clerk shall hold a primary election    days before the November election of         the year following the incorporation election and shall hold a general election on the first Tuesday         after the first Monday in November of the year following the incorporation election.                   Until the municipality is incorporated, the county clerk is the election officer for all          purposes in an election of officials to the municipality approved at an incorporation election.              Section  .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Election of governing body.                   The person who receives the greater number of votes in the November election for the          office of mayor shall be mayor and shall serve a term of four years.                   The  number of  persons receiving the greater number of votes in the November          election for the corresponding number of positions to be filled shall be commissioners or           councilmen  council members until the next municipal election at which all members of the          governing body shall be elected for two or four year terms as the case may be.                   The officers elected under this section shall begin their term of office at    o clock          noon on the first Monday in January following their election and shall be sworn in as soon as          practicable after that time.              Section  .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Governing body in cities of the third class.              The governing body of cities of the third class shall be a council composed of six members,          one of whom shall be the mayor and the remaining five shall be  councilmen  council members.

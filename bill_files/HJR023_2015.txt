and16                expresses that, due to these inherent risks, a person engaged in agricultural tourism17     activities should not be able to recover from an agricultural tourism operator for18     injuries resulting from those inherent risks.19     Special Clauses:20          None21      22     Be it resolved by the Legislature of the state of Utah:23          WHEREAS, agricultural tourism activities are practiced by a large number of residents24     of Utah;25          WHEREAS, these agricultural tourism activities contribute significantly to the26     economy of the state;27          WHEREAS, few insurance carriers are willing to provide liability insurance protection28     to agricultural tourism operators;29          WHEREAS, the premiums charged by liability insurance carriers have risen sharply in30     recent years;31          WHEREAS, the sharp rise in premiums is due to confusion as to whether a participant32     in an agricultural tourism activity assumes the risks inherent in the agricultural tourism activity;33          WHEREAS, some risks are inherent in agricultural tourism activities; and34          WHEREAS, a person engaged in agricultural tourism activities should not be able to35     recover damages from an agricultural tourism operator for injuries resulting from those36     inherent risks:37          NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of the state of Utah38     recognizes that some risks are inherent in agricultural tourism activities.39          BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of the state of Utah expresses that,40     due to these inherent risks, persons engaged in agricultural tourism activities should not be able41     to recover damages from an agricultural tourism operator for injuries resulting from those42     inherent risks.43          BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this resolution be sent to the Utah44     Insurance Department, the Utah Association of Independent Insurance Agents, and the45     members of Utah's congressional delegation.Legislative Review Note     as of   2-11-15  11:09 AMOffice of Legislative Research and General Counsel

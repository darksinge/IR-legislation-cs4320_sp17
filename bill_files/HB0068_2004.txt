 LONG TITLE
            
7

    
 General Description:
            
8

    
    This bill modifies the Criminal Code by providing enhanced penalties if the defendant
            
9

    
selects a victim because of the defendant's bias or prejudice toward a group.
            
10

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
11

    
    This bill:
            
12

    
    .    defines the enhanced penalties and defines the term "group";
            
13

    
    .    provides that the trier of fact may not base a finding that the defendant acted
            
14

    
because of bias or prejudice solely on evidence of the defendant's mere beliefs,
            
15

    
associations, or expressions;
            
16

    
    .    allows the court, in its discretion, to impose alternative sentencing provisions in
            
17

    
cases subject to the enhanced penalties; and
            
18

    
    .    repeals the current statute commonly referred to as the hate crimes statute.
            
19

    
 Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
            
20

    
    None
            
21

    
 Other Special Clauses:
            
22

    
    None

     76-3-203.4.  Penalty for crimes motivated by bias or prejudice.
            
32

    
    (1)  If the trier of fact finds beyond a reasonable doubt that the defendant, in committing
            
33

    
an offense, selected the victim or the property primarily because of the defendant's actual bias
            
34

    
or prejudice against a group, as demonstrated by the defendant's actions related to the
            
35

    
commission of the offense, the enhanced penalty for a:
            
36

    
    (a)  class B misdemeanor is a class A misdemeanor;
            
37

    
    (b)  class A misdemeanor is a third degree felony;
            
38

    
    (c)  third degree felony is a second degree felony;
            
39

    
    (d)  second degree felony is a first degree felony; or
            
40

    
    (e)  first degree felony remains the penalty for a first degree felony, except:
            
41

    
    (i)  imposition or execution of the sentence may not be suspended unless the court finds
            
42

    
the interests of justice would be best served by the suspension and states the specific
            
43

    
circumstances justifying the disposition on the record; and
            
44

    
    (ii)  the Board of Pardons and Parole shall consider the finding by the trier of fact that
            
45

    
the convicted person selected the victim primarily because of the defendant's actual bias or
            
46

    
prejudice against a group, as demonstrated by the defendant's actions related to the commission
            
47

    
of the offense, an aggravating factor in determining the length of incarceration.
            
48

    
    (2) (a)  The prosecuting attorney, or grand jury if an indictment is returned, shall cause
            
49

    
to be subscribed upon the information or indictment notice that it is alleged that the defendant
            
50

    
is subject to the enhanced penalties provided in Subsection (1).
            
51

    
    (b)  The notice under Subsection (2)(a) shall be in a clause separate from and in
            
52

    
addition to the primary offense charged.
            
53

bias or prejudice under Subsection (1) solely on one or more of the following:

    (c)  any evidence of the defendant's expressions or associations, unless the evidence is

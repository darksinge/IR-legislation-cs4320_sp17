 LONG TITLE
            
11

    
 General Description:
            
12

    
    This bill makes technical corrections to building and construction projects provisions
            
13

    
dealing with notices of completion and definitions.
            
14

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
15

    
    This bill:
            
16

    
    .    removes duplicative provisions dealing with the filing of a notice of completion of
            
17

    
a construction project; and
            
18

    
    .    makes a conforming change to correctly reference the Fire Prevention Board in the
            
19

    
definitions section of the Uniform Building Standards Act.
            
20

    
 Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
            
21

    
    None
            
22

    
 Other Special Clauses:
            
23

    
    None
            
24

    
 Utah Code Sections Affected:
            
25

    
AMENDS:
            
26

    
     38-1-33, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2009, Chapter 239
            
27

    
     58-56-3, as last amended by Laws of Utah 2009, Chapter 339
            
28

    

            
29

    
Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

            
30


    
    Section 1.  
Section  
38-1-33

 is amended to read:
            
31

    
     38-1-33.  Notice of completion.
            
32

    
    (1) (a)  Upon final completion of a construction project, and in accordance with
            
33

    
Section 

38-1-40

, the following with a construction project registered with the database may
            
34

    
file a notice of completion with the database:
            
35

    
    (i)  an owner of the construction project;
            
36

    
    (ii)  an original contractor of the construction project;
            
37

    
    (iii)  a lender that has provided financing for the construction project;
            
38

    
    (iv)  a surety that has provided bonding for the construction project; or
            
39

    
    (v)  a title company issuing a title insurance policy on the construction project[;].
            
40

    
    [(vi)  a lender that has provided financing for the construction project;]
            
41

    
    [(vii)  a surety that has provided bonding for the construction project; or]
            
42

    
    [(viii)  a title company issuing a title insurance policy on the construction project.]
            
43

    
    (b)  Notwithstanding Section 

38-1-2

, if a subcontractor performs substantial work after
            
44

    
the applicable dates established by Subsection (1)(a), that subcontractor's subcontract is
            
45

    
considered an original contract for the sole purpose of determining:
            
46

    
    (i)  the subcontractor's time frame to file a notice to hold and claim a lien under
            
47

    
Subsection 

38-1-7

(1); and
            
48

    
    (ii)  the original contractor's time frame to file a notice to hold and claim a lien under
            
49

    
Subsection 

38-1-7

(1) for that subcontractor's work.
            
50

    
    (c)  A notice of completion shall include:
            
51

    
    (i)  the building permit number for the project, or the number assigned to the project by
            
52

    
the designated agent;
            
53

    
    (ii)  the name, address, and telephone number of the person filing the notice of
            
54

    
completion;
            
55

    
    (iii)  the name of the original contractor for the project;
            
56

    
    (iv)  the address of the project or a description of the location of the project;
            
57

    
    (v)  the date on which final completion is alleged to have occurred; and

            
58


    
    (vi)  the method used to determine final completion.
            
59

    
    (d)  For purposes of this section, final completion of the original contract does not
            
60

    
occur if work remains to be completed for which the owner is holding payment to ensure
            
61

    
completion of the work.
            
62

    
    (e) (i)  Unless a person indicates to the division or designated agent that the person
            
63

    
does not wish to receive a notice under this section, electronic notification of the filing of a
            
64

    
notice of completion or alternate notice as prescribed in Subsection (1)(a), shall be provided
            
65

    
to:
            
66

    
    (A)  each person that filed a notice of commencement for the project;
            
67

    
    (B)  each person that filed preliminary notice for the project; and
            
68

    
    (C)  all interested persons who have requested notices concerning the project.
            
69

    
    (ii)  A person to whom notice is required under this Subsection (1)(e) is responsible
            
70

    
for:
            
71

    
    (A)  providing an e-mail address, mailing address, or telefax number to which a notice
            
72

    
required by this Subsection (1)(e) is to be sent; and
            
73

    
    (B)  the accuracy of any e-mail address, mailing address, or telefax number to which
            
74

    
notice is to be sent.
            
75

    
    (iii)  The designated agent fulfills the notice requirement of Subsection (1)(e)(i) when
            
76

    
it sends the notice to the e-mail address, mailing address, or telefax number provided to the
            
77

    
designated agent, whether or not the notice is actually received.
            
78

    
    (iv)  Upon the filing of a notice of completion, the time periods for filing preliminary
            
79

    
notices stated in Section 

38-1-27

 are modified such that all preliminary notices shall be filed
            
80

    
subsequent to the notice of completion and within 10 days from the day on which the notice of
            
81

    
completion is filed.
            
82

    
    (f)  A subcontract that is considered an original contract for purposes of this section
            
83

    
does not create a requirement for an additional preliminary notice if a preliminary notice has
            
84

    
already been given for the labor, service, equipment, and material furnished to the
            
85

    
subcontractor who performs substantial work.

            
86


    
    (2) (a)  If a construction project owner, original contractor, subcontractor, or other
            
87

    
interested person believes that a notice of completion has been filed erroneously, that owner,
            
88

    
original contractor, subcontractor, or other interested person can request from the person who
            
89

    
filed the notice of completion evidence establishing the validity of the notice of completion.
            
90

    
    (b)  Within 10 days after the request described in Subsection (2)(a), the person who
            
91

    
filed the notice of completion shall provide the requesting person proof that the notice of
            
92

    
completion is valid.
            
93

    
    (c)  If the person that filed the notice of completion does not provide proof of the
            
94

    
validity of the notice of completion, that person shall immediately cancel the notice of
            
95

    
completion from the database in any manner prescribed by the division pursuant to rule.
            
96

    
    (3)  A person filing a notice of completion by alternate filing is responsible for
            
97

    
verifying and changing any incorrect information in the notice of completion before the
            
98

    
expiration of the time period during which the notice is required to be filed.
            
99

    
    Section 2.  
Section  
58-56-3

 is amended to read:
            
100

    
     58-56-3.  Definitions.
            
101

    
    In addition to the definitions in Section 

58-1-102

, as used in this chapter:
            
102

    
    (1) (a)  "Adopted code" means:
            
103

    
    (i) (A)  a new edition of or amendment to an existing code, including the standards and
            
104

    
specifications contained in the code, adopted by the Legislature under Section 

58-56-7

; and
            
105

    
    (B)  administered by the division under Section 

58-56-6

; and
            
106

    
    (ii)  adopted by a nationally recognized code authority, including:
            
107

    
    (A)  a building code;
            
108

    
    (B)  the National Electrical Code promulgated by the National Fire Protection
            
109

    
Association;
            
110

    
    (C)  a residential one and two family dwelling code;
            
111

    
    (D)  a plumbing code;
            
112

    
    (E)  a mechanical code;
            
113

    
    (F)  a fuel gas code;

            
114


    
    (G)  an energy conservation code; and
            
115

    
    (H)  a manufactured housing installation standard code.
            
116

    
    (b)  "Adopted code" includes:
            
117

    
    (i)  a fire code administered by the Fire Prevention Board under Section 

53-7-204

; and
            
118

    
    (ii)  a code listed in Subsection (1)(a)(ii) that was adopted or amended by the division
            
119

    
and Uniform Building Code Commission or the Fire [Protection] Prevention Board before
            
120

    
May 12, 2009.
            
121

    
    (2) (a) [(i)]  "Approved code" means a code, including the standards and specifications
            
122

    
contained in the code, approved by the division under Section 

58-56-4

 for use by a compliance
            
123

    
agency[;] and [(ii)] administered by the division under Section 

58-56-6

.
            
124

    
    (b)  "Approved code" does not include an adopted code listed under Subsection (1)(b).
            
125

    
    (3)  "Board" means the Fire Prevention Board created in Section 

53-7-203

.
            
126

    
    (4)  "Building" means a structure used or intended for supporting or sheltering any use
            
127

    
or occupancy and any improvements attached to it.
            
128

    
    (5)  "Commission" means the Uniform Building Code Commission created under this
            
129

    
chapter.
            
130

    
    (6)  "Compliance agency" means:
            
131

    
    (a)  an agency of the state or any of its political subdivisions which issues permits for
            
132

    
construction regulated under the codes;
            
133

    
    (b)  any other agency of the state or its political subdivisions specifically empowered to
            
134

    
enforce compliance with the codes; or
            
135

    
    (c)  any other state agency which chooses to enforce codes adopted under this chapter
            
136

    
by authority given the agency under a title other than Title 58, Occupations and Professions.
            
137

    
    (7)  "Factory built housing" means manufactured homes or mobile homes.
            
138

    
    (8) (a)  "Factory built housing set-up contractor" means an individual licensed by the
            
139

    
division to set up or install factory built housing on a temporary or permanent basis.
            
140

    
    (b)  The scope of the work included under the license includes the placement or
            
141

    
securing, or both placement and securing, of the factory built housing on a permanent or

            
142


    
temporary foundation, securing the units together if required, and connection of the utilities to
            
143

    
the factory built housing unit, but does not include site preparation, construction of a
            
144

    
permanent foundation, and construction of utility services to the near proximity of the factory
            
145

    
built housing unit.
            
146

    
    (c)  If a dealer is not licensed as a factory built housing set-up contractor, that
            
147

    
individual must subcontract the connection services to individuals who are licensed by the
            
148

    
division to perform those specific functions under Title 58, Chapter 55, Utah Construction
            
149

    
Trades Licensing Act.
            
150

    
    (9)  "HUD code" means the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety
            
151

    
Standards Act, 42 U.S.C. Sec. 5401 et seq.
            
152

    
    (10)  "Legislative action" means the Legislature's:
            
153

    
    (a)  adoption of a new adopted code;
            
154

    
    (b)  adoption of an amendment to an existing adopted code;
            
155

    
    (c)  repeal of an adopted code; or
            
156

    
    (d)  repeal of a provision of an adopted code.
            
157

    
    (11)  "Local regulator" means each political subdivision of the state which is
            
158

    
empowered to engage in the regulation of construction, alteration, remodeling, building,
            
159

    
repair, and other activities subject to the codes.
            
160

    
    (12) (a)  "Manufactured home" means a transportable factory built housing unit
            
161

    
constructed on or after June 15, 1976, according to the HUD Code, in one or more sections,
            
162

    
which:
            
163

    
    (i)  in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in
            
164

    
length, or when erected on site, is 400 or more square feet; and
            
165

    
    (ii)  is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or
            
166

    
without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities, and includes the
            
167

    
plumbing, heating, air-conditioning, and electrical systems.
            
168

    
    (b)  Manufactured homes constructed on or after June 15, 1976, shall be identifiable by
            
169

    
the manufacturer's data plate bearing the date the unit was manufactured and a HUD label

            
170


    
attached to the exterior of the home certifying the home was manufactured to HUD standards.
            
171

    
    (13)  "Mobile home" means a transportable factory built housing unit built prior to
            
172

    
June 15, 1976, in accordance with a state mobile home code which existed prior to the HUD
            
173

    
Code.
            
174

    
    (14)  "Modular unit" means a structure built from sections which are manufactured in
            
175

    
accordance with the construction standards adopted pursuant to Section 

58-56-4

 and
            
176

    
transported to a building site, the purpose of which is for human habitation, occupancy, or use.
            
177

    
    (15)  "Opinion" means a written, nonbinding, and advisory statement issued by the
            
178

    
commission concerning an interpretation of the meaning of the codes or the application of the
            
179

    
codes in a specific circumstance issued in response to a specific request by a party to the issue.
            
180

    
    (16)  "State regulator" means an agency of the state which is empowered to engage in
            
181

    
the regulation of construction, alteration, remodeling, building, repair, and other activities
            
182

    
subject to the codes adopted pursuant to this chapter.

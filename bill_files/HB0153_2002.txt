STANDARDS FOR ILLEGAL DRUG LAB                           DECONTAMINATION                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Sponsor   A. Lamont Tyler                             This act modifies the Environmental Quality Code to create the Illegal Drug Manufacturing                          and Storage Site Decontamination Act.  Under this new act, the local health authorities will                          oversee the identification and decontamination of controlled substances manufacturing sites.                           This act provides for notification to the local health department by a law enforcement agency                           when a drug lab site is found.  The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board will                           have rulemaking authority to set standards and license inspectors and contractors to                           decontaminate sites.  This act creates a new surcharge on ephedrine products for the purpose                           of funding the clean up of property contaminated by illegal drug manufacturing activity.                            This act takes effect on January  ,     . Title.                               This part is known as the  Illegal Drug Manufacturing and Storage Site Decontamination                          Act.                                Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                        .  Definitions.                               As used in this part                                      Authorized contractor  means a person or firm who decontaminates, demolishes, or                          disposes of contaminated property as required by this part and who is certified by the Utah Solid                          and Hazardous Waste Control Board as provided in Section                 .                                     Board  means a local board of health as established under Section       A          .                                     Certified inspector  means a person who is certified by the Utah Solid and Hazardous                          Waste Control Board to perform inspections in accordance with Section                 .                                     Commission  means the Utah State Tax Commission.                                     Contaminated  or  contamination  means polluted by hazardous materials so that the                          property is unfit for human habitation or use due to immediate or long term health hazards.                           Property that at one time was contaminated but has been satisfactorily decontaminated according                          to procedures and standards established by the Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board                          is not  contaminated.                                      Decontamination fund  means the Illegal Drug Manufacturing and Storage Site                          Decontamination Account created by Section                 .                                     Dose  means    milligrams of ephedrine products.                                     Ephedrine products  means ephedrine, pseudoephedrine, and norpseudoephedrine.                            Ephedrine products  does not include the naturally occurring product referred to as ephedra.                                     Hazardous materials  has the same meaning as  hazardous or dangerous materials                           as that term is defined in Section          d      .  For purposes of this part,  hazardous materials  shall                          include illegally manufactured controlled substances.                                      Health officer  means a local health officer authorized under Title   A, Local Health                          Authorities.                                         Property  means any property, site, structure, part of a structure, or the grounds                          surrounding the structure which is involved in the illegal manufacture or storage of hazardous                          materials.  This includes but is not limited to single family residences, out buildings, garages, units                          of multiplexes, condominiums, apartment buildings, warehouses, hotels, motels, boats, motor                          vehicles, trailers, manufactured housing, or any shop, or booth.                                      Retailer  has the same definition as found in Section                  .                               Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                        .  Local health authority regulations    Exception.                                    Local health departments may establish regulations in accordance with Title   A,                          Local Health Authorities, concerning the posting of notices, securing structures, notification of                          property owners, hearings, and  appeals.                                    Local health departments regulations shall require that those who inspect or                          decontaminate property shall be certified under the provisions of Section                 , however, an                          exception shall be made for an owner of record who indicates his intention to decontaminate his                          property personally.                                    Local health department regulations pertaining to owners who decontaminate their own                          property shall require that the owner comply with local health department regulations governing                          safety, decontamination standards, and disposal of contaminated debris.                               Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                        .  Reporting    Notice    Duties of local health officer.                                    Whenever a law enforcement agency has lawfully entered property and has reason to                          believe that property has been contaminated by hazardous materials related to the illegal                          manufacturing or consumption of controlled substances, the agency shall report the suspected                          contamination to the local health officer within    hours.                                    The local health officer shall                                 a   proceed under regulations established by the local health department pertaining to the                          posting of notices, securing structures, notification of property owners, hearings, and appeals                                 b   report property determined to be contaminated to the Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste                          Control Board  and                                c   provide information to the property owner concerning requirements for inspection,                          decontamination, and disposal of contaminated debris.                                       The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board shall keep a list of contaminated                          properties and make the list available upon request.  The list shall be promptly updated to remove                          those properties which have been decontaminated according to standards established under Section                                         .                               Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                        .  Municipality or county options.                                    If the local health officer does not receive a timely response from the owner of                          contaminated property or the owner notifies him that he intends to abandon the property, the local                          health officer shall notify the municipality, or county for unincorporated areas, in which the                          contaminated property is located.  For the purposes of this section,  timely  means a period of not                           less than    days, but may be longer in accordance with local health department regulations.                                     The municipality or county may take action to                                  a   bring an action in public nuisance against the property in accordance with the law                                  b   decontaminate the property under the provisions of this chapter  or                                 c   demolish the property under existing county or municipal ordinance.                                     The municipality or county shall notify all persons whose interest in the property is                           recorded in the records of the recorder s office of the county in which the property is located of its                           intent to take action with regard to the property.                                     A municipality or county may not bring an action in public nuisance, decontaminate,                           or demolish property pursuant to this section until all procedures granting the right of notice and                           the opportunity to appeal have been exhausted.                                     The municipality or county shall use a certified contractor if property is                           decontaminated, demolished, or removed under this section.                                Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                         .  Certification of contractors and inspectors    Denial, suspension, or                            revocation of certificate    Duties of Department of Health and Utah Solid and Hazardous                            Waste Control Board.                                     After July  ,     , an inspector or a contractor may not perform inspections,                           decontamination, demolition, or disposal work at a property determined to be contaminated under                           this part unless issued a certificate by the Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board.                                     The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board in consultation with the Utah                              Department of Health shall establish performance and certification standards for inspectors and                           contractors by rule in accordance with Title   , Chapter   a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking                           Act.                                     The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board in consultation with the                           Department of Health shall train and test, or may approve courses to train and test, inspectors and                           contractors and their employees on the essential elements in assessing and inspecting property used                           as an illegal drug manufacturing or storage site to determine                                  a   hazard reduction measures needed                                  b   techniques for adequately reducing contaminants                                  c   use of personal protective equipment                                  d   methods for proper demolition, removal, and disposal of contaminated property,                           including preparation of work plans for decontamination  and                                 e   relevant federal and state regulations.                                     Upon successful completion of the training and testing, the inspector, contractor, or                           their employees shall be certified.                                     The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board in consultation with the                           Department of Health may require the successful completion of annual refresher courses provided                           or approved by the Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board for the continued certification                           of inspectors and contractors.                                     a   The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board shall provide for reciprocal                           certification of any individual trained to engage in decontamination, demolition, or disposal work                           in another state when the prior training is shown to be substantially similar to the training required                           by the Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board.                                 b   The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board may require individuals to take                           an examination or refresher course before certification.                                     The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board may deny, suspend, or revoke a                           certificate for failure to comply with the requirements of this part or any rule adopted pursuant to                           this part.  A certificate may be denied, suspended, or revoked on any of the following grounds                                  a   failure to perform decontamination, demolition, or disposal work under the supervision                           of trained and certified personnel                                  b   failure to perform work that meets the requirements of the Utah Solid and Hazardous                              Waste Control Board                                  c   failure to properly dispose of contaminated materials  or                                 d   the certificate was obtained by error, misrepresentation, or fraud.                                     Any contractor or inspector who violates any provision of this part, or any rule, order,                           certificate, or other requirement issued or adopted under this part, is subject in a civil proceeding                           to the penalties found in Subsection                    .                                     The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board shall set fees in accordance with                           Section            .      for the issuance and renewal of certificates, the administration of examinations,                           and for the review of training courses.                                      Fees collected under Subsection     shall be deposited in the General Fund as                           dedicated credits for the administration of this section.                                Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                         .  Rules and standards    Authority to develop.                                     The Utah Solid and Hazardous Waste Control Board, in accordance with Title   ,                           Chapter   a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, shall make rules in consultation with the                           Department of Health and local health officers                                  a   to carry out the provisions of Sections                                                   b   providing standards and best management practices for the inspection and                           decontamination of property, and disposal of contaminated debris under this part                                  c   determining appropriate methods for the testing of ground water, surface water, soil,                           and septic tanks  and                                 d   to determine when testing of ground water, surface water, soil, and septic tanks may                           be required.                                     The Departments of Health and Environmental Quality shall provide technical                           assistance to local health boards and officers to carry out their duties under this part.                                     All rules developed under this part shall be consistent with other state and federal                           environmental requirements and shall balance the interests of the public and private property                           owners.                                Section  .    Section                 is enacted to read                                         .  Illegal Drug Manufacturing and Storage Site Decontamination Fund                               Creation.                                        There is created within the General Fund a restricted account known as the Illegal Drug                           Manufacturing and Storage Site Decontamination Account.                                     Funds which shall be deposited in this account include                                  a   surcharges collected under the provisions of Section                                                   b   direct appropriations by the Legislature  and                                 c   repayments and interest on loans from the account to property owners to help with                           environmental decontamination under the provisions of this part.                                     Moneys in the account may only be spent after appropriation for costs incurred by the                           Department of Health in the administration and enforcement of this part.

 LONG TITLE
            
8

    
 General Description:
            
9

    
    This bill changes the name of the Utah Telehealth Commission to the Utah Digital
            
10

    
Healthcare Commission to better reflect the role of the commission in the digitization
            
11

    
of medicine.
            
12

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
13

    
    This bill:
            
14

    
    .    amends the name of the Utah Telehealth Commission to the Utah Digital Healthcare
            
15

    
Commission.
            
16

    
 Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
            
17

    
    None
            
18

    
 Other Special Clauses:
            
19

    
    None
            
20

    
 Utah Code Sections Affected:
            
21

    
AMENDS:
            
22

    
     26-9f-101, as enacted by Chapter 263, Laws of Utah 2000
            
23

    
     26-9f-102, as enacted by Chapter 263, Laws of Utah 2000
            
24

    
     26-9f-103, as enacted by Chapter 263, Laws of Utah 2000
            
25

    
     63-55-226, as last amended by Chapters 152 and 155, Laws of Utah 2002

Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:


            
28


    
    Section 1.  
Section  
26-9f-101

 is amended to read:
            
29

    
 CHAPTER 9f.  UTAH DIGITAL HEALTHCARE COMMISSION ACT

            
30

    
     26-9f-101.  Title.
            
31

    
    This chapter is known as the "Utah [Telehealth] Digital Healthcare Commission Act."
            
32

    
    Section 2.  
Section  
26-9f-102

 is amended to read:
            
33

    
     26-9f-102.  Definitions.
            
34

    
    As used in this chapter:
            
35

    
    (1)  "Commission" means the Utah [Telehealth] Digital Healthcare Commission created
            
36

    
in Section 

26-9f-103

.
            
37

    
    (2)  "Telehealth" means the electronic transfer or exchange of medically related data for
            
38

    
diagnosis, treatment, consultation, educational, or other related purposes.
            
39

    
    Section 3.  
Section  
26-9f-103

 is amended to read:
            
40

    
     26-9f-103.  Utah Digital Healthcare Commission.
            
41

    
    (1)  There is created within the department the Utah [Telehealth] Digital Healthcare
            
42

    
Commission.
            
43

    
    (2)  The governor shall appoint 11 members to the commission with the consent of the
            
44

    
Senate, as follows:
            
45

    
    (a)  a physician who is involved in telehealth;
            
46

    
    (b)  a representative of a licensed health care facility as defined in Section 

26-21-2

;
            
47

    
    (c)  a representative of rural Utah, which may be a person nominated by an advisory
            
48

    
committee on rural health issues created pursuant to Section 

26-1-20

;
            
49

    
    (d)  a member of the public who is not involved with telehealth; and
            
50

    
    (e)  seven members:
            
51

    
    (i)  selected from a list of three nominees for each open position submitted by the
            
52

    
division over health systems improvement; and
            
53

    
    (ii)  who fall into one or more of the following categories:
            
54

    
    (A)  individuals who use telehealth in a public or private institution;
            
55

    
    (B)  individuals who use telehealth in serving medically underserved populations;
            
56

    
    (C)  nonphysician health care providers involved in telehealth;
            
57

    
    (D)  information technology professionals involved in telehealth;
            
58

    
    (E)  representatives of the health insurance industry; and


            
59


    
    (F)  telehealth consumer advocates.
            
60

    
    (3) (a)  The commission shall annually elect a chairperson from its membership.
            
61

    
    (b)  The commission shall hold meetings at least once every three months.  Meetings
            
62

    
may be held from time to time on the call of the chair or a majority of the board members.
            
63

    
    (c)  Six commission members are necessary to constitute a quorum at any meeting and,
            
64

    
if a quorum exists, the action of a majority of members present shall be the action of the
            
65

    
commission.
            
66

    
    (4) (a)  Except as provided in Subsection (4)(b), a commission member shall be
            
67

    
appointed for a three-year term and eligible for two reappointments.
            
68

    
    (b)  Notwithstanding Subsection (4)(a), the governor shall, at the time of appointment
            
69

    
or reappointment, adjust the length of terms to ensure that the terms of commission members
            
70

    
are staggered so that approximately 1/3 of the commission is appointed each year.
            
71

    
    (c)  A commission member shall continue in office until the expiration of the member's
            
72

    
term and until a successor is appointed, which may not exceed 90 days after the formal
            
73

    
expiration of the term.
            
74

    
    (d)  Notwithstanding Subsection (4)(c), a commission member who fails to attend 75%
            
75

    
of the scheduled meetings in a calendar year shall be disqualified from serving.
            
76

    
    (e)  When a vacancy occurs in membership for any reason, the replacement shall be
            
77

    
appointed for the unexpired term.
            
78

    
    (5) (a)  Board members who are not government employees may not receive
            
79

    
compensation or benefits for the services, but may receive per diem and expenses incurred in
            
80

    
the performance of their official duties at rates established by the Division of Finance under
            
81

    
Sections 

63A-3-106

 and 

63A-3-107

.
            
82

    
    (b)  A commission member may decline to receive per diem and expenses for service to
            
83

    
the commission.
            
84

    
    (6)  The department shall provide staff support to the commission.
            
85

    
    (7)  The funding of the commission shall be a separate line item to the department in
            
86

    
the annual appropriations act.
            
87

    
    Section 4.  
Section  
63-55-226

 is amended to read:
            
88

    
     63-55-226.  Repeal dates, Title 26.
            
89

    
    (1)  Title 26, Chapter 1, Department of Health Organization, is repealed July 1, 2006.

            
90


    
    (2)  Title 26, Chapter 4, Utah Medical Examiner Act, is repealed July 1, 2010.
            
91

    
    (3)  Title 26, Chapter 9f, Utah [Telehealth] Digital Healthcare Commission, is repealed
            
92

    
July 1, 2005.
            
93

    
    (4) Title 26, Chapter 10, Family Health Services, is repealed July 1, 2010.
            
94

    
    (5)  Title 26, Chapter 18, Medical Assistance Act, is repealed July 1, 2004.
            
95

    
    (6)  Title 26, Chapter 23b, Detection of Public Health Emergencies Act, is repealed July
            
96

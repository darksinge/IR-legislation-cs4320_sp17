H.B. 404





1     USE OF CIGARETTE TAX




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor:  Steve  Barth


5    AN ACT RELATING TO REVENUE AND TAXATION; CREATING A RESTRICTED

6    ACCOUNT KNOWN AS THE "CIGARETTE TAX RESTRICTED ACCOUNT";

7    REQUIRING $1,000,000 OF THE REVENUES GENERATED BY THE 1997 CIGARETTE

8    TAX INCREASE TO BE DEPOSITED ANNUALLY INTO THE ACCOUNT AND USED BY

9    THE DIVISION OF SUBSTANCE ABUSE FOR A TOBACCO PREVENTION AND

10    CONTROL MEDIA CAMPAIGN TARGETED TOWARDS CHILDREN; MAKING

11    TECHNICAL CHANGES; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.

12    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

13    AMENDS:

14         59-14-204, as last amended by Chapter 279, Laws of Utah 1997

15    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

16        Section 1.  Section  59-14-204 is amended to read:

17         59-14-204.  Tax basis -- Rate -- Future increase -- Restricted account -- Use of

18     revenues.

19        (1)  There is levied a tax upon the sale, use, or storage of cigarettes in the state.

20        (2)  The rates of the tax levied under Subsection (1) are:

21        (a) 2.575 cents on each cigarette, for all cigarettes weighing not more than three pounds

22    per thousand cigarettes; and

23        (b) 3.175 cents on each cigarette, for all cigarettes weighing in excess of three pounds per

24    thousand cigarettes.

25        (3)  The tax levied under Subsection (1) shall be paid by the manufacturer, jobber,

26    distributor, wholesaler, retailer, user, or consumer.

27        (4)  The tax rates specified in this section shall be increased by the commission by the same


1    amount as any future reduction in the federal excise tax on cigarettes.

2        (5) (a)  There is created within the General Fund a restricted account known as the

3    "Cigarette Tax Restricted Account."

4        (b)  Beginning on July 1, 1998, $1,000,000 of the revenues generated by the increase in the

5    cigarette tax under this section enacted during the 1997 Annual General Session shall be annually

6    deposited into the account.

7        (c)  The Division of Substance Abuse created in Section 62A-8-103 shall expend the funds

8    in the account for a tobacco prevention and control media campaign targeted towards children.

9        Section 2.   Effective date.

10        This act takes effect on July 1, 1998.

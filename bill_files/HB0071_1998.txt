1     ALCOHOL AND DRUG TESTING FEE




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: Don E. Bush


5    AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC SAFETY; INCREASING THE FEE FOR DRIVER

6    LICENSE REINSTATEMENT; APPLYING A PORTION OF THE FEE TO COSTS OF

7    DRUG AND ALCOHOL TESTING BY THE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH; AND

8    CREATING A RESTRICTED ACCOUNT FOR THAT FEE PORTION.

9    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

10    AMENDS:

11         53-1-117, as enacted by Chapter 290, Laws of Utah 1997

12         53-3-105, as last amended by Chapter 290, Laws of Utah 1997

13         53-3-106, as last amended by Chapter 290, Laws of Utah 1997

14    ENACTS:

15         26-1-34, Utah Code Annotated 1953

16    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

17        Section 1.  Section  26-1-34 is enacted to read:

18         26-1-34.  Restricted account created to fund drug testing for law enforcement

19     agencies.

20        (1)  There is created within the General Fund a restricted account known as the State

21    Laboratory Drug Testing Account.

22        (2)  The account consists of a specified portion of fees generated under Subsection

23    53-3-106(5) from the reinstatement of certain licenses, which shall be deposited in this account.

24        (3)  The Department of Health shall use funds in this account solely for the costs of

25    performing drug analysis tests for state and local law enforcement agencies, and may use no other

26    funds for this purpose.

27        Section 2.  Section  53-1-117 is amended to read:


1         53-1-117.   Acquisition of alcohol or drug enforcement equipment -- Rulemaking --

2     Legislative findings.

3        (1)  From monies appropriated by the Legislature and any other funds made available for

4    the purposes described under this section, the department shall assist the law enforcement agencies

5    of the state and its political subdivisions in the enforcement of alcohol or drug-related offenses.

6        (2)  In accordance with Title 63, Chapter 46a, Utah Administrative Rulemaking Act, the

7    commissioner shall make rules for granting monies and providing equipment, including drug and

8    alcohol testing equipment, to law enforcement agencies under this section based on criteria

9    established in the rules.

10        (3)  The Legislature finds that these monies are for a general and statewide public purpose.

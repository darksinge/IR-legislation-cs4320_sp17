Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read            TITLE   D.  OVERSIGHT OF GOVERNMENT ENTITIES            CHAPTER  .  INDEPENDENT PUBLIC CORPORATION ACT            Part  .  General Provisions                  D      .  Title.                   This title shall be known as  Oversight of Government Entities.                    This chapter shall be known as the  Independent Public Corporation Act.               Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read                  D      .  Definitions.              For purposes of this title                     Authorizing statutes  means the statutes creating an entity as an independent public          corporation                     Business interest  means                a   holding the position of trustee, director, officer, or other similar position with a          business entity  or               b   the ownership, either legally or equitably, by an individual, the individual s spouse, or          the individual s minor children, of at least     of the outstanding shares of a corporation or              interest in any other business entity.                    Cause  includes inefficiency, neglect of duty, malfeasance, or misfeasance in office.                    Legislative Management Committee  means the Legislative Management Committee          created in Section        .                    Oversight statutes  means                       a   Title   , Chapter  , Funds Consolidation Act               b   Title   , Chapter  , State Money Management Act               c   Title   , Chapter  , Open and Public Meetings               d   Title   , Chapter   , Budgetary Procedures Act               e   Title   , Chapter   a, Administrative Rulemaking Act               f   Title   , Chapter   b, Utah Administrative Procedures Act               g   Title   , Chapter   , Utah Procurement Code               h   Title   A, Administrative Services Code  and              i   Title   , Chapter   , Utah Personnel Management Act.                    Public funds  means monies, funds, and accounts received by an independent public          corporation from a public source.                    Private sector  means a market in which the potential for profit exists.                    Public source  means                a   the state                b   a state board, commission, institution, department, division, agency, bureau, laboratory,          or other similar instrumentality of the state  or               c   a county, municipality, school district, special district, political subdivision, or other          public body.                    State agency  means the                a   Department of Administrative Services                b   Department of Corrections                c   Department of Financial Institutions                d   Department of Alcoholic Beverage Control                e   Insurance Department                f   Public Service Commission                g   Department of Agriculture                h   Department of Human Services                i   State Board of Education                j   Department of Natural Resources                k   Department of Transportation                l   Department of Commerce                       m   Department of Workforce Services               n   State Tax Commission               o   Department of Community and Economic Development               p   Department of Health               q   National Guard               r   Department of Environmental Quality               s   Department of Public Safety               t   Department of Human Resource Management  or              u   any entity under the direct supervisory control of the                i   governor                ii   attorney general                iii   state treasurer                iv   state auditor  or               v   an agency described in this Subsection    .                     State services  means services that are supported in whole or in part by state funds,          including                a   general services provided under Section   A                      b   use of state vehicles in accordance with Title   A, Chapter  , Division of Operations          and Administration of State Motor Vehicles                c   coverage by the risk management fund created under Section   A                      d   information technology services provided by the Division of Information Technology          Services, in accordance with Title   A, Chapter  , Division of Information Technology Services          and State Information Coordinator                e   archive services provided by the Division of Archives and Records in accordance with          Title   , Chapter  , Part  , Archives and Records Service                f   state retirement benefits under Title   , Utah State Retirement Act  or               g   purchasing from state contracts.              Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read            Part  .  Creation and Operations                  D      .  Creation    Nature of independent public corporation    Legislative           findings    Board of trustees.                          An entity having the following characteristics is an independent public corporation         subject to the requirements of this chapter               a   the entity is created by the Legislature               b   the entity is not               i   part of the legislative or judicial branch               ii   a political subdivision of the state               iii   required to exist under the Utah Constitution  or              iv   a state agency  and              c   the entity is structured as a legal entity having the power to sue and be sued.                   a   An independent public corporation is                i   a government entity governed by a board of trustees appointed in accordance with this          section  and               ii   granted a corporate franchise to operate in this state to serve a specific public purpose.                b   An independent public corporation is not a state agency.                   In the authorizing statutes of an independent public corporation, the Legislature shall          state                a   a public purpose served by the public corporation                b   i   if the public corporation receives public funds, that the independent public          corporation operates in the private sector to promote a public purpose not being addressed by          private entities  or               ii   if the public corporation does not receive public funds, that to meet a public purpose          the corporation needs affiliation with the state to gain exemption from federal or state income tax           and               c   that the board of trustees shall be appointed in accordance with Subsections     and    .                   a   The governor shall appoint the board of trustees of an independent public          corporation with the advice and consent of the Senate.               b   A member of the board of trustees may be removed                i   by the governor only for cause                ii   by the board                A   only for cause  and               B   by the affirmative vote of more than     of all voting members of the board.                      c   The members of the board of trustees shall elect a chair of the board and any other         officer that the board determines is necessary.              d   The authorizing statutes of an independent public corporation shall state the number         of members of a board of trustees.                  The board of trustees are subject to fiduciary duties of a trustee under Section                 .                  Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, except for the requirements of Section           D      , the following entities are not subject to this chapter until July  ,                    a   Dairy Commission created in Title  , Chapter   , Dairy Promotion Act                b   Utah Technology Finance Corporation created in Title  , Chapter  , Part  , Utah          Technology Finance Corporation Act                c   Heber Valley Railroad Authority created in Title  , Chapter  , Part  , Heber Valley          Historic Railroad Authority                d   Utah Science Center Authority created in Title  , Chapter  , Part  , Utah Science          Center Authority                e   Utah Housing Finance Agency created in Title  , Chapter  , Part  , Utah Housing          Finance Agency                f   Utah State Fair Corporation created in Title  , Chapter  , Part   , Utah State Fair          Corporation Act  or               g   Workers  Compensation Fund of Utah created in Title   A, Chapter   , Workers           Compensation Fund                h   Utah State Retirement Office created in Title   , Chapter  , Part  , Retirement Office          and Board                i   School and Institutional Trust Lands Administration created in Title   C, Chapter  ,          Part  , School and Institutional Trust Lands Administration  and               j   Crime Victims Reparations Office created in Title   , Chapter   a, Part  , Crime          Victims  Reparations Act.              Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read                  D      .  Powers and duties.                   An independent public corporation has the powers and duties of a nonprofit corporation          established under Title   , Chapter  , Utah Nonprofit Corporation and Co Operative Association                 Act, to the extent that the powers and duties do not conflict with               a   this title  or              b   the authorizing statutes of the corporation.                  An independent public corporation may               a   hire and retain legal counsel independent of the attorney general  and              b   create, cause to be created, or have a controlling interest in a subsidiary, except a         subsidiary shall be subject to the requirements of this title to the same extent as the independent         public corporation.                  An independent public corporation may not participate in state services unless          expressly permitted by statute.              Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read                  D      .  Compliance with state laws applicable to state agencies    Exemptions.              An independent public corporation is exempt from all oversight statutes.              Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read                  D      .  Annual audit requirements    Legislative audits.                   An independent public corporation shall obtain an annual audit                a   prepared by a nationally recognized accounting firm  and               b   conducted in accordance with government auditing standards.                   The independent public corporation shall distribute the annual audit required under          Subsection     within    days of completion to the                a   state auditor                b   Division of Finance                c   Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel  and               d   legislative fiscal analyst.                   An independent public corporation is subject to audit by the legislative auditor general          pursuant to Section         .              Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read                  D      .  Funds of independent public corporation.              Monies held by an independent public corporation may not be required to be maintained          in the custody of the state treasurer.              Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read                         D      .  Conflicts of interest.             A board member of an independent public corporation may not have a business interest in         any entity doing business with the independent public corporation.             Section  .  Section    D       is enacted to read                 D      .  Termination of independent public corporation status.                  An independent public corporation s status as an independent public corporation may         not be terminated except as provided in Subsection    .                  a   Any of the following may file with the Legislative Management Committee a         request for termination of an independent public corporation s status as an independent public          corporation                i   a legislator                ii   a legislative committee                iii   the governor  or               iv   the independent public corporation.               b   The Legislative Management Committee shall assign an interim or standing committee          to review a request for termination filed under Subsection     a  and make a recommendation to          the Legislature concerning                i   whether the independent public corporation status should be terminated  and               ii   if the assigned committee determines that the independent public corporation status          should be terminated, whether the assigned committee recommends                A   dissolution                B   sale                C   merger                D   state absorption  or               E   any other disposition of the independent public corporation as seems reasonable or in          the best business or financial interests of the state or the independent public corporation.               c   As part of the review of a request for termination by the assigned committee                i   the independent public corporation shall provide the assigned committee, in a form          approved by the assigned committee, an accounting of                A   all assets held or controlled by the independent public corporation  and               B   all liabilities of the independent public corporation                       ii   the assigned committee may request any additional information from the independent         public corporation that the assigned committee considers necessary  and              iii   the assigned committee shall consider the recommendations of the               A   state treasurer  and              B   governor s Office of Planning and Budget.                  The Legislature may               a   repeal the authorizing statutes of the independent public corporation  and              b   through a joint resolution provide for the disposition of the independent public         corporation including any action provided for in Subsections     b  i  A  through  E .              Section   .  Section    D       is enacted to read                  D      .  Sunrise process.                   Any of the following may propose the creation of an independent public corporation          by submitting to the Legislative Management Committee a proposal for creation                a   a legislator                b   a legislative committee                c   the governor  or               d   an executive branch agency.                   a   The Legislative Management Committee shall assign a legislative interim or          standing committee to review a proposal to create an independent public corporation submitted          under Subsection    .               b   After reviewing the proposal to create an independent public corporation, the assigned          committee shall make a recommendation on creation and provide the recommendation to the          person who requested the review.                   If the assigned committee recommends the creation of an independent public          corporation, the assigned committee shall include in its recommendation a detailed structure for          the independent public corporation.              Section   .  Section    D       is enacted to read            CHAPTER  .  STATE OVERSIGHT OF GOVERNMENT ENTITIES                  D      .  Title.              This chapter shall be known as the  State Oversight of Government Entities.               Section   .  Section    D       is enacted to read                         D      .  Application of chapter.             Beginning July  ,     , an agency is subject to all state oversight statutes unless the         agency is expressly exempted in statute in accordance with this chapter.             Section   .  Section    D       is enacted to read                 D      .  Exemption from oversight statutes.                  An agency may seek exemption from an oversight statute by filing an application for         exemption with the Legislative Management Committee.                  The Legislative Management Committee shall assign an interim or standing committee         to review the application and make its recommendation to the Legislature.                   An agency s exemption from an oversight statute begins after                a   the assigned committee s review  and               b    the Legislature s express exemption of the entity from the oversight statute.              Section   .   Oversight of Governmental Agencies Task Force    Creation              Membership    Quorum    Compensation    Staff.                   There is created the Oversight of Governmental Agencies Task Force consisting of the          following eight members                a   three members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate, no more than          two of whom may be from the same political party  and               b   five members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the House          of Representatives, no more than three of whom may be from the same political party.                   a   The president of the Senate shall designate a member of the Senate appointed under          Subsection     a  as a cochair of the task force.               b   The speaker of the House of Representatives shall designate a member of the House          of Representatives appointed under Subsection     b  as a cochair of the task force.                   In conducting its business, the task force shall comply with the rules of legislative          interim committees.                   The task force is authorized to meet until November   ,     .                   Salaries and expenses of the members of the task force who are legislators shall be paid          in accordance with Section        and Legislative Joint Rule   .  .                   The Office of Legislative Research and General Counsel shall provide staff support          to the task force.                           The task force shall               a   review Title   D, Oversight of Governmental Entities               b   make a recommendation for each entity listed in Subsection     as to whether the entity         should               i   have the entity s authorizing states repealed and the relationship to the state terminated         in accordance with Section   D                     ii   be restructured as an executive branch entity  or              iii   have the entity s authorizing states amended to bring into conformance with Title   D,         Chapter  , Independent Public Corporation Act  and               c   recommend legislation to                i   if necessary, refine Title   D, Oversight of Governmental Entities                ii   implement the recommendations made under Subsection     b   and               iii   make any changes to the Utah Code that are necessary to implement Title   D.

TRANSPORTATION CORRIDOR ACQUISITION,           PRESERVATION, AND REGULATION                  GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Marda Dillree          AN ACT RELATING TO CITIES AND TOWNS AND COUNTIES  AUTHORIZING         MUNICIPALITIES AND COUNTIES TO ENACT TEMPORARY REGULATIONS         PROHIBITING DEVELOPMENT IN AREAS DESIGNATED FOR STUDY AS         TRANSPORTATION CORRIDOR  AND MAKING TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS.          This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows           AMENDS                        , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah                             , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section           is amended to read                        .  Temporary regulations.                   a    The  A municipal legislative body may, without a public hearing, enact           ordinances  an ordinance establishing a temporary zoning  regulations  regulation for any part or          all of the area within the municipality if                i   the legislative body makes a finding of compelling, countervailing public interest  or               ii   the area is unzoned.               b    Those  A temporary zoning  regulations  regulation under Subsection     a  may          prohibit or regulate the erection, construction, reconstruction, or alteration of any building or          structure or subdivision approval.                   The municipal legislative body shall establish a period of limited effect for the           ordinances  ordinance not to exceed six months.                   a   A municipal legislative body may, without a public hearing, enact an ordinance          establishing a temporary zoning regulation prohibiting construction, subdivision approval, and         other development activities within an area that is the subject of an Environmental Impact         Statement or a Major Investment Study examining the area as a proposed highway or         transportation corridor.              b   A zoning regulation under Subsection     a                i   may not exceed six months in duration               ii   may be renewed for up to two additional six month periods by ordinance enacted         before the expiration of the previous zoning regulation  and              iii   notwithstanding Subsections     b  i  and  ii , is effective only as long as the          Environmental Impact Statement or Major Investment Study is in progress.

EVIDENCE   ACCESS TO PATIENT RECORDS                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor  Greg J. Curtis          AN ACT RELATING TO JUDICIAL CODE  CLARIFYING THAT A PATIENT IS ENTITLED         TO OBTAIN THE PATIENT S MEDICAL RECORDS WITHOUT ATTORNEY         ASSISTANCE  AND MAKING TECHNICAL AMENDMENTS.         This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows          REPEALS AND REENACTS                        , as enacted by Chapter    , Laws of Utah               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah               Section  .  Section           is repealed and reenacted to read                        .  Patients  records    Authorized requester    Inspection and copying.                   As used in this section                a    authorized requester  means                i   the patient  or               ii   an attorney at law who is                A   duly licensed to practice in the state  and               B   authorized to represent the interest of a patient of any health provider  and               b    health provider  means any physician, surgeon, dentist, osteopathic physician,          registered nurse, psychologist, chiropractor, or licensed hospital that has the custody or control of          a patient s records.                   Any authorized requester shall be entitled to examine or to obtain copies of any of the          patient s records from a health provider.                   The patient s records shall remain in the possession of the health provider  however,          the health provider shall make the patient s records available, at the health provider s regular place          of business, for inspection and copying by the authorized requester.  If the authorized requester is          an attorney, the attorney shall submit to the health provider a written authorization signed and         acknowledged before a notary public by               a   the patient               b   a parent or guardian in the case of a minor  or              c   the personal representative or an heir in the case of a deceased patient.                  Copying costs may be charged by the health provider to               a   the patient if the patient makes the request  or              b   the requesting attorney, as part of the costs advanced on behalf of the attorney s client.      Legislative Review Note      as of                 PM    A limited legal review of this bill raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

REQUIREMENTS FOR CONCEALED FIREARM PERMIT                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Lowell A. Nelson          AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC SAFETY  MODIFYING ISSUANCE REQUIREMENTS          PRESCRIBING THAT INSTRUCTORS BE CERTIFIED  OUTLINING CERTIFICATION         REQUIREMENTS  REQUIRING THE DIVISION TO KEEP STATISTICS AND         DISSEMINATE CERTAIN INFORMATION ANNUALLY TO INSTRUCTORS          PROVIDING A FEE FOR REISSUANCE OF LOST PERMIT  AND PERMITTING A FEE          TO BE CHARGED FOR USAGE OF FIRING RANGES. Section           is amended to read                        .  Division duties    Permit to carry concealed firearm    Requirements for           issuance    Violation    Appeal procedure.                   The division or its designated agent shall issue a permit to carry a concealed firearm          for lawful self defense to an applicant who is    years of age or older within    days after          receiving an application and upon proof that the person applying is of good character.  The permit          is valid throughout the state, without restriction, for two years.                   An applicant satisfactorily demonstrates good character if he                a   has not been convicted of a felony                 b   has not been convicted of any crime of violence               c   has not been convicted of any offenses involving the use of alcohol               d   has not been convicted of any offense involving the unlawful use of narcotics or other         controlled substances               e   has not been convicted of any offenses involving moral turpitude                f   has not been convicted of any offense involving domestic violence  and              g   has not been adjudicated by a court of a state or of the United States as mentally         incompetent, unless the adjudication has been withdrawn or reversed.                  a   The division may deny a concealed firearm permit if the licensing authority has          reasonable cause to believe that the applicant has been or is a danger to self or others as          demonstrated by evidence including, but not limited to                i   past pattern of behavior involving unlawful violence or threats of unlawful violence           or               ii   past participation in incidents involving unlawful violence or threats of unlawful          violence.               b   In determining whether the applicant has been or is a danger to self or others, the          division may inspect                i   expunged records of arrests and convictions of adults as provided in Section                    and               ii   juvenile court records as provided in Section     a    .                   A former peace officer who departs full time employment as a peace officer, in an          honorable manner, shall be issued a concealed firearm permit within five years of that departure          if the officer meets the requirements of this section.                   In assessing good character under Subsection    , the  licensing authority shall consider          mitigating circumstances.                   The licensing authority shall also require the applicant to provide                a   letters of character reference                b   two recent dated photographs                c   two sets of fingerprints                d   a five year employment history                e   a five year residential history  and                      f   evidence of general familiarity with the types of firearms to be concealed as defined         in Subsection    .                  a   General familiarity with the types of firearms to be concealed includes               i   training in                i    A   the safe loading, unloading, storage, and carrying of the types of firearms to be         concealed  and               ii    B   current laws defining lawful use of a firearm by a private citizen, including         lawful self defense, use of deadly force, transportation, and concealment .   and              ii   training in the fundamentals of shooting to include firing of the type of firearms to be          concealed in practical range exercises and in determination of proficiency as specified by the          division pursuant to Section         . .               b   Evidence of general familiarity with the types of firearms to be concealed is satisfied          by one of the following                i   completion of a course of instruction conducted by any national, state, or local firearms          training organization  approved  certified by the division pursuant to Section         .   or               ii   certification of general familiarity by a person who has been  approved  certified by          the division pursuant to Section         . , which may include a law enforcement officer, military          or civilian firearms instructor, or hunter safety instructor   or .                iii   equivalent experience with a firearm through participation in an organized shooting          competition, law enforcement, or military service.                    In issuing a permit under this part, the licensing authority is not vicariously liable for          damages caused by the permit holder.                   If any person knowingly and willfully provides false information on an application          filed under this part, he is guilty of a class B misdemeanor.  Upon conviction, if a decision on the          application is pending, the application shall be denied  if the permit has been issued, it shall be          immediately revoked.                    a   In the event of a denial or revocation by the agency, the applicant may, within             days of the date the denial or revocation is received by the applicant, file a petition for review with          the board.               b   The denial of a permit shall be in writing and shall include the general reasons for the          action.                      c   If an applicant appeals his denial to the review board, the applicant may have access         to the evidence upon which the denial is based in accordance with Title   , Chapter  , Government         Records Access and Management Act.             Section  .  Section          .  is enacted to read                       . .  Certification of instructors.                  For purposes of this part,  instructor  means a person certified as a concealed firearm         permit instructor under this section.                  The division, or a qualified person designated by the division who is not an employee         of the division, shall hold training classes and set qualification standards for  instructors that          include                a   all of the familiarity requirements imposed on concealed firearm permit holders in          Section                         b   firearm proficiency requirements  and               c   firing range exercises or approved course of live fire.                   a   The qualification standards and examinations of instructors for certification may          not exceed the level of information provided in the training classes for instructors.               b   To certify, an instructor applicant shall pass the qualification standards and          examinations with a score of     or higher.                   a   For certification of each instructor applicant, the division, or the person designated          under Subsection    , shall determine                i   the training time required not to exceed    hours  and               ii   training and application fees, not to exceed the cost of providing the program or fees          assessed by other local training organizations for equivalent training time.               b   Attendance at instructor training can be waived for an instructor applicant who can          pass the examination prescribed in Subsection    .                   The instructor certification classes shall be offered at least every six months provided          there are pending instructor applications.                   An instructor must recertify every five years.                   The division shall maintain and make available to the public a list of certified          instructors.              Section  .

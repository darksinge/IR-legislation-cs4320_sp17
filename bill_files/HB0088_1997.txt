TOURISM MARKETING PERFORMANCE FUND                 GENERAL SESSION          STATE OF UTAH           Sponsor   Sheryl L. Allen          AN ACT RELATING TO COMMUNITY AND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT  CREATING         THE TOURISM MARKETING PERFORMANCE FUND  PROVIDING DEFINITIONS          PROVIDING CRITERIA TO DETERMINE ECONOMIC GROWTH IN THE TRAVEL AND         TOURISM INDUSTRY  PROVIDING FOR ALLOCATION OF FUNDS TO THE DIVISION         OF TRAVEL DEVELOPMENT  CREATING THE FUND S COMMITTEE, ITS          MEMBERSHIP AND PURPOSE  AND UTILIZING SURPLUS GENERAL FUND MONIES. Section           is enacted to read                        .  Purpose.              The Legislature finds and declares that the development of travel and tourism in Utah is          a state public purpose necessary to assure the welfare of its citizens, the growth of its economy,          and adequate employment of its citizens.              Section  .  Section           is enacted to read                        .  Definitions.              As used in this part                     Administrator  means the Department of Community and Economic Development.                    Commissioners  means the board of commissioners on the Division of Travel         Development.                   Committee  means the Tourism Marketing Performance Fund Committee as created         in Section         .                   Department  means the Department of Community and Economic Development.                   Fund  means the restricted account known as the Tourism Marketing Performance         Fund created in Section         .                   Industry  means the travel and tourism industry.                   Institutional advertising  means advertising of Utah s cultural, historical, recreational,          scenic, and tourist attractions to consumers outside of Utah.                    Performance measurement  means a standard to measure the economic growth in the          travel and tourism industry in comparison with the previous year s sales tax receipts based on          criteria as provided in Section         . Section           is enacted to read                        .  Creation of Tourism Marketing Performance Fund Committee    Members              Appointment    Qualifications   Terms    Quorum    Duties    Per diem and expenses.                          There is created a Tourism Marketing Performance Fund Committee which shall         consist of    members.                  The members of the committee shall include               a   five members appointed by the Board of Commissioners of the Division of Travel         Development, three of whom are members of the Board of Commissioners  and              b      members of a private coalition of the industry known as the Utah Tourism Industry         Coalition recommended by the board of directors of the coalition.                  The governor shall appoint the committee members in Subsection     b .                  The first committee appointments shall be for terms as follows                a   seven members shall be appointed for terms of two years                b   seven members shall be appointed for terms of three years  and               c   seven members shall be appointed for terms of four years.                   If a vacancy occurs, the Board of Commissioners or the governor shall appoint a          qualified replacement to fill the unexpired term.                   The committee shall elect a chair and a vice chair from its membership.                   Eleven members of the committee shall constitute a quorum.                   The committee shall advise the administrator on the recommended allocation of the          monies in the fund as provided in Section         .  The administrator shall distribute the monies          in the fund to the Division of Travel Development.                   The committee shall determine the time and place of meetings and any other          procedural matter not specified in this chapter.                    Members shall receive no compensation or benefits for their services, and shall          receive no per diem and expenses incurred in the performance of the member s official duties.

UTAH MISSING PERSON CLEARINGHOUSE                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                           Sponsor  Gary F. Cox                           AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC SAFETY  APPROPRIATING    ,    FROM THE                          GENERAL FUND TO THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SAFETY FOR OPERATING                          COSTS OF THE UTAH MISSING PERSON CLEARINGHOUSE.                          This act enacts uncodified material.                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .   Appropriation.                                    Except as provided in H.B.  , Appropriations Coordination Act, there is appropriated                          from the General Fund to the Department of Public Safety    ,   , to be applied to the operation                          of the Utah Missing Person Clearinghouse, which is within the Criminal Investigations and                          Technical Services Division.                                   This appropriation shall be used to provide for the following purposes as necessary to                          operate the clearinghouse and to effectively serve the citizens of Utah                                a   one full time employee                                b   daily expenses, including postage, telephone and fax, printing and binding, and                          promotional items                                c   communication equipment, including toll free line, telephone and fax, voice mail,                          pager, and long distance charges                                d   data processing services                                e   travel to provide public and law enforcement agency education and to attend                          conferences                                f   inservice training  and                               g   computer equipment upgrades.        Legislative Review Note      as of                PM     A limited legal review of this legislation raises no obvious constitutional or statutory concerns.

H.B. 197









1     TRAFFIC OFFENSE TRIAL PROCESS




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: Nora B. Stephens


5    AN ACT RELATING TO JUDICIAL CODE; ELIMINATING TRIAL BY JURY IN CLASS B

6    OR CLASS C MISDEMEANOR OR INFRACTION TRAFFIC OFFENSES.

7    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

8    AMENDS:

9         78-46-5, as repealed and reenacted by Chapter 198, Laws of Utah 1996

10    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

11        Section 1.  Section  78-46-5 is amended to read:

12         78-46-5.  Trial by jury.  

13        (1)  A trial jury shall consist of:

14        (a)  twelve persons in a capital case;

15        (b)  eight persons in a criminal case which carries a term of incarceration of more than one

16    year as a possible sentence for the most serious offense charged;

17        (c)  six persons in a criminal case which carries a term of incarceration of more than six

18    months but not more than one year as a possible sentence for the most serious offense charged;

19        (d)  four persons in a criminal case which carries a term of incarceration of six months or

20    less as a possible sentence for the most serious offense charged; and

21        (e)  eight persons in a civil case at law except that the jury shall be four persons in a civil

22    case for damages of less than $20,000, exclusive of costs, interest, and attorney fees.

23        (2)  Except in the trial of a capital offense, the parties may stipulate upon the record to a

24    jury of a lesser number than established by this section.

25        (3)  The verdict in a criminal case shall be unanimous.  The verdict in a civil case shall be

26    by not less than three-fourths of the jurors.

27        (4)  There is no jury in the trial of small claims cases.


1        (5)  There is no jury in the adjudication of a minor charged with what would constitute a

2    crime if committed by an adult.

3        (6)  There is no jury in the trial of any class B or class C misdemeanor or infraction charged

4    under Title 41, Motor Vehicles, or under other traffic offense, including local ordinances.

H.B. 206









1     ECO-TERRORISM PROHIBITION AND


2     PENALTIES




3    1998 GENERAL SESSION


4    STATE OF UTAH


5     Sponsor:  Dennis H. Iverson


6    AN ACT RELATING TO THE CRIMINAL CODE; MAKING IT A CRIME TO OBSTRUCT

7    OR IMPEDE THE LAWFUL MANAGEMENT OF FOREST, MINING, OR

8    AGRICULTURAL ACTIVITIES OR TO SOLICIT OR CONSPIRE WITH ANYONE TO DO

9    SO; AND PROVIDING A PENALTY.

10    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

11    ENACTS:

12         76-4-205, Utah Code Annotated 1953

13         76-6-108, Utah Code Annotated 1953

14    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

15        Section 1.  Section  76-4-205 is enacted to read:

16         76-4-205.   Conspiracy or solicitation to halt or impede forest, mining, or agricultural

17     practices.

18        A person who solicits or conspires with any other person to commit any crime against

19    property or person with the specific intent to halt, impede, obstruct, or interfere with the lawful

20    management, cultivation, or harvesting of trees or timber, or the management or operations of

21    agricultural or mining industries is guilty of a third degree felony.

22        Section 2.  Section  76-6-108 is enacted to read:

23         76-6-108.   Obstruction of forest, mining, or agricultural activities.

24        (1)  A person who commits any crime against property or persons with the specific intent

25    to halt, impede, obstruct, or interfere with the lawful management, cultivation, or harvesting of

26    trees or timber, or the management or operation of agricultural or mining industries is guilty of a

27    third degree felony.


1        (2)  A peaceful demonstration, not involving civil disobedience or criminal action, is not

2    a violation of this section.

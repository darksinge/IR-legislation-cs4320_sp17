 COHABITANT DEFINITION

2013 GENERAL SESSION

            
3

    
STATE OF UTAH

            
4

    


 Chief Sponsor:  Brad R. Wilson



            
5

    



Senate Sponsor: 






  Jerry W. Stevenson

 LONG TITLE
            
8

    
 General Description:
            
9

    
    This bill modifies the Judicial Code by amending the definition of a cohabitant.
            
10

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
11

    
    This bill:
            
12

    
    .    includes in the definition of "cohabitant" a person who not only has one or more
            
13

    
children in common with the other party, but also a person who had one or more
            
14

    
children in common with the other party.
            
15

    
 Money Appropriated in this Bill:
            
16

    
    None
            
17

    
 Other Special Clauses:
            
18

    
    None
            
19

    
 Utah Code Sections Affected:
            
20

    
AMENDS:
            
21

    
     78B-7-102, as renumbered and amended by Laws of Utah 2008, Chapter 3
            
22

    

            
23

    
Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
            
24

    
    Section 1.  
Section  
78B-7-102

 is amended to read:
            
25

    
     78B-7-102.  Definitions.
            
26

    
    As used in this chapter:
            
27

    
    (1)  "Abuse" means intentionally or knowingly causing or attempting to cause a

            
28


    
cohabitant physical harm or intentionally or knowingly placing a cohabitant in reasonable fear
            
29

    
of imminent physical harm.
            
30

    
    (2)  "Cohabitant" means an emancipated person pursuant to Section 

15-2-1

 or a person
            
31

    
who is 16 years of age or older who:
            
32

    
    (a)  is or was a spouse of the other party;
            
33

    
    (b)  is or was living as if a spouse of the other party;
            
34

    
    (c)  is related by blood or marriage to the other party;
            
35

    
    (d)  has or had one or more children in common with the other party;
            
36

    
    (e)  is the biological parent of the other party's unborn child; or
            
37

    
    (f)  resides or has resided in the same residence as the other party.
            
38

    
    (3)  Notwithstanding Subsection (2), "cohabitant" does not include:
            
39

    
    (a)  the relationship of natural parent, adoptive parent, or step-parent to a minor; or
            
40

    
    (b)  the relationship between natural, adoptive, step, or foster siblings who are under 18
            
41

    
years of age.
            
42

    
    (4)  "Court clerk" means a district court clerk.
            
43

    
    (5)  "Domestic violence" means the same as that term is defined in Section 

77-36-1

.
            
44

    
    (6)  "Ex parte protective order" means an order issued without notice to the defendant in
            
45

    
accordance with this chapter.
            
46

    
    (7)  "Foreign protection order" is as defined in Section 

78B-7-302

.
            
47

    
    (8)  "Law enforcement unit" or "law enforcement agency" means any public agency
            
48

    
having general police power and charged with making arrests in connection with enforcement
            
49

    
of the criminal statutes and ordinances of this state or any political subdivision.
            
50

    
    (9)  "Peace officer" means those persons specified in Title 53, Chapter 13, Peace
            
51

    
Officer Classifications.
            
52

    
    (10)  "Protective order" means an order issued pursuant to this chapter subsequent to a
            
53

    
hearing on the petition, of which the petitioner and respondent have been given notice in
            
54

    
accordance with this chapter.

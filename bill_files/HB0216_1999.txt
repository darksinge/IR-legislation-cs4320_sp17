APPOINTING SCHOOL SUPERINTENDENTS                           AND BUSINESS ADMINISTRATORS                                  GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                           Sponsor   Judy Ann Buffmire                           AN ACT RELATING TO PUBLIC EDUCATION  PROVIDING PROCEDURES FOR THE                          APPOINTMENT OF LOCAL SCHOOL SUPERINTENDENTS AND BUSINESS                          ADMINISTRATORS IN THE EVENT OF VACANCIES DURING THEIR TERMS OF                          APPOINTMENT  AND MAKING CERTAIN TECHNICAL CHANGES.                           This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated      as follows                            AMENDS                                  A      , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah                                      A      , as last amended by Chapter   , Laws of Utah                               Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section      A           is amended to read                                   A      .  Superintendent of schools    Appointment    Term    Qualifications                              Compensation.                                      A local school board shall appoint a district superintendent of schools who serves as                           the board s chief executive officer.                                     The appointment shall occur at the board s first meeting in June.  The superintendent s                           term of office begins July   and is for up to two years  or  and until a successor is appointed and                           qualified.                                    If it becomes necessary to begin a term at a time other than as provided in Subsection                              due to a vacancy in the office of superintendent, then the board shall make an appointment                          during a public meeting for a term not to exceed the remainder of the vacated term.                                    The superintendent shall hold an administrative supervisory certificate issued by the                           State Board of Education.                                             The board shall set the superintendent s compensation for services.                                          The superintendent qualifies for office by taking the constitutional oath of office.                                Section  .    Section      A           is amended to read                                   A      .  Business administrator    Term    Oath and bond.                                      Each board shall appoint a business administrator  whose .                                    The business administrator s term of office begins July  , and is for up to two years and                           until a successor is appointed and qualified.                                    If it becomes necessary to begin a term at a time other than as provided in Subsection                              due to a vacancy in the office of business administrator, then the board shall make an                          appointment during a public meeting for a term not to exceed the remainder of the vacated term.                                          The business administrator qualifies for office by taking the constitutional oath                           of office and by posting any required bond or undertaking.

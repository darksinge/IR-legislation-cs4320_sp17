Olsen          AN ACT RELATING TO STATE AFFAIRS IN GENERAL  ESTABLISHING ADDITIONAL         PRIVATE PROPERTY PROTECTIONS  REQUIRING GOVERNMENT ENTITIES TO         ANALYZE TAKINGS AND COMPENSATE PROPERTY OWNERS FOR THEM  AND         MAKING TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS. Section       b     is enacted to read            CHAPTER   b.  PRIVATE PROPERTY REGULATORY FAIRNESS ACT            Part  .  General Provisions                     b    .  Title.              This chapter is known as the  Private Property Regulatory Fairness Act.               Section  .  Section       b     is enacted to read                     b    .  Definitions.              As used in this chapter                     Full compensation  means the reduction in the fair market value of the portion or         parcel of property taken for general public use that is attributable to the regulation or restraint         measured as of the date of adoption of the regulation or imposition of restraint on the use of private         property.                   Governmental entity  means Utah, state agencies, agencies and commissions funded         fully or partially by the state, counties, municipalities, school districts, special districts, and other         political subdivisions.                   Nongovernmental entity  means an entity that is not a governmental entity.                   Private property  includes                a  land                b   any interest in land or improvements on land                c   any proprietary water right  and               d   any crops, forest products, or resources capable of being harvested or extracted that are          owned by a person or a nongovernmental entity and are protected by either the Utah Constitution          or the Fifth or Fourteenth Amendments to the U.S. Constitution.                    Restraint of land use  means any action, requirement, or restriction by a governmental          entity, other than actions to prevent or abate public nuisances, that limits the use or development          of private property.              Section  .  Section       b     is enacted to read            Part  .  Analysis and Compensation for Takings                     b    .  Written analysis required.                   A regulation of private property or restraint of land use by a governmental entity is          prohibited unless a statement containing a full analysis of the total economic impact on private          property of the regulation or restraint is prepared by the entity and made available to the public at          least    days before adoption of the regulation or imposition of the restraint.                   The statement shall identify the manner in which the proposed action will substantially          advance the purpose of protecting public health and safety against identified public health or safety          risks created by the use of private property, and analyze the economic impact of all reasonable          alternatives to the regulation or restraint.                   If the governmental entity chooses to adopt a proposed regulation or restraint on the          use of private property, the governmental entity shall adopt the regulation or restraint that has the                 least possible impact on private property and still accomplishes the necessary public purpose.             Section  .  Section       b     is enacted to read                    b    .  Takings    Defined    Compensation required.                  A portion or parcel of private property shall be considered to have been taken for         general public use when               a   a governmental entity regulates or imposes a restraint of land use on such portion or         parcel of property for public benefit including wetlands, fish or wildlife habitat, buffer zone, or         other public benefit designations  and              b   no public nuisance will be created absent the regulation.                   a   When private property is taken for general public use, the governmental entity shall          pay full compensation of reduction in value to the owner.               b   If the governmental entity fails to pay full compensation of reduction in value to the          owner within the time required by this section, the use of the land by the owner may not be          restricted because of the regulation or restraint.               c   The jurisdiction may not require waiving this compensation as a condition of approval          of use or another permit, nor as a condition of subdivision of land.                   The governmental entity shall pay the compensation required by this section to the          owner of the private property within three months of the adoption of a regulation of restraint that          results in a taking for general public use.                   A governmental entity may not deflate the value of property by suggesting or          threatening a designation to avoid full compensation to the owner.                   A governmental entity that places restrictions on the use of public or private property          that deprive a landowner of access to his property shall either                a   provide alternative access to the property at the governmental entity s expense  or               b   purchase the inaccessible property.                   The county assessor shall                a   adjust property valuation for tax purposes                b   notify the owner of the new tax valuation  and               c   ensure that the new property valuation is reflected and identified in the next tax          assessment notice.                   The state shall provide other governmental entities with the total compensation owed                 to a property owner for any action that restricts the use of property when the action is mandated         by state law or by a state agency.                  Claims for compensation as a result of a taking of private property under this chapter         must be brought within ten years.             Section  .  Section       b     is enacted to read                    b    .  Prohibited charges.             A governmental entity may not require any private property owner to provide or pay for         any studies, maps, plans, or reports used in decisions to consider restricting the use of private         property for public use.              Section  .  Section       b     is enacted to read            Part  .  Enforcement                     b    .  Interpretation and application.              This chapter is intended to provide remedies to property owners in addition to any          constitutional rights under the state and federal constitutions and is not intended to restrict or          replace any constitutional rights.              Section  .  Section       b     is enacted to read                     b    .  Enforcement    Attorney fees.                   This chapter may be enforced in Superior Court against any governmental entity that          fails to comply with the provisions of this chapter by any owner of property subject to the          jurisdiction of that governmental entity.                   Each prevailing plaintiff may recover the costs of litigation, including reasonable          attorney s fees.

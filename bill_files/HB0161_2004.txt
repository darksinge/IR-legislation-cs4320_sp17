 LONG TITLE

 General Description:
            
8

    
    This bill updates public safety law references regarding federal homeland security
            
9

    
agents and officers operating during the Olympics.
            
10

    
 Highlighted Provisions:
            
11

    
    This bill:
            
12

    
    .    updates the reference to federal Customs Service agents to refer to Department of
            
13

    
Homeland Security agents; and
            
14

    
    .    removes references to "federal Olympic officers" that are no longer applicable.
            
15

    
 Monies Appropriated in this Bill:
            
16

    
    None

 Other Special Clauses:

    None

 Utah Code Sections Affected:

22

    

            
23

    
Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:
            
24

    
    Section 1.  
Section  
53-13-106

 is amended to read:
            
25

    
     53-13-106.  Federal officers -- State law enforcement authority.
            
26

    
    (1) (a)  "Federal officer" includes:
            
27

    
    (i)  a special agent of the Federal Bureau of Investigation;

            
28


    
    (ii)  a special agent of the United States Secret Service;
            
29

    
    (iii)  a special agent of the United States [Customs Service] Department of Homeland
            
30

    
Security, excluding a customs inspector or detention removal officer;
            
31

    
    (iv)  a special agent of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms;
            
32

    
    (v)  a special agent of the Federal Drug Enforcement Agency;
            
33

    
    (vi)  a United States marshal, deputy marshal, and special deputy United States marshal;
            
34

    
and
            
35

    
    (vii)  a U.S. Postal Inspector of the United States Postal Inspection Service.
            
36

    
    (b)  Notwithstanding Subsection (2), federal officers listed in Subsection (1)(a) have
            
37

    
statewide law enforcement authority relating to felony offenses under the laws of this state.
            
38

    
    (c)  The council may designate other federal peace officers, as necessary, if the officers:
            
39

    
    (i)  are persons employed full-time by the United States government as federally
            
40

    
recognized law enforcement officers primarily responsible for the investigation and
            
41

    
enforcement of the federal laws;
            
42

    
    (ii)  have successfully completed formal law enforcement training offered by an agency
            
43

    
of the federal government consisting of not less than 400 hours; and
            
44

    
    (iii)  maintain in-service training in accordance with the standards set forth in Section
            
45

    

53-13-103

.
            
46

    
    (2) Except as otherwise provided under Title 63, Chapter 8, Federal Jurisdiction, and
            
47

    
Title 77, Chapter 9,  Uniform Act on Fresh Pursuit, a federal officer may exercise state law
            
48

    
enforcement authority only if:
            
49

    
    (a)  the state law enforcement agencies and county sheriffs with jurisdiction enter into
            
50

    
an  agreement with the federal agency to be given authority; and
            
51

    
    (b)  except as provided in Subsection (3) [of this section], each federal officer employed
            
52

    
by the federal agency meets the waiver requirements set forth in Section 

53-6-206

.
            
53

    
    (3)  A federal officer working as such in the state on or before July 1, 1995, may
            
54

    
exercise state law enforcement authority without meeting the waiver requirement.
            
55

    
    (4)  At any time, consistent with any contract with a federal agency, a state or local law
            
56

    
enforcement authority may withdraw state law enforcement authority from any individual
            
57

    
federal officer by sending written notice to the federal agency and to the division.
            
58

    
    (5)  The authority of a federal officer under this section is limited to the jurisdiction of

            
59


    
the authorizing state or local agency, and may be further limited by the state or local agency to
            
60

    
enforcing specific statutes, codes, or ordinances.
            
61

    
    [(6) (a)  As used in this section, "Federal Olympic Officer" means any federally sworn
            
62

    
law enforcement officer operating under the control of the United States Secret Service or the
            
63

    
Olympic Public Safety Command created in Section 

53-12-201

, beginning on January 1, 2002,
            
64

    
and ending at midnight on March 30, 2002.]
            
65

    
    [(b)  Federal Olympic Officers are exempt from the application of the provisions in
            
66

    
Subsection (1)(c), Subsection (2), and Subsection (3) of this section.]
            
67

    
    [(c)  To the extent permitted by federal law or regulation, and not withstanding any
            
68

    
other provision of this section, Federal Olympic Officers shall have full-spectrum peace officer
            
69

    
authority in Utah as though the officer or agent were an employee of the state, as provided in
            
70

    
Subsection 

53-13-103

(3)(b).]

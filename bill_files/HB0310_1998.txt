H.B. 310





1     WRONGFUL APPROPRIATION




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor: Bryan D. Holladay


5    AN ACT RELATING TO THE CRIMINAL CODE; CREATING THE OFFENSE OF

6    WRONGFUL APPROPRIATION; AND PROVIDING PENALTIES.

7    This act affects sections of Utah Code Annotated 1953 as follows:

8    ENACTS:

9         76-6-404.5, Utah Code Annotated 1953

10    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

11        Section 1.  Section  76-6-404.5 is enacted to read:

12         76-6-404.5.  Wrongful appropriation -- Penalties.

13        (1)  A person commits wrongful appropriation if he obtains or exercises unauthorized

14    control over the property of another, without the consent of the owner or legal custodian and with

15    intent to temporarily appropriate, possess, or use the property or to temporarily deprive the owner

16    or legal custodian of possession of the property.

17        (2)  The consent of the owner or legal custodian of the property to its control by the actor

18    is not presumed or implied because of the owner's or legal custodian's consent on a previous

19    occasion to the control of the property by any person.

20        (3)  Wrongful appropriation is punishable one degree lower than theft, as provided in

21    Section 76-6-412, so that a violation which would have been:

22        (a)  a second degree felony under Section 76-6-412 if it had been theft is a third degree

23    felony if it is wrongful appropriation;

24        (b)  a third degree felony under Section 76-6-412 if it had been theft is a class A

25    misdemeanor if it is wrongful appropriation;

26        (c)  a class A misdemeanor under Section 76-6-412 if it had been theft is a class B

27    misdemeanor if it is wrongful appropriation;


1        (d)  a class B misdemeanor under Section 76-6-412 if it had been theft is a class C

2    misdemeanor if it is wrongful appropriation; and

3        (e)  an act of unauthorized control of motor vehicles, trailers, or semitrailers which does

4    not constitute theft is punishable under Section 41-1a-1311.

H.B. 399





1     ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY STUDY




2    1998 GENERAL SESSION


3    STATE OF UTAH


4     Sponsor:  Daniel H. Tuttle


5    AN ACT RELATING TO ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY; REQUIRING THE DEPARTMENT

6    OF ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY TO STUDY PERCHLORATE CONTAMINATION OF

7    DRINKING WATER AND TO REPORT ITS FINDINGS TO A LEGISLATIVE INTERIM

8    COMMITTEE; AND PROVIDING A REPEAL DATE.

9    This act enacts uncodified material.

10    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah:

11        Section 1.   Study of perchlorate contamination of ground water.

12        The Department of Environmental Quality shall study, review, and make findings and

13    recommendations concerning:

14        (1)  the magnitude and extent of perchlorate contamination of ground water, aquifers, and

15    drinking water systems in selected areas within the state as determined by the Department of

16    Environmental Quality;

17        (2)  research being conducted on perchlorate contamination and remedial measures by the

18    Environmental Protection Agency and others;

19        (3)  research being conducted by the U.S.  Air Force, the Environmental Protection

20    Agency, and others on the health effects of perchlorate in drinking water;

21        (4)  acceptable standards for concentration levels of perchlorate in drinking water adopted

22    or under review by other states or the federal government; and

23        (5)  the acceptable concentration of perchlorate in drinking water to be considered for

24    adoption by rule by the Safe Drinking Water Board.

25        Section 2.   Report to the Interim Committee.

26        The Department of Environmental Quality shall report its findings and recommendations

27    to the Natural Resources, Agriculture, and Environment Interim Committee at or before the


1    committee's October, 1998, meeting.

2        Section 3.   Repeal date.

3        This act is repealed October 31, 1998.

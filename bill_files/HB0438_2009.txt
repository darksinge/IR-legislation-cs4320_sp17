FORECLOSURE CLARIFICATIONS                                 GENERAL SESSION                          STATE OF UTAH                             Chief Sponsor   Gregory H. Hughes                                Senate Sponsor                                                                                 LONG TITLE                          General Description                               This bill requires lenders to foreclose against real estate before pursuing remedies from                           a guarantor.                           Highlighted Provisions                                This bill                                .    modifies the  one action rule  to clarify that only one action may be pursued with                           regard to obligations secured by a mortgage or trust deed                                .    defines  obligation,   action,  and  fair market value                                 .    creates consistency between the  one action rule  and the Utah Trust Deed Act  and                               .    makes technical corrections.                           Monies Appropriated in this Bill                                None                           Other Special Clauses                                None                           Utah Code Sections Affected                            AMENDS                                       , as last amended by Laws of Utah     , Chapter                                     B      , as last amended by Laws of Utah     , Chapter     and renumbered and                           amended by Laws of Utah     , Chapter                                                         Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah                                Section  .    Section                is amended to read                                        .  Sale of trust property by trustee    Action to recover balance due upon                           obligation for which trust deed was given as security    Collection of costs and attorney                           fees.                                    For purposes of this section,  fair market value  means the price which would                          result from a mutual agreement between a vendor who is willing, but not compelled to sell, and                          a willing purchaser.                                    At any time within three months after any sale of property under a trust deed as                           provided in Sections                ,                , and                , an action may be commenced to recover                                 a   the balance due upon the obligation for which the trust deed was given as security ,                          and in that action the complaint shall set forth                                  b   any obligation relating to the obligation for which the trust deed was given as                          surety  or                                c   the entire amount of the indebtedness that was secured by the trust deed, the amount                           for which the property was sold, and the fair market value of the property at the date of sale.                                    a   Before rendering judgment ,  with respect to any obligation relating to the                          obligation for which a trust deed is given as surety, the court shall find the fair market value of                           the property at the date of sale.                                b   The court may not render judgment for more than the amount by which the amount                           of the indebtedness with interest, costs, and expenses of sale, including trustee s and attorney s                           fees, exceeds the fair market value of the property as of the date of the sale.  In any action                           brought under this section, the prevailing party shall be entitled to collect its costs and                           reasonable attorney fees incurred.                                c   A judgment may be rendered with respect to an obligation incurred in connection to                          an obligation secured by a trust deed only after the court finds the fair market value, as of the                          date of sale, of the property subject to the trust deed.                                    No person may waive or modify any restrictions on the court or the court s                          obligations under this section, except that a trust deed may by express language, prohibit the                          recovery of any balance due after property is sold pursuant to the trustee s power of sale or the                          trust deed is foreclosed.                                       This section applies to a foreclosure action under a trust deed or a mortgage.                               Section  .    Section      B           is amended to read                                   B      .  Form of action    Judgment    Special execution.                                    For purposes of this chapter and Title   , Chapter  , Conveyances                                 a    action  includes any foreclosure on a mortgage, trust deed or similar instrument,                          and the obtaining of a judgment for payment or performance against a guarantor, indemnitor, or                          surety  and                                b    obligation  includes any guarantee, indemnity, or other obligation of a guarantor,                          indemnitor, or surety.                                          There is only one action for the recovery of  any  the following and that action                          shall be in accordance with this chapter                                 a   any debt or the enforcement of any right or obligation secured  solely  by a                           mortgage or trust deed upon real estate  and  that action shall be in accordance with the                          provisions of this chapter.                                 b   any right or obligation created in connection with a debt or right obligation secured                          by a mortgage or trust deed upon real estate.                                          A judgment shall include                                 a   the amount due, with costs and disbursements                                 b   an order for the sale of mortgaged property, or a portion of it to satisfy the amount                           and accruing costs                                 c   direction to the sheriff to proceed and sell the property according to the provisions                           of law relating to sales on execution  and                                d   a special execution or order of sale  shall be  issued for that purpose.                                    A person may not waive or modify any of the restrictions on the court or the court s                          obligations under this section, except that a mortgage may, by express language, prohibit the                          recovery of any balance due after the mortgage is foreclosed.
